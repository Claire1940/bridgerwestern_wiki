## Bridge Constructor Stunts: Hardware Store Achievement

One of the most sought-after milestones in the stunt-based bridge building world is the "Hardware Store" achievement. This requires the player to collect 25 bolts scattered across various levels. While there are more than 25 bolts available in the game, missing early ones can make the endgame grind much harder.

### Bolt Locations (Campaign 1)

To secure this achievement, you must complete the level after collecting the bolt for it to count toward your total.

| Level | Difficulty | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-1** | Easy | Drive slowly over the second drop; no construction is required for this bolt. |
| **1-3** | Medium | Jump the first ramp, then decelerate significantly to avoid overshooting the bolt. |
| **1-4** | Easy | Maintain high speed in the second area to reach the bolt positioned high in the air. |
| **1-5** | Hard | Build two specific ramps. Aim the second ramp toward the bolt rather than the star. |
| **1-6** | Medium | Construct a simple bridge and time your jump to land at the very start of the structure. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself overshooting a bolt, try tapping the brakes mid-air to adjust your trajectory. Precision is more important than speed for the Hardware Store achievement.

## West of Loathing: The 100% Completion Roadmap

For those following a **bridger western achievements guide** for RPGs, *West of Loathing* offers a massive list of missable objectives. Achieving 100% requires at least two playthroughs, specifically to see different partner outcomes and class-specific perks like the Cowpuncher.

### The "Handful of Cutlets" Challenge
To earn this achievement, you must leave the starting area of Boring Springs with exactly **1,337 Meat**. This requires meticulous resource management and avoiding any unnecessary spending.

| Action | Meat/Item Gain | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ask Mom for Meat** | +6 Meat | Read the book first to boost stats. |
| **Kill Gary the Goblin** | XP & Progression | Essential for the ruthless perk later. |
| **Trade Dynamite** | Needle | Trade with the Sheriff’s office for a needle. |
| **Sell Silver Nugget** | Variable Meat | Sell all unrefined ore to the "Crazy People" vendor. |
| **Gambling** | High Reward | Level up Moxie twice and bet "crazily" to win big. |

### Building the Great Bridge
A major turning point in your **bridger western achievements guide** journey involves the railroad company. You are tasked with helping them cross gaps, and your choice of bridge material dictates your rewards and specific achievements.

1.  **The Wooden Bridge:** Requires solving all problems in the town of Breadwood. This is the "standard" path.
2.  **The Bone Bridge:** Requires high "Nex-Mex" skills. You must read at least five Nex-Mex books to unlock the ability to construct a bridge out of skeletons.
3.  **The El Vibrato Bridge:** Involves finding the humming cave and repairing ancient technology using scrap and punch cards.

## Bounty Hunting: Wanted Dead or Alive

The "Wanted" achievements are binary. You can either arrest bandits for the law-abiding achievements or eliminate them for the ruthless ones. For a "Wanted Live" run, you must utilize the jail system efficiently.

*   **The Outhouse Trick:** In the town with big buildings, lure the bandits toward the jail by interacting with them in a specific order. Once they are positioned, cut the rope at the outhouse to trap them.
*   **The Ghostly Pickles:** Visit the abandoned pickle factory. Align the levers based on the ghost woman's clues and set the furnace to exactly 90 degrees to secure ghost pickles, a powerful consumable for hard mode.
*   **The Dog Oil:** Buy a can of oil from the wandering shop to fix the gate at the House in the Desert, allowing you to lock the final bandits inside.

## Advanced Strategies: El Vibrato and Nex-Mex

To truly master the **bridger western achievements guide** requirements for 2026, you must delve into the "weird" science of the West. The El Vibrato storyline is particularly complex, requiring the player to manage power pipelines and satellite orientations.

### Roberto Containment Steps
1.  **Locate the Deepest Oath Mine:** Use the "percussive maintenance" skill (kicking things) to fix the elevator.
2.  **Collect Keystones:** These are found in lockers (requires Safecracking) and hidden wells.
3.  **Initialize the System:** Navigate to the terminal on Floor 4 of the mine and select "Initialize Roberto Containment" to trigger the achievement.

> **Warning:** Entering the Necromancer's Tower is a point of no return for many side quests. Ensure you have completed the El Vibrato and Breadwood quests before ascending.

## Hard Mode: The "Hard Hat" Run

For the ultimate challenge, players must wear the "Hard Hat" found early in the game. This locks the game into a higher difficulty where mistakes are punished severely.

*   **No Sleeping:** You cannot sleep to recover health or reset the day, as this removes the Hard Hat's effect.
*   **Gumption is King:** In hard mode, speed (Gumption) is the most important stat. You must act first in every encounter to avoid taking lethal damage.
*   **Limited Farming:** Unlike normal mode, enemies in areas like the Daveyard will eventually run out. You cannot grind infinitely for XP.
*   **Class Choice:** Ranged classes are generally safer for Hard Mode, as they allow you to apply debuffs or finish enemies before they close the distance.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the Bone Bridge and Wooden Bridge achievements in one save?**

A: No. These are mutually exclusive based on how you choose to help the railroad company. You will need to use a second playthrough or a strategic save-file backup to earn both using this **bridger western achievements guide**.

**Q: What happens if I lose a fight in Hard Mode?**

A: If you lose a fight while wearing the Hard Hat, you may lose the "Hard Mode" status for that run. It is highly recommended to keep your "Grit" stat high to increase your pain tolerance and prevent immediate failure.

**Q: How do I find the hidden Nex-Mex books?**

A: Nex-Mex books are scattered in various locations, including the Buffalo Pile, the Military Cemetery (Plot 666), and occasionally as drops from "Ghost Stories" encounters while wandering.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many bolts I can miss for the Hardware Store achievement?**

A: Yes, though the game provides a few extra bolts across the campaign. Generally, you can miss about 3-5 bolts and still reach the 25 required for the achievement, but it is safer to collect them as you go.
