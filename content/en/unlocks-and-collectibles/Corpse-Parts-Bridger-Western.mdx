> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not tunnel on “best” spots. In Corpse Parts Bridger Western, the right spawn is the one your team can lock down *first* and exit from safely.

### Route Priority for Faster Conversion

1. **If you spawn near town:** Check Dig Site → Trackside Hill → rotate to Death Valley.
2. **If you spawn near fishing:** Check Watchtower → Ant Hill Island → Swamp → Grassland.
3. **If server is chaotic:** Hold one cluster (town side *or* fishing side) and deny crosses.

This reduces wasted travel and increases first-touch chances when the beam appears.

## Best Farming Strategy: Solo, Duo, and Full Squad

You can farm alone, but coordinated play scales much better because spawn chance and defense both improve with organized numbers.

### Recommended Team Structure

| Team Size | Roles | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Solo** | 1 flex runner | Off-hours, low population servers |
| **Duo** | 1 carrier + 1 overwatch | Quick rotations and safer merges |
| **4-6 players** | 2 scouts + 2 guards + 1 reserve | Balanced control in medium lobbies |
| **8-10+ players** | Sector squads + queue manager | High-efficiency farming sessions |

For large groups, use a simple queue (first in, first claim) so internal conflict does not kill momentum.

### High-Efficiency Public Server Method

1. **Enter a low/medium population server together.**
2. **Invite until near full** for better spawn odds.
3. **Split map into two clusters** (town side vs fishing/swamp side).
4. **Call beam fast** with exact landmark name.
5. **Assign claim rights by queue order.**
6. **Escort carrier through merge and exit.**
7. **Reset to sectors, repeat.**

This method increases trust and keeps your group farming instead of arguing.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your squad has no claim rules, you will lose more parts to friendly chaos than to enemy players.

## Loadout, Positioning, and Escape Fundamentals

Winning in Corpse Parts Bridger Western is less about “best weapon” and more about smart positioning around vertical paths, choke points, and line-of-sight denial.

### Positioning Principles

- **High ground first:** Death Valley rock and watchtower top reward defenders.
- **Don’t overstack:** Two defenders can cover one carrier; extras should screen approaches.
- **Plan exit before pickup:** Know your fallback route when you touch the part.
- **Use horse for long transfers, dismount for tight climbs:** Especially near tower routes.

### Escape Priority by Spawn Type

| Spawn Type | First Move After Pickup | Backup Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Pit/Dig Site** | Climb immediate rope/edge path | Smoke movement with crossfire support |
| **Single-exit hill** | Hold choke with one defender | Rotate only after pulse progress |
| **Island/Swamp** | Move toward firmer terrain | Re-enter island if outnumbered |
| **Tower top** | Hold stairs/ledge angles | Drop only with team cover |

If you play solo, avoid low-ground trap spawns unless nearby enemies are confirmed far. If you run a group, bait enemy pushes into narrow climbs where your defenders can trade efficiently.
