## Locating the Seamstress in Richman County

The journey to obtaining your advanced gear begins in Richman County. This area serves as a central hub for many players, but the specific NPC you need is tucked away slightly off the main thoroughfare. To find the Seamstress, start your journey from the Richman County Bank.

Follow the main road and keep an eye out for a specialized tailor shop. The Seamstress NPC is unique, featuring custom animations that set her apart from standard static vendors. Engaging with her is the only way to access the legitimate Advanced Poncho at the lowest possible market price. While other shops might claim to offer similar gear, the Seamstress is the primary source for the 2026 update content.

## Understanding Advanced Poncho Stats

Once you have purchased your first poncho for 800 Moolah, you can view its current attributes by pressing the **M** key. This opens your character menu where the poncho's specific buffs are listed. 

One of the most important things to realize about the **bridger western poncho guide** mechanics is that the numbers displayed are percentage-based. Many players are initially confused because the UI does not always show the "%" symbol next to the numbers. However, through rigorous community testing, it has been confirmed that these values modify your base damage and health stats. For example, a "5" in damage doesn't mean +5 flat damage; it means a 5% increase to your total output.

### Why Percentage Stats Matter
In the late-game stages of Bridger: Western, your base stats will naturally increase through leveling and better weaponry. Because the poncho provides a percentage boost, its value actually increases as you get stronger. A 10% boost on a starter revolver is minor, but a 10% boost on a high-tier rifle is massive.

## How to Reroll Poncho Stats

It is highly unlikely that your first purchase will grant you "God Tier" stats. Most players will find themselves with low rolls or attributes that don't fit their playstyle (such as defensive stats when they want a glass-cannon build). This is where the rerolling mechanic comes into play.

To reroll, you must return to the Seamstress. Unlike the initial purchase, rerolling does not cost any Moolah. This makes it one of the most player-friendly progression systems in the game. However, you cannot reroll for free; you must provide the Seamstress with a **Poncho Blueprint**.

### The Rerolling Process
1.  Equip the poncho you wish to change.
2.  Ensure you have at least one **Poncho Blueprint** in your inventory.
3.  Speak to the Seamstress and select the reroll option.
4.  Confirm the change (Note: This will overwrite your previous stats entirely).

| Item Needed | Source | Cost | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Poncho Blueprint** | Fishing Chests | Free (Time Investment) | **Common** |
| **Moolah** | Bounties/Jobs | N/A | Variable |

## Farming Poncho Blueprints via Fishing

Since rerolling is free in terms of currency, your only bottleneck is the collection of Blueprints. Fortunately, these are not rare drops. The most efficient way to gather them is through the fishing mechanic.

To start farming, head to the nearest fishing spot from the tailor's shop. You don't need expensive bait or high-end rods to find Blueprints, though better gear may speed up the rate at which you catch chests. When fishing, you are looking specifically for **Chests** rather than fish. These chests have a very high drop rate for Poncho Blueprints, often yielding two or more blueprints from a single successful catch.

### Fishing Strategy for Blueprints
*   **Location:** Any standard fishing hole works, but staying near Richman County minimizes travel time back to the Seamstress.
*   **Efficiency:** Focus on "chest" ripples if the game's visual cues allow for it.
*   **Inventory Management:** Clear your inventory before a session, as you may end up with a high volume of blueprints quickly.

> **💡 Tip:** Since blueprints are common, don't settle for "average" stats. Keep a stack of 10-20 blueprints and reroll until you hit a damage percentage that significantly impacts your TTK (Time to Kill).

## FAQ

**Q: Can I trade Poncho Blueprints with other players?**

A: Currently, blueprints are tied to your inventory for personal use at the Seamstress. However, because they are common drops from fishing chests, most players find it easier to farm them than to trade for them.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many times I can reroll my poncho?**

A: No, there is no hard limit on rerolls. As long as you have a Poncho Blueprint in your inventory, the Seamstress will allow you to attempt a new stat roll. This is why the **bridger western poncho guide** emphasizes fishing as a core part of the gear-grinding process.

**Q: Do the poncho stats apply to all weapons or just my primary?**

A: The percentage-based buffs provided by the Advanced Poncho apply to your character's overall output, meaning both your primary and secondary weapons will benefit from the damage increase.

**Q: What is the maximum percentage roll possible?**

A: While the exact cap is subject to balance patches in 2026, most players report that rolls between 8% and 12% for damage are considered high-tier. If you see a double-digit percentage, it is generally recommended to keep that roll.
