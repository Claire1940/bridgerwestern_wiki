## The Hardware Store Achievement: Collecting Bolts

One of the primary goals for any completionist is the "Hardware Store" achievement. This specific milestone requires you to collect 25 bolts throughout the game. While there are more than 25 bolts available across all levels, missing them early on can force you to backtrack through difficult stages later. 

The key to this achievement is not just reaching the end of the level, but doing so after successfully making contact with the bolt icon. Note that you must complete the level successfully for the bolt count to register in your permanent progress. If you crash after grabbing a bolt, you will need to restart the attempt.

| Level | Bolt Location | Recommended Strategy | Difficulty |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|------------|
| **1-1** | Second Drive | Drive slowly over the second platform. | Very Low |
| **1-3** | Mid-Air | Moderate speed; avoid over-jumping the ramp. | Low |

> **💡 Tip:** You do not need to collect the star and the bolt in the same run to earn the achievement, but you must reach the finish line for the bolt to count toward your total.

## Level-by-Level Walkthrough

Navigating the early stages of the campaign sets the foundation for the more complex engineering feats required in the later chapters. Below are detailed strategies for the initial levels where bolts are found, ensuring your **bridger western achievement guide** progress remains on track.

### Level 1-1 to 1-3: Basic Maneuvers
In Level 1-1, the challenge is minimal. You don't need to build anything; simply drive slowly over the second drive to pick up your first bolt. Level 1-3 introduces the concept of speed control. You will encounter three ramps. While it is tempting to floor the accelerator, you should jump the first ramp normally but slow down significantly for the second and third. If you go too fast, you will sail right over the bolt and miss it entirely.

### Level 1-4: The High Jump
Level 1-4 is the first real test of your vehicle's power. The bolt is positioned high in the second area. To reach it, you must "floor it"—maintain maximum speed through the transition to ensure your arc is high enough to clip the bolt. This level demonstrates that "slow and steady" isn't always the answer in a **bridger western achievement guide**.

### Level 1-5: Custom Ramp Construction
This is the first level where building becomes mandatory for bolt collection. You will need to construct a series of ramps. Instead of building toward the star, focus your construction on a trajectory that leads directly through the bolt. 

1.  Build the first ramp with a steep incline.
2.  Construct the second ramp on the plateaus to bridge the gap.
3.  Ensure the angle points toward the bolt rather than the level exit.

## Advanced Engineering Principles

As you progress through the **bridger western achievement guide**, the simple wooden planks of the early game will give way to more complex materials. Understanding the stress limits of your materials is vital for the "Stunt" portion of the game, where your bridges must survive not just the weight of the vehicle, but the impact of a landing.

### Material Selection and Stress
| Material | Cost | Strength | Best Use Case |
|----------|------|----------|---------------|
| **Wood** | Low | Low | Basic roadbeds and light supports. |
| **Steel** | High | High | Long spans and heavy landing zones. |
| **Cables** | Medium | High (Tension) | Suspending bridges over deep ravines. |

When building for achievements, always over-engineer your landing zones. A vehicle landing from a high jump exerts significantly more force than a vehicle simply driving across. Use steel reinforcements at the points where the wheels first make contact with the structure.

## Physics and Stunt Mastery

The "Stunts" aspect of the game adds a layer of complexity to the traditional bridge-building formula. To unlock all achievements in 2026, you must master the art of the mid-air flip. 

*   **Front-flips:** Lean forward during high-altitude jumps to rotate the vehicle. This is often necessary to align the wheels for a flat landing on a slanted ramp.
*   **Back-flips:** Useful for slowing down forward momentum if you are at risk of overshooting a bolt or a landing zone.
*   **Speed Management:** Use the brake mid-air to stabilize the vehicle.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Excessive rotation can lead to a "head-first" crash. Always prioritize a level landing over extra rotations unless a specific achievement requires a stunt combo.

## Strategies for Completionists

To fully satisfy the requirements of a **bridger western achievement guide**, you should aim to minimize costs while maximizing stunt points. Many levels have hidden "budget" achievements that require you to spend less than a certain amount of money on materials.

1.  **Use Triangles:** The triangle is the strongest shape in engineering. Always build your supports in triangular patterns to distribute weight effectively.
2.  **Check Stress View:** Before running your vehicle, use the simulation view to identify red "stress" points. If a beam is red before the car even moves, it will definitely break during the stunt.
3.  **Iterative Design:** Don't be afraid to delete and rebuild. Sometimes a slightly different angle on a ramp can be the difference between a successful bolt collection and a total structural failure.

The frontier is a harsh place for an engineer, but with the right blueprints and a bit of daring, the "Hardware Store" achievement and others will be yours. Keep your speed in check, your beams reinforced, and your eyes on the bolts.

## FAQ

**Q: How many bolts do I actually need for the Hardware Store achievement?**

A: You need to collect 25 bolts in total. While there are more than 25 available in the campaign, this **bridger western achievement guide** recommends picking them up as early as possible to avoid replaying the much harder late-game levels.

**Q: Do I have to survive the jump to keep the bolt?**

A: Yes. You must successfully reach the finish line and complete the level for any bolts collected during that run to be added to your total count. If your vehicle is destroyed, the bolt is not saved.

**Q: Can I use different materials to get the same achievement?**

A: Absolutely. As long as you meet the level's objectives and collect the required items, the materials you use (wood, steel, or cable) do not affect the achievement itself, though they will affect your final score and budget.

**Q: What is the most common reason for failing a bolt collection?**

A: Usually, it is a matter of speed. Many players drive too fast and jump over the bolt. Learning when to let off the gas is just as important as knowing when to floor it in this **bridger western achievement guide**.
