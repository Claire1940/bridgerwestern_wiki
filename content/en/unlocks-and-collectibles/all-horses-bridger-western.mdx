## Finding the Horse Seller and Initial Selection

Before you can start building your stable, you need to know where to go. The primary horse seller is located near the fishing docks and the tackle base store. If you are familiar with the starter areas, simply head toward the water and follow the path leading away from the main hub. The seller stands near a corral where you can view potential purchases.

When interacting with the horse seller, you are presented with two main options: "I'll take it" or "Show me another." The "Show me another" option acts as a reroll mechanic. Each time you ask to see another horse, the game generates a new set of stats, a new name, and a new price point. It is important to remember that you are not just looking at a static list; you are essentially "spinning" for a better roll.

| Action | Result | Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I'll take it** | Finalizes the purchase and adds the horse to your stable. | Current listed price |
| **Show me another** | Rerolls the horse's name, appearance, rarity, and stats. | Small reroll fee |
| **View Stats** | Allows you to see the potential of the horse before buying. | **Free** |

> **💡 Tip:** Never settle for the first horse you see. If you have the currency, rerolling a few times can often lead to a "Top of the line" horse with significantly better base stats.

## Understanding Rarity: Legendary vs. Mythical

One of the most confusing aspects for players is the naming convention used for high-tier horses. Unlike other games that use clear color-coded UI elements, Bridger: Western utilizes text formatting to denote rarity. All high-rarity horses will have their names displayed in **ALL CAPS**. However, the brackets surrounding the name tell the real story of the horse's potential.

### Identifying Brackets
*   **Legendary Horses:** These horses feature standard, thin brackets (e.g., `[OLD MOON]`). A Legendary horse receives a significant boost to **one** specific stat, allowing it to exceed the standard maximum.
*   **Mythical Horses:** These are the pinnacle of the horse system. They feature thick, bold brackets (e.g., `[[BEAST]]`). A Mythical horse receives a stat boost to **two** different attributes, making them objectively superior for multi-purpose builds.

## The 1.25 Stat Threshold

In Bridger: Western, standard horses are limited by a hard cap on their attributes. No "normal" horse can have a stat higher than **1.25**. This is where the Legendary and Mythical tiers become vital. These rare variants are the only ones capable of "breaking" this limit.

A Legendary horse might have a Speed stat of 1.35 or 1.40. Because this exceeds the 1.25 limit, the horse is automatically categorized as Legendary. However, it is a common misconception that every Legendary horse is "good." If a horse is Legendary because its *Jump* stat is 1.30, but its Speed and Stamina are only 0.80, it will still perform poorly in a race.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always check which specific stat is boosted. A Legendary horse with a boost in an undesirable stat is often worse than a high-priced normal horse with balanced, high-tier stats.

## Key Stats to Prioritize

When evaluating **all horses bridger western** stats, focus on the attributes that impact your daily gameplay the most. While there are four primary stats, two stand out as the most critical for survival and efficiency.

1.  **Speed:** Determines your maximum velocity. Essential for outrunning bounty hunters or reaching objectives quickly.
2.  **Stamina:** Determines how long you can maintain a full gallop. High stamina is often more useful than raw speed for long-distance travel.
3.  **Jump:** Affects the height and distance of your leaps. Useful for navigating mountainous terrain.
4.  **Damage:** Influences the impact damage dealt if you trample NPCs or other players.

**Q: How do I know if I found a Mythical horse?**

A: Look for two things: the name must be in ALL CAPS, and it must be surrounded by thick, double brackets like `[[THIS]]`. If it has single brackets `[THIS]`, it is only a Legendary.

**Q: What is the best way to get enough money for all horses bridger western top-tiers?**

A: The most efficient way is to participate in community "flooding" events or farm corpse parts in low-population servers. This allows you to consistently earn high-value items to sell or trade for currency.
