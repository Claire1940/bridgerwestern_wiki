## Overview of Saint's Corpse Parts

There are five primary corpse parts currently available in the game, along with one hidden variant used for specific evolutions. When a player successfully merges with a part, they are granted a Stand—a powerful spiritual manifestation with unique combat abilities.

| Corpse Part | Potential Stands | Special Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ribcage** | Star Platinum, The World, The World AU, etc. | Standard spawn part. |
| **Left Arm** | Tusk (10% chance), Star Platinum, The World | Only part that grants Tusk. |
| **Right Arm** | Star Platinum, The World, The World AU | Standard spawn part. |
| **Left Leg** | Star Platinum, The World, The World AU | Standard spawn part. |

Each part (excluding the Heart) has an equal base chance to grant most Stands, but rare Stands like Star Platinum and The World (including the Alternate Universe version) have significantly lower drop rates. If you are specifically targeting Tusk, you must focus exclusively on obtaining the Left Arm.

## Spawn Mechanics and Probabilities

Corpse parts do not stay on the map permanently. They spawn dynamically based on the number of players currently in the server. Every 30 seconds, the game "rolls" to see if a part will appear. A global message appears on the screen of every player in the server when a spawn is successful, accompanied by a massive beam of light that points directly to the location.

To optimize your farming, you should understand the 2026 spawn rates:

| Players in Server | Spawn Chance (Per 30 Seconds) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1-2 Players** | Extremely low / Null |
| **3+ Players** | 1 in 150 |
| **Full Server** | 1 in 40 |

> **💡 Tip:** Always try to play in full servers if you are hunting parts. While the competition is higher, the frequency of spawns is nearly four times higher than in low-population servers.

## All 7 Corpse Part Spawn Locations

Knowing where to look the moment that light beam appears can mean the difference between getting a Stand and getting sent back to the graveyard. Here are the seven confirmed locations as of 2026.

### 1. The Dig Site
Located to the left of the main bridge exiting the town. You will see a cluster of tents in the distance. The spawn is at the very bottom of the excavation pit. 
*   **Pros:** Out of the line of sight from the main road.
*   **Cons:** Extremely difficult to escape. The rope climbs are slow, making you a sitting duck for snipers.

### 2. The Train Track Path
Follow the train tracks to the right of the town (opposite the train's direction). Look for a small path leading uphill to the left. 
*   **Pros:** Large open area where you can spot enemies approaching.
*   **Cons:** Only one primary exit, making it easy for a gang to corner you.

### 3. Death Valley High Rock
Continue following the train tracks deep into Death Valley. The spawn is located on a high rock formation along the valley walls. 
*   **Pros:** One of the best defensive spots. You can shoot anyone trying to climb up.
*   **Cons:** Requires precise movement to reach.
*   **How to reach:** Stand on your horse and fire a weapon (shotgun/revolver) to gain a slight height boost or use the horse to mantle onto the ledge.

### 4. The Watchtower
Near the fishing area, there is a mountain with a wooden watchtower. You must climb the rocks on foot to reach the top.
*   **Pros:** Total visibility of the surrounding area.
*   **Cons:** Mantling the ledge at the top is glitchy. Do not run while climbing the final ladder, or you may fall off.

### 5. Ant Hill Island
Located in the water to the right of the fishing hut. This is a small, isolated island.
*   **Pros:** Many players ignore this area, making it a "sleeper" spot for quiet farming.
*   **Cons:** You are exposed while swimming or riding your horse through the water.

### 6. The Swamp
Directly across from the fishing hut and Ant Hill Island. The part spawns in the murky center of the swamp.
*   **Pros:** Easy to see the light beam from a distance.
*   **Cons:** The water slows your movement, making it hard to dodge bullets.

### 7. The Grasslands
To the left of the swamp area, there is an open field with several large rocks. The part usually spawns near a specific central rock formation.
*   **Pros:** Very easy to navigate on horseback.
*   **Cons:** No cover. If a fight breaks out, it becomes a pure test of aim.

## The Merging Process

Simply picking up a part isn't enough. Once you have it, you must "merge" with it. This is a timed event where your character will pulse with energy. 

*   **Merge Time:** This is randomized. It can take as little as 10 seconds or as long as 2 minutes.
*   **Vulnerability:** You cannot move effectively while merging. This is the most dangerous time for a player.
*   **Death:** If you are killed during the merging process, you drop the part, and your progress is reset.

> **Warning:** If you see a player merging, they are likely being guarded by a posse. Do not approach unless you are prepared for a high-stakes shootout.

## 2026 Farming Strategies

The most efficient way to use this **bridger western corpse parts guide** is to play with a dedicated group. Solo farming is possible but extremely high-risk.

1.  **Server Filling:** Join a server with 5-10 friends. This artificially inflates the player count to hit the 1/40 spawn rate while ensuring that most players in the server are friendly to you.
2.  **The Priority List:** Establish an order of who gets the next part. This prevents infighting and ensures the group stays focused on protection rather than greed.

4.  **Observation Posts:** Position players at the Watchtower and Death Valley. These two spots provide the best vantage points to see which direction the beam of light is spawning in.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I have more than one Stand at a time?**

A: No, merging with a new corpse part will overwrite your current Stand. Make sure you are willing to lose your current ability before picking up a new part.

**Q: How do I get the secret Heart part?**

A: The Heart is a secret item used specifically for D4C. It involves a much more complex questline than the standard parts found via the light beams. Most players obtain it through specific high-level NPC interactions or extremely rare hidden world events.

**Q: Does the time of day affect the bridger western corpse parts guide spawn rates?**

A: No, the day/night cycle in Bridger: Western does not impact the 30-second spawn roll. Only the player count in the server changes the mathematical probability.

**Q: What happens if I disconnect while holding a corpse part?**

A: If you disconnect before the merging process is 100% complete, the part is lost and will likely respawn in the world after the next 30-second interval check. Always ensure you have a stable connection before attempting to merge.
