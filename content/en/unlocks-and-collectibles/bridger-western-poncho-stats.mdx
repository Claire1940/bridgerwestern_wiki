<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the 10-Point Stat System">

The core of the **bridger western poncho stats** system revolves around a fixed pool of 10 points. Unlike other RPGs where stats might scale infinitely, this game uses a balanced distribution method. This ensures that no single player becomes overly "broken," while still allowing for specialized builds.

The points are split between three primary categories: **Defense, Damage, and Speed**. If you roll a poncho with high defense, your points in damage or speed will naturally be lower to maintain the 10-point equilibrium.

| Stat Type | Effect on Player | Max Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defense** | Reduces incoming damage from enemies and players | 10.0 (if solo stat) |
| **Damage** | Increases the output of your equipped weaponry | 10.0 (if solo stat) |
| **Speed** | Enhances movement velocity and agility | 10.0 (if solo stat) |

<Section icon={Trophy} title="How to Obtain and Craft Advanced Ponchos">

To move beyond the basic gear, you must engage with the blueprint system. Advanced **bridger western poncho stats** are only available through random rolls initiated by blueprints. These items are rare and require specific activities to acquire.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Find a Blueprint">
    Blueprints can be found by fishing in crates at Amir's Lake or by discovering rare loot drops in high-tier desert areas.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Visit the Blueprint Girl">
    Take your blueprint to the specific female NPC located near the Tailor in the desert city.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate the Roll">
    Select the option "I have a blueprint right here." This will consume the blueprint and generate a random poncho design.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Verify Your Stats">
    Press 'M' to see how the 10 points were distributed. If you aren't happy with the roll, you will need to find another blueprint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Acquisition Method | Location | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fishing (Crates)** | Amir's Lake | Common / Uncommon |
| **Desert Looting** | Sunken Ruins | Rare |
| **Bounty Rewards** | Sheriff's Office | Legendary |

<Callout type="success" title="Stat Floor Bonus">
Even if a stat rolls below 1.0, you do not receive a debuff. You will always receive at least a "very small buff" regardless of how low the number appears, ensuring your gear is always an improvement over nothing.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Gear Comparison: Basic vs. Advanced">

Choosing between a standard store-bought poncho and an advanced crafted version depends on your current resources. While basic ponchos offer stability, advanced versions are the only way to reach the 10-point cap for specific **bridger western poncho stats**.

| Feature | Basic Poncho | Advanced Poncho |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stat Total** | 1.0 - 3.0 Total | Fixed 10.0 Total |
| **Customization** | Standard Colors | Unique Designs / Patterns |
| **Requirement** | Gold Only | Blueprint + Gold |
| **Stat Control** | Predictable / Low | Random / High Potential |

The advanced poncho is essentially the "end-game" for character outfitting. Because the stats are rolled randomly, players often spend hours fishing at Amir's Lake just to get enough blueprints for that perfect 8.0 Damage / 2.0 Speed split.

<Callout type="info" title="Optimization Tip">
If you are just starting, don't worry about the 10-point cap. A basic poncho with 2.0 Defense is significantly better for early-game survival than having no poncho at all.
</Callout>
