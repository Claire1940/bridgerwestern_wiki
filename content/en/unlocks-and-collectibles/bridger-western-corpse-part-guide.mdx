## Understanding Corpse Part Mechanics

Before heading out into the wilderness, you must understand how the spawning system works. Unlike standard loot or fishing rewards, Corpse Parts are world events that trigger globally across the server.

When a Corpse Part spawns, a notification text will appear in the sky, followed by a massive beam of light that is visible from almost anywhere on the map. This beam serves as a beacon for every player in the session. Once a player picks up the part, the beam disappears, but the holder's struggle is only beginning.

| Mechanic | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global Notification** | Text appears in the sky signaling a spawn has occurred. |
| **Visual Beacon** | A bright beam of light marks the exact location of the part. |
| **The Shine** | Holders pulse with a white light every few seconds, revealing their position. |
| **Drop on Death** | If a player holding a part is killed, the item drops to the ground for others. |
| **Merging** | Every few seconds, there is a chance the part will "merge" with the holder, granting a Stand. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Picking up a Corpse Part makes you a primary target. The "Shine" effect ensures you cannot hide for long, so having a fast horse is mandatory for survival.

## All Corpse Part Spawn Locations

Finding a part requires knowing the map's hotspots. While spawns can feel random, they occur at specific fixed points. Using this **bridger western corpse part guide** list will allow you to position yourself near multiple potential spots to minimize travel time.

### 1. Taco Bay & Fishing Village
This is arguably the most active area for spawns. There are three distinct points here:
*   **The Tower:** A part can spawn at the very top of the wooden tower in Taco Bay.
*   **The Shop Front:** Directly across from the Taco Bay shop.
*   **The Bridge Area:** Near the bridge connecting the village to the mainland.

### 2. The Dig Site
Located near Rich B County, the Dig Site is a high-risk location. The part spawns at the bottom of the excavation pit. 
*   **Risk Factor:** High. You must use a rope to climb out, making you an easy target for players hovering at the top of the pit.

### 3. Rich B County (Upper Ridges)
Near the county borders, parts often spawn on the elevated rocky plateaus. These are safer than the Dig Site because they offer multiple escape routes across the ridges.

### 4. The Wilderness (Hidden Rock)
In the open plains between the main towns, there is a spawn point tucked behind a large rock formation. This is a "luck-based" find for players who happen to be traveling between zones.

### 5. The Valley
This is the furthest spawn point from the main hubs. While it is less crowded, the distance makes it difficult to reach if you aren't already nearby. Taking the train is the fastest way to reach this zone.

| Location | Difficulty | Best Escape Route |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Taco Bay Tower** | Medium | Jump down and head toward the train tracks. |
| **Dig Site Pit** | Extreme | Requires a fast climb; highly susceptible to camping. |
| **Rich B Heights** | Low | Ride across the open desert toward the Hotel. |
| **The Valley** | High | Use the train or follow the riverbed. |

## How to Obtain D4C (Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap)

While most Stands can be obtained through the standard merging process or Stand Arrows (shards from fishing), **D4C** requires a much more specific and grueling ritual. Simply finding a part is not enough.

To unlock D4C, you must follow these exact steps:
1.  **Accumulate 100 Total Kills:** This is a lifetime stat across all servers.
2.  **Achieve a 10-Player Killstreak:** This must be done in a single server without dying.
3.  **Go to the Desert:** Once you have the streak, move to the desert biome.
4.  **Get Knocked:** You must be in the "downed" state (knocked) while holding your killstreak.
5.  **Spawn Event:** While knocked, a Corpse Part must spawn in the world.
6.  **The Heart Merge:** There is a **10% RNG chance** that the "Heart" part will merge with you while you are knocked, granting D4C.

> **💡 Tip:** Because of the 10% RNG, many players fail this multiple times. It is recommended to have a friend protect you while you are knocked in the desert to prevent other players from finishing you off.

## Recommended Gear for Part Hunting

If you intend to follow this **bridger western corpse part guide** to success, you cannot go out in basic clothes. You need gear that mitigates damage and increases your mobility.

*   **The Cowboy Hat:** Purchased at the General Store. It acts as a "one-hit" shield for headshots. The first shot to your head will simply knock the hat off, dealing zero damage.
*   **The Poncho:** This item reduces damage taken to your torso. While it increases limb damage slightly, most players aim for the center mass, making the Poncho a net positive for survivability.
*   **Winchester Rifle:** High accuracy and damage are necessary for fending off pursuers while you wait for a part to merge.
*   **High-Speed Horse:** Visit the stables and look for a horse with a description emphasizing speed. A horse costing over 1,000 Moola is generally the baseline for competitive play.

| Item | Cost | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cowboy Hat** | Cheap | Blocks one headshot entirely. |
| **Poncho** | Moderate | Reduces torso damage significantly. |
| **Whitney Dragon** | High | Excellent sidearm for close-quarters defense. |
| **Speed Horse** | 1,000+ | Essential for outrunning "The Shine" hunters. |

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take for a Corpse Part to merge?**

A: There is no fixed timer. Every few seconds you hold the part, the game rolls a chance for it to merge. It can happen in 10 seconds or take several minutes of surviving the "Shine."

**Q: Can I lose my Stand if I die?**

A: No, once a Stand is merged with your character, it is permanent. You can only change or remove it by visiting the Witch NPC and paying Moola to reset your Stand.

**Q: What is the best way to find a part if I'm a solo player?**

A: Use this **bridger western corpse part guide** to identify the Taco Bay Tower spawn. It is the easiest to spot from a distance. Stay near the train tracks so you can quickly move between the Fishing Village and the Desert spawns.

**Q: Does the "Ocular Prowess" ability help find Corpse Parts?**

A: Ocular Prowess helps you see player highlights, which is useful for spotting the person who *has* the part, but it does not highlight the part itself before it is picked up. Use the beam of light for the initial hunt.
