## Getting Started: Character Creation and Factions

When you first launch the game, you are presented with three primary choices: Outlaws, Inlaws, and Vampires. For most new players, starting as an Outlaw is recommended due to the sheer freedom it provides. Outlaws have more flexibility in their progression and can easily access the early-game money-making spots.

Upon spawning, your first goal is to check your starting cash. You will receive a random amount between $50 and $500. If you spawn with less than $200, many veteran players suggest using the "Wipe" feature to reset and try for a higher starting purse. This initial capital is vital for purchasing your first fishing rod and bait.

| Faction | Playstyle | Key Advantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outlaws** | Aggressive/Freedom | Access to more black market gear and freedom of movement. |
| **Inlaws** | Defensive/Order | Stronger group synergy and protection in civilized areas. |
| **Vampires** | Supernatural/Stealth | Enhanced physical stats; requires blood and fears silver. |

> **💡 Tip:** Spawn at Red Corner if possible. It is the closest location to the fishing docks, which is the safest way to grind XP and money as a beginner.

## Core Controls and Movement

The movement system in this game is more complex than your average shooter. It utilizes a limb-based damage system where losing specific body parts significantly hinders your performance. For example, losing your right arm prevents you from shooting, while losing both legs will cause you to fall to the ground entirely.

| Action | Keybind | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Summon Horse** | **H** | Calls your current horse to your location. |
| **Mount/Dismount** | **N** | Enter or exit your horse's saddle. |
| **Ocular Vision** | **X** | Activates an ESP-like vision to highlight players. |
| **Melee/Hands** | **C / G** | Toggle fists or perform a melee strike with a weapon. |
| **Dash** | **Q** | Quick directional movement to dodge bullets. |

## The Supernatural Frontier: Stands and Vampires

One of the most distinct features of the game is the inclusion of Stands and the **bridger western vampire** faction. To combat vampires effectively, you must utilize Silver Bullets or Silver Stakes, as standard lead ammunition is far less effective against their regenerative hides.

### How to Obtain a Stand
There are two primary ways to gain a Stand in 2026:
1.  **Fishing:** There is a rare 0.5% chance to pull an Arrow Shard from the water while fishing. This is a time-consuming but "safe" method for solo players.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** Keep an eye on the top of your screen for a golden message stating, "It appears once again." This signals that a Corpse Part has spawned. A massive golden beam will appear on the map. If you reach it, you have a 15% to 20% chance per pulse to obtain a Stand.

### Vampire Mechanics
Playing as a vampire changes the game into a hunt for blood. While you possess superior speed and strength, you are vulnerable to specialized anti-vampire tools. If you are an Outlaw or Inlaw, always keep a stack of silver bullets in your bank to ensure you aren't caught off guard by a night-time ambush.

## Best Weapons and PvP Gear

Choosing the right firearm is a matter of balancing recoil, reload speed, and raw stopping power. The current meta in 2026 favors weapons that can end a fight in two to three well-placed shots.

### Revolvers and Rifles
*   **Whitney Dragon:** The undisputed king of damage. It has massive recoil and a slow reload, but a single hit can cripple an opponent.
*   **The Colt:** A unique choice for tactical players. Its bullets bounce off surfaces, allowing you to hit enemies hiding behind cover.
*   **Winchester Rifle:** Generally considered superior to the Mare's Leg due to better range and handling.

| Weapon Type | Top Choice | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Revolver** | **Whitney Dragon** | Highest raw damage per shot in the game. |
| **Rifle** | **Winchester** | Excellent mid-to-long range reliability. |
| **Melee** | **Saber** | Can parry bullets if you have the right talent cards. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Using a Saber prevents you from equipping guns. Only choose this path if you intend to build fully into the "Show Me a Good Time" talent card.

## Progression: The Witch and Talent Cards

As you gain XP through fishing, quests, or combat, you will reach higher Tiers (up to Tier 3). Each Tier allows you to "weave" a talent card into your character at the Witch’s Hut, located deep within the swamp.

To find the Witch, you must cross the water (spamming the jump key while on horseback is a common trick to cross quickly). For a fee of 150 Mula, she will read your fortune and offer you a selection of cards.

### Recommended Talent Cards
*   **True Cowboy:** Increases gun damage by 17% while on horseback.
*   **Veteran:** Helps players who struggle with ammo management by providing better tracking.
*   **Time Heals All Wounds:** Essential for PvP, as it provides passive health regeneration.
*   **High Noon:** Provides auto-aim during Ocular Vision (X) but permanently reduces your max HP by 20%. Use this only if you are confident in your positioning.

## Horse Management and Economy

Your horse is your lifeline. Without it, you are a slow target for any **bridger western vampire** or outlaw posse. While your starter horse is functional, you should aim to save up at least $1,000 to $1,500 for a Legendary horse.

You can buy horses from stable NPCs in various towns. If the NPC offers a "mid" horse like the Dusty Ghost, you can reroll their inventory until they show you a more expensive, faster breed. Legendary horses have higher health pools and can even be used as weapons; pressing **F** while riding allows you to ram enemies for significant damage.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I stop being a Bridger Western vampire?**

A: To reset your race or stand, you must either "Wipe" your character entirely or find a specific rare plant while fishing and bring it to the Witch in the swamp.

**Q: What happens when my character grows old?**

A: As your age increases (displayed in the M menu), your character will develop wrinkles and grey hair. While there is a community myth that you die of old age, it currently serves primarily as a visual indicator of how many times you have died or combat-logged.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Corpse Parts?**

A: Corpse Parts spawn randomly across the map. The best strategy is to stay in a server with roughly 10-15 players. This balances the spawn rate (which is higher in populated servers) with the risk of being "jumped" by the entire server when the golden beam appears.

**Q: Can I play the game without using guns?**

A: Yes, the Saber is a viable melee alternative. When paired with the "Show Me a Good Time" talent card, you can parry incoming bullets and deal increased melee damage, though you sacrifice all ranged capabilities.
