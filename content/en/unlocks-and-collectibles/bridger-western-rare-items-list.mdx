## Hunting the Skinwalker: Ritual and Rewards

The Skinwalker remains the most formidable challenge in the game, and its drops are the crown jewels of any **bridger western rare items list**. To even face this creature, players must engage in a complex ritual involving items that are no longer naturally obtainable in the world.

### Summoning Requirements

To summon the Skinwalker, you must travel to the Great Plains near Fort Henry. There, an ancient altar surrounded by stones awaits. You will need an **Extractor** and at least **three empty vials**. Because these items are no longer sold by NPCs, they must be acquired through player trading or found in rare treasure chests, significantly increasing their value on the **bridger western rare items list**.

| Required Blood | Animal Source | Altar Symbol |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bear Blood** | Grizzly Bear | Claw Mark |
| **Bison Blood** | American Bison | Horns |
| **Deer Blood** | Whitetail Deer | Antlers |

> **Warning:** The Skinwalker is nearly impossible to solo. It possesses telepathic abilities that can damage players from a distance and will relentlessly lock onto a single target until they are defeated. It is highly recommended to bring a posse of at least three players.

Once the ritual is completed at the altar, the Skinwalker will spawn at 6:00 PM in-game time. Upon its defeat, it drops a single **Bone Part**. These bones are unique because they are not dropped upon player death, meaning once you pick one up, it is safe in your inventory. These bones can be traded to the NPC **Kaia**, located near the Windmill Camp, for exclusive rewards.

## Rare Cosmetics and Limited-Time Items

Not every item on the **bridger western rare items list** is designed for combat. Some of the most prestigious items are purely cosmetic or provide utility in the form of speed. 

### The Watermelon Hat
A new addition to the rare items roster is the Watermelon Hat. This quirky cosmetic is obtained through a specific interaction in the new biome.
1. Spawn at **Dolores's Ranch**.
2. Purchase a **Watermelon** from the General Store.
3. Locate the **Capiara** animal in the nearby wetlands.
4. Approach the Capiara with the watermelon equipped until it eats the fruit.
5. The Capiara will reward you with the Watermelon Hat.

### The Skeleton Horse and Spitfire
For those seeking the fastest transportation in the game, the Skeleton Horse is the ultimate prize. While it was previously an event-exclusive, it can now be obtained by giving Kaia the specific **Skeleton Horse Bones**. These bones are currently only obtainable through high-tier trading, making the Skeleton Horse one of the rarest sights on the frontier in 2026.

Additionally, players should keep a close eye on the **Spitfire** sniper rifle. Signs in the Auction House have recently changed to indicate that this weapon will be "Out of Stock Soon." Once it leaves the rotation, its value on the secondary market is expected to skyrocket, cementing its place on the **bridger western rare items list**.

## FAQ

**Q: What is currently the rarest item on the bridger western rare items list?**

A: Currently, the **Skeleton Horse Bones** and the **Spitfire** (post-discontinuation) are considered the rarest items. The Skeleton Horse is the fastest mount in the game, and the bones required to summon it are extremely difficult to find in the trading economy.

**Q: Can I still get the Extractor if I missed the original event?**

A: You cannot buy the Extractor from any NPC store in 2026. The only way to obtain one is through trading with other players or finding one in rare high-tier treasure chests scattered across the map.

**Q: Where is Kaia located for the bone exchange?**

A: Kaia is situated to the left of the **Windmill Camp**. She is the primary NPC for turning in Skinwalker and Skeleton Horse bones in exchange for rare gear and mounts.

**Q: Is the Windigo still in the game?**

A: Yes, the Windigo still appears, but it only spawns during **Blood Moons**, which are exceptionally rare weather events. While its current drop table is somewhat mysterious in the 4.1 update, it remains a legendary encounter for veteran hunters.
