## Ridgeby County: The Main Hub

Ridgeby County serves as the primary civilization center for most players. It is the safest place to conduct business, though you should always stay alert for sudden duels. Most essential service NPCs, such as the banker and the gunsmith, are located within a short walk of each other in the town square.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize buying the **Cowboy Hat** from Gerald early on. It provides a mechanical advantage by occasionally negating headshot damage, which is a life-saver in unpredictable PVP encounters.

## Weaponry and Combat Vendors

To defend yourself against the various threats of the frontier, you will need more than just a basic sidearm. There are two primary locations to purchase firearms: the law-abiding shop in Ridgeby and the grit-filled Outlaw camp.

### Mo's Guns (Ridgeby County)
Harold is the premier gunsmith for those with a clean record or enough cash to ignore the law. He specializes in reliable, high-accuracy weapons. We recommend the **Windy Dragoon** as a solid secondary and the **Winchester Repeater** for mid-to-long-range engagements.

### Old Man Jenkins (Outlaw Area)
Located in the more rugged Outlaw-friendly territory, Old Man Jenkins offers a different variety of hardware. While some players find his shotguns to be somewhat inaccurate at range, they excel in close-quarters bushwhacking. He also sells various throwables that can flush enemies out of cover.

## The Stable and Horse Trading

In Bridger: Western, your horse is your lifeline. It provides mobility, storage, and can even be used as a weapon. Horses have randomized stats, and finding a high-tier mount is often a matter of luck and persistence.

### How to Buy and Manage Horses
You can summon your horse by pressing **H** and mount it with **N**. If your horse is killed, it typically takes about two minutes to respawn. When looking for a new mount, pay close attention to the naming conventions in the shop menu.

| Horse Tier | Estimated Cost | Identification Marker | Stats |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Common** | 200 - 400 | Standard Name | Low Speed/Stamina |
| **Mid-Tier** | 500 - 900 | Descriptive Name | Balanced Stats |
| **Legendary** | 1,000 - 1,500 | **ALL CAPS** or **[Brackets]** | Uncapped Stats (>1.25) |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not settle for the first horse you see. You can "reroll" the available selection by clicking "Show me another" at the Stable NPC until you find a legendary mount with superior speed.

## Specialized and Quest NPCs

Beyond the standard vendors, several NPCs offer unique progression paths, including supernatural abilities known as "Stands" and specialized character talents.

### The Witch (Witch's Hut)
The Witch is perhaps the most important NPC for late-game progression. She is located in a secluded hut within the swamp area. To reach her, you often need to navigate through deep water; a common trick is to sprint your horse into the water while spamming the spacebar to "jump-swim" to the other side.

*   **Fortune Reading:** Costs 150 Moola. This allows you to select and "weave" talents into your character slots.
*   **Talent Tiers:** As you progress through Tiers 1, 2, and 3, you can unlock up to three talent slots.
*   **Stand Reset:** If you are unhappy with your current Stand, the Witch can reset it if you provide her with specific rare plants found via fishing.

### Flint (Flint and Steel)
Flint is a quest-giver who challenges your marksmanship. His initial quest involves a mini-game where you must shoot cans. 
1.  **Stage 1:** Hit 3 cans.
2.  **Stage 2:** Hit 4 cans.
3.  **Stage 3:** Hit 6 cans.
Using **Ocular Prowess (X key)** makes these challenges significantly easier. Completing his questline eventually leads to the Vampire Ruins, where you can hunt high-level targets for significant XP.

### Gappy (The Fishing Docks)
Fishing is the most reliable way for new players to earn money without risking combat. Gappy sells fishing rods and bait.
*   **Location:** The Docks (Red Corner is the closest spawn point).
*   **Rewards:** Fish (sell for profit), Chests, and the ultra-rare **Arrow Shard** (0.5% chance), which can grant you a Stand.

## Understanding the Stand System

While Bridger: Western is a cowboy game at its core, the inclusion of Stands adds a layer of supernatural strategy. There are two primary ways to interact with this system through NPCs and world events.

1.  **The Corpse Part:** A server-wide message ("It appears once again") signals the spawn of a Corpse Part. A golden beam will appear on the map. If you reach it and hold it, you have a 15-20% chance per "pulse" to obtain a Stand.
2.  **Fishing:** As mentioned, Gappy’s fishing spot offers a 0.5% chance to pull up an Arrow Shard. This is the "peaceful" route to obtaining power, though it requires immense patience.

## Character Maintenance and Alignment

Your character's age and alignment change based on your actions and deaths. Pressing **M** allows you to view your current stats.

*   **Age:** Increases by 1-2 years per death. Combat logging is penalized heavily, increasing age by 10 years. While aging causes cosmetic changes like gray hair and wrinkles, it is a constant reminder of your character's mortality.
*   **Alignment:** Your actions determine if you are viewed as an In-law or an Outlaw. Outlaws generally have more freedom but cannot set spawn points in "civilized" areas like Ridgeby County.
*   **Wiping:** If you are unhappy with your character's appearance or stats, you can "Wipe" from the main menu. This resets your progress but allows you to reroll your starting cash (ranging from 50 to 500 Moola).

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to spawn for beginners in Bridger: Western?**

A: It is highly recommended to spawn at **Red Corner**. It is the closest location to the fishing docks, allowing you to quickly buy a rod from Gappy and start generating income with minimal travel time.

**Q: How do I get a Stand if I don't want to fight for the Corpse Part?**

A: The best alternative is fishing at the docks. While the chance is very low (0.5%), you can eventually pull up an Arrow Shard. Additionally, you can find rare plants while fishing that can be given to the Witch to reset your current Stand.

**Q: Can I keep my items in the Bank if I wipe my character?**

A: No. Items stored with Alfred the Banker in Ridgeby County are tied to that specific character's life. If you choose to wipe your character to reroll stats or appearance, all stored items will be lost.

**Q: What is the fastest way to find the Witch's Hut?**

A: Head to the swamp area across the river from the main plains. Look for a small, wooden shack isolated in the marsh. Using a horse to "spam jump" across the water is the most efficient way to reach her without drowning or being slowed down by the terrain.

**Q: Is there any way to protect myself from headshots?**

A: Yes, purchase the **Cowboy Hat** from Gerald at the General Store in Ridgeby County. It is specifically designed to register hits that would otherwise be lethal headshots, providing a significant defensive boost in gunfights.
