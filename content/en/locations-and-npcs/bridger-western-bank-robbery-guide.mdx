## Choosing Your Path: Outlaw vs. In-law

Before you can pull off a successful heist or engage in the darker side of the frontier, you must choose your alignment. For those following a **bridger western bank robbery guide**, the choice is almost always Outlaw. Outlaws can spawn in locations like Red Corner or the Campsite, which are strategically placed near high-value resources and NPC spawns.

| Alignment | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outlaw** | High freedom, access to robbery mechanics, easier XP from chaos. | Constant PvP threat, bounty hunters, higher risk of losing items. |
| **In-law** | Safer progression, protected status in certain zones, reliable income. | Restricted freedom, fewer high-reward "illegal" opportunities. |

> **💡 Tip:** When you first create your character, you receive a random amount of starting cash (between $50 and $500). If you start with less than $200, it is often worth "wiping" your character and restarting until you get a higher starting balance to buy your first gun or fishing rod.

## The Bank and Weapon Storage Strategy

In Bridger, the bank serves a dual purpose. While it is a central hub for the economy, savvy Outlaws use it for a specific weapon-stacking technique. Normally, buying a new primary weapon like a Shotgun will replace your current Rifle. However, by using the bank, you can bypass this limitation.

1. **Store your current primary weapon** in the bank vault.
2. **Purchase a different primary weapon** (e.g., if you had a Rifle, buy a Shotgun).
3. **Return to the bank** and withdraw your stored weapon.
4. **Equip both**, allowing you to carry a diverse arsenal for any **bridger western bank robbery guide** situation.

## Securing a Legendary Mount

A successful getaway is the most important part of any robbery. Your starter mule won't cut it when the law is on your tail. You need to visit horse traders and look for specific dialogue cues that indicate a high-tier mount.

| Horse Phrase | Estimated Cost | Quality Tier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "Solid enough for travel" | $500 - $650 | **Common/Mid** |
| "Faster than a bullet" | $1,000+ | **Rare** |
| "It's a beast" | $1,200 - $1,500 | **Legendary** |

The "Beast" tier horses are generally considered the best in the game for 2026. They offer the highest speed and stamina stats (up to 1.25), ensuring you can outrun almost any pursuer after a successful heist.

## Advanced Power: Stands and Corpse Parts

To truly dominate the server, you may want to acquire a Stand. This supernatural ability can turn the tide of a fight during a **bridger western bank robbery guide** attempt. Stands are acquired through Corpse Parts, which spawn randomly across the map, signaled by a massive golden beam of light.

### Corpse Part Spawn Rates
The chance of a Corpse Part appearing increases with the number of players in the server. Every 30 seconds, the game checks for a spawn:

*   **3 Players:** 1 in 150 chance.
*   **10 Players:** 1 in 115 chance.
*   **25+ Players:** 1 in 40 chance.

Once you pick up a Corpse Part, you must remain alive while it pulses. Each pulse has a 15-20% chance to grant you a Stand. If you are killed before the process completes, the killer can steal the part from your body.

## Survival Tips for the Frontier

*   **Watch the Text:** Yellow text at the top of the screen indicates bandits have spawned nearby. Red/White text often signals zombies or "The Dead." Both provide crates with money, ammo, and tonics.
*   **Manage Your Age:** Every death has a chance to increase your character's age. While aging makes your hair gray and skin wrinkled, combat logging is the real danger—it increases your age by 10 years instantly.
*   **The Witch’s Hut:** Located in the swamp, the Witch allows you to roll for "Talents" or "Cards" at Tier 2 and Tier 3. These provide passive buffs, such as the "True Cowboy" talent which increases gun damage by 17% while on horseback.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I pull off a bank robbery in Bridger Western?**

A: While the specific mechanics vary by update, the most effective **bridger western bank robbery guide** strategy involves securing a "Beast" tier horse for the getaway and using the bank storage trick to carry both a Shotgun and a Rifle for maximum firepower against guards.

**Q: Where is the best place to sell fish for quick money?**

A: The docks near Red Corner are the primary location for buying fishing rods and selling your catch. Fishing is the safest way to build up the $1,000+ needed for a top-tier horse.

**Q: Can I lose my Stand if I die?**

A: No, once a Stand is fully absorbed into your body, it is permanent unless you choose to reset it by "wiping" or using a specific rare plant found through fishing to reset your stats at the Witch's Hut.

**Q: What happens if someone kills my horse?**

A: If your horse is killed, you must wait approximately two minutes before you can summon it again using the 'H' key. Always keep a Tonic on hand to heal yourself if you're forced to fight on foot.
