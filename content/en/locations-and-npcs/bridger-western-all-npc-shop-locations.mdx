## Ridgeby County: The Primary Trade Hub

Ridgeby County serves as the main civilization center in Bridger: Western. Most "In-law" players will spend the majority of their time here, as it contains the highest concentration of utility shops. From banking your gear to customizing your physical appearance, this town is the cornerstone of the local economy.

| NPC Name | Shop Type | Primary Goods/Services | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Harold** | Gunsmith (Mo's Guns) | Pistols, Rifles, Sabers, Bows | Main Street, Ridgeby |
| **Gerald** | General Store | Cowboy Hats, Ponchos, Lanterns | Near Town Entrance |
| **Alfred** | Banker | Equipment Storage | Ridgeby Bank |
| **Joe Joestar** | Tailor | Clothing and Accessory Colors | Ridgeby Plaza |
| **The Barber** | Stylist | Haircuts and Hair Color | Near the Tailor |

### Essential Gear at Mo's Guns
Harold is arguably the most important NPC for combat progression. Beginners should prioritize the **Windy Dragoon** for a reliable secondary or the **Winchester Repeater** for long-range engagements. If you prefer melee, Harold also stocks Sabers, which can be devastating when paired with the right talent cards.

### The General Store Utility
Gerald’s inventory is often overlooked by new players, but his items provide passive survival bonuses. The **Cowboy Hat** is a must-buy as it can occasionally negate headshot damage, while the **Poncho** offers general damage resistance at the cost of increased limb damage.

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry a Lantern from Gerald's shop. Nighttime in the wilderness is extremely dark, and being caught without a light source makes you an easy target for ambushers.

## Outlaw Territories and Wilderness NPC Locations

If you have chosen the path of the Outlaw, or if you simply need to find specialized items not found in town, you must venture into the dangerous outskirts. These locations are often hotspots for PvP, so approach with caution.

### The Fishing Docks (Gappy)
Located near the water's edge, Gappy is the go-to NPC for generating early-game income. Fishing is not only a safe way to earn moola but also offers a 0.5% chance to pull up an **Arrow Shard**, which is used to obtain a Stand.

*   **Fishing Rod:** Essential for catching bass and rare chests.
*   **Bait:** Increases the frequency of bites.
*   **Sell Fish:** Return your catch to Gappy to convert them into currency.

### The Witch’s Hut (Swamp)
Hidden within the swampy region, the Witch is an NPC that every player must visit to finalize their build. She deals in "Fortunes," which are essentially the game's talent and perk system.

| Service | Cost | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Read Fortune** | 150 Moola | Grants a random Talent card (first is free). |
| **Reroll Talent** | 50 Moola | Replaces an existing talent in your active slot. |
| **Stand Reset** | Rare Plant | Clears your current Stand so you can obtain a new one. |

### Old Man Jenkins (Outlaw Camp)
For those with a bounty on their heads, Old Man Jenkins provides a selection of weaponry tailored for aggressive playstyles. He is located in the hideout camp and sells high-impact shotguns and various throwables. While his guns are often less accurate than Harold's, they excel in close-quarters combat during raids.

## Horse Stables: Finding the Fastest Mounts

Your horse is your lifeline in the frontier. While every player starts with a basic mount, upgrading to a higher-tier horse is necessary for escaping bounties or chasing down targets. Stables can be found in both Ridgeby County and the Outlaw settlements.

| Horse Grade | Typical Price | Identification Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | 200 - 450 Moola | Basic names like "Dusty Ghost." |
| **Mid-Tier** | 500 - 900 Moola | Improved speed and stamina stats. |
| **Legendary** | 1,000 - 1,500 Moola | Names are usually in **ALL CAPS** or **[Brackets]**. |

When browsing the stable, you can "Reroll" the available selection by asking the NPC to show you another horse. In 2026, the meta revolves around horses with uncapped stats (over 1.25), which are exclusively found in the Legendary tier.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your horse is killed in combat, it takes approximately two minutes to respawn. During this cooldown, you are extremely vulnerable to "Horse Kicking" attacks from mounted players.

## Specialized Quest NPCs

Beyond simple shops, several NPCs provide unique items through challenges. These are vital for players looking for "Mare's Leg" or specific combat styles.

1.  **Flint & Steel:** Found in the wilderness, this NPC offers a multi-stage target practice quest. You must hit cans using your firearm (using Ocular Prowess by pressing X is highly recommended). Completing his trials allows you to purchase the **Mare's Leg**, a powerful hybrid weapon.
2.  **The Vampire Hunter:** Located near the Vampire Ruins, this NPC tasks you with eliminating supernatural threats. This is a high-level XP farm that also rewards unique materials used for crafting or trading.
3.  **Spawn Setters:** Various NPCs around the map allow you to set your spawn point. Note that Outlaws cannot set their spawn in Ridgeby County and must rely on hidden camps.

## Combat and Survival Mechanics

Searching for **bridger western all npc shop locations** is only half the battle. You must also master the controls to defend your purchases.

*   **H Key:** Summon your horse to your location.
*   **N Key:** Mount or dismount your horse.
*   **F Key:** Perform a horse kick while mounted (deals significant damage).
*   **X Key:** Activate Ocular Prowess (ESP) to see hidden players and targets.
*   **M Key:** Check your character's age, alignment, and current XP.

### The Corpse Part System
Occasionally, a golden beam will appear on the map accompanied by a server-wide message. This indicates a **Corpse Part** has spawned. These are not sold in any NPC shop; they must be fought for. Holding a Corpse Part gives you a periodic chance to "fuse" with it, granting you a Stand. The chance of fusing increases based on the number of players in the server, making high-population servers more rewarding but significantly more dangerous.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the best bridger western all npc shop locations for guns?**

A: The best general gunsmith is Harold at Mo's Guns in Ridgeby County. However, if you are looking for shotguns or throwables and have a high bounty, you should visit Old Man Jenkins in the Outlaw Camp.

**Q: Do items stored in the Bank transfer if I wipe my character?**

A: No. Items stored with Alfred the Banker in Ridgeby County are tied to that specific character slot. If you choose to wipe your character to reroll your appearance or stats, the bank storage will be cleared.

**Q: How do I reset my Stand if I don't like it?**

A: You must visit the Witch in the swamp. You will need to provide her with a specific rare plant found in the wilderness. Alternatively, you can reset your Stand by wiping your character entirely, though this loses all progress.

**Q: What is the benefit of the Cowboy Hat sold by Gerald?**

A: The Cowboy Hat acts as a piece of armor that can block headshots. In Bridger Western, headshots deal critical damage, so having an item that can negate that hit is a massive advantage in duels.
