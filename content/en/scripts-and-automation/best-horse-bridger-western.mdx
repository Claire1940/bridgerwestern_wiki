## Where to Find the Horse Vendor

The journey to obtaining a high-quality mount begins at the Horse Seller NPC. While the map is vast, most players use the fishing area as their primary landmark. To find the vendor, head toward the tackle base store and follow the coastal path. You will eventually encounter a stable area where the Horse Seller resides.

Talking to this NPC opens a simple menu with two primary options:
1.  **I'll take it:** Purchases the currently displayed horse for the listed price.
2.  **Show me another:** Rerolls the horse, presenting a new animal with different stats, colors, and prices.

> **💡 Tip:** Never settle for the first horse you see. Rerolling is the only way to find high-stat mounts, though it requires patience and a healthy supply of in-game currency.

## Understanding Horse Stats and Pricing

In Bridger Western, the price of a horse is the most immediate indicator of its potential quality. The game uses a formula that aggregates all of a horse's stat points to determine the final cost. Generally, a higher price tag suggests that the horse has higher base numbers in Speed, Stamina, Jump, and Damage.

| Price Range (Currency) | Quality Tier | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **150 - 500** | Low/Starter | Slow movement, very low stamina. Best for temporary travel only. |
| **500 - 1,000** | Decent/Mid | Reliable for general exploration. Balanced stats but rarely exceptional. |

While price is a strong indicator, it is not a guarantee of perfection. A 1,400-currency horse might have maximum Jump and Damage stats, which are often considered less valuable than Speed and Stamina. Always prioritize horses that offer high performance in the areas that match your playstyle.

## The Importance of Speed and Stamina

When searching for the **best horse Bridger Western** players can buy, you should focus almost exclusively on two stats: Speed and Stamina.

*   **Speed:** This determines your maximum velocity. In a game where travel times are significant, a fast horse saves hours of real-world time.
*   **Stamina:** This dictates how long your horse can maintain a gallop before needing to rest. High stamina is crucial for escaping PvP encounters or reaching distant quest markers without stopping.
*   **Jump & Damage:** While useful for navigating rocky terrain or trampling enemies, these are secondary stats. A horse that can jump high but runs slowly is rarely worth a high price tag.

## Legendary vs. Mythical Rarity Explained

The most confusing aspect for many players is the distinction between "good stats" and "rarity." A horse can be expensive but not Legendary, or it can be Legendary but have poor stats. Rarity in Bridger Western refers to a horse's ability to exceed the standard stat cap.

### Stat Caps and Rarity Indicators

Standard horses have a hard cap on their stats, usually around **1.25**. Legendary and Mythical horses are the only mounts capable of pushing past this limit to reach values like **1.35** or higher.

| Rarity | Visual Indicator | Stat Boosts |
|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | Standard Name (e.g., Brownie) | No stats exceed the 1.25 cap. |
| **Legendary** | ALL CAPS, thin brackets `[OLD MOON]` | **One** random stat exceeds the 1.25 cap. |
| **Mythical** | ALL CAPS, thick brackets `{SOLARIS}` | **Two** random stats exceed the 1.25 cap. |

It is important to note that a Legendary horse might receive its "boost" in the Jump stat. In this scenario, a high-priced Normal horse with 1.25 Speed and 1.25 Stamina would actually be better for travel than a Legendary horse with 1.35 Jump but only 0.80 Speed.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not buy a horse just because the name is in all caps. Check the price and the description to ensure the stats are actually allocated into Speed or Stamina.

## How to Reroll for the Best Horse

To optimize your search for the **best horse Bridger Western** offers, you should follow a specific rerolling strategy. This process requires a significant amount of money, so it is recommended to farm currency through fishing or bounty hunting before visiting the vendor.

1.  **Check the Price First:** Look for horses priced above 1,000. If the price is low (e.g., 400), it is unlikely to have the stats you need, even if it is a Legendary.
2.  **Look for Keywords:** Pay attention to the NPC's description. Phrases like "top of the line," "beast," or "best runner" indicate a horse with high cumulative stats.
3.  **Identify the Brackets:** Look for the square `[...]` or thick `{...}` brackets and all-caps names. These are your potential Mythical mounts.
4.  **Confirm the Color:** Legendary and Mythical horses often feature unique coat colors or patterns not found on standard mounts, making them easy to spot once you become familiar with the base models.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the highest speed a horse can reach in Bridger Western?**

A: While standard horses are capped at a 1.25 stat value, Legendary and Mythical horses can reach speeds of 1.35 or higher. In terms of UI numbers, some players have reported max speeds reaching up to 97-98 after the 15-minute reveal.

**Q: Can I change the name of my horse?**

A: No, the name is tied to the horse's rarity and stat roll. Legendary and Mythical horses have fixed names in all caps with specific brackets to denote their tier.

**Q: Is a Legendary horse always better than a normal horse?**

A: Not necessarily. A Legendary horse simply means one stat has the potential to exceed the 1.25 cap. If that stat is "Jump" and the horse has very low Speed, a high-tier Normal horse with balanced 1.20+ stats in everything will likely perform better for most players.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm money for the best horse Bridger Western?**

A: Most players recommend fishing near the tackle base or participating in "server flooding" for corpse parts, which can be a lucrative way to build up the 1,500+ currency needed for elite horses.
