## How to Obtain the Tomahawk in Bridger Western

Before you can start harvesting rare herbs or engaging in high-level trades, you must first secure your equipment. The primary source for all woodcutting and melee tools is the Lumber NPC. Finding this character is the first step for any new player or veteran looking to restock.

The Lumber NPC is typically located within a small, unassuming house on the outskirts of the main settlement. Unlike other vendors who might be out in the open, this NPC stays indoors, surrounded by woodworking tools and raw timber. Once you locate him, you can purchase a variety of axes, including the Tomahawk variant, which offers a balance of speed and power.

| Item Name | Cost (In-Game Currency) | Primary Use | Attack Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Basic Axe** | 500 | Woodcutting | Slow |
| **Tomahawk** | 1,200 | **Combat & Utility** | Fast |
| **Heavy Hatchet** | 2,500 | Resource Farming | Medium |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a spare Tomahawk in your bank. If you find yourself in the Swamp biome without a tool, the trek back to the Lumber NPC can be long and dangerous.

## Woodcutting Mechanics and Roka Farming

The core loop of Bridger Western involves using your Tomahawk to chop down trees. This isn't just for cosmetic purposes; tree-felling is the primary method for obtaining **Rokas** (Rokakaka fruit) and **Dog Vein Herbs**. These items are the lifeblood of the game's progression system, allowing you to reset stats or prepare for Stand trading.

### Biome Differences: Forest vs. Swamp

Not all trees are created equal. Depending on where you choose to set up your camp, your efficiency will vary significantly.

1.  **The Forest:** This is the ideal location for beginners. Trees here have lower durability, meaning you can fell them quickly with a standard Tomahawk. The drop rate for Rokas is consistent, making it the most efficient place to farm if you are looking to reset your Stand or gather trading materials.
2.  **The Swamp:** Trees in the Swamp are much hardier. It takes significantly longer to chop them down compared to the Forest. However, the Swamp offers a chance to find Dog Vein Herbs in addition to Rokas. These herbs are rare and highly sought after for high-tier crafting and trading.

| Biome | Difficulty | Main Drops | Efficiency Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Forest** | Easy | **Roka** | ★★★★★ |
| **Swamp** | Hard | **Roka, Dog Vein** | ★★★☆☆ |

## The Bridger Western Tomahawk Guide to Combat

While many players view the Tomahawk strictly as a tool, its combat potential should not be overlooked. In the current 2026 meta, melee weapons have received a slight buff to their stagger potential, making the Tomahawk a viable sidearm when your primary firearm runs dry.

When using the Tomahawk in combat, focus on "hit-and-run" tactics. The weapon has a shorter reach than a saber but a faster recovery time than a heavy axe. This allows you to land a quick strike and dodge away before your opponent can retaliate. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to face-tank players using long-range Stands or high-caliber rifles. Use the environment—specifically the trees you haven't chopped down yet—as cover to close the distance.

### Melee Stat Breakdown

*   **Swing Speed:** 0.8 seconds per strike.
*   **Stun Duration:** 0.5 seconds on a successful headshot.
*   **Bleed Chance:** 5% per hit (scales with Strength stat).

## Trading Stands and Skins: The Tier 3 Requirement

One of the most significant updates in 2026 is the revamped trading system. To participate in trading, you must utilize a Roka obtained through woodcutting. Once you have a Roka in your inventory, you can initiate a trade by clicking on another player.

However, there is a major gatekeeper: **You must be Tier 3.**

### Understanding the Trading Process

When you click on another player with a Roka, they will receive a notification asking for confirmation. If both parties agree, the trade menu opens. This system allows for the equalization of "Age" (a stat viewable by pressing 'M') and the exchange of Stands and Skins.

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Stand Trading** | Exchange your current Stand for another player's Stand. |
| **Skin Chance** | If a Stand has no skin, there is a **1% chance** to generate one during a trade. |
| **Card Exchange** | Trade utility cards that buff your character's passive stats. |
| **Age Equalization** | Matches the "Age" stat between players to ensure fair trading tiers. |

Almost every Stand in Bridger Western now features unique skins, with the notable exceptions of **Golden Experience Requiem** and **Tusk**. These skins are purely cosmetic but hold immense value in the player-driven economy.

## Advanced Strategies for Resource Management

To maximize your efficiency as outlined in this **bridger western tomahawk guide**, you must manage your inventory and time effectively. Farming Rokas is a numbers game; the more trees you hit, the better your chances.

### Optimized Farming Route

1.  **Spawn at the Outskirts:** Visit the Lumber NPC and ensure your Tomahawk is at full durability.
2.  **Head to the Forest Edge:** Focus on clusters of 3-4 trees. Avoid isolated trees to minimize travel time.
3.  **Check for Dog Veins:** If you are feeling brave, dip into the Swamp for 10 minutes to see if you can snag a Dog Vein Herb, but return to the Forest if your Roka count is low.
4.  **Reach Tier 3:** Do not attempt to trade your hard-earned Rokas until you have reached Tier 3. Leveling up can be done by completing bounty missions or successfully defending against bandits.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Lumber NPC in Bridger Western?**

A: The Lumber NPC is located in a small house near the forest outskirts. You need to enter the building to interact with him and purchase your Tomahawk or Axe.

**Q: Why is it taking so long to chop trees in the Swamp?**

A: Swamp trees have a higher "toughness" rating than Forest trees. While they take longer to fell, they are the only source for Dog Vein Herbs, making them a high-risk, high-reward option.

**Q: Can I trade Stands without a Roka?**

A: No, the Roka is a required item to initiate the trade notification. Additionally, as mentioned in this **bridger western tomahawk guide**, you must be at least Tier 3 to successfully complete a trade.

**Q: How do I get a skin for my Stand?**

A: Skins can be obtained through the gacha system or by trading. When trading a Stand that doesn't have a skin, there is a 1% chance that a skin will be applied to the Stand during the transfer.

**Q: What happens if I press 'M' in the game?**

A: Pressing 'M' opens the player menu, where you can view your current Age, Tier, and Stand stats. This is essential for verifying if you are eligible for trading with other players.
