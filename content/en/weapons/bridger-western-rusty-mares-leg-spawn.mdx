Unlike standard firearms that can be purchased directly from a gunsmith, the Rusted Mare's Leg is an "earned" item that relies on the game’s gathering mechanics. To obtain the rusted frame, you must equip a fishing rod and spend time at any of the major bodies of water across the map. While fishing is primarily used for gathering food or items to sell, there is a low-percentage "loot table" chance to hook the Rusted Mare's Leg.

## Repairing the Rusted Mare's Leg

Once you have successfully triggered the **bridger western rusty mares leg spawn** and have the item in your inventory, you will notice it is completely unusable in its current state. It appears as a rusted, broken hunk of iron. To make it functional, you need to visit a specific specialist NPC who deals in firearm restoration.

### Finding the Repair Specialist
The NPC required for this quest is located near the bridge crossing from Ridge County (sometimes referred to by locals as Risby County). Cross the main bridge and look for a lone figure standing near the path. This character is the only one capable of cleaning the decades of rust off your new find.

### The Cost of Restoration
Repairing a vintage weapon isn't free. You will need to have a significant amount of "moola" (the in-game currency) ready before the NPC will even look at the gun. In the current 2026 meta, the price is fixed at **1,500 money**.

## Retrieving Your Weapon from the Bank

A common point of frustration for players is that the weapon does not immediately appear in their hands after paying the 1,500 moola. The NPC will inform you that the gun has been "moved to storage." In Bridger Western, your global storage is managed through the banking system.

### The City Colony Bank
To claim your functional Mare's Leg, you must travel to the **City Colony**. Locate the Bank building, which is usually one of the most prominent structures in the town center. 

1. Enter the Bank and approach the clerk behind the desk.
2. Select the "Open Storage" or "Access Vault" option.
3. Look for the item now labeled as "Mare's Leg" (the "Rusty" prefix will be gone).
4. Equip the weapon to your active loadout.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ensure you have enough space in your active inventory before attempting to withdraw the weapon, or you may need to drop an existing item first.

## Secondary Quests and the Vampire Ruins

The NPC responsible for repairing your Mare's Leg isn't just a mechanic; he is also a quest-giver. Engaging with him can lead to some of the more supernatural elements of Bridger Western. After you interact with him for the gun repair, you can initiate a series of target practice challenges.

### The Can Shooting Challenge
The NPC will ask you to prove your marksmanship by hitting cans placed in the environment. This is a progressive questline:
*   **Stage 1:** Hit 3 cans.
*   **Stage 2:** Hit 4 cans.
*   **Stage 3:** Hit 6 cans.

Completing these challenges eventually unlocks the "Vampire Quest." This high-level mission sends you to the **Vampire Ruins**, a dangerous area filled with unique environmental hazards and potentially lucrative loot. Using your newly repaired Mare's Leg is a great way to tackle these challenges, as the fast fire rate helps manage the mobs found within the ruins.
