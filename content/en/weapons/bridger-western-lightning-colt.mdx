## Getting Started: Your First Steps in the West

When you first spawn into the world, you must choose an alignment: Outlaw or In-law. While this choice currently has minimal impact on gameplay mechanics, it sets the tone for your roleplay experience. Your immediate goal should be to familiarize yourself with the movement and combat controls to ensure you aren't an easy target for opportunistic players.

| Action | Keybind | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Sprint** | Double-tap **W** | Increases movement speed and automatically vaults small obstacles. |
| **Roll** | **Q** | A defensive maneuver to dodge incoming fire or Stand abilities. |
| **Melee** | **G** | Strike an opponent with the butt of your weapon. |
| **Ocular Prowess** | **X** | Highlights enemies, increases accuracy, and boosts reload speed. |
| **Summon Horse** | **H** | Calls your trusty steed to your side instantly. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your Ocular Prowess (X) ready during a shootout. It provides a significant tactical advantage by making targets easier to track in dusty or dark environments.

## How to Earn Money Fast

Before you can afford the **bridger western lightning colt**, you need "moola." While PvP and world events provide rewards, the most consistent and "chill" way to build your bank account in 2026 is through fishing. 

Head to the train tracks and follow them until you reach the Fishing Village (often referred to as the Taco Bait Shop area). Purchase a fishing rod and bait from the local NPC. Fishing not only provides a steady stream of income but also offers a rare 0.5% chance to hook a Stand Arrow, which is essential for unlocking powerful supernatural abilities.

### Fishing Profit Table

| Item | Average Sell Price | Rarity |
|---|---|---|
| **Common Fish (Bass/Cod)** | 50 - 150 Moola | High |
| **Rare Fish** | 300 - 500 Moola | Medium |
| **Chest / Special Items** | 500+ Moola | Low |
| **Stand Arrow** | Priceless (Utility) | Very Low (0.5%) |

## Where to Buy Weapons and Gear

There are two primary hubs for purchasing equipment. The most common is **Ridgeby County**, located right in front of the train station. Here, you can find the general gun shop, clothing stores, and the bank to store your items.

If you are looking for something a bit more specialized or "off the books," head to the **Outlaw Hideout**. To find it, go straight from the Taco Bait shop towards the rocky outcrops. The NPC there sells specific weapons like the Double Barrel Shotgun and various throwables.

### Essential Supplies Checklist
1. **Ammo Packs:** Never leave town without at least two. You can buy these at any gun shop.
2. **Tonics:** These are your primary healing items. Use them mid-fight to stay in the action.
3. **Steel Wires/Dynamite:** Useful for crowd control and flushing out campers.
4. **Dog's Bane Herb:** Every time you die, your character ages. Use this herb to reduce your age and maintain your peak physical stats.

## The Card System and "Aimbot" Builds

To truly master the **bridger western lightning colt**, you must unlock your potential through the Witch’s Hut. Located in the swampy biome near the Fishing Village, the Witch allows you to "read your fortune" to unlock passive cards. You can equip up to three cards as you level up your Tier (Max Tier 3).

Certain card combinations can create "illegal" feeling builds. For example, combining **High Noon**, **Evil Eye**, and **Folk's High Noon** allows for a pseudo-aimbot effect while using Ocular Prowess. This makes your bullets curve slightly toward targets, ensuring that even your "near misses" become lethal hits.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Rerolling cards costs 50 Moola per slot. It can get expensive quickly, so only reroll once you have a solid financial foundation from fishing.

## Horse Maintenance and Advanced Travel

Your horse is your lifeline. While beginners start with a basic Mule, you should aim to upgrade as soon as possible. When buying a horse from the Ridgeby NPC, look for one that costs **1,000 Moola or more**. These horses have significantly better speed and stamina stats.

### Horse Stat Comparison

| Horse Type | Cost | Speed | Stamina |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mule (Starter)** | Free | Very Low | Low |
| **Standard Steed** | ~500 Moola | Medium | Medium |
| **Elite Stallion** | 1,000+ Moola | **High** | **High** |

**The Water Tech:** You can travel across water much faster by holding **Shift** to sprint and tapping **Space** while on horseback. This allows you to bypass bridges and take shortcuts through the swamp that would normally slow you down to a crawl.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the bridger western lightning colt good for beginners?**

A: While iconic, the Colt family of weapons requires good aim and knowledge of ricochet points. Beginners might find more success with the Winchester Repeater or the Taurus due to their more forgiving fire rates and range.

**Q: How do I get a Stand in Bridger Western?**

A: You can obtain a Stand by using a Stand Arrow. These are found rarely through fishing (0.5% chance) or by locating Corpse Parts that spawn every 30 seconds in servers with multiple players.

**Q: What happens when my character gets too old?**

A: Aging occurs every time you die. As you get older, your stats may fluctuate. You can reset your age by consuming Dog's Bane herbs, which are occasionally found while fishing or exploring the map.

**Q: Where is the best place to PvP?**

A: Ridgeby County is the most common hotspot for gunfights. However, the Fishing Village often sees "resource wars" between players trying to secure Stand Arrows and rare fish.
