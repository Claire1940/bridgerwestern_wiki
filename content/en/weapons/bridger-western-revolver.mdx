## Detailed Revolver Profiles

### Whitney LeMat (The Heavy Hitter)
The Whitney stands at the top of the food chain for players who can handle its kick. It deals devastating damage that can often two-tap opponents if your aim is true. However, it suffers from a notoriously slow reload speed and significant recoil. In the 2026 competitive scene, it is the preferred choice for "one-shot" builds that utilize damage-boosting cards.

### LeMat (The Shredder)
Not to be confused with its Whitney counterpart, the standard LeMat is the king of fire rate. No other revolver in the game can empty a cylinder as fast. While its accuracy and range fall off significantly past medium distances, it is an absolute monster in close-quarters combat (CQC). If you find yourself frequently ambushed, the LeMat’s ability to spray lead quickly is a life-saver.

### Schofield (The Professional's Choice)
The Schofield is often described as the most "consistent" **bridger western revolver**. It doesn't have the extreme damage of the Whitney or the speed of the LeMat, but it excels in reliability. With manageable recoil and a respectable reload time, it is the perfect "all-rounder" for players who want a weapon that performs well in any situation.

## Essential Cards for Revolver Builds

To maximize the potential of your sidearm, you must pair it with the right Talents (Cards). These passive abilities can mitigate the weaknesses of your chosen **bridger western revolver** or amplify its strengths.

| Card Name | Effect | Best Paired With |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Veteran** | Improved bullet tracking/management | Whitney LeMat |
| **Desperado** | Increased gun damage when HP is low | Schofield / Taurus |
| **Time Heals All Wounds**| Passive health regeneration | Any Revolver |
| **High Noon** | Grants auto-aim during Ocular Vision | LeMat / Whitney |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Equipping the **High Noon** card reduces your maximum HP by 20%. While the auto-aim is powerful, missing your shots during the ability can leave you extremely vulnerable due to the long cooldown.

## Advanced Combat Strategies

### Mastering the Ricochet
The Colt Peacemaker features a unique ricochet mechanic where bullets bounce off surfaces. While many players dismiss this as a gimmick, expert players use it to flush out enemies hiding behind cover in buildings. By aiming at specific floor or wall angles, you can land hits on opponents who think they are safe.

### Managing Recoil Reset
When using high-recoil weapons like the Whitney, don't just spam the fire button. There is a specific rhythm to the recoil reset. Learning to time your shots with the gun's natural "settle" point will significantly increase your accuracy at mid-range.

### The Ocular Vision Combo
Using the "X" key activates Ocular Vision, slowing down time and highlighting enemies. This is the optimal time to fan your revolver. If you have the **Evil Eye** card equipped, you can stay in this state longer, allowing you to pick off multiple targets with precision.

## Revolvers vs. Rifles and Shotguns

While the revolver is your primary sidearm, it's important to know when to switch to your longarms. The 2026 meta favors the Winchester for long-range engagements, but a well-handled revolver can still out-duel a rifle at close range.

| Weapon Type | Optimal Range | Advantage | Disadvantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Revolver** | Close to Mid | Fast draw, versatile | Lower ammo capacity |
| **Winchester** | Mid to Long | High accuracy, 8 bullets | Slower draw speed |
| **Shotgun** | Point Blank | Ragdoll effects | Very inaccurate |
| **Malcolm 70** | Extreme Long | Infinite range potential | Very high skill ceiling |

## FAQ

**Q: Which bridger western revolver is best for a new player?**

A: The **Schofield** is the best choice for beginners. It offers a perfect balance of damage and low recoil, making it much easier to learn the game's shooting mechanics compared to the high-recoil Whitney.

**Q: How do I get the Mare's Leg?**

A: The Mare's Leg is obtained through a mini-quest. You must find a specific NPC and pay 1,500 currency to have the weapon unrested. While it functions similarly to a shotgun/rifle hybrid, many players prefer the Winchester for its superior range.

**Q: Do silver bullets actually do more damage?**

A: Silver bullets are specifically designed for future updates involving supernatural entities like vampires. Currently, they are a valuable resource to stack up on, as they are one of the few ways to damage certain specialized enemy types alongside stands and silver stakes.

**Q: Can I use a saber and a revolver at the same time?**

A: No. Choosing to use a saber typically prevents you from using firearms. However, saber builds have access to unique cards like "Show Me a Good Time," which allows you to parry incoming bullets, offering a completely different playstyle.
