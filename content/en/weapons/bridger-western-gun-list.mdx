## Top-Tier Rifles and Long-Range Options

In the vast open spaces of Bridger Western, rifles are the undisputed kings. The **Malcolm 70** sits at the absolute top of the **bridger western gun list** for players who can aim. It features nearly infinite range, allowing you to pick off targets before they even realize you're there. However, it requires significant practice to master.

For those who prefer a more forgiving but equally deadly option, the **Winchester Repeater** is the gold standard. Unlike many revolvers that require a full cylinder dump to reload, the Winchester allows for tactical reloading, keeping your eight-round tube topped off during a chaotic firefight.

## Revolvers and Sidearms Comparison

Revolvers are your primary defensive tools when things get personal. The **Whitney LeMat** is a standout for its raw stopping power, capable of hitting for 45 damage on headshots. If you prefer speed over raw power, the standard **LeMat** offers the fastest fire rate of any revolver, though its accuracy suffers at a distance.

## Specialized and Niche Weapons

Not every weapon on the **bridger western gun list** fits into a standard category. The **Tommy Gun** is an anomaly—it provides S-tier damage up close, acting more like a modern SMG than a frontier tool. It is arguably the best weapon for clearing out buildings or tight alleyways.

The **Mare's Leg** occupies a unique space. While it functions similarly to a shotgun in terms of feel, it offers better range than the Maverick 88, though it lacks the Maverick's signature ragdoll effect. Speaking of shotguns, the **Maverick 88** is the only viable choice for those who want to knock enemies off their feet, as the **Double Barrel Shotgun** currently suffers from poor damage scaling.

> **Tip:** If you are using the Colt Peacemaker, try to bait enemies into narrow corridors. The ricochet mechanic is difficult to use in open fields but can be surprisingly effective inside buildings.

## Weapon Stats and Performance Metrics

Understanding the hidden stats of the **bridger western gun list** can help you decide which weapon fits your specific Stand build. Some weapons synergize better with high-mobility Stands, while others are meant for stationary snipers.

1. **Reload Mechanics**: Rifles like the Winchester allow for single-bullet reloads, whereas revolvers often require a full animation.
2. **Crowd Control**: The Maverick 88 is the primary choice for CC due to its ragdoll mechanic.
3. **Range Falloff**: Most pistols lose significant damage after mid-range; always switch to a rifle for long-distance engagements.
4. **Melee Utility**: While the Silver Dagger and Saber look impressive, they are currently outclassed by the range and damage of the Taurus or Mauser.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute best weapon in the bridger western gun list for 2026?**

A: For most players, the **Winchester Repeater** is the best all-around weapon due to its damage and reload style. However, for elite marksmen, the **Malcolm 70** is considered the strongest due to its infinite range potential.

**Q: Is the Bow worth using over a gun?**

A: Generally, no. Even with specific cards, the Bow is significantly slower than firearms. While it does decent damage, the fire rate and projectile travel time make it inferior to almost any rifle or revolver in a direct confrontation.

**Q: Why is the Double Barrel Shotgun ranked so low?**

A: The Double Barrel currently has the worst performance-to-cost ratio in the game. Its damage is inconsistent, and its range is extremely limited compared to the Maverick 88, which also provides a useful ragdoll effect on hit.

**Q: How do I deal with melee users if I have a gun?**

A: Keep your distance and use a weapon with high fire rate like the LeMat or Tommy Gun. Melee users must close the gap to be effective, making them vulnerable to "fanning the hammer" or rapid-fire sidearms before they can reach you.
