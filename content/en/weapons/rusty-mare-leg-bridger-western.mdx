## How to Obtain the Rusty Mare's Leg

Unlike the Winchester or the standard revolvers that can be purchased directly from the town gunsmith, the Rusty Mare's Leg is tied to the fishing mechanic. Fishing in Bridger Western is a relaxing way to earn money, but it also serves as a loot table for rare collectibles.

To find the item, you must equip a fishing rod and cast into any viable body of water. The drop rate is relatively low, making it a "trophy" item for many players. Once caught, the item will appear in your inventory as a "Rusty Mare's Leg." In this state, it cannot be equipped or fired. It is essentially a quest item that requires restoration.

> **💡 Tip:** To increase your fishing efficiency, look for ripples in the water which often indicate higher loot density, though the Rusty Mare's Leg can technically be caught in any fishing zone.

## Restoring the Rusty Mare Leg Bridger Western Item

Once the item is in your inventory, you need to find a specialist who can handle antique firearm restoration. This NPC is not located in the main hub but is tucked away in Ridge County.

### Step 1: Navigate to Ridge County
From the main spawn points, you need to head toward the Ridge County area. The landmark you are looking for is a large bridge. Cross this bridge, and you will find an NPC standing near a small outpost. This character is the only one capable of cleaning the rust and fixing the internal firing mechanism of the weapon.

### Step 2: The Cost of Repair
Restoration is not free. The frontier is a harsh place, and the blacksmith's expertise comes at a premium. You will need to have a significant amount of currency on hand before the NPC will even look at the weapon.

| Requirement | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Item** | Rusty Mare's Leg (In Inventory) |
| **Currency (Moola)** | **1,500** |
| **Location** | Ridge County (Across the Bridge) |
| **NPC Action** | Gun Repair & Move to Storage |

### Step 3: Retrieval from the Bank
A common point of confusion for players is that the weapon does not appear in your hand immediately after paying the 1,500 moola. The NPC will inform you that the gun has been repaired and moved to storage. 

To actually equip your new firearm, you must travel to the **City Colony**. Locate the Bank, speak to the clerk at the desk, and select the "Open Storage" option. Your newly minted Mare's Leg will be waiting there, ready to be moved into your active loadout.

## Weapon Performance and Stats

The Mare's Leg is essentially a "sawn-off" version of a lever-action rifle. In Bridger Western, this translates to a very specific set of combat statistics. It occupies a middle ground between a sidearm and a primary rifle, making it an excellent choice for players who value mobility and fire rate over raw, long-range stopping power.

### Comparison: Mare's Leg vs. Winchester Rifle

| Statistic | Mare's Leg | Winchester Rifle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fire Rate** | **Fast** | Moderate |
| **Damage Per Hit** | Moderate | **High** |
| **Ammo Capacity** | Lower | Higher |
| **Reload Speed** | Moderate | Moderate |
| **Effective Range** | Mid-Range | Long-Range |

The **rusty mare leg bridger western** restoration results in a gun that shoots significantly faster than the Winchester. However, you will find that it takes more hits to down an opponent, and you will be reloading more frequently due to the smaller magazine size. This weapon is ideal for "run-and-gun" players who like to keep moving and stay at mid-range during a shootout.

## The Secret Questline: From Cans to Vampires

Repairing the Mare's Leg is often just the beginning of a player's journey with the Ridge County NPC. This character offers a progression of skill-based challenges that eventually lead to some of the most mysterious content in the game.

1.  **The First Challenge:** You must hit a can 3 times. This tests your basic accuracy with the lever-action mechanics.
2.  **The Second Challenge:** The difficulty increases, requiring 4 successful hits.
3.  **The Final Challenge:** You must hit the target 6 times in rapid succession.
4.  **The Vampire Quest:** After proving your marksmanship, the NPC may grant you access to the Vampire Quest. This leads players toward the **Vampire Ruins**, a dangerous area that stands in stark contrast to the standard western aesthetic of the game.

> **Warning:** The Vampire Ruins are significantly more dangerous than standard bandit camps. Ensure you have a full stock of ammunition and perhaps a few allies before venturing into the ruins.

## Tactical Strategy for the Mare's Leg

To maximize the effectiveness of the Mare's Leg, you must adapt your movement. Because the weapon has a faster fire rate, you can afford to miss a shot more than you could with a slower, high-damage rifle. 

*   **Circle Strafing:** Use the weapon's mobility to circle your opponents. The faster fire rate allows you to put pressure on them, forcing them to miss their slower, more calculated shots.
*   **Pairing with a Revolver:** Since the Mare's Leg has a lower ammo capacity, always keep a reliable revolver like the Navy or Army as a backup. Switch to your sidearm when the Mare's Leg runs dry to finish off a weakened opponent.
*   **High Ground Advantage:** While it lacks the extreme range of a sniper, the Mare's Leg is surprisingly accurate at medium distances. Positioning yourself on a rooftop in City Colony can allow you to pick off targets quickly.
