<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics & Recoil Analysis">

Mastering the **bridger western quickdraw** requires a firm grasp of the new recoil patterns. Unlike the previous version, where the spray was relatively tight, the 2026 version features a sharp upward kick followed by erratic horizontal swaying. This makes the card devastating at close range but extremely punishing for those attempting to "spray and pray" at a distance.

| Weapon Category | Quickdraw Synergy | Recoil Management Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Repeaters** | Good | High (Significant Sway) |
| **Shotguns** | Poor | Low (Spread Overlap) |
| **Snipers** | Not Recommended | Extreme (Visual Reset Required) |

To effectively use the card, you must anticipate the initial jump. The moment you hit the crouch key and begin firing, your mouse or analog stick must compensate downward. Because the fire rate is so high, the time-to-kill (TTK) is incredibly low, meaning you only need to land the first three to four shots to secure a victory in most duels.

<Callout type="warning" title="Recoil Warning">
The horizontal sway kicks in after the fifth consecutive shot. It is highly recommended to fire in short, controlled bursts rather than holding the trigger down until the cylinder is empty.
</Callout>
