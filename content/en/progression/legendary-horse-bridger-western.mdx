## How to Locate the Horse Dealer

Before you can hunt for a **legendary horse bridger western**, you need to know where the market is. The primary horse seller is located near the fishing docks and the tackle base store. Most players recognize this area as a central hub for early-game progression.

1.  **Navigate to the Docks**: Head toward the water where the fishing NPCs are stationed.
2.  **Follow the Path**: From the tackle store, look for a small clearing where the Horse Dealer NPC stands.
3.  **Interact with the Dealer**: You will be presented with two choices: "I'll take it" or "Show me another."
4.  **Reroll Strategy**: Clicking "Show me another" costs moolah but allows you to cycle through different horses until a rare variant appears.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep at least 1,000 moolah in reserve before you start spinning for a new horse. You don't want to find a Mythical horse and realize you can't afford to finalize the purchase.

## The Stat System: Breaking the 1.25 Limit

In Bridger: Western, most horses are capped at a stat value of 1.25. This applies to speed, stamina, jump, and damage. However, the true value of a **legendary horse bridger western** lies in its ability to ignore this ceiling.

When a horse is generated as a Legendary or Mythical, the game selects one or two stats to receive a "breakthrough" boost. This means a Legendary horse could potentially have a speed stat of 1.35, allowing it to outrun any common horse in the game.

### Critical Stats to Prioritize
1.  **Speed**: Determines the maximum velocity. A horse with 1.29 speed can reach a max speed of approximately 103, which is elite tier.
2.  **Stamina**: Determines how long the horse can gallop before tiring. 
3.  **Jump**: Useful for navigating the mountainous terrain of the ridge.
4.  **Damage**: Important if you plan on using your horse as a weapon in mounted combat.

> **Warning:** A horse can be legendary but still have "trash" stats. If the legendary boost is applied to a stat that was already very low (e.g., a base of 0.70), the final result might still be worse than a high-tier Common horse. Always check the numerical values!

## Advanced Tips for Horse Hunters

*   **The 15-Minute Rule**: After purchasing a new horse, there is often a cooldown (roughly 916 seconds or 15 minutes) before you can fully assess the max speed through testing. Use this time to bond with the horse or farm more moolah.
*   **Auto-Clicker Caution**: While some players use auto-clickers to speed up the "Show me another" process, be extremely careful. It is easy to accidentally skip over a Legendary or Mythical horse if the clicker is set too fast.
*   **Cosmetic Uniqueness**: Legendary and Mythical horses often feature unique colors, such as glowing blue eyes or distinct tail gradients. If a horse looks unusual, pay close attention to the name brackets.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a common horse ever be faster than a legendary horse?**

A: Yes. Because legendary status only boosts *existing* stats, a legendary horse with a very low base speed (e.g., 0.60) might only reach 0.85 after the boost. A high-tier common horse with a base speed of 1.25 would still be significantly faster. Always prioritize the raw numbers over the rarity color.

**Q: What is the highest speed a legendary horse bridger western can reach?**

A: While 1.25 is the standard cap, legendary and mythical horses can reach stats of 1.30 or higher. In terms of "Max Speed" (the MPH shown while riding), anything above 100 is considered elite, with some lucky players reporting speeds up to 105-107 on perfect Mythical rolls.

**Q: Does the horse's color affect its stats?**

A: No, color is purely cosmetic. However, certain rare colors are exclusive to the legendary and mythical tiers. If you see a horse with blue eyes or a unique black-and-blue coat, it is almost certainly a high-rarity variant.

**Q: Is it better to buy many cheap horses or save for one expensive one?**

A: It is generally better to save for horses in the 800-1,200 moolah range. While legendary variants *can* spawn at lower prices, their base stats are usually so low that the legendary boost is wasted. Buying more expensive horses ensures that if you do hit a legendary roll, the horse will actually be useful.
