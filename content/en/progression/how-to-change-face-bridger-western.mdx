## Locating the Customization Hubs

To begin your transformation, you must first reach the main Town. If you find yourself lost in the wilderness, the most reliable way to navigate is by finding the train tracks. Following these tracks will eventually lead you to the central hub where the majority of service NPCs reside.

The town is home to several key establishments: the Barber Shop, the Tailor, the General Store, and the Bank. Each of these locations plays a specific role in how your character is presented to the world.

The Barber Shop is your primary destination for physical alterations. While many players focus on hair color, this is also where you can adjust the "Style" of your character's head. In the current 2026 build of Bridger: Western, the barber offers a selection of presets that can drastically change your character's aesthetic.

## How to Change Face and Body at the Tailor

If you are looking for a more comprehensive change beyond just the head, the Tailor is located just down the street from the Barber Shop. The Tailor handles everything related to your "skin" and clothing layers. This is where you can swap out your starting rags for more professional outlaw attire.

## Tactical Customization: The General Store

While the Barber and Tailor focus on aesthetics, the General Store provides customization options that actually impact your survival. In Bridger: Western, certain clothing items provide defensive buffs. If you want to change your appearance while also gaining a competitive edge, you should prioritize these two items:

| Item | Visual Change | Gameplay Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Cowboy Hat** | Adds a hat to your head | Absorbs one headshot for 0 damage (hat is removed after). |
| **Poncho** | Covers the torso area | Reduces damage to the torso but increases limb damage. |

Wearing a Poncho and a Cowboy Hat is the "meta" look for 2026. It signals to other players that you are prepared for a gunfight. If you are tired of being picked off by snipers, these items are non-negotiable.

## The Witch: Changing Your Age

One of the unique features of Bridger: Western is the aging mechanic. By pressing **M**, you can view your character's current age. As you spend time in the game, your character will naturally age, which can change your physical appearance over long periods. If you wish to revert to a younger version of yourself, you must seek out the Witch.

The Witch is located at the "Witch's Heart" area. To find her, you generally need to head away from the main town toward the more mystical regions of the map. She provides several high-level services:

*   **De-aging:** By providing the Witch with a **Dog Bane herb**, she can de-age your character by 20 years. This is the only way to "reset" your physical age without starting a new character.
*   **Card Management:** She allows you to select and equip cards like Sturdy, Desperado, or Quick Draw.
*   **Stand Reset:** If you are unhappy with your current Stand, she can reset it, allowing you to use a new Shard or Stand Arrow.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Dog Bane herb is a rare drop from Zombie Bosses or can be found in high-tier fishing chests. Do not visit the Witch for de-aging unless you have this item in your inventory.

## Managing Your Inventory at the Bank

As you collect different hats, ponchos, and weapons, you will quickly realize that you have a "Max Equip" limit. You cannot carry every cosmetic item at once. The Bank in town allows you to store your extra gear. If you want to change your look back to a previous outfit, you must visit the Bank to retrieve your stored items.

1.  **Store Guns:** Keep your Winchester or Whitney Dragon safe while you experiment with other weapons.
2.  **Store Apparel:** Save your rare event items or expensive hats here.
3.  **Withdraw Moola:** Keep your hard-earned money safe from other players who might kill you for a small percentage of your wallet.

## Earning Moola for Customization

Changing your face and clothes isn't free. You will need a steady supply of Moola. For beginners, the best way to earn money is through fishing.

1.  **Buy a Rod:** Visit the Fishing Village (follow the tracks to the bridge).
2.  **Fish Peacefully:** Fish near the village or along the river to avoid bandit encounters.
3.  **Sell Fish:** Return to the hut in the village to sell your catch for Moola.
4.  **Open Chests:** Occasionally, you will hook a chest. These contain Moola, shards, and the rare herbs needed for the Witch's services.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I change my face in Bridger: Western if the Barber doesn't have the style I want?**

A: The Barber Shop offers a rotating or set selection of styles. If you are looking for a completely different facial structure, you may need to check the Tailor for masks or accessories, or wait for future game updates that expand the Barber's inventory.

**Q: Does changing my clothes affect my Stand's appearance?**

A: No, your Stand's appearance is fixed based on the type of Stand you have (e.g., Star Platinum, The World, or Silver Chariot). However, your character's clothing and accessories will remain visible while your Stand is summoned.

**Q: Can I change my character's gender after I've already started?**

A: Currently, character customization is limited to hair, face style, and clothing. There is no specific "Gender Swap" button in the 2026 version of the game, but you can achieve different looks by combining specific hair styles and clothing items from the Tailor.

**Q: Where do I get the Dog Bane herb to change my age?**

A: Dog Bane is a rare item. You can obtain it by killing the Boss Zombie encounter or as a rare drop from fishing chests. Once you have it, take it to the Witch at the Witch's Heart to de-age by 20 years.
