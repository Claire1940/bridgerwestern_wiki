## Understanding Primary Horse Stats

In Bridger Western, horses are defined by four core attributes. While most players prioritize pure velocity, a balanced mount often performs better in the rugged terrain of the swamp and desert biomes.

| Stat | Function | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | Determines the maximum velocity while sprinting. | **High** |
| **Stamina** | Affects how long the horse can maintain a sprint before slowing down. | **High** |
| **Jump** | Dictates the height of the horse's leap (Spacebar). | Medium |
| **Damage** | Increases the impact damage dealt when hitting players with F. | Low |

While speed is the most sought-after stat, you should never ignore stamina. A horse with a 1.35 speed stat but very low stamina will frequently run out of breath, leaving you vulnerable during long-distance travel. Expert players generally look for a "Speed-Stamina" hybrid to ensure consistent performance across the map.

## Rarity Tiers and Stat Caps

The rarity of a horse in Bridger Western is visually indicated by the brackets surrounding its name and the capitalization of the text. These rarities aren't just for show; they determine the "stat ceiling" of the animal. Most standard horses have a hard cap of **1.25** on any given stat. To go beyond this, you must find Legendary or Mythical variants.

### Legendary Horses
Legendary horses are identified by names in **ALL CAPS** with special brackets. A Legendary horse has the unique ability to "break" the stat cap for **one specific attribute**. For instance, while a normal horse might max out at 1.25 speed, a Legendary mount can reach **1.35 speed**. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Rarity does not guarantee a "good" horse. If a Legendary horse has a boosted Jump stat but terrible Speed, it may still be slower than a high-tier Common horse. Always check the numerical values.

### Mythical Horses
Mythical horses are the pinnacle of equine performance in 2026. These are distinguished by thicker, more ornate brackets and often feature unique coat colors. A Mythical horse can boost **two stats** above the 1.25 limit. The "God Roll" for any player is a Mythical horse with both Speed and Stamina breaking the 1.35 threshold.

## How to Get the Best Horse

To acquire a new mount, you must visit the Horse Seller NPC. Most players find the vendor by following the path from the fishing docks toward the main town. 

### The Rerolling Process
When you speak to the vendor, you will be presented with a horse and its current price. If the stats or rarity do not meet your standards, you can use the **"Show me another"** option to reroll. Each reroll generates a completely new horse with randomized stats and rarity.

| Price Range | Tier Expectation | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **150 - 500** | Low Tier / Starter | Good for beginners; skip once you have 1k+ mula. |
| **500 - 1,000** | Mid Tier / Decent | Reliable for general exploration and basic combat. |
| **1,000 - 1,500** | High Tier / Legendary | The sweet spot for finding competitive speed stats. |
| **1,500+** | Top Tier / Mythical | Rare spawns; buy immediately if Speed/Stamina are high. |

> **💡 Tip:** After purchasing a horse, it may take up to 15 minutes for the full, accurate stats to display in your menu (M). Be patient before deciding to wipe or resell.

## Advanced Horse Maneuvers and Combat

Your horse is more than just a taxi; it is a weapon and a tool for survival. Mastering the controls is vital for navigating the treacherous landscape of [Bridger Western on Roblox](https://www.roblox.com/games/18151608637/Bridger-WESTERN).

1.  **The Charge (F Key):** Pressing F while riding allows your horse to ram into NPCs or players. This deals significant damage and can ragdoll opponents, giving you time to draw your weapon or reposition.
2.  **Water Crossing:** Horses normally slow down in water. However, you can "spam" the spacebar while sprinting into water to maintain momentum. This is particularly useful for crossing the swamp to reach the Witch's Hut.
3.  **Mounting/Dismounting:** Use **H** to summon your horse and **N** to mount. If your horse is killed, there is a **two-minute respawn timer**. Protect your mount at all costs during a firefight.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the most important bridger western horse speed stats to look for?**

A: Speed and Stamina are the most critical stats. Speed determines your top velocity, while Stamina dictates how long you can maintain that speed. For most players, a Speed stat above 1.20 is considered "good," while 1.30+ is elite.

**Q: Does the price of a horse always mean it has better stats?**

A: Generally, yes, but not always. Price is heavily influenced by rarity. A Legendary horse might cost 1,400 mula because it has a 1.35 Jump stat, but it could have lower Speed than a 800 mula Common horse. Always check the individual stats before buying.

**Q: How do I see my horse's stats in Bridger Western?**

A: You can view your horse's stats by pressing the **M** key to open your character menu. Note that there is sometimes a delay (up to 15 minutes) after purchasing a new horse before the stats update accurately in the UI.

**Q: Can I change my horse's stats after I buy it?**

A: No, horse stats are permanent once purchased. If you are unhappy with your horse's performance, you must return to the Horse Seller NPC and reroll for a new one.
