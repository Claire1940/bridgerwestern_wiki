## How to Get the Best Horse

To upgrade your ride, you must travel to **Ridge County**. This is the central hub where most progression happens. Locate the Horse NPC situated directly in front of the train station. 

### Rerolling for Stats
When you interact with the Horse NPC, he will offer you a random horse with a specific name (like "Dusty Ghost") and a price. The name is often cosmetic, but the **price is the indicator of quality**. 

1. **Check the Price:** If the horse is under 1,000 Moola, it is likely a mid-tier mount.
2. **Reroll:** Click "Show me another" to cycle through the inventory.
3. **The 1.5k Limit:** In 2026, the maximum price for a legendary horse is approximately 1,500 Moola. If you find one in the 1,400+ range, buy it immediately.

## Horse Controls and Combat Mechanics

Your mount is also a potent weapon. By mastering the controls, you can utilize "Horse Tech" to navigate terrain that would normally slow you down.

| Action | Keybind | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Summon Horse** | **H** | Calls your horse to your current location. |
| **Mount / Dismount** | **N** | Enter or exit the saddle. |
| **Sprint** | **Shift** | Increases movement speed significantly. |
| **Horse Attack** | **F** | Performs a kick/strike that deals massive damage to players. |
| **Jump** | **Space** | Useful for clearing fences and small obstacles. |

### The "Water Tech" Trick
Standard horses typically slow down to a crawl when entering deep water. However, you can bypass this by sprinting (Shift) into the water and **spamming the Space bar**. This allows your horse to "hop" across the water surface at near-sprinting speeds, which is vital for reaching the Swamp or Outlaw Hideout quickly.

## Managing Horse Health and Aging

Horses in Bridger Western are not invincible. They have a health pool and an aging mechanic that players must monitor.

*   **Death & Respawn:** If your horse is killed in a gunfight, it will take approximately **two minutes** to become available for summoning again.
*   **Aging:** Every time you die or your horse is downed, the "Age" stat increases. While aging primarily changes the horse's appearance (adding gray hair or wrinkles), a very old horse may eventually require replacement to maintain peak performance.
*   **Combat Logging:** Avoid logging out while in combat. This can penalize your character's age by +10 years and may impact your horse's stats.

For more information on the latest updates, check the [official Bridger Western community pages](https://www.roblox.com/games/15104523317/Bridger-Western) to stay ahead of the meta.

## Advanced Strategies for Outlaws

If you have chosen the Outlaw alignment, your horse is your best tool for raiding the bank in Ridge County. High-tier horses from the **bridger western horse tier list** allow you to outrun the lawmen who spawn during robberies. 

> **Warning:** Using a Mule or a C-Tier horse for a bank heist is a recipe for disaster. Lawmen NPCs have high accuracy, and a slow horse will be downed before you reach the forest biome.

Always keep a "Tonic" in your inventory. While tonics are primarily for player healing, staying alive ensures you aren't separated from your mount during a chase. If you are knocked off your horse, press **G** to melee nearby enemies and create enough space to remount with **N**.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest horse in the bridger western horse tier list?**

A: There isn't one specific "named" horse that is the fastest. Instead, the speed is tied to the price. Any horse purchased for **1,300 to 1,500 Moola** will have the maximum possible speed stats currently available in the game.

**Q: Can I change my horse's name or appearance?**

A: Appearance is tied to the specific "breed" offered by the NPC (like Dusty Ghost or Traveler). You cannot manually change the skin, but you can reroll the NPC's shop until you find a high-stat horse with a look you prefer.

**Q: Does alignment affect horse stats?**

A: No, being an Outlaw or an In-law does not change the base stats of your horse. However, Outlaws often require faster horses to escape player hunters and NPC lawmen.

**Q: How do I heal my horse?**

A: Horses regenerate health slowly over time when not in combat. Ensure you dismount and move away from active gunfire to allow your horse's health bar to recover.
