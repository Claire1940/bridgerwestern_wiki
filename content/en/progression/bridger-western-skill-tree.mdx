## Essential Gear for the Melee Build

## Combat Mechanics and Mastering the Parry

The most powerful aspect of the current melee meta is the ability to parry. By equipping the **Show me a good time** card, your M1 (primary attack) gains the ability to reflect incoming bullets. This turns your offense into a mobile shield.

### How to Parrying Effectively
Parrying is not a passive ability; it requires precise timing. When an enemy is about to fire, performing an M1 swing will reflect the projectile. 
*   **Close Range:** High risk, high reward. Reflecting a shotgun blast or a rapid-fire Tommy gun at point-blank range can result in an instant kill as the bullets bounce directly back into the attacker.
*   **Long Range:** More difficult to land due to RNG-based reflection angles. It is better used as a defensive tool to close the gap rather than a primary source of damage.

### The M1 Stun Window
Every weapon has a limit. After performing four consecutive M1 swings with the Saber, your character will enter a brief "stun" or recovery animation. During this window, you are completely vulnerable. 

| Action | Result | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Swings 1-4** | High Damage (21 per hit) | Aggressive pressure |
| **Post-4th Swing** | Recovery Stun | Switch to Dual Dingers immediately |
| **Parry Timing** | Bullet Reflection | Time swings with enemy reload cycles |

## Advanced Tactics: Horse Combat and Stands

To truly maximize the **bridger western skill tree** potential, you must incorporate your mount into the fight. Horses in Bridger: Western are not just for transport; they are powerful tactical assets.

1.  **The Headbutt Combo:** Call your horse (ensure it is a breed that prioritizes damage). While mounted, pull out your Saber.
2.  **The Engagement:** Ride toward your target and press **F** to perform a horse headbutt. This deals significant damage and briefly stuns the enemy.
3.  **The Follow-up:** While the enemy is stunned from the headbutt, dismount and unleash your 4-hit Saber combo. This sequence can take a full-health player down to 20% HP in less than two seconds.

### Stand Synergies
If you are lucky enough to possess a Stand, certain abilities pair exceptionally well with the Saber:
*   **Hermit Purple:** Use the pull ability to drag enemies into melee range, preventing them from kiting you.
*   **The World / Star Platinum:** Time stops allow for guaranteed 4-hit combos without the risk of being shot during your recovery frames.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the Silver Card increase Saber damage in the bridger western skill tree?**

A: No. The Silver Card specifically buffs "Silver Pellets" and the "Silver Dagger." It provides zero benefits to the Saber. For damage buffs, use "Boy with Fist" or "Show me a good time."

**Q: How do I stop the stun animation after attacking with the sword?**

A: You cannot remove the stun entirely as it is a balance mechanic. However, you can mitigate the risk by switching to your Dual Dingers immediately after the fourth swing. This allows you to keep pressure on the enemy while your melee cooldown resets.

**Q: Is the Saber better than the Katana?**

A: In the current 2026 meta, the Saber is often preferred for its accessibility and the specific synergy with the "Show me a good time" card, which allows for bullet parrying—a feature that makes it superior for closing the gap against gun users.

**Q: Where is Mo's Guns located?**

A: Mo's Guns is located in the main Desert Town area. Look for the building with the gun sign near the center of the town. The Saber is found under the "Something Nifty" dialogue option.
