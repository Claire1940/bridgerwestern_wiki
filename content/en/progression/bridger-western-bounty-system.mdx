## Getting Started: Choosing Your Alignment

When you first launch the game, you are presented with a pivotal choice: your career. This choice directly influences how you interact with the **bridger western bounty system**. While the game offers a tutorial, most veteran players suggest diving straight into the action to begin building your tier level.

Most starters are encouraged to choose the Outlaw path. This career provides the most freedom, allowing you to engage in various activities without the strictures of the law, though it makes you a primary target for players looking to collect a bounty.

| Career Path | Primary Goal | Freedom Level | Risk Factor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outlaw** | Accumulate wealth and power through any means. | **High** | High (Targeted by Lawmen) |
| **Lawman** | Protect the innocent and hunt down wanted players. | **Medium** | Medium (Combat focused) |
| **Neutral** | Focus on progression, fishing, and stand acquisition. | **Low** | Low (Avoids conflict) |

> **💡 Tip:** If you spawn with less than 200 mula, consider using the "Wipe" feature in the main menu to reroll your starting cash. You can start with anywhere between 50 and 500 mula, and having at least 300 makes purchasing your first fishing rod and bait much easier.

## Core Controls and Interface

Before engaging with the **bridger western bounty system**, you must master the basic controls. Movement and combat in Bridger: Western are physics-based, meaning your horse's speed and your own positioning are vital.

*   **H Key:** Summon your horse. If your horse dies, it takes approximately two minutes to respawn.
*   **N Key:** Mount or demount your horse.
*   **Shift:** Sprint while on foot or gallop while riding.
*   **F Key:** Perform a horse bash. This does significant damage to players and NPCs.
*   **M Key:** Open the main menu to view your Age, Alignment, XP, and Tier.
*   **X Key:** Activate "ESP" vision to highlight nearby entities and players.
*   **C Key:** Toggle fisticuffs. Use this when you run out of ammunition during a heated shootout.

## The Mechanics of the Bounty System

The **bridger western bounty system** functions through a combination of player actions and NPC interactions. When an Outlaw commits a crime—such as killing a peaceful NPC or another player—their bounty increases. This bounty is visible to others, making the player a lucrative target.

### Earning and Clearing Bounties
As an Outlaw, your alignment shifts further toward "Chaos" as you commit crimes. Conversely, Lawmen earn "Order" by finishing off downed outlaws. To finish a player, you must shoot them in the head or body while they are in the "downed" state.

### Tier Progression
Your Tier represents your overall power level. There are currently three tiers available, and each tier unlocks a new "Card" or talent slot. You gain XP for your tiers by:
1.  Clearing NPC ambushes in the wilderness.
2.  Engaging in PvP combat.
3.  Completing quests found in major towns.

| Tier Level | Rewards Unlocked | Total Card Slots |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | Basic Gear Access | 1 |
| **Tier 2** | Advanced Gun Access | 2 |
| **Tier 3** | Elite Talents & Maximum HP | 3 |

## Economic Survival: Fishing and Horses

To effectively participate in the **bridger western bounty system**, you need a reliable source of income. While bounties pay well, they are risky. Fishing is the safest way for beginners to build their initial bankroll.

### The Fishing Strategy
Head to the docks near the Red Corner. Purchase a fishing rod and bait from the local vendor. Fishing not only provides fish that can be sold for mula but also offers a 0.5% chance to pull up a **Stand Arrow Shard**.

### Upgrading Your Steed
Your horse is your lifeline. A slow horse makes you an easy target for bounty hunters. Visit the horse seller in the main town to browse different breeds.

| Horse Quality | Price (Mula) | Speed Rating | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starter** | Free / 100 | Low | Initial travel |
| **Dusty Ghost** | 500 | Medium | General purpose |
| **Traveler** | 800 - 1,000 | High | Escaping Lawmen |
| **Legendary** | 1,500 | Elite | High-tier PvP |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always keep an eye on your horse's stamina. If you are caught in the swamp without stamina, you will be unable to outrun attackers, making you an easy claim for the **bridger western bounty system**.

## The Witch's Hut and Talent Cards

Located deep within the swamp area is the Witch's Hut. This NPC is crucial for refining your build. For 150 mula, the Witch will "read your fortune," allowing you to select or reroll your Talent Cards.

Each card provides a passive buff. For example, the **True Cowboy** talent increases gun damage by 17% while on horseback. If you are unhappy with your current cards, you can pay 50 mula for subsequent rerolls. These talents are essential for surviving high-bounty hunts where every percentage of damage matters.

## Stands and the Corpse Part System

For players seeking the ultimate power in Bridger: Western, the Stand system (inspired by JoJo's Bizarre Adventure) is the end-game goal. Stands are powerful ethereal guardians that provide unique abilities, such as time stop or rapid-fire strikes.

### Obtaining a Stand
There are two primary ways to obtain a Stand in 2026:
1.  **Arrow Shards:** Obtained via fishing (0.5% chance).
2.  **Corpse Parts:** A world event where a golden beam of light appears on the map.

When a Corpse Part spawns, the entire server is notified. Picking it up initiates a "merging" process. Every few seconds, there is a percentage chance that the part will grant you a Stand. The chance of success scales with the number of players in the server.

| Server Population | Success Chance (Every 30s) | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-5 Players** | 1 in 150 | Low |
| **10 Players** | 1 in 115 | Medium |
| **25 Players** | 1 in 40 | Extreme |

## Advanced Combat Tips

To master the **bridger western bounty system**, you must understand the gear mechanics.
*   **The Cowboy Hat:** Always purchase a cowboy hat from the Tailor. It acts as a one-time "headshot shield." When you are shot in the head, the hat flies off, absorbing the massive damage bonus that would otherwise kill you instantly.
*   **Silver Bullets:** While standard lead works for humans, keep an eye out for updates regarding Vampires and Zombies. Silver bullets are required for supernatural threats.
*   **The Train Tracks:** If you ever get lost, find the nearest train tracks. Following them in either direction will eventually lead you back to a major town or hub.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I clear my bounty in the bridger western bounty system?**

A: You can clear your bounty by visiting a Lawman NPC in town and paying a fine, or by waiting for your alignment to naturally drift back toward neutral through peaceful activities like fishing. Alternatively, being caught and "processed" by a Lawman player will reset your bounty upon respawn.

**Q: Does my age affect my gameplay?**

A: Yes. As you die or combat log, your age increases. Combat logging (leaving during a fight) increases your age by 10 years. While the exact "death by old age" mechanic is debated, older characters will develop wrinkles and gray hair.

**Q: Can I have more than one Stand at a time?**

A: No, you can only hold one Stand per character slot. To get a new Stand, you must either wipe your character or find a rare herb while fishing to give to the Witch, who can then reset your Stand.

**Q: What happens if someone kills my horse?**

A: If your horse is killed, it will despawn and enter a cooldown period. You will need to wait approximately two minutes before you can press "H" to summon it again. Be very careful, as being on foot makes you highly vulnerable to the **bridger western bounty system** hunters.
