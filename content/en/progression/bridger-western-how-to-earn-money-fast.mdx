## The Fishing Meta: Your Primary Income Stream

In the current 2026 meta, fishing remains the undisputed king of income generation. Unlike other activities that require high-level gear or specific Stands, fishing only requires a small initial investment in a rod and bait. To get started, players should head to the fishing village by following the train tracks to the forest biome. Locate the Taco Bait Shop and purchase a basic fishing rod and a supply of bait.

While you can fish in various bodies of water, the most profitable location is the river near the **Dig Site**. This specific spot is highly recommended because it allows you to multitask. While waiting for a bite, the area is prone to NPC invasions. Killing these NPCs provides a secondary stream of income and XP, making it the most efficient "Moola per hour" location in the game.

## Strategic NPC Farming and Invasions

While fishing provides a passive income, active players should look for NPC invasions. These events occur randomly throughout the map but are frequent in populated zones like Ridge County and the Dig Site. NPCs drop direct Moola upon death and occasionally drop ammo packs, which saves you the expense of buying them at the gun shop.

To maximize your earnings from combat, utilize the **Ocular Proess** (X key). This ability highlights enemies and increases your accuracy and reload speed. If you have unlocked specific cards like "High Noon" or "Evil Eye," you can even enable auto-targeting features that make clearing waves of NPCs trivial.

Your first major purchase should be a reliable horse. Do not settle for the cheapest option (the Mule). Instead, save up at least **1,000 to 1,100 Moola** and visit the NPC in front of the Ridge County train station.

## The Witch's Hut and Card Synergy

A hidden aspect of the economy is the Witch's Hut located in the swamp biome. While the Witch charges Moola to "read your fortune," this is how you unlock **Cards**. Cards are passive abilities that drastically increase your combat effectiveness. By spending 50 Moola per slot, you can create builds that make you nearly invincible against NPCs, allowing you to farm higher-tier areas like the Outlaw Hideout without fear of death.

If you find yourself with excess herbs from fishing, the Witch is also a primary buyer. Selling rare herbs here can net you thousands of Moola in seconds, providing a massive boost to your bank account. ## Advanced Wealth Tactics: Corpse Parts and Trading

For veteran players, the "Corpse Part" hunt is a high-risk, high-reward method. These parts spawn every 30 seconds, and the spawn rate increases with the number of players in the server. While they are primarily used to unlock powerful Stands, they can be traded or used to reach the "Max Tier" (Tier 3), which maximizes your card slots and overall power.

However, be prepared for heavy PvP. Unlike the chill fishing villages, Corpse Part spawns are combat hotspots. Only attempt this if you have a fast horse and a reliable firearm.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single fastest way for a beginner to get 5,000 Moola?**

A: The fastest way is to buy a fishing rod and bait immediately, then head to the river near the Dig Site. Fish consistently and sell everything you catch—including rare herbs—to the bait shop NPC or the Witch. You can easily reach 5,000 Moola in under 30 minutes with a bit of luck.

**Q: Why should I spend 1,000 Moola on a horse instead of the cheap one?**

A: Cheap horses have very low speed and stamina. A 1,000+ Moola horse allows you to outrun most threats and travel between Ridge County and the fishing spots much faster, which indirectly increases your hourly earnings.

**Q: Can I earn money through PvP in Bridger: Western?**

A: While you can earn money by killing other players, it is generally less efficient than fishing or NPC farming. PvP carries the risk of death, which increases your character's age and can result in losing your current progress or items if you aren't careful.

**Q: Where do I sell the Dog's Bane herb for the most money?**

A: While general stores might buy it, the Witch's Hut in the swamp biome is the best place to sell rare herbs for maximum profit, often reaching 2,500 Moola per herb.
