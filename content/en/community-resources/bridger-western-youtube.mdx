## The Perfect Start: Red Corner Strategy

When you first create a character in 2026, you are given a random amount of starting Mula, typically ranging from 50 to 500. To ensure a smooth progression, you should aim for a starting balance of at least 300 Mula.

1. **Choose the Outlaw Career:** This provides more freedom to roam and fewer restrictions on early-game activities.
2. **Spawn at Red Corner:** This location is the closest to the fishing docks, which is the most reliable way to make money.
3. **The "Wipe" Method:** If you spawn with less than 200 Mula, open the menu and select "Wipe." Re-create your character until you start with a higher balance. This allows you to buy a fishing rod and bait immediately.

## Economics: Fishing and Looting

Fishing is the backbone of the Bridger: WESTERN economy. It is a low-risk, high-reward activity that can even grant you endgame items if you are lucky.

### How to Fish Effectively
Go to the docks and purchase a **Fishing Rod (150 Mula)** and several pieces of bait. Watch for bubbles appearing near your line; as soon as they reach the rod, click to reel in your catch.

| Item | Rarity | Value/Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Bass** | Common | Low sell value; good for early XP. |
| **Treasure Chest** | Rare | Contains Mula, Dynamite, or rare items. |
| **Arrow Shard** | Legendary | **0.5% chance**; used to obtain a Stand. |
| **Saint's Plant** | Rare | Used to reset your current Stand. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The fishing docks are a "hot zone" for PvP. Many players will attempt to rob fishers of their chests. Keep your **X** key (ESP) active to spot approaching threats.

## Mastering Horses and Mobility

Your starter Mule is slow and has low stamina. Upgrading your mount is the first major investment you should make after securing a weapon.

*   **The Horse Merchant:** Located in the main town, the merchant sells horses with randomized stats.
*   **The Price Guide:** A mid-tier horse costs around 500 Mula. However, a **Legendary Horse** can cost up to **1,500 Mula**. These horses have maximum speed and health.
*   **Rerolling:** If the merchant is selling a "Dusty Ghost" or a low-tier horse, you can pay a small fee to see another option until a legendary variant appears.

For long-distance travel, consider using the **Train**. It stops at major stations periodically and is the safest way to cross the map without depleting horse stamina.

## The Witch’s Hut and Talent Cards

Once you reach Tier 2 or Tier 3 through fishing or combat, you will earn XP that can be spent at the **Witch's Hut** located in the swamp area.

To reach the swamp, you must ride your horse across the river by spamming the **Space bar** while sprinting through the water. Once at the hut, talk to the Witch to "Read your fortune."

### Talent Mechanics
*   **First Roll:** Free (or low cost depending on current server events).
*   **Rerolls:** 50 Mula per roll.
*   **Slots:** You can have up to 3 active talents.
*   **Weaving:** You can "weave" a new talent into an existing slot to replace an old ability.

| Talent Name | Effect |
|---|---|
| **True Cowboy** | 17% more gun damage while on horseback. |
| **Snake Eater** | Go invisible after standing still for 5 seconds. |
| **Winged Man** | Deal 25% more damage while in mid-air. |
| **Sturdy** | Grants 15% more maximum health. |

## Obtaining and Using Stands

Stands are the ultimate power-up in Bridger: WESTERN. They provide unique abilities like time stop, poison clouds, or rapid-fire barrages.

### The Saint's Corpse Event
Periodically, a message will appear at the top of the screen: *"It appears once again."* A massive golden beam will shoot into the sky, marking the location of a **Corpse Part**.

1. **The Merge:** If you pick up the part, you will begin "merging" with it. This takes about one minute.
2. **The Pulse:** Every pulse during the merge gives you a **15% to 20% chance** to obtain a Stand.
3. **The Risk:** Your location is revealed to the entire server. You will likely be hunted by everyone nearby.

### Stand Combat Tips
Using a Stand consumes **Energy**. If you run out of energy, your Stand will de-summon, leaving you vulnerable.
*   **M1 (Click):** Basic Stand attack/barrage.
*   **E, R, T Keys:** Special Stand abilities. For example, Purple Haze uses **T** to launch a poison bulb that auto-aims.
*   **Self-Damage:** Be careful with Stands like Purple Haze; you can accidentally poison yourself if you stand in your own clouds.

## Combat and Survival Events

The Wild West is dangerous, especially at night. You will encounter several random events that provide high XP but carry significant risk.

*   **Bandit Raids:** Groups of NPCs will spawn and attack the nearest town. They drop Mula and occasionally weapons like the **Shotgun**.
*   **Zombie Hordes:** During specific night cycles, zombies will rise. They are slow but can overwhelm you in numbers.
*   **The Coin Trick:** If you have the "Coin Roller" talent, you can toss a coin into the air and shoot it. The bullet will automatically ricochet toward the nearest enemy's head.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I lose my Stand if I die in Bridger: WESTERN?**

A: No, Stands are permanent unless you choose to reset them using a Saint's Plant at the Witch's Hut or by wiping your character. However, your "Age" increases with each death, which eventually leads to cosmetic changes like gray hair.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get a Stand in 2026?**

A: While the Saint's Corpse event is the most direct way, it is very dangerous. For a safer route, many players on **bridger western youtube** recommend "AFK Fishing" to find an Arrow Shard, which has a 0.5% drop rate.

**Q: How do I fix a "Rusty" weapon?**

A: You must find the Gunsmith NPC in the main town. He can repair rusty weapons, such as the Mayor's Leg, for a fee of 1,000 to 1,500 Mula.

**Q: Does alignment (Lawman vs. Outlaw) matter?**

A: Yes. Lawmen receive rewards for capturing outlaws with bounties, while outlaws have more freedom to rob banks and players without losing reputation. Most beginners prefer the Outlaw path for easier gold farming.
