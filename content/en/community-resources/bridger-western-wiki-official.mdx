## Getting Started in Bridger Western

Your adventure in Bridger Western begins with a few crucial choices. First, you'll create your character and select an alignment: **Outlaw** or **In-law**. Currently, this choice doesn't significantly impact gameplay mechanics, so feel free to pick whichever resonates with your desired role-play. After character creation, you'll choose a spawn point and enter the game. The world of Bridger Western can be unforgiving, with gunfights potentially erupting at any moment, so understanding your basic controls is paramount.

Here's a quick rundown of essential controls to get you started:

## Moola and Leveling Up: The Fishing Advantage

In Bridger Western, "Moola" is your currency, and leveling up, or "tiering up," unlocks crucial abilities like card slots. One of the most effective and peaceful ways to earn both is through fishing.

**Finding the Fishing Village:**
No matter where you spawn, seek out the train tracks. Follow them, or take the train directly, until you reach **Richby County**. From there, continue to the **Fishing Village**, which is often bustling with other players. This area is your primary hub for early game progression.

**Starting Your Fishing Career:**
Locate the **Taco Bait shop** within the Fishing Village. Talk to the NPC there and purchase a **fishing rod** and **bait**. Once equipped, find a spot by the water and begin fishing.

**The Fishing Process:**
Fishing involves a skill check where you'll need to press specific keys. Successfully completing these checks yields fish, which can be sold for Moola. Occasionally, you'll reel in a chest containing more valuable items.

| :---------------------- | :-------------------- | :---------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fish**                | Cod, Tuna, Salmon     | N/A                                       | Primary source of **Moola** when sold.                                |
| **Special Items**       | Rusty Mayor's Leg     | Dog Bane Herb, Steel Wires, Dynamite      | Crafting materials, de-aging (Dog Bane), throwable weapons.           |
| **Stand Acquisition**   | N/A                   | Stand Arrows (0.5% chance from fishing)   | Essential for obtaining powerful Stands.                              |
| **XP & Leveling**       | All successful catches | N/A                                       | Contributes to your overall XP and **Tier** progression.              |

**Selling Your Catch:**
Return to the NPC at the Taco Bait shop to sell your accumulated fish for Moola. This steady income will allow you to purchase better gear and unlock more cards. Remember, every time you die, your character's age increases. **Dog Bane Herbs**, rarely found through fishing or other encounters, can be sold to the Witch or used to de-age your character by 20 years.

## Gear Up: Weapons, Horses, and Accessories

Once you've accumulated some Moola, it’s time to upgrade your arsenal and mobility. Richby County is your central hub for all shopping needs.

**Horse Stables:**
Located near the train station in Richby County, the horse stables are where you can purchase a mount. Always aim for horses costing **1000 Moola or more**, as these generally have superior speed and stats. Keep re-rolling until you find a horse that meets this criterion. A good horse significantly improves your travel speed and survivability in Bridger Western.

> **💡 Tip:** Utilize the "horse tech" by holding Shift and then Space while in water to cross bodies of water quickly.

**Weaponry and Combat:**
Richby County features a main gun store (often referred to as "Bow Gun") offering a variety of firearms, from pistols like the **Winchester** and **Maverick** to larger guns like the **Tommy Gun**. You can also find bows and other specialized weapons here. Ammo packs, crucial for prolonged engagements, can be bought from shops or found at bandit outposts.

For a more clandestine selection, head to the **Outlaw Hideout**. From the Fishing Village, go straight from the Taco Bait shop. This hideout sells unique weapons like the **Cold** and **Double Barrel** shotgun, as well as throwables like **Steel Wires**, **Dynamite**, and **Molotovs** (though some throwables can also be found through fishing chests).

**Essential Accessories and Utilities:**
The **General Store** in Richby County is your go-to for protective gear and useful items.

## Unleashing Power: Stands and Cards

The most unique and powerful aspect of Bridger Western, inspired by JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, is the acquisition and utilization of **Stands** and **Cards**.

**Acquiring Stands:**
There are two primary methods to obtain a Stand:

1.  **Stand Arrows/Shards:** These are rare drops from fishing chests (0.5% chance) or found as shards in chests from fishing.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** This is a faster but more dangerous method. A message will appear in the sky indicating a corpse part has spawned, followed by a beam of light. Players who pick it up will glow white, making them targets for others. If you hold it for a few seconds, it will merge with you, granting a Stand. If you're killed while holding it, it drops and can be picked up by another player.

**The Elusive D4C:**
While most Stands are obtainable through the above methods, **D4C** (Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap) has a highly challenging and specific acquisition requirement:
-   Achieve 100 overall kills.
-   Get a 10-player kill streak in a single server.
-   Be "knocked" (downed) in the desert while on this kill streak.
-   Have a corpse part spawn during this knocked state, which then has a 10% chance to merge a "heart" with you, granting D4C.

**Unlocking and Using Cards:**
Cards provide passive abilities and buffs, significantly customizing your playstyle. You unlock card slots by "tiering up" (leveling). You can have a maximum of three card slots.

To unlock cards, you must visit the **Witch's Hut**, located in the swamp area near the Fishing Village.
-   The first card unlock is free.
-   Subsequent cards cost 50 Moola each.
-   The Witch also offers services like de-aging (using Dog Bane Herb) and resetting your current Stand.

## Navigating the Wild West

The world of Bridger Western is expansive, but knowing key landmarks and how to get around efficiently will save you time and keep you safe.

The **train tracks** are your most reliable guide; following them will lead you to most major locations, including Richby County and the Fishing Village. Keep an eye on your map (M key) to track your location and stats, including your XP, tier, and age.

**Encounters and Dangers:**
The Wild West is fraught with peril. You'll encounter two main types of hostile NPCs:
-   **Bandits:** These are common human enemies found across the map. Defeating them can yield XP and money chests.
-   **Zombies:** These undead foes emerge from the ground. Occasionally, a **Boss Zombie** may spawn, offering a greater challenge but rewarding Moola or Dog Bane Herbs upon defeat.

Be prepared for player-versus-player combat at any moment, as Bridger Western encourages open-world interaction and rivalry.

To truly excel in Bridger Western, combining your knowledge of mechanics with smart strategies is key.

*   **Early Game Focus:** Prioritize fishing in the Fishing Village to quickly accumulate Moola and level up. This allows you to buy a good horse and essential accessories like the Cowboy Hat early on.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Moola in Bridger Western?**

A: Fishing in the Fishing Village is generally considered the fastest and safest way to earn Moola early on. Selling your catches to the NPC at the Taco Bait shop provides a steady income. Killing bandits and zombies can also provide Moola through chest drops.

**Q: How do I get a Stand in Bridger Western?**

A: Stands can be obtained in two main ways: by finding Stand Arrows or shards (rare drops from fishing chests) or by acquiring a Corpse Part. Corpse Parts spawn periodically with a beam of light; picking one up and surviving for a short period will merge it with you, granting a Stand.

**Q: Where can I find the Witch's Hut for cards?**

A: The Witch's Hut is located in the swamp area, which is accessible from the Fishing Village. It's where you can unlock your card slots, select new cards, and use Dog Bane Herbs to de-age your character.

**Q: Are there any unobtainable Stands in Bridger Western?**

A: Yes, the Stand called White Snake is currently an admin-exclusive Stand and cannot be obtained by regular players. D4C is technically obtainable but requires a very specific and difficult set of conditions to be met.
