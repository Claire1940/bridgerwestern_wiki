## The Corpse Part Method: High-Stakes Hunting

The most direct and arguably fastest way to acquire a Stand is through the Corpse Part system. This is a dynamic world event that triggers based on server activity. When a Corpse Part spawns, a massive yellow beam of light shoots into the sky, visible to every player on the map. This immediately turns the spawn location into a war zone.

### Spawn Mechanics and Requirements
Corpse Parts do not spawn randomly; they follow specific server logic. To maximize your efficiency, you need to understand the timing and player count requirements.

| Mechanic | Requirement/Detail |
|---|---|
| **Minimum Players** | At least **3 players** must be in the server. |
| **Spawn Frequency** | Approximately every **30 seconds** (chance based). |
| **Visual Indicator** | A bright **Yellow Beam** of light in the sky. |
| **Global Announcement** | The entire server receives a notification when a part spawns. |
| **Risk Factor** | Parts can be **dropped** if you take damage while holding them. |

### Pro Strategy: Low-Population Farming
While more players increase the spawn frequency, they also increase the number of hunters coming for your prize. If you want a safer experience, navigate to the server browser. Sort the servers by "Ascending" to find those with exactly three or four players. This allows the Corpse Part to spawn while minimizing the number of enemies you have to fight off once you claim the item.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Once you pick up a Corpse Part, you must survive until the "Merge" process is complete. If you are hit by another player during this time, there is a significant chance the part will drop, allowing someone else to steal your hard-earned progress.

## The Fishing Method: The Patient Path

If the chaos of PvP isn't for you, fishing offers a solo-friendly alternative for **how to get a stand in bridger western**. By fishing, you have a rare chance to pull an **Arrow Shard**, which can be used to obtain a Stand. This method is considered the "easiest" because it involves zero combat, but it is the "longest" due to the low drop rates.

### How to Start Fishing
1.  **Locate the Taco Bait Shop:** If you are new, follow the train tracks. The tracks lead directly to the shop.
2.  **Purchase Gear:** Speak to the NPC to buy a Fishing Rod and Bait.
3.  **Find Water:** Head to any fishing spot and cast your line.
4.  **The Mini-game:** When the blue indicator moves near your bobber, left-click to hook the catch.

### Fishing Loot and Odds
The Arrow Shard is a Legendary-tier drop. It is important to note that you cannot store this item in a bank. If you die or leave the server while holding an Arrow Shard, it is lost forever.

| Item | Drop Chance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Arrow Shard** | **0.5%** | Used to get a Stand; lost on death or disconnect. |
| **Common Fish** | High | Can be sold for cash to buy better gear. |
| **Rare Fish** | Medium | Provides a decent income stream while grinding. |

> **💡 Tip:** Never fish in a populated area if you are hunting for an Arrow Shard. If you successfully pull one, use it immediately. Do not attempt to travel across the map with it, as you become a prime target for griefers.

### Key Horse Stats
For Stand hunters, **Stamina** is the king of stats. You need to be able to outrun pursuers for long distances without stopping to rest.

| Stat | Priority | Ideal Value |

## The Product Shop and Stand Arrows

For players with Robux to spare, the Product Shop offers a shortcut. You can purchase **Stand Arrows**, but be warned: the rates are not necessarily better than the free methods.

*   **Stand Arrow Rate:** There is a **0.5% chance** to get a high-tier Stand (like White Snake) from a shop arrow.
*   **Skin Chance:** There is a **5% chance** to roll a unique skin for your Stand.
*   **The Catch:** Developers have noted that "normal obtainment" (Corpse Parts) actually has more favorable rates for rare Stands than the Robux-purchased arrows.

## Obtaining Specific Stands: D4C and Tusk

Some Stands have unique requirements beyond just finding a Corpse Part. 

### Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap (D4C)
D4C is highly sought after for its dimension-hopping abilities. To get it, you don't just need a part; you need to meet specific combat criteria:
*   **100 Total Kills:** A baseline requirement for the Stand to recognize your worth.
*   **10 Kill Streak:** You must prove your dominance in a single life.
*   **The Desert Knockdown:** You must be "knocked" (downed) in the Desert Biome while a Corpse Part is active in the world. There is a **10% chance** upon being knocked that you will receive D4C.

### Tusk
Tusk is currently the highest-demand Stand in the game. It is primarily obtained through the **Left Arm of the Corpse**. Because of its power, players in Discord groups often have separate priority lists specifically for Tusk parts.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I have more than one Stand at a time?**

A: No. If you already possess a Stand, you cannot use a Stand Arrow or merge with a Corpse Part. You must first remove your current Stand if you wish to obtain a new one.

**Q: Does the yellow beam always mean a Stand?**

A: The yellow beam indicates a Corpse Part. While these are the primary way **how to get a stand in bridger western**, the specific Stand you get depends on which part of the corpse you find (e.g., Left Arm, Heart, etc.).

**Q: What happens if I leave the game with a Corpse Part in my inventory?**

A: Corpse Parts and Arrow Shards are "session items." They do not save to your permanent inventory. If you disconnect, leave, or die, the item is lost. You must use the item to gain the Stand before exiting the game.

**Q: Is there a way to change my character's face for free?**

A: Yes. While the shop sells face tools for 75 Robux, getting hit by a **Crazy Diamond** Stand's punches has a 10% chance to permanently change your face for free.
