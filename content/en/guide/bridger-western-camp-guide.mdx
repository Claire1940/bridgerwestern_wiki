## Getting Started: Your First Steps in the Frontier

When you first launch the game, you are presented with the character creation screen. For most players, selecting the **Outlaw** career path is recommended. Outlaws enjoy significantly more freedom to explore and engage with the world's mechanics without the immediate restrictions of the law. 

One of the most critical "pro tips" for new players involves your starting capital. When you spawn, you receive a random amount of money ranging from $50 to $500. If you find yourself starting with less than $200, it is often beneficial to "wipe" your character and restart until you secure a higher starting balance. This initial "mula" is vital for purchasing your first fishing rod and bait, which are the cornerstones of the early-game economy.

## The Fishing Economy and Early Wealth

Once you have secured at least $300, head directly to **Red Corner**. This location is favored by the community because of its proximity to the fishing docks and the horse vendor. Fishing is the most reliable way to gain experience (XP) and money simultaneously.

To begin, visit the dock vendor and purchase a **Fishing Rod ($150)** and at least one **Bait ($15)**. The fishing minigame is straightforward: cast your line and wait for bubbles to appear around the bobber. Once the bubbles converge, click to reel in your catch. While most catches will be common fish like Bass, which can be sold back to the vendor, there is a small chance to pull up treasure chests or even the legendary **Arrow Shard**.

| Catch Type | Rarity | Value/Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Bass/Common Fish** | Common | Low sell value; good for early XP. |
| **Rare Fish** | Uncommon | High sell value at any dock vendor. |
| **Treasure Chest** | Rare | Contains high-tier loot and significant gold. |
| **Arrow Shard** | Legendary (0.5%) | Used to obtain a Stand power. |
| **Dog Herb** | Rare | Used to reset Stand powers at the Witch. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling with money, use an alt account to "wipe" and transfer the starting funds to your main character. This is a common strategy to afford a high-tier horse early on.

## Horse Management and Upgrades

In Bridger, your horse is more than just transportation; it is your lifeline. You start with a basic Mule, which is slow and has low stamina. Your primary goal should be to save between $1,000 and $1,500 to purchase a **Legendary Horse**. 

When talking to a horse vendor, they will offer you a random horse. You can "reroll" the selection by asking to see another horse. Look for horses in the $1,500 price range, as these possess the best speed, health, and jump stats.

## The Witch’s Hut and Tarot Talents

As you gain XP through fishing or combat, you will progress through Tiers. Each Tier allows you to equip a new **Tarot Card**, which grants passive talents. To unlock these, you must find the **Witch’s Hut**, typically located in the swampy regions across the water from the main docks.

The first fortune reading is free, but subsequent rolls or changes will cost $50 to $150. These talents can drastically change your playstyle. For example, the **True Cowboy** talent increases gun damage by 17% while on horseback, making it essential for mounted combat builds.

| Talent Name | Effect | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **True Cowboy** | +17% Gun Damage on Horse | Mounted Combat |
| **Agile Runner** | Increased Sprint Speed | Exploration/Escape |
| **Tough Hide** | Reduced Incoming Damage | PvP/Tanking |
| **Master Fisher** | Increased Rare Catch Chance | Economy/Grinding |

## Advanced Progression: Stands and Corpse Parts

Bridger incorporates unique supernatural elements inspired by popular media, specifically the "Stand" system. Obtaining a Stand is the ultimate goal for most players. There are two primary ways to acquire one:

1.  **Fishing:** A 0.5% chance to find an Arrow Shard.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** A golden beam will occasionally appear on the map with the message "It appears once again." This signals a Corpse Part spawn.

When you pick up a Corpse Part, you must hold it while a pulse emanates from your character. Each pulse has a 15-20% chance to grant you a Stand. However, the entire server can see the golden beam, meaning you will likely have to defend yourself against every other player while the process completes.

| Server Population | Spawn Chance (Every 30s) | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| **1-5 Players** | 1 in 150 | Low |
| **10 Players** | 1 in 115 | Medium |
| **25 Players** | 1 in 40 | Very High |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Aging is a real mechanic. Every time you die, your character ages by 1-2 years. Combat logging (leaving during a fight) increases your age by 10 years. As you get older, your hair will turn grey and wrinkles will appear.

## Key Locations and Shopping

Outside of Red Corner, the desert town is the main hub for equipment. Following the train tracks is the easiest way to navigate between the major "camps" and settlements.

*   **Mo’s Gun Store:** The place to buy primary weapons like rifles and shotguns.
*   **Coyote General:** Sells essential cowboy gear, including lassos, hats, and ponchos.
*   **Barber/Tailor:** Used for cosmetic customization to refine your outlaw look.
*   **The Bank:** Secure your funds here to prevent losing them upon death.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to start in this bridger western camp guide?**

A: Red Corner is widely considered the best starting location. It provides immediate access to the fishing docks for money grinding and is close to the horse vendor for your first mount upgrade.

**Q: How do I reset my Stand if I don't like the one I got?**

A: You need to find a **Dog Herb** through fishing. Once you have the herb, take it to the Witch at her hut in the swamp, and she can reset your Stand power for a fee.

**Q: Does character age actually kill you?**

A: While there are community rumors that extreme old age causes a permanent wipe (heart attack), the current 2026 version primarily uses age for cosmetic changes like grey hair and wrinkles. However, avoid combat logging, as the 10-year penalty is severe.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get a Legendary Horse?**

A: The fastest method is to grind fishing at the docks and sell everything you catch. Alternatively, you can use alt accounts to pool starting money onto one character to reach the $1,500 requirement quickly.
