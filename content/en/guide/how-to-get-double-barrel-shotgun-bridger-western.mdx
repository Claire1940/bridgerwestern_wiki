## Where to Find the Double Barrel Shotgun

In the world of Bridger Western, weapons are divided between general gunsmiths and specialized hidden shops. While the Maverick 88 is commonly found in the main town, the Double Barrel Shotgun is tucked away in a more secluded location. 

To find the vendor, you must head toward the **Outlaw Hideout**. This location is situated near the swampy regions, not far from the popular Fishing Village. Most players use the Fishing Village as their primary hub for progression, making it the perfect starting point for your journey.

### Step-by-Step Navigation
1.  **Locate the Fishing Village**: Follow the train tracks from Richby County until you reach the forest biome or the train station near the Taco Bait Shop.
2.  **Head Toward the Swamp**: From the Taco Bait Shop, move straight into the wilderness toward the Outlaw territory.
3.  **Find the Outlaw NPC**: Look for a specialized vendor standing near the hideout. This NPC sells unique gear that isn't available in the standard Richby County gun shop.
4.  **Purchase the Weapon**: Interact with the NPC to view his inventory. He typically offers the "Cold" revolver and the **Double Barrel Shotgun**.

## Combat Mechanics and Weapon Synergy

Owning the shotgun is only half the battle. To be effective in Bridger Western, you must master the game's unique combat mechanics. The Double Barrel Shotgun excels when paired with **Ocular Prowess** (activated by pressing X). This ability highlights enemies, increases your reload speed, and improves your overall accuracy.

### Mastering the Double Barrel
The Double Barrel is a high-risk, high-reward weapon. It typically holds only two shells, meaning you must make every shot count. If you miss, you are left vulnerable during the reload animation. 

*   **Close Range**: This is where the shotgun shines. A point-blank blast can down most players instantly.
*   **Secondary Pairing**: Always carry a reliable secondary, such as the Whitney Dragoon or the Schofield. Use the shotgun for the initial burst, then swap to your revolver to finish off the target at medium range.
*   **Mobility**: Use the roll mechanic (Q) to close the distance or dodge incoming fire while you reload your shells.

| Control | Action | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1** | Fire Weapon | Standard attack |
| **R** | Reload | Essential for the 2-shot capacity |
| **X** | Ocular Prowess | Boosts reload speed and accuracy |
| **G** | Melee | Use if an enemy survives the blast |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when traveling with high-value fish or large amounts of cash. If you die, your age increases, and you risk losing your current progress toward your next gear upgrade.

## Advanced Progression: Cards and Stands

As you progress in Bridger Western, you will eventually want to enhance your shotgun build with Cards. By visiting the Witch’s Hut in the swamp, you can "Read Your Fortune" to unlock passive abilities. Some cards, like **High Noon**, can even grant aim-assist capabilities when combined with Ocular Prowess.

If you are focusing on a "Shotgun Build," look for cards that increase your movement speed or damage resistance. Since you need to get close to your enemies to be effective, being "tanky" is a significant advantage. Additionally, keep an eye out for **Corpse Parts** or **Stand Arrows** while fishing. While the shotgun is powerful, having a Stand ability provides a secondary layer of defense that can protect you while you reload your Double Barrel.

## FAQ

**Q: How much does the Double Barrel Shotgun cost in Bridger Western?**

A: Prices can fluctuate based on game updates, but generally, you should have at least 1,000 to 2,000 Moolah saved up to cover the weapon and initial ammo packs. Fishing for 15-20 minutes is usually enough to afford it.

**Q: Can I get the Double Barrel Shotgun in Richby County?**

A: No, the standard gunsmith in Richby County sells weapons like the Winchester and Maverick 88. To learn **how to get double barrel shotgun bridger western**, you must visit the Outlaw Hideout vendor located near the swamp.

**Q: Is the Double Barrel better than the Maverick 88?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. The Maverick 88 has more shells (4) and is better for PvE (zombies/bandits). The Double Barrel has higher burst damage, making it superior for quick PvP eliminations if you have good aim.

**Q: What happens if I die after buying the shotgun?**

A: You generally keep your purchased weapons, but your character's age will increase. If you reach a certain age, you may need to find items like Dog's Bane to reduce it. Always store excess Moolah in the bank located in Richby County to prevent loss upon death.
