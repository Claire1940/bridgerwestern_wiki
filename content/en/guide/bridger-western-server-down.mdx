## The Trading System and Rokakaka Seeds

With the servers back up, the economy has shifted toward the new Rokakaka system. Trading is no longer a simple menu interaction; it requires a specific item and level requirement.

To trade stands or cards, you must obtain a **Rokakaka fruit**. These are acquired by first getting a lumber axe and chopping down trees in the Forest or Swamp biomes. This adds a "survival" layer to the game, moving it away from being a pure "fishing simulator."

### How to Trade Successfully
1.  **Obtain a Lumber Axe:** Purchase this from the new Lumber NPC.
2.  **Chop Trees:** Head to the Swamp or Forest. Swamp trees have a rare chance to drop Dog Vein.
3.  **Get the Seed:** Use the wood to eventually secure a Rokakaka.
4.  **Reach Level 3:** Both players must be at least Tier 3 (Level 3) to initiate a trade.
5.  **The Split:** Click on another player with the Rokakaka to prompt a trade.

> **💡 Tip:** There is a 1% chance during a trade that your stand skin will become "Shiny." This is currently the rarest cosmetic tier in the game.

## The Pay-to-Win Controversy and New Shop Items

While the **bridger western server down** period brought many requested features like console support, it also introduced "Developer Products" that have divided the community. In the top-left corner of the new UI, players can now purchase various rerolls using Robux.

| Product | Effect | Community Sentiment |
|---|---|---|
| **Stand Reroll** | 5% chance for a skin | Highly controversial; seen as "pay-to-win." |
| **Horse Reroll** | Changes horse stats/appearance | Generally accepted as cosmetic. |
| **Face/Name Reroll** | Customizes character appearance | Positive; allows for better roleplay. |
| **Ocular Prowess** | Randomizes eye color/ability | Mixed; depends on the rarity of the roll. |

Critics argue that allowing players to buy stand rerolls diminishes the value of the grind, especially for a game often described as a "passion project." However, the developers maintain that these products help fund the ongoing maintenance and server costs.

## UI Overhaul and Console Integration

If you are playing on a console, the controls are mapped to provide a fluid combat experience, though some menu navigation still requires the use of a virtual cursor. This expansion to console players is expected to significantly increase the player count throughout 2026.

## FAQ

**Q: Why was the bridger western server down for so long?**

A: The servers were down for approximately four days to implement Update 1. This included a total UI overhaul, the addition of the trading system, and the integration of console support for Xbox and PlayStation.

**Q: How do I get a skin for my stand in the new update?**

A: There are two main ways: you have a 5% chance to get a skin when rolling for a stand using an arrow, or a 1% chance to get a "Shiny" skin when trading with another player using a Rokakaka fruit.

**Q: What happened to my Golden Experience Requiem (GER)?**

A: GER has been temporarily removed from the game for a total rework. If you owned it, it has been replaced by a rare shiny stand. The developers have confirmed that GER will return in a future 2026 update with new requirements.

**Q: Where can I find the Lumber NPC?**

A: The Lumber NPC is located near the entrance to the forest area. You will need to interact with him to get the axe required for chopping trees and obtaining Rokakaka seeds.
