### The Lumber Axe and Roa Seed Quest

To trade for a **white snake bridger western** stand, you cannot simply open a menu. You must earn your right to trade. First, head to the campsite located on the far edge of the map. Inside a small wooden shed, you will find an NPC who provides the Lumber Axe. 

Once you have the axe, you must find compatible trees to chop down. Not every tree is interactable, so look for the sturdier models near the forest outskirts. Chopping these trees gives you a chance to drop a **Roa Seed**. This seed is the "key" to the trading economy. You must take this seed to the Greenlands—a specific fertile zone on the map—and plant it. Once the plant matures, you gain the ability to initiate trades with other players.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Greenlands are a high-traffic PVP zone. Players often camp the planting spots to steal Roa Seeds from unsuspecting farmers. Always bring a horse for a quick getaway.

The **white snake bridger western** stand is a mid-to-long-range powerhouse that focuses on utility and "Stand Stripping." Unlike brute-force stands like Star Platinum, White Snake requires precision and timing.

### The Power of the "R" Move (Disc Extraction)

The defining feature of White Snake is the **Disc Extraction**. When you successfully land the R move, a physical disc flies out of the opponent's body. This disc contains their stand. Until the player walks over and picks that disc back up, they are completely defenseless, unable to summon their stand for protection or counter-attacks.

In a 2026 combat environment, this is incredibly powerful. You can use the G move (Acid Melt) to ragdoll the enemy right after taking their disc, making it nearly impossible for them to reach it before you finish them off with a Winchester or Tommy Gun.

## Advanced Strategy: Remote Control Ganking

The V move, **Remote Control**, is where White Snake truly shines in the open-world setting of Bridger: Western. When you activate Remote Control, your physical body remains stationary while you pilot White Snake. 

In this mode, White Snake has **infinite stamina**. You can chase down players on horseback or catch up to those trying to flee into safe zones. The range is extensive, though not infinite. If you move too far from your character's body, the connection will snap back.

**Tactical Tips for Remote Control:**
1.  **Hide your body:** Before using V, hide in a bush or behind a rock. If someone finds your physical body while you are controlling the stand, you are a sitting duck.
2.  **Pressure the Disc:** Use the Remote Control to knock the disc out of an enemy (R move) and then use the stand to body-block them from reaching it.
3.  **Acid Traps:** While in remote mode, you can still use the G move to create "no-go zones" for enemies, forcing them into corners where your barrage can finish them.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I pick up someone else's stand disc?**

A: No. Only the player who lost the disc can pick it up to regain their stand. However, as the White Snake user, you can physically block them or use moves to push them away from the disc's location.

**Q: Does White Snake have a pilot mode?**

A: Yes, the V key activates a remote control variant. In this mode, the stand has infinite stamina and can travel a significant distance from the user, though the user's body remains vulnerable during this time.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Roa Seeds for a white snake bridger western trade?**

A: Roa Seeds are obtained by chopping down specific trees using the Lumber Axe. These trees are most commonly found around the outskirts of the forest and near the campsite. Once you have a seed, you must plant it in the Greenlands to enable trading.

**Q: What happened to Gold Experience?**

A: In the latest update, Gold Experience was removed/reset for all players. It has been replaced in the meta by newer stands like White Snake and Stone Free. If you previously had GE, you may need to re-roll or trade for a new stand.
