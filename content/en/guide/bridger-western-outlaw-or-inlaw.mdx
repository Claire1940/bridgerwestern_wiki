## Choosing Your Path: Outlaw vs. Inlaw

The core of the Bridger experience lies in its alignment system. While the game is constantly evolving, the fundamental differences between these two roles remain the primary driver for player interaction. Choosing your alignment isn't just a cosmetic choice; it affects where you start and how you earn your keep.

| Feature | Outlaw | Inlaw (Lawman) |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Spawn** | Campsite / Red Corner | Ridge B County / Townships |
| **Playstyle** | High freedom, banditry, and raiding. | Protective, bounty-focused, and orderly. |
| **NPC Interaction** | Targeted by lawmen; friendly with bandits. | Targeted by bandits; supported by town guards. |
| **Best For** | Players who enjoy PvP and high-risk fishing. | Players who prefer trading and structured quests. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are a new player, starting as an Outlaw and spawning at the **Red Corner** is highly recommended. This location provides the quickest access to the fishing docks, which is the most reliable way to earn early-game currency.

## Getting Started: The First 30 Minutes

Once you have decided on your **bridger western outlaw or inlaw** status, your first priority is money. Upon character creation, you will spawn with a random amount of cash ranging from $50 to $500. If you spawn with less than $200, many veteran players recommend "wiping" your character and restarting until you get a better starting purse. This initial capital is vital for purchasing your first fishing rod and bait.

### The Fishing Meta
Fishing is not just a side activity in Bridger; it is a core progression mechanic. By visiting the docks near Red Corner, you can purchase a rod and begin gathering resources.

1.  **Buy a Fishing Rod:** Visit the dock vendor and grab a rod and at least one stack of bait.
2.  **Look for Bubbles:** Cast your line and wait for the bubble animation. Click immediately to reel in your catch.
3.  **Rare Drops:** While you will mostly catch Bass, fishing has a **0.5% chance** to drop a Stand Arrow shard.
4.  **Sell Your Catch:** Return to the vendor to exchange your fish for money, which you will need for horses and guns.

## Mastering the Horse System

In the vast expanse of Bridger, your horse is your lifeline. Whether you are a **bridger western outlaw or inlaw**, you cannot survive long on foot. Horses in 2026 come with randomized stats, and understanding the vendor's dialogue is key to finding a "God-tier" mount.

### Horse Stat Caps
Every horse has stats for Speed, Stamina, and Health. The maximum value for any individual stat is **1.25**. When purchasing a horse, pay close attention to the price and the description provided by the stable master.

| Vendor Dialogue | Estimated Quality | Typical Price |
|---|---|---|
| "Solid enough for a traveler." | Low to Mid Tier | $500 - $700 |
| "Faster than a bullet!" | High Tier | $1,000 - $1,200 |
| "It's a beast!" | **Legendary Tier** | $1,200 - $1,500 |

> **Warning:** If your horse is killed in combat, it takes approximately two minutes to recover. During this time, you are extremely vulnerable to both NPCs and other players.

## Acquiring Your Stand: Corpse Parts and Shards

Bridger is set within the Steel Ball Run universe, meaning the ultimate goal for most players is to acquire a Stand. There are two primary ways to obtain these powerful abilities: finding a Corpse Part or using a Stand Arrow shard.

### Corpse Part Spawn Rates
Corpse Parts are world events that trigger a massive beam of light visible to everyone on the map. The spawn rate depends heavily on the server population.

| Server Population | Spawn Chance (Every 30s) | Estimated Spawn Time |
|---|---|---|
| 3 Players | 1 in 240 | 2+ Hours |
| 10 Players | 1 in 115 | ~1 Hour |
| 25+ Players | **1 in 30** | 15 - 30 Minutes |

When a Corpse Part spawns, it becomes a race. Once you pick it up, you must stay alive while it "absorbs" into your body. During this process, a white light will emanate from your character, signaling your location to every **bridger western outlaw or inlaw** in the vicinity. If you die before the absorption is complete, you drop the part.

## Character Progression: Talents and the Witch

As you gain XP by killing zombies, bandits, or completing quests, you will increase your Tier. Each Tier allows you to equip new talents. To manage these, you must locate the **Witch's Hut** hidden in the swamp area.

### Talent Weaving
For a fee of 150 currency, the Witch will read your fortune and present you with talent options. 
- **First Talent:** Usually free or low cost.
- **Rerolling:** Costs 50 currency per attempt.
- **Slots:** You can have up to three active talents at higher tiers.

One of the most sought-after talents is **True Cowboy**, which increases your gun damage by 17% while on horseback. This is particularly effective for those playing as an outlaw who frequently engage in mounted drive-by attacks.

## Survival Mechanics and Aging

Bridger features a unique aging system that adds a layer of consequence to your actions. By pressing 'M', you can view your character's stats, including your current age.

- **Dying:** Increases your age by 1 to 2 years.
- **Combat Logging:** A severe penalty that increases your age by 10 years.
- **Visual Changes:** As you age, your character will develop wrinkles and gray hair. 

While there is debate in the community about whether "old age" leads to a permanent character wipe, it is generally best to avoid unnecessary deaths to keep your character in peak physical condition. ## FAQ

**Q: Is it better to be a bridger western outlaw or inlaw for solo players?**

A: Outlaws generally have an easier time solo because they can spawn near the fishing docks and don't have to worry about maintaining a high reputation in towns. However, Inlaws benefit from the protection of town guards.

**Q: How do I get more ammo for my guns?**

A: You can purchase ammo packs from gunsmiths in Ridge B County or find them in crates dropped by defeated bandit and zombie NPCs. Using an ammo pack refills all equipped firearms.

**Q: Can I change my Stand after I get one?**

A: Yes, but it requires a specific plant found through fishing or high-tier exploration. You must give this plant to the Witch in the swamp to reset your Stand. Alternatively, wiping your character will also reset your Stand.

**Q: What is the fastest way to travel across the map?**

A: The fastest travel method is using a "Beast" tier horse with maxed-out speed stats (1.25). Additionally, holding the 'Shift' key while mounted allows your horse to gallop at its maximum velocity.
