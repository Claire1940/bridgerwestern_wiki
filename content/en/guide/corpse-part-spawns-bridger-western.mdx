## Complete List of Corpse Part Spawns in Bridger: Western

There are currently seven confirmed locations where Saint’s Corpse parts can manifest. When a part spawns, a global message appears on the screen and a vertical beam of light marks the exact coordinates. Familiarizing yourself with these landmarks is the first step toward successful farming.

### 1. The Dig Site
To find this spawn, head out of the main town toward the bridge and follow the left-hand path. You will eventually see a cluster of tents marking a dig site. The corpse part spawns at the very bottom of the excavation. While easy to find, this is statistically one of the most dangerous spots. The verticality makes it easy for "snipers" to shoot down at you while you are attempting to merge, and the rope-climbing exit is notoriously slow.

### 2. The Elevated Track Path
Head to the right of the town and follow the train tracks in the opposite direction of the train's movement. Look for a small path branching off to the left. This area is relatively spacious, allowing you to spot incoming enemies early. However, because there is only one clear exit, you can easily be cornered by a rival posse.

### 3. Death Valley High Rock
This is widely considered the best **corpse part spawns bridger western** location for solo players. Located deep in Death Valley along the canyon walls, the part spawns on an elevated rock platform. To reach it, you generally need to stand on your horse and use the momentum of a jump or weapon recoil (like a shotgun blast) to boost yourself up. Once you are on the rock, you have a massive height advantage over anyone trying to knock you off.

### 4. The Mountain Watchtower
Starting from the fishing area, head right and look for the watchtower perched on the mountain peak. This requires a tedious climb on foot—horses cannot navigate the steep rocks easily. 

> **Warning:** Do not run/sprint while climbing the ladder or the final ledge of the Watchtower. The mantling mechanic in Bridger: Western can glitch, causing you to fly off the edge and fall to your death, resetting your progress.

### 5. Ant Hill Island
From the fishing hut, look out over the water to the right. There is a small landmass known as Ant Hill Island. This is a premier farming spot because most players congregate near the town or the swamp, leaving this island relatively peaceful. If you are server hopping, checking Ant Hill Island first is a high-efficiency strategy.

## Spawn Mechanics and Probabilities

In Bridger: Western, corpse parts do not spawn on a fixed timer. Instead, the game runs a "check" every 30 seconds to determine if a part should appear. The probability of a successful spawn is directly tied to the number of players currently in the server. 

| Player Count | Spawn Chance (Every 30 Seconds) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3 - 5 Players** | 1 in 150 |
| **10 - 15 Players** | 1 in 80 |
| **Full Server (Max)** | **1 in 40** |

If you are serious about farming, you should aim for full servers. While the competition is higher, the frequency of the **corpse part spawns bridger western** events is nearly four times higher than in a nearly empty server. 

### The Merging Process
Once you pick up a part, you do not gain a Stand immediately. You must undergo a "Merging" phase. During this time, your character will pulse with energy.
*   **Merge Time:** Randomly determined between **10 seconds and 2 minutes**.
*   **Vulnerability:** If you are killed during the merge, you drop the corpse part, and the next person to pick it up starts their own merge timer.
*   **Stealth:** The beam of light disappears once the part is picked up, but your character will still emit visible pulses.

## Saint's Corpse Parts and Stand Rewards

There are five standard corpse parts and one hidden part. While most parts provide a random roll from the general Stand pool, the Left Arm is the most coveted due to its unique drop table.

| Corpse Part | Potential Stand Rewards | Unique Properties |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ribcage** | Star Platinum, The World, Silver Chariot, etc. | Most common find. |
| **Left Arm** | All standard Stands + **Tusk (10% chance)** | Only way to obtain Tusk. |
| **Right Arm** | All standard Stands. | Balanced roll chances. |
| **Left Leg** | All standard Stands. | Identical to Right Leg. |
| **Right Leg** | All standard Stands. | Identical to Left Leg. |
| **The Heart** | **D4C (Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap)** | Secret part, requires questline. |

The rarity of the Stand is not determined by the part (except for Tusk). Legendary Stands like **Star Platinum** and **The World AU** have significantly lower hatch rates regardless of which limb you find. You cannot see what part it is until you have already picked it up and started the merge.

## Professional Farming Strategies for 2026

To maximize your efficiency in finding **corpse part spawns bridger western**, you should move away from solo play and embrace the community aspects of the game.

### The "Queue" Method
The most effective way to farm is to join a dedicated Discord community (like the one maintained by the [official Bridger: Western developers](https://www.roblox.com/games/13247008087/Bridger-Western)). 
1.  Join a server with a group of 10+ friends/allies.
2.  Create a "claim list" or queue.
3.  When a part spawns, everyone defends the person at the top of the list while they merge.
4.  Once the merge is complete, the group stays in the server to wait for the next 1/40 chance, moving to the next person in line.

### Defensive Positioning
If you are playing solo and manage to grab a part, your priority is breaking line of sight. 
*   **Use Water:** Swimming toward Ant Hill Island can make it harder for players to hit you with revolvers due to the water physics and diving.
*   **Horse Stamina:** Keep your horse nearby. A quick mount and sprint to the edges of the map (like the far corners of the Swamp) can buy you the two minutes needed for a long merge.
*   **Height Advantage:** As mentioned, the Death Valley rock is the ultimate defensive position. If the part spawns there, you are almost guaranteed a successful merge if you have a decent long-range weapon to keep attackers at bay.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I drop a Corpse Part once the merge has started?**

A: You cannot manually drop it to a friend. The only way the part leaves your possession once the merge begins is if you are killed by another player or an environmental hazard.

**Q: Does the Left Arm always give Tusk?**

A: No. The Left Arm only has a **10% chance** to grant Tusk. If you miss that 10% roll, you will receive a random Stand from the standard pool (like Star Platinum or Magician's Red).

**Q: How do I know when a corpse part spawns in Bridger: Western?**

A: A large text notification will appear at the top of your screen saying a Saint's Part has appeared. Simultaneously, a massive beam of light will shoot into the sky from one of the seven spawn locations.

**Q: Is there a way to speed up the merge time?**

A: Currently, there are no items or buffs in 2026 that decrease the merge time. It is a purely RNG-based timer ranging from 10 to 120 seconds.

**Q: What is the rarest Stand I can get from a corpse part?**

A: While Tusk is unique to the Left Arm, **The World Alternative Universe (TW AU)** and **Star Platinum** are generally considered the rarest pulls from the standard **corpse part spawns bridger western** pool due to their extremely low drop weight.
