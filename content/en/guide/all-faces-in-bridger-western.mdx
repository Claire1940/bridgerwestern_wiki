## Obtaining Your First Horse

Traversing the map on foot is a recipe for disaster. Once you have earned enough moola from fishing, head back to the Ridgeby County train station. Directly in front of the station, you will find the Horse NPC.

Do not settle for the cheapest horse available. Beginner players should aim for a mount costing at least **1,000 Moola**. If the NPC offers a cheaper version, you should "re-roll" or wait for a better selection. Higher-priced horses have significantly better speed and stamina stats, which are vital for escaping outlaws or chasing down bounties.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Horse Tech" to travel across water quickly. Hold **Shift** to sprint toward the water, and once you enter it, hold **Space** to maintain your speed and glide across the surface.

## The Card System and Passives

As you level up (Tier 1 to Tier 3), you unlock slots for passive cards. These cards provide game-changing buffs that can define your build. To unlock or change your cards, you must visit the **Witch's Hut** located deep within the swamp area.

The first card reading is free, but subsequent rolls cost **50 Moola** each. Some powerful combinations, such as "High Noon" and "Evil Eye," can even grant a pseudo-aimbot effect when using Ocular Prowess, making your shots nearly impossible to miss.

## How to Get Stands and Corpse Parts

The most sought-after power in Bridger Western is the **Stand**. While you can find Stand Arrows through fishing (0.5% chance), the most reliable method in 2026 is hunting for **Corpse Parts**.

Corpse Parts spawn randomly across the map every 30 seconds. The spawn rate increases with the number of players in the server. However, these items are highly contested. Once a Corpse Part spawns, a notification usually alerts the server, leading to a massive "swarm" of players fighting for the item. If you manage to secure one, you can unlock some of the most powerful abilities in the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find a list of all faces in bridger western?**

**Q: How do I stop aging in the game?**
