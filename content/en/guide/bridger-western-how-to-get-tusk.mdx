## The Foundation: Understanding the Corpse Part System

Before you can unlock Tusk, you must understand how Stands are distributed in Bridger: Western. Unlike other items that can be purchased from vendors, Stands are tied to the "Saint’s Corpse." At random intervals, a golden message will appear at the top of the screen stating, "It appears once again." This signals that a Corpse Part has spawned somewhere on the map.

When this happens, a massive golden beam of light will shoot into the sky. Every player in the server can see this beam, making it a high-conflict zone. Once you reach the beam and pick up the part, a pulse effect begins. Every pulse provides a 15% to 20% chance of obtaining a Stand. However, the spawn rates for these parts are heavily influenced by the number of players currently in your session.

### Corpse Part Spawn Probabilities

| Player Count | Spawn Chance (Every 30 Seconds) | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-5 Players** | 1 out of 150 | Low (Safe farming) |
| **10 Players** | 1 out of 115 | Moderate (The "Sweet Spot") |
| **25 Players (Max)** | **1 out of 40** | Very High (Constant combat) |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling to secure a Corpse Part alone, consider joining a community Discord to "flood" a server. This allows a group of friendly players to take turns holding the part until everyone receives their Stand.

## bridger western how to get tusk: The Left Arm Requirement

While any Corpse Part can grant a Stand, Tusk is unique. You cannot obtain Tusk from a Rib Cage, a Leg, or the Saint’s Right Arm. To get Tusk, you must specifically interact with the **Left Arm of the Saint**. 

Even with the Left Arm in your possession, there is only a **10% chance** that the Stand granted will be Tusk Act 1. If you fail the roll, you may end up with a different Stand or nothing at all, requiring you to hunt for another Left Arm. 

### Steps to Acquire Tusk
1.  **Monitor the Chat:** Wait for the golden "It appears once again" notification.
2.  **Locate the Beam:** Use your horse (H to summon, N to mount) to reach the golden light as fast as possible.
3.  **Identify the Part:** Ensure the item is the **Left Arm**. 
4.  **Survive the Pulse:** Hold the item and survive the onslaught of other players. Each pulse has a chance to trigger the Stand awakening.
5.  **Check your Stand:** If successful, Tusk Act 1 will appear behind your character.

## The Crippled Status: A Major Trade-off

One of the most important things to realize when learning **bridger western how to get tusk** is the permanent debuff associated with the Stand. In accordance with the lore of Johnny Joestar, obtaining Tusk makes your character "crippled."

*   **Mobility Loss:** You lose the ability to walk or run. Your character will crawl on the ground at a very slow pace.
*   **Weapon Restriction:** You can no longer use traditional weapons. The moment you acquire Tusk, the game will delete your guns, sabers, and other handheld armaments. If you try to buy a new gun, the shopkeeper will tell you that you lack the capability to hold it.
*   **Horse Dependency:** Because you cannot walk, your horse becomes your primary mode of transportation. Without a high-quality horse, a Tusk user is a sitting duck.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never attempt a Tusk build without at least 1,000 to 1,500 cash saved up for a Legendary horse. You need the speed and health of a top-tier mount to compensate for your lack of leg mobility.

## Tusk Act Evolution and Move Sets

Tusk is a progressive Stand. By pressing the 'V' key, you can cycle through the different Acts you have unlocked. Each Act changes your combat style and damage output.

### Tusk Act 1: The Starter
Act 1 focuses on utility and basic harassment. It features the "Nail Glide," which allows you to slide across the ground for a short duration, providing a much-needed mobility boost.
*   **Nail Bullets:** Low damage (approx. 10 per hit), but can be fired rapidly like a mini-gun.
*   **Nail Jump:** A small vertical hop to clear minor obstacles.

### Tusk Act 2: The Sniper
Act 2 is widely considered the most effective for PvP in 2026. It introduces homing mechanics and massive burst damage.
*   **Ricochet Shot:** Firing near a target allows the bullet to track and hit them automatically.
*   **Sniper Shot (G):** A high-damage blast (62+ damage) that can sever limbs.

### Tusk Act 3: The Wormhole Specialist
Act 3 increases the raw power of your nail bullets even further. While it loses some of the utility of Act 1, its damage per second (DPS) is unrivaled.
*   **Double Hit:** Bullets often strike the target twice, dealing upwards of 35 damage per shot.
*   **Spatial Movement:** While the wormhole teleportation is limited in the current build, it sets the stage for high-level repositioning.

## Supporting Your Tusk Build: The Witch's Hut

Since you cannot use guns, you must optimize your character's stats through the Witch's Hut. Located in the swamp area (accessible by "water-jumping" your horse across the river), the Witch can read your fortune for 150 mula.

For a Tusk user, you want to aim for talents that buff horseback combat or survivability. The "True Cowboy" talent, for instance, increases gun damage on horseback—while Tusk uses nails, certain "projectile" buffs may apply depending on your specific path. 

If you ever wish to rid yourself of the "crippled" status and Tusk, you must find a specific rare plant while fishing (0.5% chance) and bring it to the Witch to reset your Stand.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get Tusk from a Stand Arrow?**

A: No. While Stand Arrows (or Arrow Shards) can be found via fishing with a 0.5% chance, Tusk is strictly tied to the Saint's Corpse Left Arm. Arrows provide a different pool of Stands.

**Q: What happens if my horse dies while I have Tusk?**

A: This is a dangerous situation. You will be forced to crawl at a very slow speed. It takes approximately two minutes for your horse to respawn (summoned via H). During this time, you are extremely vulnerable to other players.

**Q: Is Tusk Act 4 in Bridger: Western yet?**

A: As of the current 2026 updates, Tusk Act 4 is not yet fully implemented for public use. Players are limited to Acts 1 through 3, though developers have teased the Infinite Rotation for future expansions.

**Q: Does the "bridger western how to get tusk" process require a specific level?**

A: No specific level is required to pick up a Corpse Part, but it is highly recommended that you are at least Tier 2 or 3 so you have enough health and talent slots to survive the fight for the golden beam.
