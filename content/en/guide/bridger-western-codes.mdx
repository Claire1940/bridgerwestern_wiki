## Getting Started: The Perfect Beginning

When you first create your character, you are presented with a choice between becoming an **Outlaw** or an **In-law**. Currently, the Outlaw path offers more freedom for exploration and is generally recommended for players who want to progress quickly without the constraints of bounty systems.

### The Reroll Strategy
Upon spawning for the first time, you are given a random amount of starting Moola, ranging from 50 to 500. For an optimal start, you should aim for at least 300 Moola. If you spawn with a low amount, use the "Wipe" feature in the character creation screen to reset and try again. This initial capital is necessary to purchase your first fishing rod and bait.

## Mastering Mounts: Beyond the Basics

Your horse is your lifeline in the vast desert. While the starter mule is slow, investing in a high-tier horse is the best use of your **bridger western codes** currency.

### Buying a Legendary Horse
Visit the horse merchant in **Ridge County**. When browsing horses, look at the price tag. A horse costing between **1,000 and 1,500 Moola** is considered top-tier with superior speed and health stats. If the merchant offers a cheap horse, use the "Show me another" option to reroll the selection until a legendary mount appears.

### The Water Sprint Technique
Horses normally struggle in deep water, but you can bypass this by using the "Horse Tech." Sprint toward the water and spam the **Spacebar** while crossing. This allows you to "hop" across rivers and swamps much faster than swimming, which is essential for reaching the Witch's Hut quickly.

## Unlocking Stands and Talents

The JoJo elements of Bridger Western are what set it apart. To obtain a Stand, you generally need a **Corpse Part** or a **Stand Arrow**.

### The Corpse Part Event
Periodically, a message will appear in gold at the top of the screen: *"It appears once again."* This signals that a Corpse Part has spawned. Look for a massive golden beam of light stretching into the sky. 
*   **The Pulse Mechanic:** Once you pick it up, the part will pulse every 30 seconds. 
*   **Success Rate:** Each pulse has a 15-20% chance to grant you a Stand.
*   **Server Scaling:** In a full server (25 players), the spawn rate is higher (1 out of 40 every 30 seconds), but the competition is fierce.

### The Witch's Hut and Cards
Located in the swamp area, the Witch allows you to "read your fortune" for 150 Moola. This unlocks **Cards** (Passive Talents). You can equip up to three cards as you increase your Tier level.

**Popular Card Combinations:**
1.  **Aimbot Build:** Combining *High Noon*, *Evil Eye*, and *Folk's High Noon* allows you to land shots even when not aiming directly at a target while using Ocular Pro.
2.  **Cavalry Build:** Using *True Cowboy* increases gun damage by 17% while on horseback, making you a deadly mobile threat.

## Combat and Survival Tips

Combat in Bridger Western is fast-paced and unforgiving. Always keep a **Tonic** in your inventory for quick healing during a shootout. If you run out of ammunition, don't forget the **C** key to switch to your fists—sometimes "throwing hands" is the only way to survive a close-quarters ambush.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be mindful of your character's age. Dying frequently or "combat logging" (exiting the game during a fight) will significantly increase your age. While facial wrinkles and gray hair are cosmetic, many veteran players believe extreme old age can lead to a permanent character wipe. Use Dog's Bane herbs found through fishing to stay young.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the latest bridger western codes?**

**Q: How do I get more Moola quickly?**

A: The fastest way for beginners to earn Moola is by fishing in the village and selling the catch to the local vendor. Once you have a better horse and weapons, you can take on bandit outposts and world events for higher rewards.

**Q: Can I change my Stand after I get one?**

A: Yes, but it requires a specific reset item. You can obtain a reset plant through high-level fishing or by "wiping" your character, though wiping will reset all your progress including levels and Moola.

**Q: What happens if my horse dies?**

A: If your horse is killed in combat, it will take approximately two minutes to respawn. You can summon it again by pressing the **H** key once the cooldown has expired. Higher-tier horses generally have more health and can survive more damage.
