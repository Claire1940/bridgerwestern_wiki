## Step-by-Step D4C Obtainment Guide

Following these steps in order is the most efficient way to secure the Stand. Many players find that using an alt account or a dedicated friend is the fastest way to clear the combat hurdles safely.

### 1. Accumulating the 100 Kills
The first step is a simple grind. You need 100 total player kills. While you can do this through natural PvP, it is often safer to farm these kills in a secluded area to avoid being interrupted by high-level bounty hunters. If you are using an alt account, ensure you are not in a safe zone, as kills there may not register toward your total count.

### 2. Securing the 10-Player Kill Streak
Once you have your 100 total kills, you need a fresh 10-player kill streak. This is the "active" requirement. If you are farming with a friend, have them reset or let you kill them ten times in a row. Be extremely cautious of NPCs or roaming players during this time. In Bridger: Western, a single stray bullet from a zombie or an outlaw can end your streak and force a restart.

### 3. The Desert Ritual
This is where most players get confused. You must travel to the specific region labeled **"The Deserts."** A common mistake is attempting this in Death Valley. While both are sandy, the game engine tracks the specific "Desert" zone for the Heart spawn.

Once in the Desert, you must wait for a **Corpse Part** to spawn. When the notification for a Corpse Part appearing in the world triggers, you need to be in the "knocked down" state. To do this safely, have a friend shoot you in the torso with a low-damage revolver until you fall over. 

> **💡 Tip:** Avoid headshots when being knocked down. Headshots have a much higher chance of "full killing" you rather than just knocking you into the downed state, which would ruin your kill streak.

### 4. The 10% RNG Factor
When a Corpse Part spawns while you are knocked down in the Desert, there is a **10% chance** for the Heart to appear inside you. If successful, you will immediately obtain D4C. If not, you must wait for the next Corpse Part spawn. This is the most time-consuming part of the **d4c obtainment bridger western** loop, as it relies entirely on timing and luck.

## D4C Ability Showcase

Once you have completed the **d4c obtainment bridger western** process, you unlock one of the most versatile Stands in the game. D4C focuses on survivability, high-range projectile attacks, and unique dimensional mechanics that allow you to dictate the flow of any fight.

### Passive: Dimension Hop
This is the core of D4C’s utility. To use this effectively, you **must have a poncho equipped**. 
*   **Automatic Trigger:** If you take more than 20 damage in a single hit, D4C will automatically pull you into a different dimension for a short duration. This negates all damage from the triggering blow.
*   **Manual Trigger:** You can manually enter the dimension by interact-using your poncho.
*   **Team Utility:** You can bring allies into the dimension with you, providing a safe haven for healing or repositioning.

### Skill Breakdown

| Move | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **E / M1** | **Standard Barrage** | A rapid series of punches dealing moderate damage. |
| **R** | **Tracking Shot** | D4C fires a projectile with incredible range that tracks the target. |
| **G** | **Dimension Warp** | While inside the dimension, this teleports you directly to a nearby enemy. |
| **V** | **Clone Swap** | Swaps places with a dimensional clone, restoring you to **Max HP**. |

### The Cost of Clones
While the **Clone Swap (V)** is incredibly powerful because it resets your current health to 100%, it comes with a significant penalty. Every time you swap with a clone to heal, your **Maximum HP** is lowered. This effect persists until you officially respawn. This makes D4C a master of the "long fight," but you become increasingly fragile as the battle drags on and you rely on clones to stay alive.
