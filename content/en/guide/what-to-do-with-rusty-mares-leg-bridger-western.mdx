## Finding the Rusted Mare's Leg

Before you can worry about repairs, you must first obtain the item. In the current 2026 meta of Bridger Western, the Rusted Mare's Leg is exclusively found through the fishing mechanic. It is considered a rare drop, meaning you might spend several in-game days at the docks before seeing one.

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary Method** | Fishing in any deep water body |
| **Drop Rarity** | Rare / Legendary Tier |
| **Inventory Type** | Quest/Utility Item (Until Repaired) |
| **Initial State** | Non-functional, "Rusted" status |

> **Warning:** Do not discard the rusted version of this weapon. While it may look like junk, it is the only way to obtain the Mare's Leg without spending massive amounts of Robux or completing high-level end-game challenges.

## Locating the Repair NPC

Once the item is in your inventory, your next destination is the region of Ridge County. Many players make the mistake of visiting the standard Gunsmith in the main town, but he will not touch rusted relics. Instead, you need to find a specialized NPC located near the outskirts of the map.

Follow these steps to find the repair specialist:
1.  **Spawn at Ridge County:** This is the closest starting point to the NPC.
2.  **Locate the Bridge:** Head toward the large bridge that connects Ridge County to the neighboring territories.
3.  **Cross the Bridge:** The NPC is stationed just on the other side of the bridge, often standing near a small campsite or shack.
4.  **Initiate Dialogue:** With the Rusted Mare's Leg in your hand or inventory, speak to the NPC.

| NPC Location | Landmark | Cost to Repair |
|---|---|---|
| **Ridge County Outskirts** | Across the Main Bridge | **1,500 Moola** |

## The Repair Process and "Moola" Requirements

To successfully restore the weapon, you must have exactly 1,500 Moola (the in-game currency) ready. If you are short on cash, consider hunting bounties or selling high-value fish before making the trek to the bridge. 

When you speak to the NPC and confirm the repair, the Rusted Mare's Leg will be removed from your inventory. It does not immediately reappear in your hands. Instead, the game "ships" the repaired weapon to your secure storage. This is a common point of confusion for players who think their weapon has disappeared.

## Retrieving Your Repaired Mare's Leg

After paying the 1,500 Moola, you must travel to the **City Colony Bank**. In Bridger Western, the bank acts as your global storage for high-value items and weapons that aren't currently equipped.

1.  Enter the City Colony Bank.
2.  Approach the Clerk NPC behind the counter.
3.  Select the **"Open Storage"** option.
4.  Locate the "Mare's Leg" (now cleaned and functional) in your storage slots.
5.  Click **"Equip"** to move it to your active inventory.

> **Tip:** If the weapon does not appear in your storage immediately, try relogging or waiting five minutes for the server to update your inventory state.

## Mare's Leg vs. Winchester: Which is Better?

The Mare's Leg is essentially a sawed-off version of the Winchester rifle. While it shares the same lever-action DNA, its performance in the field is significantly different. Understanding these stats is vital when deciding if the 1,500 Moola investment was worth it.

| Feature | Mare's Leg | Winchester Rifle |
|---|---|---|
| **Fire Rate** | **Very Fast** | Moderate |
| **Damage** | Moderate | **High** |
| **Magazine Size** | 6 Rounds | **10-12 Rounds** |
| **Reload Speed** | Fast | Moderate |
| **Effective Range** | Mid-Range | **Long-Range** |

The Mare's Leg is a "build-specific" weapon. It excels in close-to-mid-range skirmishes where you need to put bullets downrange quickly. However, because it deals less damage per hit than the full-sized Winchester, you must be accurate. It is a favorite for "Run and Gun" playstyles in 2026.

## Advanced Combat Tactics with the Mare's Leg

Once you have mastered **what to do with rusty mares leg bridger western**, you need to know how to use it effectively. Because of its reduced weight and barrel length, the Mare's Leg has a much tighter hip-fire spread than traditional rifles.

**1. The "Fan the Lever" Technique**
Since the fire rate is the primary advantage, players should focus on clicking in a rhythmic pattern rather than spamming. This allows the recoil to reset slightly between shots, ensuring that your mid-range accuracy remains high.

**2. Pairing with a Revolver**
The Mare's Leg only holds 6 rounds. In a heated gunfight, you will run dry quickly. Expert players often pair the Mare's Leg with a high-capacity revolver like the Navy or the Peacemaker. Fire your 6 rifle rounds, then immediately swap to your sidearm to finish the job without waiting for the reload animation.

**3. Mobility is Key**
The Mare's Leg does not penalize movement as heavily as the Winchester. You can stay mobile, jumping and strafing, while maintaining a decent shot group. Use this to your advantage against players using heavy rifles who are forced to stand still to aim accurately.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find the Mare's Leg anywhere other than fishing?**

A: In the current version of the game, fishing is the only way to find the "Rusted" version. Some limited-time events may offer the repaired version as a reward, but fishing remains the most consistent method for all players.

**Q: I paid the 1,500 Moola but the NPC didn't give me the gun. Is it a bug?**

A: No, this is a common point of confusion. The NPC sends the gun directly to your storage. You must travel to the Bank in City Colony and talk to the clerk to retrieve it. It will not appear in your hand immediately after the transaction.

**Q: Is the Mare's Leg better than the Winchester?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. The Mare's Leg shoots faster but does less damage and has a smaller magazine. It is better for aggressive, mobile players, while the Winchester is superior for long-range sniping and high-damage single shots.

**Q: What happens if I lose the Mare's Leg after repairing it?**

A: If you have the weapon equipped and are "looted" in a hardcore server (depending on the specific server rules), you may lose it. However, in standard play, once it is in your storage, it is safe until you withdraw it. Always check your bank storage if you can't find it in your inventory.

**Q: Does the NPC move locations?**

A: As of the April 2026 update, the NPC remains stationed across the bridge from Ridge County. While developers sometimes move NPCs during major map overhauls, he has been a staple of that location for several seasons.
