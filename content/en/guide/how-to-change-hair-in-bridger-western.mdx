## Earning Moola for Customization

Everything in the West has a price. If you want to frequently change your style, you’ll need a steady stream of income. The most efficient way for beginners to earn Moola is through fishing. 

Head to the Fishing Village (often referred to as the "Taco Bait Shop" area). Purchase a fishing rod and bait from the local NPC. Fishing is not only a "chill" way to level up your tier, but it is also incredibly lucrative. You can catch common fish like Cod or rarer items that sell for significant amounts.

## Accessories and Advanced Customization

Once your hair is settled, you should look into accessories to complete the look. The "Food and Supplies" shop in Richby County sells items like the Lasso, Poncho, and various Cowboy Hats. These items are added to your inventory and can be toggled on or off.

Another advanced customization feature is the "Age" mechanic. Every time you die, your character ages. If you want to maintain a youthful look (or if your character has aged into their 80s and you want to reset), you need to find **Dogs Bane**. This herb allows you to reduce your age, effectively resetting your character's physical maturity. You can often find Dogs Bane while exploring or as a rare drop while fishing in the swamp areas.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when changing your hair or outfit in "Outlaw" zones. Other players can attack you while you are in a shop menu if the area isn't a designated Safe Zone. Always check your surroundings before opening a customization menu.

## Customizing Your Horse and Gear

Your look isn't just about your hair; it’s about your entire kit. In Richby County, you can find the Horse NPC right in front of the train station. Much like hair customization, getting the right horse is a matter of "re-rolling." 

Don't settle for the cheapest mule. Aim for a horse that costs at least 1,100 Moola. These higher-tier horses have better base stats and higher speeds. If the NPC offers you a slow horse, decline and re-roll until you get a stallion with the stats you desire. You can also customize your horse's tack and saddle at specific stables to match your character's new hairstyle.
