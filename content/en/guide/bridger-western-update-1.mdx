## The Lumber System and NPC Locations

One of the most significant additions in this expansion is the introduction of the lumbering profession. Players are no longer restricted to fishing for income; you can now take up an axe and head into the wilderness. To begin your journey as a woodsman, you must first locate the Lumber NPC.

### Finding the Lumberjack
The Lumber NPC is situated in a new hut near the fishing area. If you are familiar with the Tackle Bait Shop, the path is straightforward. Alternatively, you can take the train from Rich B County and make a right turn to find the NPC's cabin.

| Item | Purpose | Price (Mula) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lumber Axe** | Required tool for chopping trees | 250 |
| **Wood (Sale)** | Sold back to the NPC for profit | 20 (per unit) |
| **Wooden Stake** | Secondary resource from chopping | N/A |

### How to Chop Trees and Get Rokakaka Seeds
Once you have purchased the Lumber Axe for 250 Mula, head to the Forest or the Swamp. Chopping a tree typically takes about 8 to 10 swings. Each successful harvest grants you Wood and 10 EXP. More importantly, this is the primary method for obtaining **Rokakaka seeds**.

> **⚠️ Warning:** While the Forest is safe for beginners, the Swamp offers a chance to obtain **Dogbane** alongside wood. However, the Swamp is often more dangerous due to environmental hazards and player density.

## New Stands: Stone Free and White Snake Rework

The Stand roster has seen a major shakeup in **bridger western update 1**. The developers have focused on bringing more "Part 6" representation to the game while ensuring existing Stands remain balanced and fun to play in 2026.

### Stone Free
Stone Free makes its debut as a versatile Stand with high utility. While many players expected Soft & Wet, Stone Free offers a unique playstyle that rewards tactical movement and string-based attacks.

### White Snake Rework
White Snake has been completely overhauled. It is now fully obtainable and features a new "Command Disc" mechanic.
*   **Ally System:** If you throw a command disc at an NPC, they will now become your ally and fight alongside you.
*   **Stealing Mechanic:** The previous stealing mechanic has been adjusted to ensure better gameplay flow and fairness in PvP encounters.

### The GER Removal Controversy
In a surprising move, **Golden Experience Requiem (GER)** has been temporarily removed from the game for a full rework. Players who previously grinded for GER—spending hours on XP, arrow shards, and killstreaks—will find their Stand has been converted into a **Shiny Rare Stand**. While this has caused some frustration in the community, the developers promise that the reworked GER will be more than worth the wait when it returns.

## Rokakaka Trading and The Skin System

Trading has become much more complex with the introduction of the Rokakaka fruit. This isn't just a simple item exchange; it involves a "splitting" mechanic that is lore-accurate to the JoJo series.

### How to Trade with Rokakaka
To initiate a trade using a Rokakaka, you must click on another player to prompt them to split the fruit with you. There are strict requirements for this process to work:
1.  Both players must be at least **Tier 3 (Level 3)**.
2.  Both players must currently possess a Stand.
3.  The process can equalize ages, swap specific cards, or even swap Stands entirely.

### The Skin System and Shinies
A new cosmetic layer has been added to almost every Stand in the game (with the exception of Tusk and the current GER placeholders). When you roll for a Stand, there is a **5% chance** to obtain a unique skin. Furthermore, during a Rokakaka trade, if you do not already have a skin, there is a **1% chance** the resulting Stand will become a "Shiny" variant.

| Feature | Probability/Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Base Skin Chance** | 5% on Stand Roll |
| **Shiny Chance (Trading)** | 1% (if no skin owned) |
| **Minimum Trading Level** | Level 3 |
| **Available Skins** | Most Stands (Excluding Tusk) |

## Advanced Farming Strategies

To make the most of the new update, you should combine your activities. Since the game has transitioned from a "fishing simulator" to a broader survival-action hybrid, efficiency is key.

1.  **The Swamp Loop:** Start at the NPC hut, buy your axe, and head straight to the Swamp. By farming here, you can collect Wood (for Mula), Rokakaka Seeds (for trading), and Dogbane (for crafting/quests) all in one trip.
2.  **Mula Optimization:** Selling wood is a consistent way to earn money. At 20 Mula per wood, a full inventory can quickly pay for new gear or Stand rolls.
3.  **Golden Experience Utility:** If you use Golden Experience, remember that clicking the ground now shoots a branch toward the nearest target. This is incredibly useful for keeping aggressive NPCs or players at bay while you are busy chopping trees.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the Lumber NPC in Bridger Western Update 1?**

A: The Lumber NPC is located in a small hut near the Tackle Bait Shop. You can also find it by heading right after taking the train from Rich B County. He sells the Lumber Axe for 250 Mula.

**Q: How do I get Rokakaka seeds?**

A: Rokakaka seeds are obtained by chopping down trees in the Forest or Swamp areas. Each tree has a chance to drop seeds, which are essential for the new trading system introduced in **bridger western update 1**.

**Q: Why did my Golden Experience Requiem (GER) disappear?**

A: The developers have temporarily removed GER to perform a complete rework of its moveset. If you owned GER before the update, it has been replaced with a rare Shiny Stand as compensation until the rework is completed later in 2026.

**Q: Can I play Bridger Western on Xbox or PlayStation?**

A: Yes! Update 1 officially added console support for both Xbox and PlayStation, including a revamped UI that works seamlessly with controllers.
