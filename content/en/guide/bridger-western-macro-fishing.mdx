## Core Requirements for Fishing Automation

Regardless of which script you choose, there are three non-negotiable requirements for **bridger western macro fishing** to work in 2026. If any of these are misconfigured, the macro will likely fail to "see" the mini-game letters or "hear" the fish biting.

1.  **AutoHotkey (AHK):** Most scripts are built on the AutoHotkey engine. Ensure you have the latest 2026 version installed on your PC.
2.  **Resolution Scale:** Your PC's display settings must be set to exactly **100% scaling**. If your Windows settings are at 125% or 150%, the pixel coordinates the macro scans will be offset.
3.  **Roblox Graphics:** Set your graphics to a level where the water is clear but the UI elements (letters) remain sharp.

## Setting Up the Semi-AFK Macro

For those who want to stay at their computer but hate the "difficult crap" of the mini-games, a semi-AFK macro is the safest bet. This version allows you to play the game in the background while the script handles the mechanical execution of the fishing patterns.

### Configuration Steps
To get the semi-AFK version working, you will need to set specific coordinates for the scanning region. This is typically done using the **O** and **P** keys to set the top and bottom boundaries of the detection box.

*   **Step 1:** Approach a body of water and equip your fishing rod.
*   **Step 2:** Press **O** to set the top scanning position where the mini-game UI appears.
*   **Step 3:** Press **P** to set the bottom boundary.
*   **Step 4:** Press the **Left Bracket [** to toggle the macro on.

For a standard 1920x1080 resolution, many players find success with coordinates around `920, 500` for the top and `1, 580` for the bottom. If the macro is clicking too fast and missing patterns due to lag, you can edit the script's "Click Delay" value to a higher number (e.g., from 50ms to 80ms).

## Mastering the V2 Fully AFK Macro

The V2 macro is a more complex beast that utilizes sound detection to know when a fish has been hooked. This allows the script to automatically reel in and recast without any human intervention. This is the gold standard for **bridger western macro fishing** in 2026.

### Audio Synchronization
The most common reason a V2 macro fails is that it cannot "hear" the game. Because the script listens for the specific splash sound of a bite, your volume settings must be maxed out.

| Setting | Required Value | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roblox Master Volume** | **100%** | Ensures the splash sound is loud enough to trigger the reel. |
| **Windows System Volume** | **100%** | Prevents the OS from muffling the application audio. |
| **Output Device** | **Matched** | The macro must listen to the same device Roblox is using. |
| **In-Game Music** | **0% / Off** | Prevents music tracks from interfering with sound detection. |

### Visual Calibration
To ensure the OCR correctly identifies the letters (W, A, S, D) during the mini-game, your camera must be positioned perfectly.
1.  Zoom your camera all the way in (First Person).
2.  Zoom out exactly **seven clicks**.
3.  Align the F1 Overlay Region directly over the center of your screen where the letters spawn.

> **💡 Tip:** If the macro is missing letters, increase the "Tolerance" setting in the configuration menu. A tolerance of 100 is usually recommended for the bright white text used in Bridger: WESTERN.

## Advanced Optimization & Troubleshooting

Even with a perfect setup, you may encounter issues like character knockback or failed casts. Modern 2026 macros include "Anti-Cheat" measures, such as randomized scan delays, to make the automation appear more human-like to the [Roblox](https://www.roblox.com) engine.

### Common Fixes
*   **Overlay Missing:** If the detection window disappears, delete your `settings.json` or `.ini` file in the macro folder and restart. This resets the window to the center of your primary monitor.
*   **Character Moving:** If your character is being pushed by the mini-game physics, ensure the "Disable Mini-game" toggle is active. Note that this may prevent you from catching rare chests.
*   **Multiple Monitors:** If you have a dual-monitor setup, the macro might be scanning the wrong screen. Check the settings tab to ensure the "Display Index" matches the monitor where Roblox is running.

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Macro won't reel in** | Audio mismatch | Set System and Roblox volume to **100%**. |
| **Wrong keys being pressed** | Resolution Scaling | Change Windows Display Scale to **100%**. |
| **Macro fails to cast** | Inventory slot error | Ensure fishing rod is in the **1st slot**. |

Using bait significantly increases the bite rate, which in turn increases the efficiency of the **bridger western macro fishing** process. Without bait, you may find your character waiting up to 30 seconds between bites, whereas bait can reduce this to under 10 seconds.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the bridger western macro fishing script safe from bans?**

A: No automation is 100% safe. While the developers of Bridger: WESTERN primarily target game-breaking exploits, macros are technically prohibited. To minimize risk, avoid fishing in highly populated areas like the main docks for extended periods.

**Q: Why does my macro stop working after a few minutes?**

A: This is usually due to the Roblox "Idle Disconnect" or a failure in the audio detection. Ensure your system volume is at 100% and that you have a "Keep Alive" or "Anti-AFK" feature enabled within your macro settings.

**Q: Can I catch Legendary Fish with a macro?**

A: Yes. The V2 macros are capable of solving the patterns required for Legendary fish. However, if your "Click Delay" is set too high, the macro might not be fast enough to catch the fastest-moving bars.

**Q: Does the macro work on mobile or Mac?**

A: Most **bridger western macro fishing** tools are developed as .exe files using AutoHotkey, meaning they are exclusive to Windows PCs. There are currently no reliable fully-automated fishing macros for mobile or macOS in 2026.
