## Getting Started: The Path to Prosperity

When you first spawn into the world, your primary objective is to secure a steady stream of income, often referred to as "moola." The most reliable way for new players to start is by locating the train tracks. Following these tracks will eventually lead you to the Fishing Village, a central hub for early-game progression.

Inside the village, you will find a hut near a bridge where you can purchase a fishing rod and bait. Fishing is more than just a pastime; it is a vital source of "Shards" (used for obtaining stands) and various herbs.

### Early Game Income Methods

| Method | Requirements | Rewards | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fishing** | Rod & Bait | Fish, Herbs, Stand Shards | **Low** |
| **Bandit Encounters** | Basic Weapons | XP, Chests, Moola | **Medium** |
| **Player Bounties** | Combat Skill | Moola | **High** |
| **Zombie Bosses** | Group/High DPS | Dog Bane Herbs, Large Chests | **Very High** |

> **💡 Tip:** While fishing, you will encounter skill checks. Pressing the correct keys quickly is vital; failing too many times reduces your remaining time and may cause you to lose the chest entirely.

## Essential Gear and Weaponry

Once you have accumulated enough moola from selling fish or defeating bandits, you should head to the main town to upgrade your equipment. In **bridge western**, your physical gear is just as important as your stand. Defensive items like the Cowboy Hat and Poncho provide specific resistances that can turn the tide of a duel.

### Recommended Loadout for 2026

*   **Winchester Rifle:** Offers high damage and excellent accuracy for long-range engagements.
*   **Whitney Dragon:** A reliable sidearm with a solid fire rate.
*   **Cowboy Hat:** Protects against a single headshot, dealing zero damage for the first hit.
*   **Poncho:** Reduces damage to the torso but increases damage taken to the limbs.
*   **Malcolm 70:** The premier sniper rifle for those who prefer picking off targets from a distance.

### Horse Selection and Stats
Speed is the most critical stat in the frontier. When visiting the stables, look for horses priced above 1,000 moola. Always read the descriptions carefully to ensure the horse prioritizes speed and stamina over cosmetic appeal. You can check your horse's specific stats at any time by pressing the "M" key.

## The Card System and the Witch

As you increase your tier by gaining XP, you will unlock up to three card slots. These cards act as passive buffs that define your character "build." To manage these, you must travel to the Witch's Heart, a specific location hidden within the map.

### Top Tier Cards for Combat

| Card Name | Effect | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sturdy** | Increased knockback resistance | Tank Builds |
| **Desperado** | Buffs damage when at low health | High-risk Duelists |
| **Executioner** | Deals more damage to low-health enemies | Finishers |
| **Quick Draw** | Faster weapon swap and draw speed | Gunslingers |
| **Veteran** | General resistance and health buff | All-around Survival |

The Witch also provides the "De-aging" service. By providing a **Dog Bane Herb**, she can reduce your character's age by 20 years, which is often necessary to maintain peak physical performance. Furthermore, she is the NPC responsible for resetting your current stand if you wish to roll for a new one using an arrow or shards.

## Advanced Mechanics: Corpse Parts and Ocular Prowess

Periodically, a server message will announce that a "Corpse Part" has spawned. This triggers a high-stakes race as a beam of light marks the location. Picking up a corpse part makes you a target; you will periodically glow white, revealing your position to all players on the map.

If you successfully hold a corpse part, it has a random chance to merge with you every few seconds, granting a powerful stand. However, if you are killed, the part drops, allowing others to claim it.

### Ocular Prowess
Every player has access to Ocular Prowess from the start. This ability highlights other players in the environment, making it indispensable for spotting enemies in the dark or across long distances in the desert. While there are cards like "High Noon" that modify this ability, many veteran players prefer using their card slots for direct combat buffs instead.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get a stand in bridge western?**

A: You can obtain a stand by using a Stand Arrow, consuming Shards found while fishing, or by having a Corpse Part merge with you. Some stands, like D4C, require specific kill streaks and environmental conditions to unlock.

**Q: What is the best way to avoid being scammed during trading?**

A: Always refer to the latest community value lists. Remember that rarity (like White Snake or Tusk) often trumps raw combat power in the trading market. Never drop items; use the official trading interface once it is fully implemented.

**Q: Where can I find the Witch's Heart?**

A: The Witch's Heart is located in a secluded area of the map. The easiest way to find it is to follow the train tracks until you reach the outskirts of the desert, then look for a path leading toward the darker, more atmospheric region of the map.

**Q: What does the Dog Bane Herb do?**

A: The Dog Bane Herb is a versatile item. You can sell it to the Witch for 2,500 moola, use it to de-age your character by 20 years, or use it to reset/remove a low-tier stand that you no longer want.
