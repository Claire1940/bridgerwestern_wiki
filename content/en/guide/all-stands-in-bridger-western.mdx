## B-Tier and Specialized Stands
These stands are viable in the right hands but are often overshadowed by the higher tiers. They usually serve niche roles or have specific counters that are common in the 2026 meta.

### Killer Queen
Killer Queen is a burst-damage specialist. It relies on planting bombs and detonating them for massive chunks of health. While it has decent range and damage, it lacks the utility needed to compete with Time Stop users or clone summoners consistently.

### Purple Haze
If you enjoy area-of-effect (AoE) control, Purple Haze is an excellent choice. Its acid damage provides significant damage over time (DoT) and can force enemies out of specific zones. However, many players find its playstyle restrictive compared to more mobile stands.

### 20th Century Boy
This stand is unique because it consumes no stamina when summoned. It can nullify all incoming damage and redirect it as an explosion. While this sounds powerful on paper, it is often too reactive and niche for the aggressive 2026 PvP environment.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid using **Tubular Bells** or **Balloon Dog** in serious competitive play. These stands are currently considered the weakest in the game due to difficult-to-land abilities and low overall impact.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best stand for beginners in Bridger Western?**

A: Golden Experience is highly recommended for beginners. It is very balanced, offers decent damage, and its healing abilities provide a safety net while you learn the mechanics of the game.

**Q: Which stand has the highest damage output?**

A: Star Platinum is widely considered to have the highest raw punch damage among **all stands in bridger western**, though its short range requires you to be very close to your opponent to be effective.

**Q: Is it worth keeping 20th Century Boy?**

A: While it has a unique damage-nullification mechanic, most high-level players find it too passive for the current meta. If you prefer an aggressive playstyle, it is better to reset and aim for an S-tier or A-tier stand.

**Q: How do I counter D4C's clones?**

A: Using stands with AoE (Area of Effect) damage, such as Purple Haze or Red Magician, can help clear out clones quickly. Additionally, staying mobile makes it harder for the clones to swarm you.
