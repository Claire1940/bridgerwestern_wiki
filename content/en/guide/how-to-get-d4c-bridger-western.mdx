Before you even attempt the final ritual in the desert, you must meet several strict combat prerequisites. These are non-negotiable and must be completed in a specific order to qualify for the stand acquisition.

The first hurdle is accumulating **100 total kills**. It is important to note that this is a lifetime total for your current character, not a single kill streak. However, Bridger Western does not currently feature a public "kill counter" UI that tracks this specifically for stand requirements.

### The 10 Kill Streak
Once your total kills are handled, you must achieve a **10 kill streak**. This is indicated by the skull icons in the bottom right of your screen. ## The "Knocked" State Strategy

The most unique and counter-intuitive part of learning how to get d4c bridger western is the requirement to be in a "knocked" state. In Bridger Western, being knocked occurs when your health reaches zero but you are not yet fully "finished" or sent back to the spawn point.

D4C is obtained by "absorbing" a corpse part the moment it spawns in the world. However, you do not physically pick it up. Instead, there is a **10% chance** that the stand will automatically merge with you if you are knocked at the exact moment a corpse part spawns anywhere on the server.

| Step | Action | Importance |

| **2** | Enter the "Knocked" state | Must be active before the spawn notification |

It is nearly impossible to do this solo. You need a friend to continuously attack you until you are knocked. Once you are down, your friend must wait and ensure no other players or NPCs "finish" you. If the "Corpse Part Spawned" message appears while you are on the ground, the game checks your requirements. If you have the 100 kills, the 10-kill streak, and you are in the desert, you have a 1 in 10 chance of the stand appearing in your inventory immediately.

> **💡 Tip:** If a corpse part spawns and you do not get the stand, don't give up. Simply heal, maintain your streak, and get knocked again before the next spawn cycle.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I lose my progress if I die while trying to get D4C?**

A: You do not lose your 100 total kills, but you **do** lose your 10-kill streak. If you die, you must regain the 10-kill streak before you are eligible for the 10% merge chance in the desert.

**Q: Can I get D4C without a friend knocking me?**

A: It is technically possible if an NPC or an enemy player knocks you at the perfect time, but it is extremely risky. An enemy player is likely to "finish" you, which will reset your streak and prevent the merge. Using a trusted friend is the only reliable way to stay in the knocked state safely.

**Q: Does it matter which corpse part spawns?**

A: No. The game simply checks if *any* corpse part has spawned globally. You do not need to be near the physical location where the part appeared; you only need to be knocked in the desert with your requirements met.

**Q: How do I know if I have enough kills for how to get d4c bridger western?**

A: Since there is no visible counter, the best strategy is to farm kills until you are absolutely certain. Most players spend an hour or two specifically farming bandits to ensure they have surpassed the 100-kill threshold before attempting the desert ritual.

**Q: Is D4C better than Tails or other stands?**

A: "Better" is subjective, but D4C is widely considered a top-tier stand due to its rarity and unique mechanics. It offers a different playstyle compared to Tails, focusing more on tactical positioning and dimension-based abilities.
