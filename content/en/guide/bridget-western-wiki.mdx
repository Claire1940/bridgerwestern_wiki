## Getting Started: The First Steps

When you first spawn into the world, the environment can feel overwhelming and directionless. There are no hand-holding tutorials, so your primary goal should be to locate the train tracks. These tracks act as the main artery of the map, leading you to the most important landmarks. Following the tracks will eventually lead you to the **Fishing Village**, which is the undisputed hub for new players.

Before you can engage in high-level Stand battles, you need "Moola" (the in-game currency). While you can earn small amounts by killing other players or defeating random bandit and zombie encounters in the wild, fishing is the most reliable and peaceful way to build your initial fortune.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize buying the Cowboy Hat and Poncho early. The hat acts as a literal life-saver, absorbing a single headshot that would otherwise result in an instant kill.

## Combat Mechanics and Ocular Prowess

Combat in this game is a blend of traditional third-person shooter mechanics and supernatural abilities. Movement is key; you can double-tap **W** to sprint and use **Q** to roll. When using firearms, **M1** fires, while **Right-Click** allows you to aim for better precision. 

One of the most unique features available to every player from the start is **Ocular Prowess** (activated with the **X** key). This ability highlights enemies in your field of vision, making it invaluable during night-time skirmishes or when tracking targets through dense brush. Advanced players can even modify Ocular Prowess using specific Card combinations to grant bonuses like increased reload speed or improved accuracy.

### Recommended Firearms for 2026

1.  **Whitney Dragon**: A powerful sidearm with reliable stopping power.
2.  **Winchester Rifle**: The gold standard for accuracy and damage.
3.  **Malcolm 70**: A dedicated sniper rifle for those who prefer long-distance engagements.
4.  **Maverick**: A faster-firing option for players who struggle with the slower rhythm of lever-action rifles.

## The Stand System: Arrows and Corpse Parts

Stands are the supernatural manifestations that provide players with unique combat abilities. In the current 2026 meta, there are two primary ways to obtain a Stand:

### 1. Stand Arrows and Shards
While fishing, you have a small chance (approximately 0.5%) to pull a chest from the water. These chests can contain **Shards**, which function as Stand Arrows. This is the safest method to obtain a Stand, though it requires significant patience and a bit of luck with the fishing skill checks.

### 2. The Corpse Part Event
Periodically, a server-wide message will announce that a **Corpse Part** has spawned. A beam of light will shoot into the sky, marking its location. Picking up a Corpse Part is dangerous; you will occasionally "glow" white, revealing your position to every player on the map. If you hold the part long enough, there is a recurring chance it will merge with you, granting you a powerful Stand.

| Stand Name | Type | Acquisition Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Star Platinum** | Close Range | Arrow / Corpse Part |
| **The World (AU)** | Close Range | Arrow / Corpse Part |
| **D4C** | Special | 100 Kills + 10 Killstreak + Corpse Part |
| **Tusk (Act 1-3)** | Ranged | Arrow / Corpse Part |
| **Mandom** | Utility | Arrow / Corpse Part |
| **White Snake** | Admin Only | Currently Unobtainable |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you are killed while carrying a Corpse Part, it will drop for other players to steal. Never attempt to secure a Corpse Part without a fast horse for a quick getaway.

## The Card System and the Witch's Hut

As you progress through the Tiers (Level 1 to 3), you unlock Card Slots. These cards provide passive buffs that can drastically alter your playstyle. To manage your cards, you must visit the **Witch's Hut**, located in the swampy region near the Fishing Village.

The Witch allows you to "Read your Fortune" to draw new cards. While the first draw is often free, subsequent rerolls will cost Moola. 

### Top-Tier Cards to Look For
*   **Sturdy**: Increases your overall resistance to being knocked down.
*   **Desperado**: Grants combat bonuses when your health is low.
*   **Executioner**: Increases damage dealt to players with low health.
*   **Quick Draw**: Significantly reduces the time it takes to swap and ready your weapons.
*   **High Noon**: A specialized card that enhances Ocular Prowess, often used in "aimbot" style builds.

## Traversal and Survival

The map is expansive, and walking is the fastest way to get yourself killed. Purchasing a horse is mandatory. When visiting the stables in town, do not settle for the cheapest option. 

Look for a horse that costs at least **1,000 Moola**. These higher-tier horses have significantly better speed and stamina stats. Speed is the most critical attribute, as it allows you to outrun Stand users and escape the "glow" of a Corpse Part. You can also use the "Horse Tech" by holding **Shift** to sprint and **Space** to move quickly through water.

### Aging and Dog Bane
A unique mechanic in the game is **Aging**. Every time your character dies, your age increases. To reverse this process, you need to find the **Dog Bane Herb**. This rare herb can be found via fishing or by defeating boss zombies. Taking it to the Witch will allow you to "de-age" by 20 years, or you can sell the herb for a hefty 2,500 Moola.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the Fishing Village in the Bridget Western wiki?**

A: The easiest way to find the village is to locate the train tracks anywhere on the map and follow them until you see a bridge and a small hut. Most players congregate here to farm money safely.

**Q: What is the best way to get a Stand quickly?**

A: While fishing for Shards is safe, the fastest way is to participate in the Corpse Part event. However, this requires you to survive against other players who will be hunting you while you hold the part.

**Q: Can I get D4C in the current version of the game?**

A: Yes, but it is extremely difficult. You need 100 total kills, a 10-player killstreak in a single server, and then you must be knocked in the desert while a Corpse Part spawns for a small 10% chance to obtain it.

**Q: What should I do if my character gets too old?**

A: You must find the Dog Bane herb. You can obtain this through fishing or as a rare drop from zombie encounters. Give the herb to the Witch in the swamp to reduce your age by 20 years.
