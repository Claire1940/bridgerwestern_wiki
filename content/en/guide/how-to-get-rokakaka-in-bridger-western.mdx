## Understanding the Seed Drop Mechanics

The Rokakaka seed is an RNG (Random Number Generator) drop. This means there is no guaranteed number of swings that will produce a seed. However, statistical data from 2026 gameplay suggests that a consistent rotation of 15 to 20 minutes of woodcutting will almost always yield at least one seed.

While you are chopping trees, you will also passively collect wood. This wood can be sold later for Mula, making this farming method profitable even if the seeds take a while to drop. Many players utilize an auto-clicker to maintain a steady rhythm. While manual clicking is always safer, basic auto-clicking for woodcutting is generally tolerated, provided it does not involve complex macros that automate movement or combat.

> **Tip:** If you are using an auto-clicker, position your camera so that it centers on the hitboxes of both trees. This allows your character to automatically switch targets when the first tree is depleted.

## Dealing with Threats During Growth

The Green Lands are not entirely safe. A common occurrence when planting Rokakaka seeds is the spawning of hostile NPCs, such as Zombies. These NPCs are often triggered by the presence of a growing plant.

If a zombie spawns on your location, you have two primary options. You can fight the NPC if you have a decent weapon and enough health, or you can use the "flee" tactic. By moving a significant distance away from your plant, the NPC will eventually de-spawn or lose interest. Once the area is clear, you can return to your plant to check its progress. 

It is important to note that leaving your plant unattended for too long carries the risk of other players stealing your harvest. In [Bridger Western](https://www.roblox.com/games/14546599602/Bridger-Western), resource competition is a core part of the gameplay loop.
