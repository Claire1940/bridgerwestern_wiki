## In-Game Configuration and Calibration

A **bridger western fishing macro** relies on visual and auditory cues from the game. If your Roblox settings are not perfectly aligned with the macro's expectations, the script will fail to detect bites.

### Visual Alignment
The macro uses an "Overlay Region" to scan for letters during the mini-game. To align this correctly:
*   Zoom your camera all the way in.
*   Zoom out exactly **seven times**.
*   Position the F1 overlay region directly over the area where the mini-game letters appear.

### Audio Synchronization
The V2 macro uses sound detection to know when a fish has bitten the hook. This is the most common point of failure for new users.

| Setting | Required Value |
|---|---|
| **Roblox Volume** | **100% (Maximum)** |
| **System Volume** | **100% (Maximum)** |
| **Output Device** | Must match System and Roblox |
| **Sound Quality** | High / Stereo |

> **💡 Tip:** Ensure your "Output Device" in the macro settings matches the exact headphones or speakers selected in your Roblox settings. If these do not match, the macro will never "hear" the fish and will not reel in.

## Advanced Macro Settings

Once the basic setup is complete, you can fine-tune the macro's behavior through the configuration menu. These settings help prevent the game from detecting bot-like behavior and improve the catch rate.

*   **Tolerance**: This controls how much color the OCR (Optical Character Recognition) sees. A setting of 100 is usually ideal for detecting white letters against the water.
*   **Scan Delay**: Increasing this slightly can prevent the macro from clicking too fast, which can sometimes trigger anti-cheat flags.
*   **Apply Bait**: If enabled, the macro will automatically use bait from your second inventory slot before casting.
*   **Custom Cast Position**: You can set a specific coordinate on your screen for the rod to be cast, allowing you to target specific fishing holes.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even with the best **bridger western fishing macro**, you may encounter bugs. Most issues are related to the overlay disappearing or the detection failing due to lighting changes in-game.

1.  **Overlay Missing**: If you cannot find the detection window, close the macro, delete your saved settings file, and restart the application. This resets the overlay to the default center-screen position.
2.  **Failed Detections**: If the macro misses the mini-game letters, increase the "Tolerance" setting. This makes the OCR more sensitive to the white text used in Bridger: WESTERN.
3.  **Character Movement**: If your character is being knocked back, ensure the "Disable Mini-game" option is toggled. This prevents the knockback animation but may limit your ability to catch rare items like chests or arrows.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the bridger western fishing macro safe to use?**

A: While the software itself is generally free of malicious code if downloaded from the official developer, using it in-game is a violation of the terms of service. You run a significant risk of receiving a permanent ban from the game.

**Q: Why isn't the macro reeling in the fish?**

A: This is almost always an audio issue. Ensure your Roblox volume and Windows system volume are both at 100%. Also, verify that the audio output device selected in the macro matches the one Roblox is using.

**Q: Can I use the macro on multiple monitors?**

A: Yes, the macro allows you to select which monitor it should monitor. Ensure you select the correct display index in the settings tab before starting the script with the F3 key.

**Q: Does the macro work for catching chests and arrows?**

A: Yes, the V1 and V2 macros are designed to handle the letter-matching mini-game required for chests and arrows. However, if you enable the "Disable Mini-game" option to prevent knockback, you will only catch standard fish.
