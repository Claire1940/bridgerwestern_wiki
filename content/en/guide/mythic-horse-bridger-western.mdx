## Locating the Horse Seller

Before you can start your hunt for a top-tier mount, you need to know where the market is. The primary horse vendor is located near the coastal fishing area, a landmark most players visit early in their journey.

1.  **Start at the Docks:** Head to the fishing area where the tackle bait store is located.
2.  **Follow the Path:** From the tackle store, follow the main dirt path leading inland.
3.  **Find the NPC:** You will encounter a Horse Seller NPC standing near a corral. This is the only place where you can currently "spin" for new horses.

When you interact with the NPC, you are presented with two choices: "I'll take it" or "Show me another." The "Show me another" option is effectively a reroll, costing a small fee to cycle through the available stock until you find a horse that meets your criteria.

## Understanding Rarity: Legendary vs. Mythic

The rarity system in Bridger: Western is visually distinct. You can identify the quality of a horse before you even look at its detailed stat sheet by observing its name and the brackets surrounding it.

| Rarity | Name Format | Bracket Style | Stat Boosts |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | Standard Case | None | None (Max 1.25) |
| **Legendary** | ALL CAPS | `[NAME]` (Thin) | 1 Stat Exceeds 1.25 |
| **Mythical** | **ALL CAPS** | `[[NAME]]` (Thick) | **2 Stats Exceed 1.25** |

A **mythic horse bridger western** is the pinnacle of equine performance because it allows two different attributes—such as Speed and Stamina—to climb as high as 1.35 or beyond. While a normal horse is hard-capped at a 1.25 multiplier, these rare variants break the game's standard limits.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Rarity does not automatically guarantee "good" stats. A Legendary horse might have a boost in Jump Power, which is generally considered less useful than Speed. Always check the specific numbers before committing to a purchase.

## Price Tiers and Stat Correlation

In the current 2026 meta, the price of a horse is a strong indicator of its total stat pool. The game uses a formula that adds up all potential stats to determine the asking price. While a cheap horse can occasionally be a "diamond in the rough," you are much more likely to find high-performance mounts in the higher price brackets.

| Price Range (Cash) | Tier Category | General Quality |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$150 - $500** | Low Tier | Slow, low stamina; avoid for long-term use. |
| **$500 - $1,000** | Mid Tier | Decent for starters; can occasionally find a Legendary. |
| **$1,000 - $1,500+** | High Tier | High probability of maxed base stats; "Beast" category. |

If the NPC describes the horse as a "Top of the line beast" or a "Real beauty," it usually indicates a price point above $1,400. These horses are the best candidates for being a **mythic horse bridger western** because their base stats are already high before the rarity multipliers are applied.

## Key Stats to Prioritize

When evaluating a horse, you should prioritize stats based on your playstyle. For most players, mobility is king.

*   **Speed:** This is the most critical stat. It determines your maximum velocity across the map. Look for horses that can reach a max speed of 90-95 or higher.
*   **Stamina:** This dictates how long you can maintain a gallop. A fast horse with low stamina will frequently force you to slow down, making it less effective for long-distance travel.
*   **Jump:** Useful for navigating rocky terrain or jumping over fences, but generally considered a secondary stat.
*   **Damage:** Relevant if you plan on using your horse in mounted combat, though most players prefer to fight on foot or use the horse purely for positioning.

> **💡 Tip:** Aim for a balance where Speed and Stamina are both above 1.15. If you find a Mythic horse that boosts both of these specific stats, you have found a "god-tier" mount.

## The Rerolling Strategy

To get the best possible mount, you should set aside a significant amount of cash—ideally $5,000 to $10,000—to account for reroll costs and the final purchase price. 

1.  **Check the Name First:** Look for ALL CAPS and thick `[[ ]]` brackets. If you see them, you've found a Mythic.
2.  **Evaluate the Price:** If the horse is under $600 but is Legendary/Mythic, be cautious. It likely has poor base stats and is only "rare" because one stat is slightly boosted.
3.  **The "1,400 Rule":** Many veteran players won't even look at a horse's rarity if the price is below $1,400, as the base stats won't be high enough to compete in the long run.
4.  **Unique Colors:** Mythic and Legendary horses often sport unique coat colors or patterns not found on common horses. This is a great way to stand out in a server.

## Post-Purchase: The 15-Minute Rule

Once you finally purchase your **mythic horse bridger western**, you might notice that you cannot see the finalized stats immediately. The game implements a cooldown period for "breaking in" or registering the horse.

*   **Wait Time:** You must wait approximately **916 seconds** (roughly 15 minutes) of in-game time before the full stat breakdown becomes visible in your menu.
*   **Bonding:** Use this time to travel and get a feel for the horse's handling. Even if you can't see the numbers, you will notice the difference in speed and stamina drain immediately if you've upgraded from a lower tier.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a normal horse ever be faster than a mythic horse bridger western?**

A: In very rare cases, yes. If a normal horse has near-perfect base stats (1.25) and a Mythic horse has bottom-tier base stats (0.80) but boosts them slightly, the normal horse could technically be faster. However, a well-rolled Mythic will always have a higher ceiling.

**Q: How do I know if my horse is Legendary or Mythical?**

A: Look at the brackets in the name. Legendary horses have single brackets `[NAME]`, while Mythical horses have double, thicker brackets `[[NAME]]`. Both will have their names written in all capital letters.

**Q: Does horse color affect speed?**

A: No, color is purely cosmetic. However, certain rare colors are exclusive to the Legendary and Mythic tiers, serving as a status symbol for players who have spent time rerolling at the vendor.

**Q: What is the maximum stat limit for a horse?**

A: Normal horses are capped at 1.25 for any individual stat. Legendary horses can push one stat to approximately 1.35, and Mythic horses can push two stats to that same elevated limit.
