## poncho bridger western Basics: What Ponchos Actually Do

In Bridger Western, ponchos apply **player stats**, not horse stats. That distinction matters because your poncho directly influences your character performance in combat and mobility.

Here’s the core idea: poncho stats are distributed across **Defense**, **Damage**, and **Speed**, with a practical cap total of **10** points. You don’t “level” this in a traditional skill tree. Instead, your poncho roll determines how those points are split.

| Stat | What It Affects | Best For | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Defense** | Damage mitigation and survivability | Frontline fighters, bounty pushes | Lower burst if over-invested |
| **Damage** | Offensive output per hit | Duelists, aggressive PvP | Can feel fragile without defense |
| **Speed** | Movement pacing and repositioning | Hit-and-run, map rotations | Lower tankiness in extended fights |

A quick rule of thumb for new players: if your aim and positioning are still developing, lean into **Defense + Speed** before maxing damage.

> **Tip:** A stat below 1 does not automatically ruin your build. In practical terms, the build still receives positive value distribution—just heavily weighted toward other categories.
