## The Fishing Economy: Earning Moola Fast

Fishing is widely considered the most reliable way to level up and earn currency in Bridger Western. Once you have reached Reachby County or the Fishing Village, head to the **Taco Bait Shop** to purchase a fishing rod and several pieces of bait.

### How to Fish Effectively
1. Equip your rod near a body of water.
2. Wait for bubbles to appear on your lure.
3. Left-click the moment the lure is pulled under.
4. Collect your catch and sell it to the NPC at the bait shop.

## Horse Management and Advanced Tech

Your horse is your lifeline in the vast western landscape. However, not all horses are created equal. Beginners start with a basic mule, but you should aim to upgrade to a high-tier horse as soon as you have saved 1,000 to 1,500 Moola.

### The Reroll Strategy
Visit the horse NPC near the Ridge County train station. If the horse offered is cheap (under 800 Moola), it likely has poor speed and stamina. Use the "Show me another" option to reroll the horse until you find one priced at **1,100 Moola or higher**. These legendary horses offer superior stats that are essential for outrunning bounty hunters.

### Water Travel Tech
Horses are typically slow in water, but you can bypass this by using the "Space Bar Tech." While sprinting on horseback into water, **spam the space bar** rapidly. This allows the horse to maintain high speed across rivers and swamps, making it easy to reach the Witch's Hut or escape a dangerous encounter.

## Progression: Tiers and Cards

In Bridger Western, your level is represented by **Tiers**. You can reach a maximum of Tier 3, which allows you to equip up to three passive "Cards." These cards define your build and can even grant supernatural abilities.

To unlock your cards, you must travel to the **Witch's Hut** located deep within the swamp biome. 

1. **Tier 1:** Unlocks your first card slot (First read is free).
2. **Tier 2:** Unlocks the second card slot (Costs 50 Moola to roll).
3. **Tier 3:** Unlocks the final card slot (Costs 50 Moola to roll).

### The "Aimbot" Build
By combining specific cards, you can create powerful synergies. One of the most sought-after combinations for outlaws includes:
*   **High Noon**
*   **Evil Eye**
*   **Folk's High Noon**

When these are equipped alongside the **Ocular PRO (X)** ability, your accuracy is significantly boosted, often referred to by the community as a "legal aimbot."

## Obtaining Stands and Corpse Parts

Stands are the ultimate power-up in Bridger Western. There are two primary ways to obtain one: the slow, safe route of fishing, or the fast, dangerous route of hunting Corpse Parts.

### Corpse Part Spawning
When you see a golden notification stating "It appears once again," a **Corpse Part** has spawned somewhere on the map, marked by a massive golden beam. 

| Server Size | Spawn Chance (Every 30s) | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Low (1-5 Players)** | 1 out of 150 | Low - Easy to grab but rare. |
| **Medium (10 Players)** | 1 out of 115 | Moderate - Expect 1-2 competitors. |
| **High (25 Players)** | 1 out of 40 | Extreme - The whole server will fight for it. |

Once you pick up a Corpse Part, your character will emit a pulse every few seconds. Each pulse has a **15% to 20% chance** of granting you a Stand. You must survive the onslaught of other players until the Stand fully manifests.

## Gear and Weaponry Locations

To survive as an outlaw, you need more than just a starter pistol. For detailed stats on weaponry, players often consult the [official Bridger Western Trello](https://trello.com/b/8G3R0X6A/bridger-western) for the latest balance updates.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I reduce my age in Bridger Western?**

A: You can reduce your age by consuming **Dog's Bane**, a rare herb found while fishing or exploring specific biomes. Keeping your age low is aesthetically pleasing and avoids the gray hair/wrinkle effects that occur as you get older.

**Q: What is the best way to use this bridger western outlaw guide for fast leveling?**

A: The fastest way to level up (Tier up) is a combination of consistent fishing and participating in random world events, such as bandit raids. Selling high-value fish like Cod provides the Moola needed to buy the best gear, which in turn makes world events easier to complete.

**Q: Can I change my Stand after I get one?**

A: Yes, but you must "reset" your character's Stand. This is typically done by using a specific plant found through fishing and bringing it to the Witch in the swamp, or by performing a full character wipe (which resets all progress).

**Q: Does the horse's age matter?**

A: Yes, as a horse ages, its performance may slightly degrade. If your horse is killed, it will take approximately two minutes to respawn. It is always better to invest in a 1,500 Moola horse early to ensure you have the best possible stats for the long term.
