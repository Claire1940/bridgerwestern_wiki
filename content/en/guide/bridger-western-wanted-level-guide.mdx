## Understanding Alignment: Outlaw vs. In-law

When you first step into the world of Bridger: Western, your first major decision is selecting an alignment. While the game is constantly evolving in 2026, this choice sets the foundation for your "wanted" status and how you earn experience.

| Alignment | Primary Goal | Gameplay Focus | Notoriety Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outlaw** | Chaos & Profit | Attacking players, robbing, and combat | High "Wanted" potential, targeted by In-laws |
| **In-law** | Order & Protection | Hunting Outlaws, protecting towns, and NPC bounties | Low notoriety, generally safer in civilized areas |

Choosing the Outlaw path effectively functions as your "Wanted Level." As you engage in aggressive acts, your notoriety increases, making you a prime target for players seeking bounty rewards. Conversely, In-laws focus on maintaining the peace, though they are often the first targets for ambitious bandits.

## How to Increase Your Tier and Level Up

In Bridger: Western, your "Level" is referred to as your **Tier**. There are currently three tiers available, and reaching the maximum tier is the only way to unlock your full potential with cards and Stands.

### The Combat Path
The most direct way to increase your tier is through combat. You earn EXP by engaging with NPCs and players. When an enemy is downed, you must "finish" them to secure the full experience reward. Shooting a downed opponent in the head or body will complete the kill.

| Tier | EXP Requirement | Rewards & Unlocks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | Starting Level | Basic weapon access, 1 Card Slot |
| **Tier 2** | Moderate Grinding | Increased HP/Stamina, 2 Card Slots |
| **Tier 3** | High Effort | Maximum Card Slots (3), Stand potential |

### The "Chill" Path: Fishing
If you want to avoid a high **bridger western wanted level guide** notoriety early on, fishing is the most effective alternative. By visiting the Taco Bait Shop near the fishing village, you can purchase a rod and bait to begin earning money and EXP safely.

> **💡 Tip:** Selling rare fish like Cod or Batfish can net you upwards of 5,000 moola in a single session, providing the funds needed for high-end horses and guns.

## Managing Notoriety and Survival Gear

Surviving as a high-notoriety player requires the right equipment. If your **bridger western wanted level guide** strategy involves heavy PVP, you cannot afford to skimp on your loadout.

### Essential Survival Items
1.  **Cowboy Hat:** This is not just a cosmetic item. Equipping a cowboy hat provides a "headshot shield." When shot in the head, the hat will fly off, absorbing the initial massive damage and giving you a chance to counter-attack.
2.  **Silver Bullets:** Essential for dealing with supernatural threats like vampires and zombies that spawn at night.
3.  **Tonics:** Always keep a stack of healing tonics in your inventory to recover during prolonged gunfights.
4.  **Lasso:** Useful for capturing bounties or temporarily disabling aggressive players.

### Weaponry Overview
You can find the primary gun shop in **Ridge B County**. Follow the train tracks to find the town hall, and the gunsmith will be nearby.

| Weapon | Type | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Winchester** | Rifle | Long-range precision and high damage |
| **Maverick** | Shotgun | Close-quarters defense and clearing buildings |
| **Tommy Gun** | Automatic | Suppressive fire and multiple targets |
| **Colt** | Pistol | Reliable sidearm for quick draws |

## The Witch's Hut and Card System

As you progress through the tiers, you will unlock card slots. These cards provide passive buffs that can drastically change your playstyle. To unlock or reroll these cards, you must travel to the **Witch's Hut** located in the swamp area.

To find the hut, follow the dirt road away from the main train tracks near the campfire spawn point. Inside, you can speak to the Witch to "Read your fortune."

*   **First Card:** Usually free or very low cost.
*   **Rerolling:** Costs 50 moola per attempt.
*   **Card Combos:** Certain combinations, such as "High Noon" and "Evil Eye," can grant advanced abilities like enhanced accuracy or "Aimbot" style tracking during Ocular Prowess.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Rerolling is addictive and expensive. Ensure you have a steady stream of income from fishing or bounties before trying to perfect your build.

## Obtaining Stands: Arrows and Corpse Parts

For many players, the ultimate goal in Bridger: Western is obtaining a Stand. This is the peak of the **bridger western wanted level guide** progression. There are two primary ways to get a Stand in 2026:

1.  **Arrow Shards:** These have a very low drop rate (approx. 0.5%) from fishing chests. They grant access to standard Stands.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** These spawn periodically in the world. When a Corpse Part spawns, a server-wide announcement is made. You must grab the part and hold it while riding away on your horse. There is a random chance every few seconds that the part will merge with you, granting a powerful Stand like Tusk.

## Horse Mechanics and Travel

Your horse is your lifeline. If you have a high wanted level, a slow horse is a death sentence. When buying a horse from the seller in Ridge B County, aim for a horse that costs at least **1,000 moola**.

*   **Rerolling Horses:** Don't settle for the first cheap horse. Reroll until you get one with high speed and stamina stats.
*   **Water Travel:** While on a horse, hold **Shift** to sprint and **Space** to jump/move faster through water. This "horse tech" is vital for escaping pursuers in the swamp.
*   **Dismounting:** Press **N** to quickly get off your horse and engage in combat.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I lower my wanted level in Bridger: Western?**

A: Currently, your "Wanted" status is tied to your Outlaw alignment and active notoriety. Avoiding combat with players and focusing on neutral activities like fishing or NPC hunting will prevent your notoriety from spiking. If you have a bounty on your head, staying out of major towns like Ridge B County is advised.

**Q: What happens when I die in the game?**

A: Every time you die, your character's age increases. If you become too old, you may need to find a "Dog's Bane" herb to reduce your age and maintain your physical stats. You also risk losing any unbanked items or cash.

**Q: Can I change my alignment after starting?**

A: Yes, but it usually requires specific in-game actions or items. Most players find it easier to focus on one path until they reach Tier 3 before worrying about switching sides.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm EXP for the bridger western wanted level guide?**

A: The bandit outposts and the forest area are excellent for combat EXP. If you prefer a safer route, the fishing village offers consistent progression without the constant threat of player ambushes.
