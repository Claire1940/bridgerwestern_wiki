## The Corpse Part Method: High Risk, High Reward

The most direct and fastest way to obtain a Stand is through the discovery of Corpse Parts. These mystical relics appear randomly throughout the map and are signaled by a distinctive visual cue. When a Corpse Part spawns, a massive yellow beam of light shoots into the sky, visible to every player on the server. This effectively turns the acquisition process into a "King of the Hill" style battle.

Once you spot the yellow beam, you must race to the location. Upon picking up the Corpse Part, the real challenge begins. A notification is sent to the entire server, and players will likely descend upon your location to steal the part from you.

> **Warning:** You must remain vigilant while the Corpse Part merges with your character. If you take damage during this brief window, the part may drop, allowing an opponent to snatch it away.

## The Fishing Method: The Path of Patience

For those who prefer to avoid the "all-out war" of Corpse Part hunting, fishing offers a peaceful, albeit much slower, alternative. By fishing in the various water bodies across the map, players have a slim chance of pulling up an Arrow Shard. This item, when used, grants the player a Stand.

To begin this journey, you must first locate the **Taco Bait Shop**. If you are unfamiliar with the map layout, the easiest way to find it is to follow the train tracks. The tracks act as a guide through the wilderness, leading directly to the shop's vicinity.

### Fishing Equipment and Costs

Before you can cast a line, you need the proper gear. Speak to the NPC inside the Taco Bait Shop to purchase your starter equipment.

| Item | Purpose | Cost/Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fishing Rod** | Basic tool required to fish | Standard Currency |
| **Bait** | Required to attract fish and items | Varies by type |

> **Tip:** Arrow Shards cannot be banked or stored in your inventory across sessions. If you find one, use it immediately. Leaving the server or dying while holding the shard will result in its permanent loss.

Navigation is a skill in itself. Since many of the key locations for obtaining a Stand are spread across vast distances, mastering travel is essential. ## Understanding Stand Shards and Merging

Whether you use a Corpse Part or an Arrow Shard, the result is the same: a powerful manifestation. However, you must ensure your character is "ready" to receive a Stand. You cannot possess more than one Stand at a time through these basic methods. If you already have a Stand and wish to change it, you must first remove your current one before attempting to merge with a new part or shard.

The merging process for a Corpse Part is notably faster than in many other similar games, but it still requires a few seconds of uninterrupted focus. During this time, your character is vulnerable. If you are playing with friends, have them form a perimeter around you to block incoming fire or melee attacks.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I lose my Stand after I get it?**

A: No, once the merging process is complete and the Stand hotkey appears on your screen, the Stand is tied to your character. You will not lose it upon death or when leaving the server. However, unmerged items like Corpse Parts or Arrow Shards will be lost if you die.

**Q: What is the fastest way for how to get stand in bridger western?**

A: The fastest way is the Corpse Part method. Because they spawn every 30 seconds as long as there are three players in the server, you can potentially get a Stand within minutes if you are the first to reach the yellow beam.

**Q: Does the fishing rod quality affect the Arrow Shard drop rate?**

A: While better rods may improve your fishing speed or the types of fish you catch, the Arrow Shard remains a consistent 0.5% "Legendary Pool" drop. It is more about the number of casts you make rather than the specific rod used.

**Q: Why isn't the Corpse Part spawning in my server?**

A: Ensure there are at least three active players in the server. If the player count drops below three, the 30-second spawn timer pauses until another player joins. Additionally, check the sky for the yellow beam, as it might have spawned far across the map.
