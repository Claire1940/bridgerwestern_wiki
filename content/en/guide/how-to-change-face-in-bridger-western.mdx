## Initial Character Creation and RNG

When you first step into the world of Bridger Western, the game presents you with a character creation screen. This is a critical moment because the face you receive is largely determined by a random roll of available assets. There is currently no "Barber" or "Surgeon" NPC in the game world that allows you to simply pay Moola for a facial reconstruction after you have started your journey.

Because the system is RNG-based, many veteran players recommend spending time at this initial screen. If you don't like the face or hair you are given, it is much easier to restart the process immediately rather than trying to fix it hours into your progression. 

> **💡 Tip:** Take your time during the first spawn. If the face doesn't fit your roleplay style, it is better to "re-roll" now by restarting the session than to invest hours into a character you aren't happy looking at.

## Method 1: The Character Wipe

The most direct and reliable way to change your appearance is to perform a character wipe. This is the preferred method for players who have just started and realized they made a mistake in their initial selection. However, it comes with a heavy price: you will lose nearly all of your progress.

### What Happens During a Wipe?

A wipe completely deletes your current persona and returns you to the main menu. When you select "Create Character" again, you are given a fresh chance at the RNG facial selection.

| Feature | Status After Wipe |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Facial Features** | Randomized / New Selection |
| **Character Level** | Reset to 0 |
| **Moola (Cash)** | Reset (Unless banked, check latest patch) |
| **Stand / Abilities** | Permanently Lost |
| **Alignment** | Reset to Neutral |
| **Inventory Items** | Permanently Lost |

To perform a wipe, navigate to the main menu of the game. On the left-hand side, you will see the **Wipe** option. The game will prompt you for a confirmation to ensure you don't accidentally delete a high-tier character. Once confirmed, your old face is gone, and you can try again for a more rugged or refined look.

## Method 2: The Crazy Diamond Stand

If you have already made significant progress and don't want to lose your Stand or levels, there is an in-game "medical" solution. Some Stands in Bridger Western possess unique utility moves that can alter other players. Specifically, the **Crazy Diamond** Stand is capable of performing facial reconstruction.

### Using Facial Reconstruction

Crazy Diamond has a specialized "Restore Mode." When the user activates this mode and uses their **R ability**, they perform a punch that can "fix" objects or people. This punch has a roughly **10% chance** to trigger a facial change on the player being hit.

1.  **Find a Crazy Diamond User:** You will need to coordinate with another player who owns this Stand.
2.  **Enable Restore Mode:** The user must ensure they are in the correct mode to heal/fix rather than damage.
3.  **The Punch:** Have the user hit your character with the R ability.
4.  **RNG Luck:** Since there is only a 10% chance of the face changing, it may take several attempts.

> **Warning:** Being punched repeatedly can lower your HP significantly. Ensure you have **Tonics** or healing items on hand, or have the Crazy Diamond user heal you between attempts so you don't get downed.

## The Dynamic Aging System

A unique aspect of **how to change face in bridger western** involves the game's aging mechanic. Unlike static avatars, your character in Bridger Western physically ages based on time spent in-game and, more importantly, the number of times you die.

### Effects of Aging on Your Face
*   **Wrinkles:** Lines will form around the eyes, forehead, and mouth.
*   **Hair Color:** Your hair will transition from its original color to gray, and eventually to stark white.
*   **Skin Tone:** Your skin may appear more weathered or "leathery" as you reach older ages.

The fastest way to ruin a good-looking face is through "Combat Logging." If you leave the game while in a "tagged" combat state, your character will instantly age by **10 years**. This is a penalty designed to discourage players from escaping fair fights, and it results in immediate facial deterioration.

## Method 3: Reversing Age with Dog's Bane

If your search for **how to change face in bridger western** is motivated by wanting to look young again, you don't need a wipe or a Stand. You need a rare herb called **Dog's Bane**. This consumable item is the only way to reverse the clock on your character's physical appearance.

### How to Get Dog's Bane
Dog's Bane is not sold in standard shops. You must acquire it through gameplay:
1.  **Fishing:** This is the most common method. While fishing in the Fishing Village, there is a small chance to pull up Dog's Bane instead of a fish or chest.
2.  **World Spawns:** Occasionally, rare herbs spawn in the forest and swamp biomes. Keep an eye out for unique foliage that stands out from the standard grass.

| Item | Effect | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dog's Bane** | Reduces age, removes wrinkles, restores hair color | **Rare** |
| **Tonic** | Restores Health (HP) | Common |
| **Arrow Shard** | Used to obtain a Stand | Very Rare |

Using Dog's Bane will immediately smooth out the wrinkles on your face and return your hair to its original hue. It is highly recommended to keep at least one in your bank for when your character reaches their senior years.

## Enhancing Your Look in Richby County

While you cannot change the underlying bone structure of your face without a wipe or a Stand, you can change your character's overall "vibe" by visiting **Richby County**. This is the main hub of the game, accessible via the giant railroad tracks that run across the map.

### Clothing and Accessories
To the left of the train station, you will find various vendors. If you aren't happy with your face, you can use these items to mask or complement your features:
*   **Hats:** Large-brimmed cowboy hats can cast shadows over your face, hiding wrinkles or features you dislike.
*   **Ponchos:** These add a layer of mystery and can draw attention away from the facial model.
*   **Masks/Bandanas:** (Check latest updates) Some items allow you to cover the lower half of your face entirely, which is perfect for outlaws.

### The Role of Alignment
Your alignment (Outlaw vs. In-law) doesn't directly change your face, but it does change your "aura" and how NPCs perceive you. You can manage your alignment by performing deeds or crimes, which may change the visual effects surrounding your character when your Stand is active.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my face without losing my Stand?**

A: Yes, but only through two methods: using the Crazy Diamond Stand's "Facial Reconstruction" ability (10% chance on hit) or by using Dog's Bane to reverse aging if your facial changes are due to wrinkles and gray hair. A full character wipe is the only other way, but that will delete your Stand.

**Q: Where exactly is the Witch's Hut for character talents?**

A: The Witch's Hut is located in the swamp biome, near the Fishing Village. While she doesn't change your face, she allows you to awaken "Cards" or talents that add passive buffs to your character.

**Q: Does dying to NPCs age my face as much as dying to players?**

A: Any death in Bridger Western contributes to the aging process, typically adding 1-2 years per death. However, combat logging (leaving during a fight) is the most punishing, adding 10 years instantly to your character's age.

**Q: Is there a way to guarantee a specific face during a wipe?**

A: No. The faces are part of a randomized pool. If you are looking for a specific look, you may have to wipe your character multiple times until the RNG rolls the face you desire. This is why it is best to do this before you start leveling up.
