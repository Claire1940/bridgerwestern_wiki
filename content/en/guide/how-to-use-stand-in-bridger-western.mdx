## How to Obtain Stands in Bridger: Western

Before you can worry about specific ability rotations, you must first acquire a Stand. In the current 2026 meta, there are two primary paths to gaining these supernatural companions: the patient path of fishing for an Arrow Shard or the high-risk path of securing a Saint’s Corpse Part.

### The Arrow Shard Method (Fishing)
The Arrow Shard is considered the "safer" method, making it ideal for solo players or those who want to avoid the chaotic shootouts that occur around world events. To start, you must head to the **Tackle Bait shop**—if you are lost, simply follow the train tracks until you reach the station near the docks.

| Item | Cost (Mula) | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Fishing Rod** | Variable | Essential tool for catching fish and shards |
| **Bait** | Low | Increases the speed of bites |
| **Arrow Shard** | N/A | **0.5% chance** to catch; grants a random Stand |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Arrow Shard is a Legendary-tier item. It cannot be banked or stored, and it is lost upon death or leaving the server. Use it immediately to secure your Stand.

### The Saint’s Corpse Part Method
This is a world event that triggers a "gold rush" across the server. When a part spawns, a notification appears at the top of the screen stating, "It appears once again," and a massive yellow beam of light shoots into the sky.

| Server Population | Spawn Chance (Every 30s) | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **3 Players** | 1 in 150 | Low Competition |
| **25 Players** | **1 in 40** | High-Stakes War |

Once you pick up the Corpse Part, you do not get the Stand instantly. You must survive a "pulse" mechanic. Every few seconds, the part pulses, giving you a **15% to 20% chance** to merge with it. If you are hit by another player, there is a high chance you will drop the part, making speed and evasion critical.

## Bridger Western: How to Use Stand Abilities & Controls

Once you have successfully merged with a Stand, a new hotkey usually appears on your UI. Understanding the control scheme is vital because the game requires you to manage traditional western weaponry alongside your supernatural skills.

### Core Control Reference
While the primary keyboard layout for **how to use stand in bridger western** revolves around the E, R, T, and Y keys, you must also master the general movement and utility keys to stay alive during a duel.

*   **E, R, T, Y:** Standard Stand ability keys (varies by Stand).
*   **M:** Open Menu (View Age, Alignment, and Tiers).
*   **H:** Summon/Dismiss Horse.
*   **N:** Mount/Dismount Horse.
*   **X:** ESP (Allows you to see players through walls/cover).
*   **C:** Equip Fists (Useful for close-quarters backup).
*   **F:** Horse Attack (Deals heavy damage to players on foot).

> **💡 Tip:** Many Stand abilities function best in melee or mid-range. Use your horse (Shift to sprint) to close the gap or retreat while your cooldowns reset.

## Enhancing Your Build: The Witch's Hut and Talent Cards

Your Stand's raw power is only half of the equation. To truly master **how to use stand in bridger western**, you must visit the Witch’s Hut located deep within the swamp. The Witch allows you to "read your fortune," which assigns Talent Cards to your character.

### Talent Card Mechanics
You unlock a new Talent Card slot at each Tier (Max 3). These cards provide passive buffs that can drastically change how your Stand interacts with the world.

| Action | Cost (Mula) | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **First Fortune Read** | **150** | Grants your first random Talent Card |
| **Reroll Talent** | **50** | Replaces a card with a new random buff |
| **Stand Reset** | Rare Plant | Clears your current Stand to allow for a new roll |

Common talents like **True Cowboy** increase your gun damage by 17% while on horseback. This is highly effective for players using utility-based Stands like *Hermit Purple* or *Hey Ya*, allowing you to stay mobile while dealing high projectile damage.

## Survival Essentials for Stand Users

Even the most powerful Stand can’t save you from a well-placed headshot if you aren't prepared. In Bridger: Western, gear is just as important as your supernatural ability.

1.  **The Cowboy Hat:** Purchase this at the Tailor. It acts as a "headshot shield." When you are shot in the head, the hat flies off, absorbing the initial massive damage that would otherwise result in an instant kill.
2.  **The Right Horse:** A slow horse is a death sentence when carrying a Corpse Part. Save up **1,500 Mula** for a Legendary Horse to ensure you can outrun the rest of the server during a merge.
3.  **The Age System:** Be aware that death increases your age by 1-2 years, while combat logging (leaving during a fight) increases it by **10 years**. As you age, your character's appearance will change, reflecting your history on the frontier.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get a Stand for a new player?**

A: The fastest way is to join a high-population server and look for the yellow beam of the Saint’s Corpse Part. However, this is very dangerous. For a safer start, spend time fishing at the docks for an Arrow Shard.

**Q: Why can't I pick up the Corpse Part?**

A: You cannot pick up a Corpse Part if you already possess a Stand. You must first reset your current Stand at the Witch's Hut using a specific rare plant found through fishing or by wiping your character progress.

**Q: How do I use stand in bridger western when I'm on a horse?**

A: Most Stand abilities can be activated while mounted. Use the E, R, T, and Y keys as usual. Combining a Stand's stun ability with the horse's "F" attack (trample) is a common high-level tactic.

**Q: Does the server size affect how often Stands appear?**

A: Yes. Corpse Parts spawn every 30 seconds, but the *probability* of a spawn increases with the number of players. A full server of 25 players has a 1 in 40 chance, while a small server of 3 players only has a 1 in 150 chance.
