## Locating the Horse Seller

Finding the right horse begins with knowing exactly where to shop. The primary horse merchant is situated near a landmark most players frequent early in their journey. To reach him, start your journey at the **Fishing Docks**, specifically near the tackle base store. This area serves as a central hub for many starter activities.

From the tackle store, follow the shoreline path leading away from the water toward the interior of the settlement. You will see a fenced enclosure and a specialized NPC standing near the stables. This merchant is your gateway to acquiring new mounts through a "reroll" system. When interacting with him, you are presented with a specific horse and its price. If the stats or the appearance do not meet your requirements, you must use the "Show me another" option to cycle through the available stock.

> **Warning:** Every time you ask to see another horse, you are essentially gambling for better stats. Ensure you have a healthy supply of currency before attempting to roll for a high-tier mount.

## Understanding the Price Mechanic

In Bridger: Western, the price of a horse is not arbitrary. It is calculated based on a specific formula that aggregates all of the horse's hidden stat values. While you cannot see the exact numerical breakdown of Speed, Stamina, Jump, and Damage before purchasing, the price serves as a highly reliable indicator of the horse's overall potential.

| Price Range (Mula) | Quality Tier | Potential Stat Outlook |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **150 - 500** | Bad / Mid | Low base stats; likely slow with poor stamina. |
| **500 - 1,000** | Mid / Good | Reliable stats; suitable for general gameplay. |
| **1,000 - 1,400** | High Tier | Strong legs and clear eyes; very high stat totals. |
| **1,400 - 1,500+** | Top of the Line | Elite stats; often contains near-max values in key areas. |

When the merchant describes a horse as a "Beast" or a "Best Runner," and the price exceeds 1,400 Mula, you are looking at a top-tier specimen. However, keep in mind that these points are distributed randomly. A very expensive horse might have maxed-out Jump and Damage but mediocre Speed. As a rider, you should prioritize **Speed** and **Stamina** above all else.

## Understanding Rarity for Horses Bridger Western

The most sought-after mounts in the game are the Legendary and Mythical variants. These are not just cosmetic upgrades; they represent horses that break the standard "stat ceiling" of the game. Normally, a standard horse is capped at a stat value of **1.25**. Legendary and Mythical horses are the only ones capable of exceeding this limit, reaching values like 1.35 or higher.

### Visual Rarity Indicators

You can identify these elite horses by looking at their names in the merchant's menu. The game uses specific text formatting to signal that a horse has "broken" the stat cap.

| Rarity | Text Formatting | Stat Boost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | Normal Case | No stats exceed 1.25 |
| **Legendary** | ALL CAPS [NAME] | **One** stat exceeds 1.25 |
| **Mythical** | ALL CAPS [[NAME]] | **Two** stats exceed 1.25 |

It is a common misconception that a Legendary horse is automatically "good." In reality, a Legendary horse simply means one stat has been boosted beyond the 1.25 limit. If that boost happens to land on a less useful stat, like Jump Power, the horse might still feel "trash" compared to a high-priced Standard horse with balanced Speed and Stamina. Always look for the combination of a high price (1,400+) and the Legendary/Mythical naming convention for the absolute best results.

## Stat Verification and The 15-Minute Rule

Once you have committed your currency and purchased a horse, you cannot immediately see its finalized stats. Bridger: Western implements a "breaking in" period. After purchasing and mounting your new steed, you will notice a timer in the stat menu. 

Typically, you must wait **916 seconds** (approximately 15 minutes and 16 seconds) before the horse's true stats are revealed. During this time, you can still ride the horse, but its full potential remains hidden. This mechanic prevents players from rapidly buying and discarding horses without experiencing the mount first.

### Key Stats to Observe
1.  **Max Speed:** Look for values approaching 95-100 for elite performance.
2.  **Stamina:** Essential for long-distance travel without frequent stops.
3.  **Jump Power:** Useful for navigating mountainous terrain but secondary to speed.
4.  **Damage:** Relevant if you plan on using your horse in combat scenarios.

## FAQ

**Q: Does a Legendary horse always have better stats than a Standard horse?**

A: Not necessarily. A Legendary designation only means that one specific stat has exceeded the normal 1.25 cap. A Standard horse with a high price tag (1,400+) may have better overall balanced stats than a cheap Legendary horse that only has a boost in Jump Power.

**Q: How can I tell the difference between a Legendary and a Mythical horse?**

A: Look at the brackets in the name. A Legendary horse uses single brackets and all caps, like `[HORSE NAME]`. A Mythical horse uses thick double brackets and all caps, like `[[HORSE NAME]]`. Mythical horses receive boosts to two stats instead of one.

**Q: Where is the best place to buy horses bridger western?**

A: The primary horse seller is located near the Fishing Docks and the Tackle Base store. Follow the path inland from the docks to find the stables and the merchant NPC.

**Q: How long do I have to wait to see my horse's stats after buying it?**

A: You must wait exactly 916 seconds (about 15 minutes) after purchasing the horse before the full stat breakdown becomes visible in your menu.
