## Locating the Horse Seller NPC

Before you can start rolling for a **mythical horse bridger western**, you need to know where the market is. The primary horse vendor is located near the fishing docks and the tackle base store. If you are starting from the main town area, follow the coastline toward the fishing hub.

1.  **Start at the Fishing Docks:** Look for the tackle base store where players buy rods.
2.  **Follow the Path:** Head slightly inland from the docks along the main dirt trail.
3.  **Identify the Vendor:** You will see an NPC standing near a small corral. This is the Horse Seller who facilitates all purchases and rerolls.

> **💡 Tip:** Always bring at least 1,500 currency before visiting the vendor. While you can get lucky with cheaper horses, having a large bankroll allows you to reroll multiple times until a high-stat horse appears.

## Understanding Horse Rarity Tiers

The rarity of a horse in Bridger: Western is determined by its name formatting and its ability to exceed standard stat limits. Most horses have a hard cap of **1.25** on their individual stats. However, legendary and mythical variants can push these numbers much higher.

| Rarity | Name Format | Stat Boosts | Max Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | Standard Case | None | 1.25 per stat |
| **Legendary** | ALL CAPS (Thin Brackets) | **1 Stat** exceeds cap | ~1.35 on one stat |
| **Mythical** | ALL CAPS (Thick Brackets) | **2 Stats** exceed cap | ~1.35 on two stats |

When searching for a **mythical horse bridger western**, keep a close eye on the brackets surrounding the horse's name in the shop menu. Thick brackets, such as `[[HORSE NAME]]`, indicate a mythical tier, whereas thinner brackets `[HORSE NAME]` signify a legendary. It is important to note that a horse's name being in all caps is the first visual indicator that you have found something special.

## Analyzing Horse Stats and Price

In Bridger: Western, price is generally a reflection of the horse's total stat pool. The game uses a formula that adds up the horse's speed, stamina, jump, and damage to determine the final cost. However, a high price does not always mean it is the "best" horse for you; it simply means the cumulative stats are high.

| Price Range | Tier Category | Performance Expectations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **150 - 500** | Low/Bad | Slow movement, very low stamina. Avoid these. |
| **500 - 1,000** | Mid/Decent | Reliable for basic travel; okay for beginners. |
| **1,000 - 1,500** | High/Top-tier | Excellent stats. Likely to have 1-2 maxed attributes. |
| **1,500+** | Elite | The "Beast" or "Best Runner" category. High rarity chance. |

Focus your efforts on horses priced between 800 and 1,500. While a **mythical horse bridger western** can technically appear at a lower price point if its base stats are poor, your odds of getting a high-performing mount are much better in the premium price bracket.

## The Mythical Horse Stat Advantage

The reason the **mythical horse bridger western** is so highly sought after is the "double break" mechanic. In the standard game engine, no horse can naturally go faster than the 1.25 speed threshold. A mythical horse, however, can boost two separate stats—typically speed and stamina—to 1.35 or higher.

### Priority Stats for Your Mount
When evaluating a horse, prioritize stats in the following order:
1.  **Speed:** Crucial for traversing the large map and winning races.
2.  **Stamina:** Determines how long you can sprint before the horse tires.
3.  **Jump:** Useful for navigating mountainous terrain, but secondary to speed.
4.  **Damage:** Only relevant if you plan on using your horse as a weapon in specific combat scenarios.

> **Warning:** Not every **mythical horse bridger western** is a "god-tier" mount. If the mythical boost applies to Jump and Damage instead of Speed and Stamina, the horse may actually perform worse in daily travel than a high-stat Common horse. Always check the individual numbers before committing.

## How to Reroll for the Best Horse

To get a top-tier mount, you must use the "Show me another" option at the vendor. This acts as a "spin" or "reroll" mechanic. Each time you reroll, a new horse with randomized stats, rarity, and price will appear.

1.  **Talk to the Horse Seller:** Open the interaction menu.
2.  **Check the Name:** Look for ALL CAPS and thick brackets `[[ ]]`.
3.  **Check the Price:** If the price is under 500, it is likely a waste of time unless it's a rare rarity.
4.  **Reroll:** If the horse doesn't meet your criteria, click "Show me another."
5.  **Purchase:** Once you find a **mythical horse bridger western** with a high price tag (1,200+), buy it immediately.

After purchasing your horse, there is a cooldown period of **916 seconds (approximately 15 minutes)** before you can see the fully finalized stats in your menu. Be patient during this time, as the true potential of your mount won't be visible right away.

## FAQ

**Q: Does a legendary horse always have better stats than a common horse?**

A: Not necessarily. A legendary horse simply means one stat has the *potential* to exceed the 1.25 cap. A common horse with a high price (e.g., 1,400) might have better overall balanced stats than a low-priced legendary that only boosts a useless stat like jump power.

**Q: How can I tell if I found a mythical horse bridger western?**

A: Look for the name of the horse at the vendor. If it is in ALL CAPS and surrounded by thick brackets `[[NAME]]`, it is a mythical horse. This rarity allows two different stats to exceed the normal maximum limit.

**Q: How much should I spend on a horse?**

A: For a reliable, high-performing mount, aim for the 800 to 1,500 price range. Horses in this bracket are much more likely to have maxed-out speed and stamina, which are the most important stats for general gameplay.

**Q: Why can't I see my horse's stats immediately after buying?**

A: The game has a built-in "wait period" of about 15 minutes (916 seconds) before the full stat breakdown is revealed to the player. This is a standard mechanic in Bridger: Western to ensure server stability and stat calculation.
