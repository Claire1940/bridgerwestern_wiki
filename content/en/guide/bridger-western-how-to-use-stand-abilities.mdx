## How to Obtain Stands in Bridger: Western

Before you can worry about **bridger western how to use stand abilities**, you must first acquire a Stand. There are two primary methods for obtaining a Stand in the game: using an Arrow Shard or finding a Saint's Corpse Part. Each method has its own risks and rarity levels.

### The Arrow Shard Method
The Arrow Shard is the more "passive" way to get a Stand, though it requires significant patience. You can obtain an Arrow Shard through fishing at the docks. 

| Method | Acquisition Chance | Difficulty | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fishing (Arrow Shard)** | **0.5% Chance** | Low | Solo players/Beginners |
| **Corpse Part (Golden Beam)** | **Variable (15-20% per pulse)** | High | Groups/Advanced players |

To fish, you must purchase a fishing rod and bait from the shop near the docks. Once you have an Arrow Shard, you can use it to roll for a Stand. However, be aware that some high-tier Stands, such as **Tusk Act 4**, cannot be obtained through shards and require the Corpse Part.

### The Saint's Corpse Part Method
The Saint's Corpse Part is a world event. When a part spawns, a gold message will appear at the top of the screen stating "It appears once again," and a massive golden beam will shoot into the sky.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The entire server can see the golden beam. Expect a massive shootout at the location. It is highly recommended to have a fast horse ready for a quick escape once you grab the part.

Once you pick up the Corpse Part, you do not get the Stand immediately. Instead, a "pulse" mechanic begins. Every few seconds, the part will pulse, giving you a **15% to 20% chance** to merge with it and obtain a Stand. You must hold onto the part and survive until the merge is successful.

## Mastering Bridger Western How to Use Stand Abilities

Once you have successfully merged with a Stand or used an Arrow Shard, you need to understand the controls. While the game uses standard Western controls for guns and horses, **bridger western how to use stand abilities** involves a separate set of mechanics.

### Core Controls Reference
To use your Stand effectively, you must be familiar with the general control scheme of Bridger: Western:

*   **M:** Open Menu (View Age, Alignment, and Tiers)
*   **H:** Summon/Dismiss Horse
*   **N:** Mount/Dismount Horse
*   **Shift:** Sprint (on foot or on horseback)
*   **X:** ESP (See players through walls/hiding spots)
*   **C:** Equip Fists (Useful if you run out of ammunition)
*   **F:** Horse Attack (Deals significant damage to players on foot)

While specific Stand moves vary depending on the Stand you pull (such as Time Stop or projectile attacks), they generally follow a standard keybind layout (E, R, T, and Y). If you hear a distinct sound effect and the screen colors shift, it is a sign that someone nearby is using a high-tier ability like Time Stop.

> **💡 Tip:** Your Stand's power is often tied to your Tier. As you gain XP by killing NPCs and completing ambushes, you will reach Tier 2 and Tier 3, which can unlock further potential for your build.

## The Witch's Hut and Talent Cards

To truly maximize **bridger western how to use stand abilities**, you need to visit the Witch's Hut located in the swamp area. The Witch allows you to "read your fortune," which is the game's way of assigning Talent Cards.

### Talent Card Mechanics
You unlock a new Talent Card slot at each Tier (Max 3). These cards provide passive buffs that can significantly change your playstyle.

| Action | Cost (Mula) | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Fortune Read** | **150** | Grants your first Talent Card |
| **Reroll Talent** | **50** | Replaces a card with a random new one |
| **Stand Reset** | **Specific Plant** | Clears your current Stand to allow for a new one |

Common talents like **True Cowboy** increase your gun damage by 17% while on horseback, which is excellent for players who prefer to keep their distance while their Stand provides utility or defense.

## Survival Essentials: Horses and Gear

You cannot rely solely on your Stand. In the world of Bridger: Western, your horse and your gear are just as important as your supernatural abilities.

### Choosing the Right Horse
A slow horse is a death sentence when you are carrying a Corpse Part. Horses range in price from basic starters to legendary steeds.

1.  **Starter Horses:** Usually cost around 300-500 Mula. They are slow but get you from point A to point B.
2.  **Mid-Tier Horses:** Cost around 800-1,000 Mula. Balanced speed and health.
3.  **Legendary Horses:** Cost up to **1,500 Mula**. These are the fastest in the game and are essential for high-level play.

### Defensive Gear: The Cowboy Hat
One of the most overlooked items is the Cowboy Hat, purchasable at the Tailor in town. This isn't just a cosmetic item; it acts as a "headshot shield." If someone shoots you in the head, the hat will fly off, absorbing the initial massive damage. Without it, a single well-placed rifle shot can end your run instantly.

## Progression and the Age System

As you spend more time in the game and engage in combat, your character will age. You can check your age by pressing **M**. 

*   **Death:** Increases your age by 1-2 years.
*   **Combat Logging:** Increases your age by **10 years**.
*   **Visual Changes:** As you grow older, your character will develop wrinkles and your hair will eventually turn gray or white.

While there is much debate in the community about whether "old age" leads to a permanent character wipe, it is confirmed that aging is a primary tracker of your character's history and survival.

## Advanced Strategies for Stand Users

To excel in **bridger western how to use stand abilities**, you must combine your powers with the environment. For example, using a Stand ability to stun an opponent while your horse is charging (F key) can result in an instant kill.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to get a Stand for beginners?**

A: If you want to avoid high-level PvP, fishing for an Arrow Shard at the docks is the safest way. Although the 0.5% chance is low, it allows you to grind Mula by selling fish while you wait for a lucky pull.

**Q: How do I reset my Stand if I don't like the one I got?**

A: You have two options: you can either wipe your character entirely (losing all progress) or find a rare plant through fishing and bring it to the Witch at the Witch's Hut to reset your Stand.

**Q: Does the server size affect the Corpse Part spawn rate?**

A: Yes. In a server with 25 players, the spawn chance is roughly 1 out of 40 every 30 seconds. In a low-population server (3 players), the chance drops significantly to 1 out of 150.

**Q: Why can't I pick up the Corpse Part?**

A: If you already have a Stand, you cannot pick up a Corpse Part. You must reset your current Stand before you can attempt to merge with a new part. Understanding **bridger western how to use stand abilities** requires knowing when to stick with your current power or risk it all for a better one.
