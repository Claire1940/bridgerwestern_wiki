Once you have enough resources, optimization becomes a decision problem: when to reroll, when to stop, and when to save.

### Use batch rerolling, not impulse rerolling

Rerolling one blueprint at a time after every farm trip feels active, but it creates bad decisions. Instead, stack blueprints, define your target stats, then reroll in a focused block.

| Reroll Strategy | Blueprint Use | Result Quality | Recommended? |

This keeps your **bridger western poncho blueprint** supply healthy for future updates or balance changes.

## Stat Interpretation: What the Numbers Really Mean

Many players misread poncho effects because they expect flat values. In practice, poncho boosts behave like percentage-style modifiers in combat outcomes, so testing is essential.

### Quick test method you should use

1. Equip normal setup **without poncho** and hit a repeatable target.  
2. Record several hits (don’t rely on one number).  
3. Equip poncho and repeat under similar conditions.  
4. Compare average outcomes, not single highest hits.

| Test Variable | Without Poncho | With Poncho | What to Watch |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hit Range** | Baseline values | Slightly higher spread | Indicates modifier effect |
| **Average Damage** | Stable base | Improved average | Best indicator of value |
| **Consistency** | Normal variance | Similar variance + uplift | Confirms practical benefit |
| **Time to Defeat** | Longer | Shorter in repeated tests | Real combat impact |

Because of variance, don’t discard a roll after one skirmish. Run short repeated tests and judge on averages.

> **Tip:** Keep a simple note with 10-hit averages. Tiny recordkeeping beats guesswork and saves blueprints long term.
