## Getting Started: Controls and Character Creation

Before you step into the dusty streets, you must familiarize yourself with the interface. When you first launch the game in 2026, you will be prompted to create a character. Most experienced players suggest choosing the **Outlaw** path, as it offers significantly more freedom to explore and engage in various activities without the restrictions of a formal alignment.

### Essential Controls Reference

| Key | Action | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **H** | Summon Horse | Calls your current horse to your location. |
| **N** | Ride/Dismount | Mounts the horse or hops off. |
| **Shift** | Sprint | Increases movement speed on foot or horseback. |
| **F** | Horse Attack | Deals significant damage by trampling enemies. |
| **M** | Menu | Opens stats, talents, age, and horse info. |
| **X** | Ocular Prowess | Highlights players and NPCs (ESP-like ability). |
| **C** | Fists | Equips hands for melee combat when out of ammo. |
| **Space** | Jump | Hold for a high jump; tap for a mini-jump. |

Upon spawning, you are given a random amount of starting money (Moola) ranging from 50 to 500. A critical pro-tip for 2026 is to check your balance immediately. If you have less than 300 Moola, consider using the "Wipe" option and recreating your character. Starting with a healthy wallet allows you to skip the initial grind and jump straight into purchasing essential gear.

## The First Steps: Fishing and Early Economy

The most reliable way to build your fortune in Bridger: Western is through the fishing village. Follow the train tracks or navigate toward the **Red Corner** area to find the docks. You will need to purchase a Fishing Rod and some bait from the local hut.

Fishing involves a mini-game where you must click when bubbles appear or complete a skill check by pressing specific keys in sequence. Failing these checks reduces the time you have to secure the catch. While common fish can be sold for quick cash, the real prizes are the **Chests** and **Arrow Shards**.

> **💡 Tip:** Fishing in secluded areas along the river is safer than the main village docks, as high-traffic areas often attract bandits looking to steal your hard-earned catch.

### Fishing Loot Table

| Item | Rarity | Utility |
|---|---|---|
| **Bass/Common Fish** | Common | Sold for Moola at the fishing hut. |
| **Herbs** | Uncommon | Used for crafting or resetting stats. |
| **Chests** | Rare | Contains high amounts of Moola and gear. |
| **Arrow Shard** | Legendary (0.5%) | Grants a Stand when used. |

## Bridger Western How to Play Guide: Equipment and Combat

Combat in 2026 is fast and lethal. To survive encounters with both players and NPCs (like bandits or zombies), you need the right loadout. Head to the town's Gun Store and General Store to gear up.

### Recommended Loadout for 2026

1.  **Winchester Rifle:** Excellent for long-range engagements with high accuracy.
2.  **Whitney Dragon:** A powerful sidearm for close-quarters skirmishes.
3.  **Cowboy Hat:** Provides a "headshot shield." The first hit to your head knocks the hat off but deals zero damage.
4.  **Poncho:** Reduces damage taken to the torso at the cost of increased limb damage.

Store any extra weapons in the **Bank**. Since there is a maximum equipment limit, the Bank is essential for swapping builds without losing your expensive firearms.

## Horses: Your Lifeblood in the Desert

Mobility is everything. While your starter horse is functional, you should aim to upgrade as soon as you hit 1,000 Moola. Visit the stables to roll for new horses. The most expensive horses (up to 1,500 Moola) offer the highest speed and stamina stats, which are vital for escaping "gankers" or chasing down Corpse Parts.

If your horse dies, it takes approximately two minutes to respawn. During this time, you are extremely vulnerable. Always keep an eye on your horse's health in the **M** menu.

## Advanced Progression: The Witch and Cards

Once you reach Tier 2 or 3 by gaining XP from fishing or combat, you unlock card slots. To fill these, you must visit the **Witch’s Hut** located in the swamp area. Be prepared to pay 150 Moola for your first fortune reading.

### Top Tier Cards for Beginners

| Card Name | Effect | Why It's Good |
|---|---|---|
| **True Cowboy** | +17% Gun Damage | Massive boost for horseback combat. |
| **Sturdy** | Increased Defense | Helps you survive longer in PVP. |
| **Quick Draw** | Faster Equip Speed | Essential for reactive combat. |
| **Veteran** | XP Boost | Speeds up your progression to higher Tiers. |

You can "weave" these cards into your slots. If you don't like a specific talent, you can pay 50 Moola to reroll for a different one. Note that your Tier level determines how many cards you can active simultaneously (maximum of three).

## Obtaining a Stand: The Corpse Parts

The most sought-after power in Bridger: Western is the Stand. There are two primary ways to obtain one in 2026:

1.  **Fishing:** A very low (0.5%) chance to pull an Arrow Shard.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** A global message will appear: *"It appears once again."* A golden beam of light will mark a location on the map. 

If you grab a Corpse Part, it will pulse. Each pulse has a 15-20% chance to merge with you and grant a Stand. However, holding a Corpse Part makes you visible to the entire server. Everyone will be hunting you. If you already have a Stand and want a new one, you must use a **Dog Bane herb** or visit the Witch to reset your current ability.

> **Warning:** Combat logging (leaving the game while in a fight) will age your character by 10 years instantly. If you get too old, your hair turns gray, and your face develops wrinkles. While the community debates if "death by old age" is a permanent wipe, it is best to avoid the risk by playing fairly.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more Moola quickly in Bridger Western?**

A: The fastest way for beginners is fishing at the village and selling the catch. For more advanced players, hunting bandits and zombies provides chests that contain significant amounts of Moola.

**Q: What happens when my character gets old?**

A: As you age (by dying or combat logging), your character develops wrinkles and gray hair. You can de-age by 20 years by giving the Witch a **Dog Bane herb**, which is a rare drop from zombies or found while exploring.

**Q: How do I get the D4C Stand?**

A: D4C is one of the hardest Stands to obtain. You need 100 total kills, a 10-player kill streak in a single server, and then you must be "knocked" in the desert while a Corpse Part is active for a small chance to merge with the Heart.

**Q: Is there a map for Bridger Western?**

A: While there is no in-game minimap, following the train tracks is the best way to navigate. The tracks connect the major towns, the fishing village, and the desert borders. Using **Ocular Prowess (X)** can also help you find landmarks and players through obstacles.
