## Essential Movement and Interaction Controls

Navigating the rugged terrain of the valley requires fluid movement. Unlike standard adventure games, movement in this simulator is tied heavily to your character's physical state and stamina. Understanding how to transition from a casual stroll to a life-saving dash is critical when lead starts flying.

| Action | Keybinding | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick Dash** | Q | A short, rapid burst of movement to dodge incoming fire. |
| **Sprint** | W + W (Double Tap) | Increases movement speed at the cost of stamina. |
| **Summon Horse** | H | Calls your personal mount to your current location. |
| **Mount/Dismount** | N | Interaction key to get on or off your horse. |
| **Statistics Menu** | M | View your current health, bounty, and character stats. |
| **Fists** | C | Equips your hands for brawling or non-lethal interactions. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself Horseless in the middle of the wilderness, use the 'H' key to see if your mount is within range. If the horse has been killed, you may need to visit a stable in one of the two main cities to recover.

## Advanced Combat and Firearm Mechanics

The heart of the experience lies in its realistic gunplay. The **bridger western controls** for weaponry are designed to mimic the manual nature of 19th-century firearms. You cannot simply hold down the trigger; every shot must be intentional, and every reload must be timed perfectly.

### Primary Weapon Controls

| Input | Action | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **M1 (Left Click)** | Fire | Discharges the currently chambered round. |
| **M2 (Right Click)** | Aim | Transitions to a focused, over-the-shoulder view for accuracy. |
| **R** | Reload | Manually inserts new cartridges into the weapon. |
| **G** | Melee | Performs a weapon-butt strike if an enemy is too close. |
| **X** | Ocular Prowess | Activates a heightened sense to track targets or threats. |

### The Art of the Quick Draw

One of the standout features of the combat system is the "Quick Draw" mechanic. To perform this, you must hold the Right Click (M2) to hover your hand over your holster. Releasing and immediately clicking M1 allows you to "fan" or "hammer" your revolver, firing shots in rapid succession. This is particularly effective during duels or when ambushed by bandits.

## Dueling and Social Interactions

In Ridge B. Valley, combat isn't always about a chaotic shootout. The game supports a formal dueling system. By typing "duel" in the chat or using the interaction menu while facing another player, you can initiate a formal challenge. 

However, be warned: the community is often paranoid. Drawing a weapon while wearing a mask is a universal signal for hostility. If you wish to remain peaceful, keep your weapons holstered and use the dedicated chat functions to communicate. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Committing "unspeakable acts" against non-hostile players will mark you as a Killer. Once this status is active, certain shops and tailors, such as Dutch and Taylor Scurlock, may refuse to serve you, preventing you from purchasing new outfits or gear.

## Hunting and Survival in the Wilderness

The world extends far beyond the dusty streets of the main towns. Players can venture into the snow biomes to hunt for rare resources. Hunting requires a different application of the **bridger western controls**, focusing more on stealth and precision than raw firepower.

1.  **Locate Prey:** Use 'X' (Ocular Prowess) to find tracks or visual cues of deer and bears.
2.  **The Shot:** Aim for the head or heart. Wounded animals will leave a blood trail that you must follow to finish the job.
3.  **Skinning:** Once the animal is downed, interact with the carcass to skin it. This provides meat for health regeneration and pelts that can be crafted into unique items, like the coveted Bear Hat.
4.  **Predator Awareness:** Bears and wolves are significantly faster than players. If you are knocked off your horse, use 'Q' to dash away from lunges and 'C' to fight back with fists if your gun is empty.

## Character Progression and the "Prisoner" System

A unique aspect of this simulator is the lack of a traditional "Reset Character" button. If you find yourself in a disadvantageous position or wish to change your role, you cannot simply respawn. 

When a player dies, they may respawn as a "Prisoner" depending on their previous actions or the server's current state. Prisoners have limited access to weapons and must find a way to earn their freedom or find a player willing to "end their suffering" so they can respawn as a standard cowboy. This adds a layer of consequence to every gunfight, making the mastery of **bridger western controls** even more vital for long-term survival.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I fix my gun if it stops firing?**

A: Most firearms in the game require you to manually chamber a round after every shot or reload. Ensure you have pressed 'R' to reload and then M1 to chamber. If the gun is still stuck, check your ammo count in the 'M' statistics menu.

**Q: Can I change my clothes if I am marked as a Killer?**

A: Standard tailors like Taylor Scurlock will generally refuse to work with Killers. You will need to find black-market vendors or loot clothing items from other players or AI bandits to change your appearance.

**Q: What is the fastest way to travel across the map?**

A: While sprinting (W+W) is useful for short distances, your horse is the primary mode of transport. Using the **bridger western controls** to summon (H) and ride (N) your horse is essential. Note that horses have their own health and can be killed by predators or players.

**Q: Does the game cost Robux to play?**

A: As of the 2026 Alpha phase, the game typically requires a small entry fee of approximately 25 Robux. This price is subject to change as the game moves toward a full release.
