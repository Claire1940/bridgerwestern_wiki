## Initial Character Creation

When you first launch the game, you are presented with the character creation screen. This is the most direct way to determine your facial features. You can select your base look, hair, and general physical attributes. It is important to take your time here, as there is no "Barber" NPC currently in the game that allows for a simple facial reconstruction once you have entered the world.

If you are unhappy with your initial choice, the most common method for a **bridger western how to change face** request is to perform a character "Wipe." This resets your progress but allows you to access the creation menu again.

> **💡 Tip:** Before wiping your character, ensure you have stored any valuable items in the bank at Richby County, though be aware that a full wipe usually clears most personal progress.

## The Aging System and Facial Changes

One of the most unique features of Bridger Western is the dynamic aging system. Unlike many other Roblox JoJo games, your character's face is not static. As you spend time in the game and, more importantly, as you die in combat, your character ages.

### How Aging Affects Your Face
As your "Age" stat increases (visible by pressing the **M** key), your character will undergo physical transformations:
*   **Wrinkles:** Facial lines will begin to develop around the eyes and mouth.
*   **Hair Color:** Your hair will eventually turn gray and then white.
*   **Skin Texture:** The overall texture of your face becomes more weathered.

If you are searching for **bridger western how to change face** because your character looks too old, you aren't looking for a menu—you are looking for a specific herb.

### Reversing the Aging Process
To "change" your face back to a youthful state, you must find **Dog's Bane**. This rare herb is a consumable item that reduces your character's age, effectively smoothing out wrinkles and restoring your original hair color.

| Item | Purpose | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Dog's Bane** | Reduces character age and removes wrinkles | Found via Fishing (Rare) or World Spawns |
| **Tonic** | Heals HP during combat | Purchased at Tonic Shops in towns |
| **Arrow Shard** | Grants a Stand (0.5% chance) | Obtained through Fishing |

## How to Wipe Your Character

If you want a completely different facial structure—perhaps changing from a rugged look to something sleeker—you will need to utilize the Wipe mechanic. This is the only way to access the "Create Character" menu again after your initial spawn.

1.  Press the **M** key to open your menu.
2.  Navigate to the character settings.
3.  Select the **Wipe** option.
4.  Confirm the reset (Note: This will reset your level, cards, and alignment).
5.  Upon restarting, you will be back at the character creator where you can change your face, hair, and name.

## Customizing Your Look in Richby County

While the face itself is tied to age and creation, you can significantly alter your character's silhouette and "vibe" by visiting **Richby County**. This is the primary hub for fashion and utility in the game.

### Clothing and Accessories
Located to the left of the train station in Richby County, you will find vendors selling various western-themed items. These items overlay your character and can help hide facial features if you aren't happy with your current age.

*   **Cowboy Hats:** Available in various styles to shade your face.
*   **Ponchos:** Great for covering your torso and adding an outlaw aesthetic.
*   **Lassos & Lanterns:** Functional accessories that hang from your belt.

### Equipment and Gun Shops
Your appearance is also defined by the steel you carry. You can purchase various firearms and melee weapons to complete your look.

| Weapon Type | Example | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Pistols** | Colt, Maverick | Richby County Gun Shop |
| **Rifles** | Winchester, Tommy Gun | Richby County Gun Shop |
| **Special** | Steel Wires, Dynamite | Outlaw Hideout / Fishing |

## Essential Controls for New Players

To navigate the world and protect your face from the aging effects of death, you need to master the controls. Staying alive is the best way to prevent unwanted wrinkles.

| Action | Keybind | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Summon Horse** | **H** | Calls your current horse to your side |
| **Mount/Dismount** | **N** | Get on or off your horse |
| **Sprint** | **Double Tap W** | Faster movement on foot |
| **Roll** | **Q** | Evade incoming fire |
| **Ocular Prowess** | **X** | Enhanced accuracy and ESP |
| **Melee** | **G** | Strike with your weapon's butt |

> **Warning:** Combat logging (leaving the game while in a fight) will increase your age by **10 years** instantly. This is the fastest way to ruin your character's face with wrinkles.

## Unlocking Talents and Cards

As you level up (reaching Tier 2 and Tier 3), you can visit the **Witch's Hut** in the swamp area to unlock Cards. These cards provide passive buffs that can change how you interact with the world. While they don't change your physical face, they add "Aura" and special effects to your character.

To find the Witch's Hut:
1.  Go to the **Fishing Village**.
2.  Head into the swamp biome.
3.  Look for a small wooden shack.
4.  Talk to the Witch and select "Read my Fortune" (Costs 150 Moola for the first time).

## The Stand System: Appearance and Power

In Bridger Western, your "face" to the world is often defined by the Stand that towers behind you. Obtaining a Stand is a major milestone and changes your visual presence significantly.

### How to Get a Stand
There are two primary ways to obtain a Stand in 2026:
1.  **Fishing:** There is a 0.5% chance to pull up an **Arrow Shard**.
2.  **Corpse Parts:** A global message stating "It appears once again" signifies a Corpse Part has spawned. A golden beam will appear on the map. If you hold the part, you have a 15-20% chance per "pulse" to obtain a Stand.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my face without wiping my character?**

A: No, the only way to change the base facial structure after starting is to use the **Wipe** feature in the menu. However, you can remove age-related wrinkles by consuming **Dog's Bane**.

**Q: Where do I find Dog's Bane to fix my character's wrinkles?**

A: Dog's Bane is most commonly found as a rare reward from **fishing** in the Fishing Village or by exploring the forest and swamp biomes for random herb spawns.

**Q: Does my alignment (Outlaw/In-law) change my face?**

A: Currently, alignment does not change your facial features, though it may affect how certain NPCs interact with you and which shops you can access easily.

**Q: How do I stop my character from aging so fast?**

A: Avoid dying in PvP and never combat log. Each death adds 1-2 years to your age, and combat logging adds 10 years, which will quickly result in a wrinkled face and gray hair. Using the **bridger western how to change face** strategy of staying young requires careful play and frequent use of tonics.
