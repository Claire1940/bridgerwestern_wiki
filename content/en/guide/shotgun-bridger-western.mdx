### Maverick 88: The Gold Standard
The Maverick 88 is widely considered the premier **shotgun Bridger Western** choice for serious players. Unlike its counterparts, the Maverick 88 provides a consistent ragdoll effect upon impact, which is invaluable for crowd control and stopping aggressive "stand" users in their tracks. Its range is surprisingly generous for a shotgun, allowing you to engage targets before they get within melee distance.

### Mare's Leg: The Hybrid Contender
While technically a shortened lever-action, the Mare's Leg functions as a "shotgun-ish" hybrid in the current 2026 meta. It offers better range than the Maverick but lacks the crowd-controlling ragdoll effect. It is a high-skill weapon that rewards players who can land consistent shots while moving, making it a favorite for those who find standard shotguns too restrictive in open-field combat.

### The Double Barrel: A Warning
It is important to note that the Double Barrel shotgun is currently ranked as one of the worst guns in the entire game. Despite its iconic look, its damage-to-reload ratio is abysmal. Most experts recommend avoiding this weapon entirely in favor of the Maverick 88, which is essentially the "good version" of the Double Barrel in every measurable stat.

## Shotguns vs. The 2026 Firearm Meta

To truly master the **shotgun Bridger Western** playstyle, one must understand how these weapons interact with the rest of the arsenal. Shotguns occupy a specific niche that is often challenged by high-fire-rate weapons like the Tommy Gun or extreme-range rifles like the Malcolm 70.

### Close-Quarters Dominance
In tight spaces, such as inside buildings or around corners in town, a shotgun is nearly unbeatable. However, the Tommy Gun (S-tier) remains a fierce competitor due to its insane close-range DPS. While a shotgun offers burst damage and ragdoll potential, the Tommy Gun provides sustained pressure.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to engage a Malcolm 70 user across an open field with a shotgun. The Malcolm 70 has nearly infinite range and will pick you off before your pellets can even reach them.

### Comparing Shotguns to Top-Tier Rifles
While shotguns handle the "up-close and personal" encounters, rifles like the Winchester Repeater (SSS tier) dominate the mid-to-long range. The Winchester is favored for its eight-bullet capacity and quick reload, which shotguns often lack.

## Advanced Strategies for Shotgun Users

Using a **shotgun Bridger Western** effectively requires more than just pointing and clicking. Because of the reload times associated with the Maverick 88 and the Mare's Leg, you must be surgical with your shots.

1.  **Utilize the Ragdoll:** When using the Maverick 88, your first shot should ideally ground the opponent. Once they are ragdolled, you have a window to either reload, close the distance for a finishing blow, or switch to a sidearm like the Whitney LeMat for high-damage follow-up shots.
2.  **Corner Peeking:** Shotguns are the king of "peek" shooting. Use the third-person camera to look around corners, then quickly step out, fire a blast, and retreat behind cover.
3.  **Countering Melee:** Melee weapons like the Saber or Silver Dagger are currently considered weak in the 2026 meta because they require the user to run directly into your fire. A well-timed shotgun blast will stop a melee rusher instantly.

## Secondary Weapons to Pair with Shotguns

Since shotguns have limited range and often require frequent reloading, your choice of sidearm is crucial. You need a weapon that can cover your weaknesses when a target is just outside of your spread or when you've emptied your primary.

*   **Whitney LeMat:** This revolver is highly recommended for its massive headshot damage (up to 45 damage). It serves as an excellent "finisher" after you have softened a target with a shotgun blast.
*   **Schofield:** A reliable, "average" gun that reloads quickly. It is perfect for those moments when you need a dependable backup that doesn't have the accuracy issues of the LeMat.
*   **Mauser:** If you prefer speed over raw power, the Mauser's high fire rate and accuracy can help you pick off targets that are trying to flee your shotgun's effective range.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your shotgun loaded before entering a building. The Maverick 88's reload animation can be a death sentence if caught mid-cycle during an ambush.

## The Role of "Stands" in Shotgun Combat

In Bridger: WESTERN, firearms are often used in conjunction with "Stands." While this guide focuses on the hardware, it is important to remember that guns are generally superior to melee-based stands at the moment. By using a **shotgun Bridger Western** weapon, you can maintain a safe distance from a stand's barrage while still dealing significant damage. The ragdoll effect of the Maverick 88 is particularly effective at interrupting a stand's attack animation, giving you a massive tactical advantage.

## FAQ

**Q: Which is the best shotgun Bridger Western has for beginners?**

A: The Maverick 88 is the best choice for beginners and veterans alike. Its ability to ragdoll enemies provides a safety net that other weapons don't offer, making it much easier to survive close-range encounters.

**Q: Is the Mare's Leg actually a shotgun?**

A: In the context of Bridger: WESTERN, the Mare's Leg functions as a hybrid. While it lacks the wide spread and ragdoll of a traditional shotgun, its damage profile and effective range make it play very similarly to a high-precision scattergun.

**Q: Why is the Double Barrel considered D-tier in 2026?**

A: The Double Barrel suffers from poor range, a long reload time for only two shots, and a lack of special effects. Compared to the Maverick 88, which does everything better, there is currently no competitive reason to use the Double Barrel.

**Q: Can shotguns compete with the Tommy Gun?**

A: Yes, but it depends on the situation. A shotgun user wins if they can land a ragdoll shot first, but the Tommy Gun's high fire rate will win a sustained trade if the shotgun user misses their initial burst.
