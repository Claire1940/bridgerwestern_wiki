## The Lumberjack Profession and Economy

For the longest time, fishing was the primary method for players to earn money and find rare items. The **bridger western update log** introduces a viable alternative: Tree Chopping. By visiting the new Lumberjack NPC located in a newly added shack, players can purchase a Lumber Axe and begin harvesting wood.

### How to Start Woodcutting
1.  Locate the **Lumberjack Shack** (a new structure added in this update).
2.  Purchase the **Lumber Axe** from the NPC. This item stays on your back when not in use, providing a unique aesthetic "aura."
3.  Approach trees and use the axe to harvest logs.
4.  Return to the NPC to sell your wood for Moolah.

| Item/Action | Reward/Cost | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lumber Axe** | Initial Investment | Necessary for the profession |
| **Selling 10 Wood** | **200 Moolah** | Consistent income stream |
| **Dog Bane Herbs** | Random Drop | Can be found while chopping trees |
| **Fruit Seeds** | Rare Drop | Used for the new planting mechanic |
| **Wooden Stake** | Rare Drop | Specialized weapon for killing Vampires |

This new economic loop is vital because the developers have implemented a **50,000 Moolah cap**. This change was made to combat duplication glitches and stabilize the server economy. Players can no longer hoard millions, making active professions like woodcutting more relevant for daily spending.

## Fruit Seeds and the Stand Trading Mechanic

One of the more complex additions in this update involves "Fruit Seeds" and the resulting fruits (similar to the Rokakaka in JJBA). These items allow for a unique interaction between players that can completely reset or swap character attributes.

### Planting and Harvesting
When you obtain a seed from woodcutting or other activities, you must plant it on "steady ground." This has proven to be a bit of a challenge for some players, as the game is strict about the terrain requirements. Once grown, the fruit can be harvested and used for the "Split" mechanic.

### The Splitting Mechanic
To use the fruit, you must click on another player to prompt them to split it with you. There are strict requirements for this to work:
*   **Both players must have a Tier 3 Stand.**
*   Upon eating, the fruit will **equalize your ages**.
*   It may **swap a card or two** between players.
*   There is a high probability of **swapping stands** entirely.

> **Tip:** If you do not have a stand when participating in a fruit trade, there is a 1% chance the resulting stand skin becomes a "Shiny." This is currently the most prestigious way to hunt for rare cosmetics.

## Combat Balancing and Weapon Changes

No major update is complete without balancing the meta. The **bridger western update log** specifically targets two popular firearms: the Winchester Repeater and the Derringers. The goal was to make combat more tactical rather than relying on spam fire.

### Winchester Repeater Nerf
The Winchester has long been the king of mid-range combat. In this update, its **reload speed has been significantly nerfed**. Players can no longer fire off volleys and reload almost instantaneously, requiring better positioning and timing.

### Derringer Buff/Nerf Hybrid
The Derringers have seen a shift in their utility. While they now have **less ammo**, their **damage output has been increased**. This turns the Derringers into "finishing" weapons—high risk, high reward tools for close-quarters encounters.

| Weapon | Change Type | Specific Adjustment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Winchester Repeater** | Nerf | Slower reload speed |
| **Derringers** | Adjustment | Lower ammo capacity, higher damage |
| **Dual Pistols** | Stability | No major changes, remains a baseline choice |
