## How to Obtain the Rusty Mare's Leg

Before you can worry about repairs, you must first acquire the base item. In the current 2026 version of Bridger Western, the item is categorized as a high-rarity fishing drop. Unlike standard rifles or revolvers that can be purchased from a town gunsmith, this weapon requires a bit of patience and a sturdy fishing rod.

| Method | Rarity | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Fishing** | Epic / Legendary | Any deep water body |
| **Trading** | Player Dependent | Trading Hubs |

Most players report finding the item after several hours of fishing in the more remote regions of the map. It is important to note that the item appears in your inventory as a "Rusty Mare's Leg" and cannot be fired or equipped as a functional weapon until it has undergone a professional restoration process.

## Step-by-Step Repair Guide

Once you have the **rusty mares leg bridger** in your possession, your next goal is to find the specific NPC capable of restoring it. This is not a service offered by the standard merchants found in the main settlements. Instead, you must travel to the outskirts of Ridge County.

### 1. Locate the Bridge NPC
Start at Ridge County and head toward the outskirts. You are looking for a bridge that connects the main county to the surrounding wilderness. Across this bridge, you will find a lone NPC standing near a small campsite. This character acts as a specialized gunsmith for "lost" or "relic" weaponry.

### 2. The Restoration Cost
Repairing a weapon of this age is not cheap. You will need to have **1,500 currency** (often referred to by the community as "moola") ready in your wallet. When you interact with the NPC, ensure you have the Rusty Mare's Leg currently selected or in your active inventory.

### 3. Finalizing the Repair
Upon paying the fee, the NPC will take the rusted hunk of metal and perform the restoration. A notification will appear stating: *"Gun repaired and moved to storage."*

> **⚠️ Warning:** The weapon does not go directly back into your hands. Many players panic thinking the item disappeared, but it has actually been sent to your global bank storage.

## Retrieving Your New Weapon

To actually use your Mare's Leg, you must travel to the City Colony (or any major town with a banking facility). 

1.  **Enter the Bank:** Locate the bank building in the city.
2.  **Talk to the Clerk:** Interact with the NPC behind the counter.
3.  **Open Storage:** Navigate through your stored items until you see the "Mare's Leg."
4.  **Equip:** Move the item from storage to your active loadout.

The Mare's Leg is technically a "cut-down" version of a lever-action rifle. In the real-world history that Bridger Western draws from, these were often used as high-powered pistols or compact carbines. In-game, this translates to a very specific set of stats that distinguishes it from the standard Winchester.

## Weapon Stats and Comparison

Is the **rusty mares leg bridger** worth the 1,500 currency investment? The answer depends entirely on your playstyle. Below is a comparison between the fully repaired Mare's Leg and its closest competitor, the Winchester Rifle.

| Feature | Mare's Leg | Winchester Rifle |
|---|---|---|
| **Fire Rate** | **High** | Medium |
| **Damage** | Medium | **High** |
| **Ammo Capacity** | 6 Rounds | **10-12 Rounds** |
| **Range** | Medium | **Long** |
| **Mobility** | **Excellent** | Average |

The Mare's Leg functions as a hybrid between a pistol and a rifle. It fires significantly faster than the Winchester, allowing you to "fan" the lever in close-quarters combat. However, the shorter barrel results in lower damage per shot and a smaller magazine capacity. If you find yourself in frequent mid-range skirmishes where movement is key, the Mare's Leg is a top-tier choice for 2026.

## Additional Quests from the Gunsmith

The NPC who repairs your **rusty mares leg bridger** isn't just a one-time service provider. He also offers a series of skill-based challenges that can help you earn extra cash and unlock late-game content.

### The Can-Shooting Challenge
After you interact with him, he may offer a quest to test your accuracy. He will toss a tin can into the air, and you must hit it a specific number of times before it touches the ground.

*   **Tier 1:** Hit the can 3 times.
*   **Tier 2:** Hit the can 4 times (Rewards approximately $100).
*   **Tier 3:** Hit the can 6 times.

### The Vampire Questline
Completing the shooting challenges often serves as a prerequisite for the more "supernatural" elements of Bridger Western. Once you have proven your marksmanship, the NPC can provide directions to the Vampire Ruins. This leads to a specialized boss hunt that is considered one of the most difficult encounters in the 2026 update.

## Performance Review: Is It Worth It?

When evaluating the **rusty mares leg bridger**, we have to look at the "fun factor" versus "efficiency." 

From an efficiency standpoint, the Winchester is objectively a better "all-rounder" for general PvE and hunting. The higher damage ensures fewer shots are needed to take down targets, and the larger magazine is more forgiving if you miss.

However, the Mare's Leg excels in **PvP (Player vs. Player)** scenarios. Its fast fire rate allows you to overwhelm opponents who are using slower revolvers or single-shot rifles. Additionally, the animations for the Mare's Leg are some of the most polished in the game, making it a favorite for roleplayers and veteran gunfighters who want to stand out from the crowd.

> **💡 Tip:** Pair the Mare's Leg with a high-damage revolver. Use the Mare's Leg for initial pressure and switch to your revolver to finish off weakened targets.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find the Rusty Mare's Leg anywhere other than fishing?**

A: Currently, fishing is the primary way to obtain the "Rusty" version of the weapon. While some players attempt to trade it, it remains a rare drop that requires a bit of RNG luck at the docks.

**Q: What happens if I lose the gun after repairing it?**

A: In Bridger Western, once a weapon is repaired and added to your bank storage, it is tied to your account. If you die, you may lose items in your immediate inventory depending on the server settings, but your bank storage remains secure.

**Q: Is the Mare's Leg better than the Tommy Gun?**

A: These serve different roles. The Tommy Gun is an automatic weapon with high recoil, while the Mare's Leg is a precision lever-action tool. For medium-range accuracy, the Mare's Leg is superior, but for clearing rooms, the Tommy Gun wins.

**Q: Does the NPC always stay in the same spot?**

A: Yes, as of the 2026 updates, the Gunsmith NPC remains at his camp across the bridge from Ridge County. He is a permanent fixture of the map due to his involvement in the Vampire Questline.
