## Finding the Horse Seller

Before you can build your stable, you need to know where to go. The primary Horse Seller NPC is located near the coastal region of the map. Most players find it easiest to start at the **Fishing Docks** or the **Tackle Base Store**.

To reach the vendor from the Fishing Docks:
1.  Locate the main path leading away from the water.
2.  Follow the trail heading inland toward the wooded area.
3.  Look for a small corral and an NPC standing near a hitching post.

Talking to this NPC opens a menu with two primary options: "I'll take it" and "Show me another." The latter is the "Reroll" mechanic, which is essential for players hunting for specific rarities or high-stat distributions.

## Horse Pricing and Quality Tiers

In Bridger Western, price is generally a direct indicator of a horse's cumulative stat total. While it is possible to find a "diamond in the rough" at a lower price point, expensive horses have a significantly higher floor for their base attributes.

| Price Range (Mula) | Tier Classification | Recommended Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **150 - 500** | **Low Tier** | Early game travel; disposable mounts for dangerous areas. |
| **500 - 1,000** | **Mid Tier** | Reliable daily drivers; good balance of speed and cost. |
| **1,000 - 1,500+** | **Top Tier** | Competitive play; highest potential for maxed-out attributes. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not be fooled by price alone. A 1,400 mula horse with high Jump but low Speed is often less useful than an 800 mula horse with optimized Speed and Stamina. Always check the individual stat bars.

## Understanding Horse Stats

Every horse in the game is defined by four core attributes. When you purchase a horse, these stats are randomized within a range determined by the horse's price and rarity.

*   **Speed:** Determines the maximum velocity of the horse. A "good" speed stat is typically 90 or higher.
*   **Stamina:** Affects how long the horse can maintain a full gallop before needing to rest.
*   **Jump:** Dictates the height and distance the horse can clear. Useful for shortcutting through mountainous terrain.
*   **Damage:** Influences the impact damage dealt if you trample NPCs or other players.

### The 1.25 Stat Cap
For standard horses, the maximum value for any single stat is capped at **1.25**. This represents the peak physical performance of a "normal" animal. To go beyond this limit, you must find a Legendary or Mythical variant.

## Legendary and Mythical Horses

The most sought-after **bridger western horses** are the Legendary and Mythical rarities. These are not separate breeds but rather "stat-boosted" versions of existing horses. You can identify them by their unique naming conventions in the vendor menu.

### Identification Table
| Rarity | Name Format | Visual Indicator | Stat Boost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | Mixed Case (e.g., "Old Moon") | Standard colors | No stats exceed 1.25 |
| **Legendary** | **ALL CAPS [NAME]** | Skinny brackets | **One stat** can exceed 1.25 (up to 1.35) |
| **Mythical** | **ALL CAPS [[NAME]]** | Thick brackets | **Two stats** can exceed 1.25 (up to 1.35) |

It is important to note that a Legendary horse can still be "bad" if the stat it boosts is irrelevant to your needs. For example, a Legendary horse that boosts Jump to 1.35 but has a Speed of only 0.80 will feel much slower than a high-end Normal horse.

> **💡 Tip:** Mythical and Legendary horses often feature unique, vibrant coat colors that are not available on standard mounts, making them a status symbol in the community.

## The 15-Minute Reveal Rule

One of the most confusing mechanics for new players is the "Stat Settlement" period. When you first purchase one of these **bridger western horses**, its full potential isn't immediately visible in the UI.

After buying a horse, you must wait approximately **916 seconds (roughly 15 minutes)** for the stats to fully calibrate. During this time, you can ride the horse, but the numbers shown in the menu may fluctuate or appear lower than expected. Once the timer expires, the true stats—including any Legendary or Mythical breakthroughs—will be permanently locked in.

## How to Reroll for the Best Horse

To get a top-tier mount, you must be prepared to spend some time at the vendor. Follow this optimized reroll strategy:

1.  **Save your Mula:** Don't bother rerolling until you have at least 2,000 mula. This ensures you can actually afford a Top Tier horse if it appears.
2.  **Look for Keywords:** The NPC will often describe the horse. Phrases like "Top of the line," "Beast," or "Best runner" usually indicate high base stats.
3.  **Prioritize Speed and Stamina:** These are the only two stats that significantly impact your efficiency in the game.
4.  **Watch for All-Caps:** If you see a name in all caps, stop immediately and check the brackets. Even if the price is low (e.g., 600 mula), a Legendary or Mythical is almost always worth the gamble.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my horse's name after I buy it?**

A: No, the name is assigned at the vendor and is tied to the horse's rarity and identity. Legendary and Mythical horses will always have their names in all caps with brackets.

**Q: Is a more expensive horse always better than a cheaper one?**

A: Not necessarily. While higher prices generally mean higher total stat points, the distribution is random. An 800 mula horse with all points in Speed is better for racing than a 1,400 mula horse with all points in Damage and Jump.

**Q: How do I know if my horse is Mythical?**

A: Look at the name in the interaction menu. A Mythical horse will have its name in ALL CAPS and will be surrounded by thick double brackets like this: **[[HORSE NAME]]**.

**Q: Where is the best place to find bridger western horses with high speed?**

A: There is only one main Horse Seller NPC, but you can "find" high-speed variants by using the "Show me another" reroll option at the vendor until a horse with a high price (1,200+) or a Legendary/Mythical tag appears.
