<Section icon={Target} title="Setting Up Your Bridger Western Steel Wire">

Proper setup is paramount for the effectiveness and safety of your **Bridger Western Steel Wire Set**. A well-placed and securely anchored wire set minimizes escape chances and ensures humane capture. This section outlines the general steps for deploying your wire set in the field.

<Callout type="warning" title="Safety First">
Always wear protective gloves when handling steel wire to prevent cuts and abrasions. Ensure all components are free of defects before deployment.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Site Selection">
    Identify a suitable location based on animal tracks, feeding signs, and natural travel paths. Consider terrain, weather, and accessibility for checking the trap.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Anchor Placement">
    Securely drive or embed your chosen anchor (e.g., steel rod, rebar, or cable anchor) into the ground. Ensure it's deep enough to resist strong pulling forces.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Wire Attachment">
    Attach the main wire from your **Bridger Western Steel Wire Set** to the anchor using a strong knot or a specialized crimp. Double-check for any slack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Trap Connection">
    Connect your trap to the main wire using swivels. Swivels are essential to prevent the animal from twisting and damaging the wire or itself. Use at least two swivels for optimal performance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Concealment and Testing">
    Conceal the wire and trap as naturally as possible. Test the tension and stability of the entire setup before leaving the site. Ensure it blends with the environment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>
