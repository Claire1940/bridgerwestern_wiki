## Finding the Horse Seller and Rerolling

Before you can secure a high-tier mount, you must locate the horse seller NPC. Most players begin their journey near the fishing docks and tackle shop. From this central hub, you should follow the main path leading away from the water toward the equestrian stalls. The horse seller is a permanent fixture in this area, offering a rotating selection of horses to any player with enough cash.

When you interact with the horse seller, you are presented with two primary options: "I’ll take it" or "Show me another." The "Show me another" option is effectively a reroll mechanic. Each reroll costs a small fee but allows you to cycle through different horses with varying names, prices, and potential stat spreads. To find the **best horses in bridger western**, you must be prepared to spend some time rerolling until the NPC offers a horse with a high price tag or a unique rarity indicator.

> **💡 Tip:** Never settle for the first horse you see. Rerolling is the only way to encounter Legendary or Mythical variants without waiting for a natural shop reset.

Price is the most immediate indicator of a horse's quality. While it is possible for a cheaper horse to have one decent stat, the game uses a formula that adds up all potential stat points to determine the final cost. Therefore, a more expensive horse is mathematically more likely to have higher base stats across the board.

## Understanding Rarity: Legendary vs. Mythical

The true "best" horses in the game are those that can break the standard stat limitations. In Bridger Western, a standard horse has a maximum stat cap of 1.25. No matter how expensive a "Normal" horse is, its speed, stamina, jump, and damage will never exceed this 1.25 ceiling. To go beyond this, you must find a Legendary or Mythical horse.

### Visual Indicators
You can identify these rare horses by their names in the shop menu:
*   **Legendary Horses:** The name will be in **ALL CAPS** and surrounded by thin brackets, such as `[ARIZONA]`.
*   **Mythical Horses:** The name will be in **ALL CAPS** and surrounded by thick brackets, such as `[[NEVADA]]`.

### Stat Boosts and Caps
Rarity does not guarantee that a horse is "good," but it raises the potential ceiling. A Legendary horse receives a significant boost to **one** random stat, allowing that stat to reach up to 1.35 or higher. A Mythical horse receives this boost to **two** random stats.

| Rarity Type | Name Format | Stat Overcap Count | Max Stat Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | Standard Case | 0 | 1.25 |
| **Legendary** | `[ALL CAPS]` | 1 Stat | ~1.35+ |
| **Mythical** | `[[ALL CAPS]]` | 2 Stats | ~1.35+ |

> **⚠️ Warning:** A Legendary horse can still be "bad" if the boosted stat is something less useful, like Jump Power, while its Speed and Stamina remain low. Always check the price in conjunction with the rarity.

## Prioritizing Stats: Speed and Stamina

While there are several stats associated with your mount, two stand head and shoulders above the rest in terms of utility. If you are hunting for the **best horses in bridger western**, you should prioritize these in the following order:

1.  **Stamina:** This determines how long your horse can maintain a full gallop. In a game with a map as large as Bridger Western, high stamina is essential to avoid being stranded in the middle of a desert without a fast way to move.
2.  **Speed:** This determines your maximum velocity. High speed is critical for escaping combat or catching up to targets during bounties.
3.  **Jump:** Useful for navigating rocky terrain and shortcuts, but generally less vital than movement stats.
4.  **Damage:** Determines how much damage the horse deals if it tramples an NPC or player. This is widely considered the least important stat for most players.

When you purchase a horse, you won't see the exact numerical stats immediately. The game requires a "settling" period of approximately 916 seconds (roughly 15 minutes) before the full stat sheet is revealed to the player. During this time, you can still ride the horse, but you won't know if you've truly hit the jackpot until the timer expires.

To maximize your chances of getting a top-tier mount, you should follow a specific rerolling strategy. First, ensure you have a healthy bankroll. Rerolling and purchasing a high-tier horse can easily cost upwards of 2,000 cash.

If you see a horse priced at 1,400 or higher, it is almost certainly a "top-of-the-line" beast with high base stats, even if it isn't a Legendary rarity. The absolute **best horses in bridger western** are Mythical variants that also carry a high price tag, as this indicates high base stats combined with two overcapped bonuses.

## FAQ

**Q: Does a Legendary horse always have better stats than a Normal horse?**

A: Not necessarily. A Legendary horse simply has the *ability* to exceed the 1.25 stat cap on one specific attribute. A high-priced Normal horse (e.g., 1,500 cash) might have better overall balanced stats than a cheap Legendary horse (e.g., 500 cash) that only has a boost in Jump Power.

**Q: How can I tell if a horse is Mythical before I buy it?**

A: Look at the name in the seller's menu. If the name is in all capital letters and has thick double brackets like `[[NAME]]`, it is a Mythical horse. These are considered the **best horses in bridger western** because they can exceed the stat cap on two different attributes.

**Q: How long do I have to wait to see my horse's stats after buying it?**

A: You must wait exactly 916 seconds, which is approximately 15 minutes of in-game time. After this period, the horse's full potential and numerical stats will be visible in your menu.

**Q: What are the most important stats to look for?**

A: Speed and Stamina are the most important. Speed allows you to travel faster and escape danger, while Stamina ensures you can maintain that speed for longer durations across the large map.
