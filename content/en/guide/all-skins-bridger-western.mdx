## How to Obtain Skins: The 1% Chance Mechanic

Unlike many other Roblox games where skins are purchased directly through a storefront or "crates," Bridger Western utilizes a unique trading-based RNG system. This makes the process of collecting **all skins bridger western** enthusiasts desire a community-driven effort.

The primary method to generate a new skin is through the "Roka Trade" mechanic. When two players trade Stands using a Rokakaka fruit, there is a **1% base chance** for the Stand being traded to transform into its skinned version, provided it doesn't already have one. This low probability ensures that skins remain high-value assets within the server.

### The Trading Process Step-by-Step

To participate in the skin economy, you cannot simply click a button in a menu. You must interact with the world and other players.

1.  **Reach Tier 3:** You cannot engage in the Stand trading mechanic until you have reached Tier 3. This ensures that only players who have put significant time into the game can influence the skin market.
2.  **Acquire an Axe:** Visit the Lumber NPC located in the house near the forest edge. You will need this tool to gather the necessary fruits for trading.
3.  **Farm for Roka:** Head into the woods to chop down trees. This is the only way to obtain the Rokakaka fruit required for the trade.
4.  **Initiate the Trade:** With the Roka in hand, click on another player. A notification will appear asking if they wish to trade.
5.  **Equalize and Transform:** The trade will equalize the "Age" of the Stands and cards involved. If the Stand is eligible, the 1% skin chance will trigger during this exchange.

## Farming Resources: Forest vs. Swamp

To get **all skins bridger western** offers, you are going to need a massive supply of Rokakaka fruits. These fruits act as the "fuel" for your skin attempts. There are two primary locations for farming, each with its own pros and cons.

| Location | Primary Drops | Efficiency | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Forest** | **Roka** | High | Low |
| **The Swamp** | **Roka, Dog Vein Herbs** | Low | Medium |

The Forest is the recommended spot for most players. Trees in the forest are easier to chop down, allowing for a faster rotation of Roka drops. While the Swamp offers Dog Vein herbs—which are useful for other crafting and healing purposes—the trees there take significantly longer to fell. If your sole goal is to trigger the 1% skin chance as often as possible, stick to the Forest.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a macro or a dedicated farming path in the Forest to maximize your Roka-per-hour. The more trades you can perform, the faster you will beat the 1% RNG.

## Understanding Stand Age and Cards

A unique aspect of the trading system in Bridger Western is the equalization of Age. When you trade to hunt for **all skins bridger western** variants, the game looks at the "Age" of your Stand (viewable by pressing 'M' to open the menu). 

Trading doesn't just swap the visual model; it synchronizes the progress between the two players. This is particularly important for players trying to maintain high-level Stands while hunting for skins. Additionally, the trade system includes "Cards," which are modifiers that can further enhance your Stand's capabilities. Always check the card stats before confirming a trade, as a rare skin on a Stand with poor card modifiers might actually be a net loss for your combat effectiveness.
