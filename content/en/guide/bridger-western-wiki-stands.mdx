## How to Obtain Stands in Bridger Western

There are two primary methods for obtaining a stand in Bridger Western as of 2026. The first is through the fishing mechanic, which is the safest but most time-consuming route. The second is through the high-stakes "Corpse Part" events that occur periodically across the map.

### Method 1: Fishing and Stand Shards
Fishing is the primary way for beginners to earn money (Moola) and experience. By purchasing a fishing rod and bait from the Fishing Village hut, you can begin the grind. Occasionally, you will trigger a skill check during fishing. Successfully completing this check may reward you with a chest.

| Item | Drop Rate | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Stand Shard** | ~0.5% | Used to roll for a random stand. |
| **Dog Bane Herb** | Rare | Sold to the Witch to de-age your character. |
| **Rusty Mayor's Leg** | Very Rare | Can be repaired for 1.5k Moola to create a weapon. |
| **Common Fish** | 90% | Sold for Moola at the bait shop. |

### Method 2: The Corpse Part Event
Periodically, a server-wide notification will appear: "A corpse part will spawn." A massive beam of light will descend from the sky, marking the location of the part. Picking up a corpse part makes you a target; your character will occasionally glow white, revealing your position to every player on the map.

If you successfully hold the corpse part, there is a recurring chance every few seconds that the part will "merge" with you. Once merged, you are granted a random stand from the available pool. If you are killed while holding the part, it drops for others to claim.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a fast horse (1k+ Moola value) to escape the crowd once you grab a corpse part. Speed is your best defense against players trying to steal your prize.

## Obtaining the Legendary D4C Stand

Dirty Deeds Done Dirt Cheap (D4C) is arguably the most difficult stand to acquire in the game. It is not available through standard shards. Instead, you must follow a rigorous set of PvP and RNG-based requirements.

1. **Total Kills:** You must have at least 100 total kills across your account history.
2. **The Streak:** You must achieve a 10-player kill streak within a single server without dying.
3. **The Desert Sacrifice:** While maintaining your 10-kill streak, you must travel to the Desert biome and be "knocked" (downed but not killed) by an enemy or NPC.
4. **The RNG Merge:** While you are in the knocked state in the desert, a Corpse Part must spawn in the server. There is a 10% chance that the "Heart" will automatically merge with you, granting D4C.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Attempting the D4C quest is extremely risky. If you are finished off while knocked, you lose your streak and must start the process over.

## Essential Beginner Progression & Gear

Before you can effectively use your **bridger western wiki stands**, you need the right physical equipment. The town of Ridge County serves as the central hub for all your shopping needs.

### Recommended Weaponry
While your stand provides supernatural power, guns are your primary source of consistent damage.
*   **Winchester Rifle:** Excellent for mid-to-long range encounters with high accuracy.
*   **Whitney Dragon:** A powerful secondary for close-quarters self-defense.
*   **Malcolm 70:** The premiere sniper rifle for players who prefer to engage from a distance.

### Defensive Gear
Never leave town without a Cowboy Hat and a Poncho. The Cowboy Hat acts as a "one-time" shield for your head; a headshot will knock the hat off but deal zero damage to your health pool. The Poncho provides a flat damage reduction to your torso, though it slightly increases the damage you take if hit in the limbs.

### The Card System
By visiting the Witch's Hut in the swamp, you can spend Moola to roll for passive cards. You unlock up to three slots as you increase your Tier (level).

| Card Name | Effect | Build Type |

| **Executioner** | Bonus damage to players with low health. | Bounty Hunter |
| **Quick Draw** | Increases weapon swap and draw speed. | Gunslinger |

*   **Ridge County:** The main town containing the bank, gun store, tailor, and barber.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I reset my stand in Bridger Western?**

A: To reset your stand, you must visit the Witch in the swamp. She offers a reset service in exchange for Moola, allowing you to use a new Shard or Corpse Part to roll for a different ability.

**Q: Does my age affect my stand's power?**

A: While age does not directly scale stand damage, it affects your character's physical stats. If you become too old, you may experience decreased stamina. You can use the Dog Bane herb at the Witch's Hut to de-age by 20 years.

**Q: Can I trade stands with other players?**

A: Currently, there is no direct trading system for stands. Stands are bound to your character once merged. You can, however, trade items like Stand Shards or rare herbs if they are in your inventory.

**Q: What is Ocular Prowess and how do I use it?**

A: Ocular Prowess is a default ability (activated by pressing X) that highlights nearby enemies and increases your accuracy. It is particularly useful for finding players hiding in the dark or in dense brush. Certain cards, like "High Noon," can further enhance this ability to provide aim-assist features.
