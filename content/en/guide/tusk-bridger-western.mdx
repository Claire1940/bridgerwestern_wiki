Inspired by the legendary *Steel Ball Run* protagonist Johnny Joestar, Tusk is a high-skill, high-reward Stand that fundamentally changes how you interact with the game world. Obtaining **tusk bridger western** style requires patience, luck, and a willingness to sacrifice your character's mobility for devastating offensive capabilities. Unlike other Stands that allow you to maintain your standard movement and weaponry, Tusk forces you into a "crippled" state, making your horse your most valuable asset.

## Obtaining Tusk: The Saint's Left Arm

The journey to acquiring Tusk is distinct from other Stands in the game. You cannot simply use any corpse part; the process is highly specific to the lore of the Stand. To begin your journey with **tusk bridger western**, you must locate the **Saint's Left Arm**. 

It is important to note that other body parts, such as the Right Arm, Leg, or Rib Cage, will not grant you Tusk. Even after finding the correct part, there is only a **10% chance** of successfully manifesting the Stand. Because of these low odds and the rarity of the item, many players choose to "server hop" or join dedicated Discord communities to farm corpse parts collectively. Sharing resources with a group can significantly reduce the time spent searching for this elusive item.

| Item Required | Drop Chance (Tusk) | Alternative Uses |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Saint's Left Arm** | **10%** | Other Stand manifestations |
| Saint's Right Arm | 0% | Different Stand pools |
| Saint's Rib Cage | 0% | Different Stand pools |
| Saint's Leg | 0% | Different Stand pools |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Upon successfully obtaining Tusk, your character will permanently enter a "crippled" state. You will lose the ability to walk or run, and all current weapons (guns, sabers, etc.) will be deleted from your inventory.

## The Crippled Mechanic and Passives

The most defining feature of the **tusk bridger western** experience is the movement penalty. Your character will no longer be able to stand upright, forced instead to crawl or roll. This makes you incredibly vulnerable to melee attacks and fast-moving opponents if you are caught on foot.

### Movement and Mobility
To compensate for the lack of walking, you must rely entirely on your horse. It is highly recommended to invest in a **Legendary or Mythical horse** before pursuing Tusk. A high-tier mount provides the speed and durability necessary to keep you out of harm's way while your Stand does the heavy lifting.

### Weapon Restrictions
Tusk users are prohibited from using traditional firearms or melee weapons. When you attempt to purchase a weapon from a shop, NPCs will inform you that you lack the capability to hold them. Your "Nails" become your primary and only source of damage, effectively turning your fingertips into high-powered revolvers.

## Tusk Act 1: The Foundation

Once you manifest Tusk, you begin at Act 1. While this is the weakest form in terms of raw damage, it offers unique mobility tools that the later acts lack. You can switch between acts by pressing the **V key** once you have unlocked them.

**Act 1 Abilities:**
*   **Nail Glide:** A movement ability that allows you to slide across the ground. While useful, it has a relatively short duration.
*   **Nail Jump:** A small vertical boost exclusive to Act 1. This is the only "jump" available to a crippled user.
*   **Nail Bullets (E to toggle):** In this form, your nails act like a mini-gun. You can spam shots rapidly, though each individual hit deals lower damage compared to higher acts.

| Attack Type | Damage (Body) | Damage (Head) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rapid Fire Nails** | ~10 | ~19 |
| **Melee (T Key)** | Low | N/A |

## Tusk Act 2: The Sniper

Act 2 is widely considered the "sweet spot" for many **tusk bridger western** players. It trades the rapid-fire capabilities of Act 1 for precision, homing mechanics, and massive burst damage. 

The most significant addition in Act 2 is the **Sniper Shot (G Key)**. This move deals substantial damage and has the unique property of removing limbs from your opponent, severely hindering their ability to fight back. Furthermore, Act 2 introduces homing nails. If you miss a shot but the nail hits a nearby surface, the "hole" created by the spin will track the nearest enemy, making it very difficult for opponents to dodge your attacks in tight spaces.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the environment to your advantage. By shooting the ground near a retreating enemy, the homing property of Act 2 can often secure a kill that a direct shot would have missed.

## Tusk Act 3: The Wormhole Warrior

Act 3 focuses on maximizing the damage of individual shots through spatial manipulation. While it loses some of the utility of Act 2, its "Double Hit" mechanic makes it a powerhouse in 2026 PvP scenarios. When a nail from Act 3 strikes an opponent, it often triggers a second instance of damage as the "wormhole" effect passes through them.

**Act 3 Stats and Features:**
*   **High Damage Ceiling:** A single well-placed shot can deal upwards of 35-40 damage due to the piercing effect.
*   **Nail Glide:** Retains the standard movement glide found in previous acts.
*   **Stamina Management:** Act 3 consumes stamina (Blue Bar) and nail counts (Pink Bar) more aggressively, requiring the player to be more calculated with their shots.

| **Special Move** | Jump | Sniper/Limb Removal | Multi-Hit Piercing |

## Survival Strategies for Tusk Users

Playing as a Tusk user in **tusk bridger western** requires a completely different mindset than standard gunplay. Because you are vulnerable on the ground, your positioning is the difference between life and death.

1.  **Always Stay Mounted:** Your horse is not just transport; it is your legs. If your horse dies, you are a sitting duck. Always carry horse revives or health items.
2.  **Accuracy Over Volume:** Especially in Acts 2 and 3, missing shots is costly. Use the **X key** to increase your accuracy and ensure your high-damage projectiles find their mark.
3.  **Team Synergy:** Tusk is best utilized as a back-line DPS. Having a teammate with a standard Stand or high-tier firearms to draw aggro allows you to pick off enemies from a distance with Act 2's sniper shots.
4.  **Manage Your Bars:** Keep a close eye on the Pink bar (Nails) and Blue bar (Stamina). Running out of nails in the middle of a duel leaves you with only the weak melee "T" move.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get Tusk in Bridger: Western?**

A: You must find the Saint's Left Arm corpse part. Once you have it, use it for a 10% chance to obtain Tusk. Be aware that this will delete your weapons and prevent you from walking.

**Q: Can I evolve to Tusk Act 4 yet?**

A: As of the current 2026 game version, Tusk Act 4 has not been implemented into the game. Players are currently limited to switching between Acts 1, 2, and 3 using the V key.

**Q: What happens if I use a different corpse part?**

A: Using a Rib Cage, Leg, or Right Arm will result in different Stands or potentially no Stand at all. Only the Left Arm is compatible with the **tusk bridger western** evolution line.

**Q: Is Tusk good for solo players?**

A: Tusk is challenging for solo players because of the movement penalty. It is highly recommended to have a high-tier horse and potentially a friend to help defend you while you are in the vulnerable crawling state.
