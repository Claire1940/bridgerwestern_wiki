## Understanding Rarity: Legendary vs. Mythical

Many players are confused by the rarity system. In Bridger Western, a horse's rarity is indicated by its name being in **ALL CAPS** and the style of brackets surrounding it. However, rarity does not always guarantee high stats—it only guarantees a higher "ceiling" for specific attributes.

A standard horse has a stat maximum of **1.25**. Legendary and Mythical horses are the only mounts capable of exceeding this limit, reaching stats like 1.35 or higher.

### Rarity Indicators and Boosts

| Rarity | Visual Indicator | Stat Boosts | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Legendary** | `[NAME]` (Thin Brackets) | **+1 Stat** | One random stat exceeds the 1.25 maximum cap. |
| **Mythical** | `[[NAME]]` (Thick Brackets) | **+2 Stats** | Two random stats exceed the 1.25 maximum cap. |

It is important to note that a Legendary horse can still have poor base stats if its price is low (e.g., a 600 Moola Legendary). The "stat boost" might go into a less desirable attribute like "Scale" or "Jump" instead of "Speed" or "Stamina." For a truly S-Tier experience, you want a **Mythical horse with a 1,400+ Moola price tag**.

## How to Get the Best Horse in Bridger Western

To acquire a permanent, high-tier mount, you must first reach **Tier 3** in character progression. Players below Tier 3 can only purchase temporary horses for 200 Moola, which will eventually despawn.

### Step-by-Step Acquisition Guide

1.  **Reach Tier 3:** Complete enough quests or activities to level your character.
2.  **Locate the Horse NPC:** Head to **Ridge County**. The NPC is typically located near the train station or the fishing docks.
3.  **Interact and Reroll:** Talk to the NPC. If the price of the horse offered is low, click **"Show me another."** This costs **20 Moola** per reroll.
4.  **Check the Stats:** Look for a balance of high Speed and Stamina. While Jump and Damage are useful, mobility is the primary purpose of a horse in the current meta.
5.  **Finalize Purchase:** Once you find a horse in the 1,400–1,500 Moola range (ideally with Mythical brackets), buy it.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When you buy a new horse, you must wait approximately **916 seconds (15 minutes)** before you can view its detailed stat sheet. Plan your gameplay accordingly!

## Key Stats and Their Importance

Every horse generated by the NPC has randomized stats across six categories. Understanding what these do will help you decide whether to keep a reroll or try again.

*   **Speed:** Determines the maximum velocity of the horse. This is the most critical stat for outrunning other players.
*   **Stamina:** Affects how long you can hold the Shift key to sprint. Low stamina makes long-distance travel tedious.
*   **Jump:** Determines the height of the jump (Spacebar). Useful for clearing fences and navigating rocky terrain.
*   **Damage:** The amount of damage dealt when using the Horse Attack (F).
*   **Scale:** The physical size of the horse. Larger horses can be easier targets in gunfights.
*   **Turn:** How responsive the horse is when changing direction at high speeds.

## Advanced Horse Tech and Combat

A high-tier horse from our **horse tier list bridger western** is more than a taxi; it is a combat tool. Mastering these mechanics will give you an edge in Ridge County.

### The "Water Tech" Trick
Standard horses slow down significantly in deep water. To bypass this, **sprint (Shift) into the water and spam the Spacebar**. This causes the horse to "hop" across the surface, maintaining nearly 80% of its land speed. This is vital for crossing into the Swamp biome quickly.

### Horse Combat
By pressing **F**, your horse will perform a kick or strike. A high-damage horse can deal significant health loss to players on foot. Use this during reloads or when an enemy gets too close to your flank.

### Health and Aging
Horses are not invincible. If your horse dies, there is a **two-minute respawn timer** before you can summon it again using the **H** key. Furthermore, every time you or your horse dies, its "Age" increases. While mostly cosmetic (adding gray hair), extremely high age can eventually impact performance, requiring you to eventually look for a fresh mount.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute fastest horse in the horse tier list bridger western?**

A: There is no single "named" horse that is the fastest. Speed is tied to the price and rarity. A Mythical horse purchased for 1,500 Moola with a speed stat exceeding 1.30 is currently the fastest possible mount in the game.

**Q: Does it cost Moola to summon my horse?**

A: No, once you have purchased a permanent horse at Tier 3, summoning it with the **H** key is free. However, if the horse dies, you must wait for the cooldown to expire.

**Q: Can I have multiple horses?**

A: You can only have one active horse at a time. If you buy a new one, it will replace your current mount. Make sure you are certain about the stats before confirming the purchase.

**Q: How do I heal my horse after a gunfight?**

A: Horses naturally regenerate health over time when they are not taking damage. Simply dismount or ride to a safe area, and the health bar will slowly refill. Eating certain cooked fish can also provide temporary buffs to your character which may indirectly assist in horse management.
