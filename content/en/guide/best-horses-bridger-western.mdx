## Locating the Horse Seller and Rerolling Mechanics

Before you can secure a top-tier mount, you must know where to look. The primary horse merchant is located near the coastal region of the map, specifically by the fishing docks and the tackle store. If you are starting from the main town, head toward the water until you see the wooden piers and the smell of salt air.

Once you find the Horse Seller, you are presented with a "Gacha" style system. You have two primary options: "I'll take it" or "Show me another." 

*   **I'll Take It:** This finalizes the purchase of the horse currently being shown.
*   **Show me another:** This acts as a reroll. You will spend a small fee to cycle to a new horse with randomized stats and appearances.

> **💡 Expert Tip:** Do not settle for the first horse you see. Experienced players often spend thousands of dollars rerolling until they find a horse with a high price tag, as price is the most reliable indicator of high base stats.

## Legendary and Mythical Rarities

The most sought-after mounts in the game are those with Legendary or Mythical designations. These are not just cosmetic titles; they represent horses that have broken the standard "stat ceiling." In the game's code, a standard horse is capped at a 1.25 multiplier for its stats. Legendary and Mythical horses can exceed this cap, reaching values like 1.35 or higher.

### How to Identify Rarity
You can identify these rare horses by looking at their names in the seller menu. The game uses a specific text formatting system:

1.  **ALL CAPS NAMES:** If the horse's name is in all capital letters (e.g., "OLD MOON" or "LASCO"), it is either Legendary or Mythical.
2.  **Skinny Brackets `[NAME]`:** This indicates a **Legendary** horse. A Legendary horse has exactly one stat that exceeds the 1.25 maximum.
3.  **Thick Brackets `[[NAME]]`:** This indicates a **Mythical** horse. A Mythical horse has two or more stats that exceed the 1.25 maximum.

> **⚠️ Warning:** A Legendary or Mythical tag does not automatically mean the horse is "good." A Legendary horse might have a boosted Jump stat but terrible Speed. Always check the price and the individual stat bars before committing your currency.

## The 15-Minute Stat Reveal Rule

One of the most common points of confusion for new players is the inability to see their horse's exact numerical stats immediately after purchase. When you buy a new mount, the stats are "hidden" behind a cooldown period.

To see the exact numbers (such as a Speed of 91 or 97), you must wait approximately **916 seconds**, which translates to roughly **15 minutes** of real-time play. During this time, you can ride the horse and get a "feel" for its performance, but the technical breakdown will remain obscured. This mechanic prevents players from rapidly buying and discarding horses in seconds without actually engaging with the mount.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a normal horse be faster than a Legendary horse?**

A: Yes. Because Legendary status only boosts one stat, a normal horse with a very high base Speed roll can be faster than a Legendary horse that has a boosted Jump or Damage stat. Always look at the price and the speed bar specifically.

**Q: What is the highest speed a horse can reach in Bridger: Western?**

A: While stats are randomized, top-tier Mythical horses with a focus on speed have been seen reaching values in the high 90s (97-99). Most "good" horses will sit between 88 and 92.

**Q: Do horse colors affect their stats?**

A: Generally, no. Colors are cosmetic. However, Mythical and Legendary horses often have unique skins or colors that are not available to common horses, making them easily identifiable in a crowd.

**Q: What are the best horses bridger western players should aim for in the early game?**

A: In the early game, don't worry about Mythicals. Aim for any horse priced between $800 and $1,000. This will give you enough speed to complete missions efficiently without draining your bank account on rerolls.
