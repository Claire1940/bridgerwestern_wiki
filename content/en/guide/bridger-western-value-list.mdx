The Stand hierarchy is divided into six distinct tiers. While combat effectiveness often dictates value, rarity and demand play equally significant roles. ### Tier 1: The God Tiers
White Snake remains the undisputed king of the **bridger western value list**. It is widely considered priceless because it possesses the perfect trifecta: it is the rarest Stand in the game, one of the most powerful in combat, and has the highest aesthetic demand. Tusk follows closely behind; while it is incredibly strong, its value is driven primarily by how difficult it is to obtain through the questline and corpse parts.

### Tier 2: The Competitive Elites
This tier features the heavy hitters like Star Platinum and The World. Interestingly, D4C resides here despite having lower combat utility than its peers. Its value is propped up by its rarity. The World Over Heaven (TWOH) is a staple of this tier, often traded evenly with Star Platinum depending on the specific needs of the players involved.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always verify the "Age" of a Stand before finalizing a trade. Trading often equalizes age, which can affect certain stat distributions depending on your current build.

## Essential Items and Resource Values

Items are the currency of the **bridger western value list**. While Stands are the "big ticket" items, resources like Arrows, Rokas, and Dog Vein Herbs are what allow the economy to function. In 2026, the value of a standard Arrow is actually higher than most Tier 5 Stands because the *potential* of an Arrow to roll a Tier 1-3 Stand is worth more than the guaranteed mediocrity of a low-tier Stand.

| Item | Primary Use | Value Equivalent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Arrow** | Rolling new Stands | Higher than Tier 5 Stands |

| **Dog Vein Herb** | Removing Tier 6 Stands | Low (Common) |
| **Golden Base** | Collection/Selling | High Rarity / Low Utility |
| **Corpse Parts** | Evolving Stands | High (Tier 2-3 Range) |

The Golden Base is a unique case in the **bridger western value list**. While it is technically one of the rarest items to find, its actual utility is quite low. It serves mostly as a collector's item. Most veteran traders will not trade a high-value Stand for a Golden Base unless they are specifically looking to complete a collection.

## How to Trade and Farm Resources

To participate in the economy, you must understand the mechanics of the 2026 trading update. You cannot simply trade from the moment you join the game; there are specific requirements and locations you must visit to begin your journey as a merchant.

1.  **Reach Tier 3:** You must be at least Tier 3 to initiate or accept trades involving Stands.
2.  **Locate the Lumber NPC:** Visit the house in the forest to purchase an Axe.
3.  **Farm Rokas and Herbs:** Use the Axe on trees. Forest trees have a higher chance of dropping Rokas, while Swamp trees can drop both Rokas and Dog Vein Herbs. Note that Swamp trees take significantly longer to chop down.
4.  **Initiate the Trade:** Equipping a Roka and clicking on another player will send them a trade request notification.

### The Skin Market
A major factor in the **bridger western value list** is the introduction of Stand skins. Almost every Stand in the game (with the notable exceptions of Golden Experience Requiem and Tusk) can possess a skin. When you trade a Stand that does not currently have a skin, there is a 1% chance that the act of trading will trigger a skin unlock for that Stand. This "gambling" aspect adds a layer of speculative value to unskinned high-tier Stands.

> **💡 Tip:** If you have a Tier 6 Stand, do not bother trying to trade it. They are considered "useless" and actually cost you a Dog Vein Herb just to remove them from your slot. It is better to re-roll them immediately.

## Specialized Stand Services: The Crazy Diamond Exception

Crazy Diamond occupies a very strange spot on the **bridger western value list**. While its combat stats might place it in Tier 4 or 5, it possesses a unique ability: the power to change player faces in-game. 

This has created a "service economy" where Crazy Diamond users are paid in items or lower-tier Stands to provide cosmetic customization for other players. If you can provide a specific, highly-desired face to a player, the value of that single transaction can far exceed the base value of the Stand itself. This makes Crazy Diamond one of the best "passive income" Stands for players who enjoy the social and roleplay aspects of the game.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most valuable Stand in the bridger western value list?**

A: Currently, White Snake is considered the most valuable due to its extreme rarity and high performance in both PvP and PvE scenarios. It is often traded for multiple high-tier items or multiple Tier 2 Stands.

**Q: Why is the Arrow valued higher than some Stands?**

A: An Arrow represents potential. Since the **bridger western value list** ranks several Stands as "useless" (Tier 6), players would rather have an Arrow to try for a Tier 1-3 Stand than be stuck with a Tier 5 Stand that has no trading demand.

**Q: How do I get a skin for my Stand?**

A: Skins can be obtained during the initial roll or through a 1% chance when trading a Stand that doesn't already have one. Note that Tusk and GER currently do not have skins available in the 2026 update.

**Q: Can I trade if I am below Tier 3?**

A: No, the trading system requires players to be at least Tier 3 to ensure they have a basic understanding of the game and to prevent "alt-account" farming from flooding the market with low-value items.
