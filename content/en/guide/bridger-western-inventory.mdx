### Acquiring a Better Horse

Initially, you'll have a basic horse. To truly maximize your inventory and travel efficiency, investing in a better horse is a top priority. Horse merchants can be found in various towns, offering a range of steeds with different stats.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn Money">
    Engage in activities like fishing, hunting, or completing quests to accumulate sufficient funds. A good horse can cost anywhere from 1,000 to 1,500 currency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate a Horse Merchant">
    From the fishing dock, head straight behind it to find a town where a horse merchant usually resides.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Browse Horses">
    Talk to the merchant. They will offer you a horse with specific stats (e.g., "Dusty Ghost: Not the fastest, not the slowest").
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reroll for Legendary Horses">
    If the offered horse isn't ideal, you can "spam reroll" by selecting "show me another" to cycle through available horses. Look for horses priced between 1,000 and 1,500; these are often legendary and offer superior speed and stats, significantly boosting your effective **Bridger Western inventory**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Legendary Horse Advantage">
A legendary horse (costing 1,000-1,500) provides a significant boost to speed and overall performance, making resource runs and escapes much more efficient. This indirectly enhances your inventory capabilities by allowing you to transport more goods faster and safer.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Strategic Inventory Management Through Activities">

Your **Bridger Western inventory** isn't just about what you carry, but also how you acquire and manage resources. Engaging in activities like fishing and hunting provides not only valuable items but also a reliable source of income to fund inventory upgrades.

### Fishing for Profit and Rare Finds

Fishing is an excellent early-game activity to earn money and potentially find rare items that can further enhance your inventory or capabilities.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Get Fishing Gear">
    Head to the dock from Red Corner. Purchase a fishing rod and at least one bait from the merchant inside the building.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Find a Quiet Spot">
    Move away from crowded areas at the dock to avoid player conflicts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Start Fishing">
    Cast your line. Watch for bubbles forming on your rod; once they appear, click to reel in your catch. You can catch various fish, and even rare items like chests or a Stand Arrow (0.5% chance).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Sell Your Catch">
    Return to the merchant at the dock and sell your fish. This is a consistent way to earn money for horse upgrades, weapons, and other inventory essentials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
While fishing primarily yields fish for selling, it's also a rare source for a Stand Arrow Shard (0.5% chance). This can be a game-changer, offering a unique way to gain powerful abilities.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Inventory: Corpse Parts and Stand Arrows">

Beyond basic items, your **Bridger Western inventory** can also include extremely rare and powerful items like Corpse Parts and Stand Arrows. These items are highly sought after and drastically impact gameplay, but acquiring them requires specific strategies and careful inventory consideration.

### The Hunt for Corpse Parts

Corpse Parts are unique items that periodically spawn across the map, marked by a huge golden beam. Obtaining one can grant you a powerful "Stand" ability.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor for Spawns">
    Look for a gold message on your screen stating "it appears once again." This signifies a Corpse Part has spawned.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Race to the Location">
    Head towards the golden beam. Be prepared for intense player-versus-player combat, as the entire server will likely converge on the location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secure the Part">
    Once you pick up a Corpse Part, a pulse will emanate from you, giving you a 15-20% chance per pulse to obtain a Stand. This can take several minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defend Yourself">
    Expect to be targeted by other players trying to take the Corpse Part from you. Your inventory management skills, especially in combat, will be tested here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Server Size    | Chance per 30s | Player Density | Risk Level |
|----------------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Low (3 players)| 1 out of 150   | Low            | Minimal    |
| Medium (10 players)| 1 out of 115   | Moderate       | Medium     |
| High (25 players)| 1 out of 40    | High           | Extreme    |

<Callout type="tip">
If you already have a Stand, you cannot pick up a Corpse Part. You must first reset your current Stand, either by wiping your character or by obtaining a specific plant through fishing and giving it to the Witch.
</Callout>

### Stand Arrows: An Alternative Path

Another way to acquire a Stand is through a Stand Arrow, which has a 0.5% chance of dropping as a legendary result from fishing. While extremely rare, it offers a safer, albeit slower, alternative to the chaotic Corpse Part hunt.
