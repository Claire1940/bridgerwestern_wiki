## Essential Controls and Movement Mechanics

Before you can engage in high-speed horseback chases or Stand battles, you must master the basic controls. Bridger Western utilizes a responsive movement system that rewards players who use the environment to their advantage. 

| Action | Control Key | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Sprint** | Double-Tap W | Increases movement speed and allows for automatic vaulting over obstacles. |
| **Dodge Roll** | Q | Provides a brief window of mobility to avoid incoming gunfire. |
| **Reload** | R | Essential for keeping your firearms ready during a skirmish. |
| **Melee** | G | Perform a physical strike with your weapon equipped. |
| **Ocular Pro** | X | Activates a specialized vision mode that highlights enemies and boosts stats. |
| **Summon Horse** | H | Calls your mount to your current location. |
| **Horse Stop** | F | Forces an immediate full stop while sprinting on horseback. |
| **Dismount** | N | Quickly exit your horse to engage in ground combat. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your stamina while sprinting. If you find yourself in a gunfight, use the dodge roll (Q) to reposition behind cover rather than running in a straight line.

## Starting Your Journey: The Fishing Economy

For most players, the first major destination should be the Fishing Village. While Bridger Western features intense PvP and world events, fishing remains the most reliable and "chill" way to level up and earn Moola (the game's primary currency) in 2026. 

To get started, follow the train tracks to Reach B County (also known as Ridge County). Hop on the train and dismount when you reach the forest biome or the specialized fishing area. Locate the **Taco Bait Shop** and speak to the NPC to purchase your starting gear.

### Fishing Gear and Rewards

| Item | Purpose | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Fishing Rod** | Primary tool for catching fish. | Essential for early-game progression. |
| **Bait** | Increases catch rate. | Always keep a stack of bait to maximize your time at the water. |
| **Fish (Various)** | Sold for Moola. | Higher-tier fish like Cod can sell for over 1.5k Moola. |
| **Stand Arrow** | Unlocks Stands. | Has a rare 0.5% drop rate from fishing. |

Selling your catch is the fastest way to build a bankroll. It is not uncommon for a dedicated session to net upwards of 5,000 Moola, which can then be used to purchase high-tier weaponry and better horses.

## Combat Mastery and Ocular Pro

Combat in Bridger Western is fast-paced and lethal. While standard gunplay relies on your aim, the **Ocular Pro** system (activated with X) is what separates veterans from novices. When activated, Ocular Pro highlights players in the environment, increases your reload speed, and significantly boosts your overall accuracy.

In 2026, the meta revolves around combining Ocular Pro with specific "Cards" obtained from the Witch's Hut. Certain card combinations can grant what players refer to as "legal aimbot," where your bullets will track targets even if your reticle isn't perfectly centered.

### Weaponry Overview
You can purchase firearms at the gun shop in Ridge County or the Outlaw Hideout. 

*   **Pistols:** Reliable sidearms for close encounters.
*   **Winchester/Maverick:** Mid-to-long-range rifles with high stopping power.
*   **Tommy Gun:** High rate of fire, excellent for suppressing enemies.
*   **Steel Wires & Throwables:** Dynamite, Molotovs, and Smoke Bombs can be used to flush out campers.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Age is a factor in Bridger Western. Every time you die, your character ages. If you age too much without using Dog's Bane, you may face stat penalties or character expiration.

## The Card System and the Witch’s Hut

To truly maximize your potential, you must unlock your Passive Cards. These are not visible by default; you must "tier up" your character by gaining experience (fishing or PvP) and then visit the Witch's Hut located in the Swamp area.

There are three available slots for cards, which correspond to your Tier level (Max Tier 3). 

1.  **Travel to the Swamp:** Locate the isolated hut hidden in the marshlands.
2.  **Read Your Fortune:** The first card unlock is free, but subsequent rerolls or unlocks cost 50 Moola each.
3.  **Slot Your Cards:** You can equip cards like "High Noon," "Evil Eye," or "Secret Technique" to customize your build.

If you are unhappy with a card, you can choose to "Reject this Fate" and reroll for a different passive. Combining cards like **High Noon** and **Evil Eye** is the standard path for players looking to dominate in competitive shootouts.

## Horse Training and Water Tech

A horse is more than just transportation; it is your lifeline. While every player starts with a basic mule, you should aim to upgrade as soon as you have 1,100 Moola. Visit the NPC in front of the Ridge County train station to purchase a new mount.

**Pro Tip for Horse Selection:** Do not settle for the first horse offered if it is cheap. Reroll the selection until you see a horse costing 1,000+ Moola. These higher-priced horses have significantly better base speed and stamina stats.

### The Water Travel Tech
One of the most useful tricks in the current version of the game is the water travel technique. By holding **Shift** to sprint and then holding **Spacebar** while entering water, your horse can traverse deep rivers and swamps at high speeds. This allows you to bypass difficult terrain and escape pursuers who are forced to swim slowly.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get a Stand in Bridger Western?**

A: There are two primary ways to obtain a Stand. You can catch a Stand Arrow while fishing (0.5% chance), or you can find Corpse Parts that spawn throughout the map every 30 seconds. Corpse Parts are faster but highly contested by other players.

**Q: What is the maximum level/tier I can reach?**

A: Currently, the maximum level is Tier 3. Reaching this tier allows you to equip a full set of three Passive Cards from the Witch's Hut.

**Q: Where can I find more ammo for my guns?**

A: Ammo packs can be purchased at any general store, gun shop, or found at bandit outposts. You simply left-click the ammo pack in your inventory to replenish your reserves.

**Q: Does alignment (Outlaw vs. In-law) affect gameplay?**

A: As of the latest 2026 update, alignment is primarily for roleplay and does not have significant mechanical impacts on your stats or abilities, though this may change in future expansions of the **wiki bridge western** lore.
