## Locating the Horse Seller

Before you can start rolling for a legendary or mythical mount, you need to know where the business happens. The horse seller is not located in the main town square, which often confuses new players. Instead, you must head toward the coastal region.

Start your journey at the fishing docks, specifically near the tackle bait store. This is a central landmark that most players use for early-game progression. From the tackle shop, follow the path leading inland toward the stables. You will find an NPC standing near a fenced-in area. This individual is the gateway to your new mount. 

When interacting with the horse seller, you are presented with two primary options:
1.  **"I'll take it"**: This finalizes the purchase of the currently displayed horse.
2.  **"Show me another"**: This acts as a "reroll" mechanic. You pay a small fee to see a different horse with randomized stats, appearance, and rarity.

> **💡 Tip:** Never settle for the first horse you see. Rerolling is the only consistent way to find horses that exceed standard stat caps.

## Understanding Horse Rarities: Legendary vs. Mythical

The most common point of confusion in Bridger: Western is the difference between a "good" horse and a "Legendary" or "Mythical" horse. Rarity in this game is tied directly to stat breakthroughs. Most horses have a hard cap on their individual statistics, but rare variants break these rules.

### Visual Indicators
You can identify a high-rarity horse by looking at its name in the UI. The game uses specific bracket and capitalization styles to denote rarity:

| Rarity | Naming Convention | Bracket Type | Stat Breakthroughs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common/Standard** | Mixed Case (e.g., Spirit) | None | 0 (Caps at 1.25) |
| **Legendary** | ALL CAPS (e.g., OLD MOON) | Single Brackets `[ ]` | 1 Stat exceeds 1.25 |
| **Mythical** | ALL CAPS (e.g., LASCO) | Thick/Double Brackets `[[ ]]` | 2 Stats exceed 1.25 |

As shown in the table above, a **mythical horses bridger western** variant is significantly more powerful because it allows two different attributes—such as speed and stamina—to climb as high as 1.35 or beyond.

## The Stat Breakthrough Mechanic

In Bridger: Western, the standard maximum for any horse stat (Speed, Stamina, Jump, Damage) is **1.25**. For 90% of the horses you encounter, no matter how much they cost, they will never perform better than this baseline. 

However, Legendary and Mythical horses are the exception. A Legendary horse receives a "boost" to one random stat, allowing it to reach approximately **1.35**. A Mythical horse receives this boost to two separate stats.

> **⚠️ Warning:** A Legendary or Mythical horse can still be "bad" if the boost lands on an unimportant stat. For example, a Legendary horse with a 1.35 Jump stat but very low Speed is often less useful than a high-tier Standard horse with balanced stats.

### Priority Stats to Watch
When hunting for **mythical horses bridger western**, you should prioritize stats in the following order:
1.  **Speed**: Determines your maximum velocity. Essential for outrunning other players.
2.  **Stamina**: Determines how long you can maintain a full gallop.
3.  **Jump**: Useful for navigating rocky terrain and shortcuts.
4.  **Damage**: Relevant if you use your horse as a weapon or in specific combat scenarios.

## The Price Formula and Economy

The price of a horse in Bridger: Western is not random; it is a direct reflection of the sum of its total stats. If the horse seller offers you a horse for $1,500, that horse has a very high total stat pool. If the horse is $400, its stats are likely mediocre.

| Price Range | Quality Tier | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$150 - $500** | Low / Beginner | Skip unless you are just starting. |
| **$500 - $900** | Mid-Tier | Good for general travel and utility. |
| **$1,000 - $1,400** | High-Tier | Potential for near-max standard stats. |
| **$1,500+** | Elite / Top of the Line | Always check the stats; these are often the best in the game. |

It is important to note that Legendary and Mythical status is independent of the price. You could find a Legendary horse for $600 because its other stats are abysmal, or a Standard horse for $1,400 because all its stats are exactly 1.25. Always look for the brackets in the name before looking at the price tag.

## How to Roll for Mythical Horses

Rolling for a **mythical horses bridger western** mount requires patience and a significant amount of in-game currency. Follow this optimized strategy to maximize your chances:

1.  **Accumulate Wealth**: Do not start "spinning" for a mythical horse until you have at least $5,000 to $10,000. Each reroll adds up quickly.
2.  **Check the Name First**: When the NPC shows you a horse, look at the name immediately. If it isn't in ALL CAPS with `[[ ]]` brackets, it isn't mythical.
3.  **Evaluate the Price**: If you see a Mythical horse, check the price. A Mythical horse with a price of $1,500 is likely a "god-roll" mount with massive speed and stamina.
4.  **Confirm the Stats**: Once purchased, you cannot immediately see the numerical values of the stats in your menu.

### The 15-Minute Rule
After purchasing a horse, there is a hidden cooldown before you can view its detailed stat sheet. You must wait exactly **916 seconds** (approximately 15 minutes) of active playtime while owning the horse before the "Inspect" or "Stats" menu fully populates with the 1.25+ data. This prevents players from rapidly buying and discarding horses without experiencing the mount.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a standard horse ever be faster than a mythical horse?**

A: Yes. If a **mythical horses bridger western** mount has its stat boosts in Jump and Damage, but its Speed is rolled very low (e.g., 0.80), a Standard horse with a maxed 1.25 Speed will be faster. Always look at the total stat distribution, not just the rarity.

**Q: How do I know if I have a "God Roll" horse?**

A: A "God Roll" is typically a Mythical horse (double brackets) where the two boosted stats are Speed and Stamina, and both are above 1.30, while the price was over $1,500.

**Q: Does the horse seller ever run out of horses?**

A: No, the horse seller has an infinite supply. The "Show me another" option will continue to generate randomized horses as long as you have the currency to pay for the reroll.

**Q: What is the best way to farm money for mythical horses bridger western?**

A: Most players recommend fishing or participating in "flooding" servers (low-player count servers) where you can safely gather high-value loot and corpse parts to sell for quick cash.
