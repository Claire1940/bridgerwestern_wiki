## Optimizing Detection Settings for Maximum Efficiency

The heart of a successful **fishing macro bridger western** session lies in the detection settings. The macro offers several ways to "see" the game, including Pixel, NumPy, and OpenCV. While Pixel detection is fast, the **NumPy** method is widely considered the most stable for long-term AFK sessions in 2026.

### Detection Parameters
To ensure the macro never misses a catch, you should adjust the following settings in the configuration tab:

1.  **Detection Method:** Set this to NumPy for better stability across different lighting conditions in-game.
2.  **Scan Delay:** A setting of `0.05` is recommended. This creates a tiny buffer between scans, preventing your CPU from being overloaded while remaining fast enough to hit every letter.
3.  **Pixel Tolerance:** This determines how much the color can vary. If you are fishing during the day-night cycle in Bridger Western, a slightly higher tolerance helps account for shadows.
4.  **Blank Scans to Exit:** Set this to `5`. This tells the macro to wait for five empty scans before concluding that the mini-game has ended and it's time to recast.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Random Delay** | Enabled | Mimics human behavior to reduce detection risk. |
| **Audio Monitor** | On (for debugging) | Helps you see if the macro is "hearing" the fish bite. |
| **Output Device** | Default System Audio | Must match the device your game sounds are playing through. |

## Safety and Risk Management

It is important to acknowledge that using a **fishing macro bridger western** is technically against the game's terms of service. The developers of Bridger Western have the right to ban accounts that are caught using third-party automation software. However, as of 2026, many users report that the developers lack an automated server-side detection system for macros that mimic mouse and keyboard inputs.

To stay safe, follow these best practices:
*   **Don't overstay:** Avoid fishing for 24 hours straight. Take breaks to appear more human.
*   **Pick secluded spots:** Don't fish in the middle of a crowded town. Find a quiet waterfall or a hidden riverbank.
*   **Use Random Delays:** Always enable the "Randomize" settings in the macro to ensure your click patterns aren't identical every time.
*   **Monitor your Webhooks:** If you see people talking to you in-game via the Discord screenshots, stop the macro and reply to avoid being reported for botting.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

If your macro is not clicking the letters or failing to recast, the most common culprit is the **Backend Management**. If the status shows "Offline" or "Connecting," you may need to click the "Kill and Restart" button within the macro interface. This refreshes the connection between the script and the game window.

Another frequent issue is the audio detection. The macro "listens" for the splashing sound when a fish bites. If your game volume is too low or if your output device is set incorrectly in the macro settings, it will never trigger the mini-game. Always max out your in-game volume and ensure the macro's "Audio Monitor" shows activity when sounds occur.
