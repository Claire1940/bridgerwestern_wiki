## The Primary Methods of Stand Acquisition

In the current 2026 meta, there are two primary ways to obtain a Stand. The most common—and most dangerous—method involves the **Corpse Part** world event. The second method is through **Fishing**, which offers a safer but significantly lower-probability path to power.

| Method | Item Required | Difficulty | Success Rate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **World Event** | Corpse Part | **High (PvP)** | 15% - 20% per pulse |
| **Fishing** | Stand Arrow Shard | **Low (Time-Intensive)** | 0.5% catch rate |

### 1. The Corpse Part Event
The most direct way to get a Stand is to wait for the "Corpse Part" event. When this happens, a golden message will appear at the top of your screen stating: *"It appears once again."* Simultaneously, a massive golden beam of light will shoot into the sky, visible from almost anywhere on the map unless obstructed by large mountains.

To secure the Stand, you must reach the beam and pick up the Corpse Part. However, picking it up is only the beginning. Once you hold the item, your character will emit a "pulse" every few seconds. Each pulse has a roughly 15% to 20% chance to grant you a Stand. You typically need to hold the part for one to two minutes to successfully trigger the acquisition.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Picking up a Corpse Part makes you a global target. Your location will flash on the map for all players, and seasoned veterans will likely hunt you down to steal the part for themselves.

### 2. Fishing for Arrow Shards
If you prefer a quieter life or want to avoid the chaotic PvP of the golden beam, you can fish for a **Stand Arrow Shard**. This is considered a "Legendary" result from the fishing loot pool.

1.  Travel to the **Red Corner** or any major dock.
2.  Purchase a fishing rod and at least one bait (bait lasts until you die or leave).
3.  Cast your line and wait for bubbles to appear near your bobber.
4.  Complete the quick-time event (QTE) to reel in your catch.
5.  There is a **0.5% chance** that the item caught will be a Stand Arrow Shard.

The frequency of Corpse Part spawns is heavily dependent on the number of players in your current server. If you are hunting for a Stand, choosing the right server size is a strategic necessity.

| Server Population | Spawn Chance (Every 30 Seconds) | Estimated Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **10 - 15 Players** | 1 out of 115 | Moderate PvP, Moderate Spawn Rate |

While maximum capacity servers offer the best mathematical chance for a spawn, the competition is fierce. Most expert players recommend a mid-sized server (around 10-12 players) to balance spawn frequency with a manageable level of risk.

## How to Prepare for Your Stand Hunt

Before you worry about **bridger western how to summon stand**, you need to ensure your character is mobile and funded. A character on foot is an easy target during a world event.

### Step 1: Secure a Reliable Horse
You cannot chase golden beams effectively without a horse. Travel to the stables near the starter towns. Horses cost between **150 and 1,500 mula**. 
*   **Budget Option:** A basic horse for 150-300 mula will get you around.
*   **Legendary Option:** If you see a horse priced at 1,000+ mula, buy it immediately. These horses have superior speed and health stats (up to 1.25 scaling).

### Step 2: Gain XP and Talents
Before summoning a Stand, visit the **Witch's Hut** located in the swamp. For 150 mula, she can "read your fortune" and awaken your talents. These talents provide passive buffs, such as increased gun damage while on horseback, which can help you defend yourself while holding a Corpse Part.

> **💡 Tip:** To reach the swamp quickly, ride your horse into the water and spam the spacebar to "jump-swim" across. This is much faster than walking the long way around.

## Resetting and Changing Your Stand

If you are unhappy with the Stand you received, or if you want to try a different build, you cannot simply "delete" it. You have two main options in 2026:

1.  **Character Wipe:** You can choose to wipe your character entirely from the main menu. This resets your money, horse, and Stand, allowing you to start fresh.
2.  **The Witch's Reset:** You can find a specific rare plant through fishing. If you bring this plant to the Witch in the swamp, she can remove your current Stand without resetting your entire character progress.

Note that if you already have a Stand, you **cannot** pick up another Corpse Part. The game prevents "stacking" Stands to maintain balance. You must be "Stand-less" to interact with the golden beam or use an Arrow Shard.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I summon a Stand if I am playing as a Lawman?**

A: Yes. Both Outlaws and Lawmen have equal access to Stands. However, Outlaws often find it easier to navigate to the swamp and fishing docks without being harassed by NPC guards.

**Q: What happens if I die while holding a Corpse Part?**

A: If you die, you drop the Corpse Part immediately. Any nearby player can pick it up and continue the "pulse" timer for themselves. This is why it is vital to have a fast horse ready for a quick escape.

**Q: Is there a way to increase the 0.5% fishing chance for a Stand Arrow?**

A: Currently, there are no items that explicitly increase the rarity of fishing drops. The best strategy for **bridger western how to summon stand** via fishing is to find a safe, secluded spot and use a macro or dedicated clicking rhythm to maximize catches per hour.

**Q: Do Stands have different rarities?**

A: Yes, some Stands have a lower weight in the random roll than others. While the game doesn't explicitly list percentages for each specific Stand, community testing suggests that "evolved" forms or high-tier Stands are significantly rarer than basic ones.
