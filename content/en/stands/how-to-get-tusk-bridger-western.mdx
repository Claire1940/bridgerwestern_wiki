## The Left Arm Requirement: Starting Your Journey

The path to obtaining Tusk is strictly tied to the Saint's Corpse mechanics. Unlike other Stands that might accept various body parts, Tusk is incredibly selective. You cannot use a Rib Cage, a Right Arm, or a Leg to initiate this Stand.

To begin, you must acquire the **Saint's Left Arm**. This item is a rare drop found within the game world or through specific community events. Once you have the Left Arm in your inventory, using it does not guarantee Tusk immediately. There is a **10% chance** that using the Left Arm will grant you Tusk Act 1. If the roll fails, you may receive a different Stand or no Stand at all, meaning you will need to farm for another arm.

> **Warning:** Obtaining Tusk will permanently change your character's movement. You will lose the ability to walk or run, replaced by a crawling mechanic. Ensure you have a high-tier horse before committing to this Stand.

## Understanding the "Crippled" Passive

The moment you successfully learn **how to get tusk bridger western**, your character's physical state changes to reflect the lore of Johnny Joestar. This brings several unique passives that can be jarring for new players.

1.  **Mobility Restrictions:** You can no longer walk or run. Your primary form of movement on foot is crawling or rolling. While rolling provides a brief burst of speed, you are extremely vulnerable to faster opponents.
2.  **Weapon Restriction:** The game will automatically delete or disable your ability to use traditional firearms, sabers, and melee weapons. Tusk is your only tool for offense. If you visit a gun shop, the NPC will refuse to sell to you, stating you lack the "capability" to hold their weapons.
3.  **Horse Dependency:** Because your ground movement is so limited, your horse becomes your lifeline. It is highly recommended to secure a **Legendary or Mythical horse** to maintain distance from enemies and navigate the map effectively.

## Combat Strategy and Resource Management

Playing with Tusk requires monitoring two specific UI elements: the **Pink Bar** (Bullets) and the **Blue Bar** (Stamina). 

*   **Ammo Management:** Every time you fire a nail, your pink bar decreases. If you deplete this, you must wait for your nails to "regrow" before you can attack again. Act 1 depletes this bar the fastest due to its high fire rate.
*   **Stamina Usage:** Moving via Nail Glide or rolling consumes your blue stamina bar. Because you cannot walk, managing this bar is a matter of life and death. If you run out of stamina while an enemy is closing in, you will be unable to escape their melee range.

> **Tip:** Use the **T key** for a quick melee strike if an enemy gets too close. While not your primary source of damage, it can provide the knockback needed to mount your horse and reset the engagement.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I heal my legs after getting Tusk in Bridger: Western?**

A: No, the "crippled" state is a permanent trade-off for having the Tusk Stand. You cannot use items to restore normal walking or running animations while Tusk is equipped.

**Q: What happens if I use a different corpse part while I have Tusk?**

A: Using a different corpse part (like a Rib Cage) while you already have a Stand will usually result in an error or prompt you to replace your current Stand. If you want to keep Tusk, do not use other corpse parts.

**Q: Is Tusk Act 4 in the game yet?**

A: As of the April 2026 update, Tusk Act 4 is not yet implemented in the game. Players are currently limited to evolving between Acts 1, 2, and 3 using the V key.

**Q: How do I increase my accuracy when learning how to get tusk bridger western?**

A: While in your firing form (E), you can press the **X key** to enter a focused state which significantly reduces the spread of your nails, making it much easier to land headshots for maximum damage.
