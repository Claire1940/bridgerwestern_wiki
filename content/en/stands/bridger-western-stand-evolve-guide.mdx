## How to Obtain Your First Stand

In Bridger: Western, Stands are not simply handed to you. There are two primary methods to obtain a Stand: fishing for an Arrow Shard or hunting for the Saint's Corpse.

### The Fishing Method
Fishing is the safest way for new players to progress. By purchasing a rod and bait at the docks, you can grind for XP and money. However, there is a **0.5% chance** to pull up an **Arrow Shard**. This legendary item allows you to obtain a Stand without engaging in the high-risk "golden beam" chase.

### The Corpse Part Method
The most common way to get a Stand is by finding a Corpse Part. When the message "It appears once again" flashes in gold at the top of your screen, a golden beam will spawn somewhere on the map. 

| Server Population | Spawn Chance (Every 30s) | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| **3 Players** | 1 out of 150 | Low |
| **10 Players** | 1 out of 115 | Medium |
| **25 Players** | **1 out of 40** | **High** |

Once you pick up a Corpse Part, a pulse will emit from your character. Each pulse has a **15% to 20% chance** of granting you a Stand. Be warned: the entire server can see the beam and will likely hunt you down for the part.

## Bridger Western Stand Evolve Guide: The Tusk Path

Tusk is one of the most iconic and complex Stands in the game. Unlike other Stands, Tusk requires a specific Corpse Part and forces a massive change in your playstyle.

### Obtaining Tusk Act 1
To get Tusk, you must find the **Saint’s Left Arm**. Using any other part (like the Rib Cage or Right Arm) will result in a different Stand. There is a **10% chance** to obtain Tusk when using the Left Arm. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Obtaining Tusk will permanently cripple your character. You will no longer be able to walk or run; you will be forced to crawl or roll. Additionally, you cannot use traditional weapons like sabers or guns.

### Evolution through the Acts
To evolve Tusk, you must reach specific Tier levels and use the evolution key (default **V**). Each Act changes your utility and damage output significantly.

| Act | Primary Feature | Key Moves |
|---|---|---|
| **Act 1** | Mobility & Spam | **Nail Glide**, **Mini-jump**, Mini-gun fire (10-19 dmg). |
| **Act 2** | Precision Sniper | **Ricochet Shot** (62+ dmg), Homing Bullets. |
| **Act 3** | High DPS | **Wormhole Bullets** (Double-hit for 35+ dmg). |

## The Witch’s Hut and Talent Weaving

To maximize your Stand's potential, you must visit the Witch’s Hut located in the swamp area. To cross the water safely, sprint your horse into the water and spam the spacebar to hop across.

1. **Read Your Fortune:** Costs **150 mula** for the first time.
2. **Select Talents:** You can hold up to three talents (Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3).
3. **Reroll:** Subsequent fortunes cost **50 mula**.
4. **Weave Talents:** You can "weave" a new talent into an existing slot to replace it without wiping your entire build.

**Recommended Talents for Stand Users:**
*   **True Cowboy:** Increases gun damage (applies to nail shots for some Acts).
*   **Vitality Perks:** Essential for Tusk users who are vulnerable while crawling.

## Managing Age and Character Progression

As you play through 2026, your character will age. You can check your age by pressing **M**. 
*   **Death:** Increases age by 1-2 years.
*   **Combat Logging:** Increases age by **10 years** (Avoid this at all costs).
*   **Visual Changes:** As you grow older, your character will develop wrinkles and gray hair. 

While there is community debate about "dying of old age," the primary penalty for aging is the visual degradation and the risk of losing your progress upon a full wipe. If you need to reset your Stand without wiping your character, you must find a specific rare plant while fishing and bring it to the Witch.

## Advanced Strategy: The Legendary Horse

Since Tusk users are crippled, your horse becomes your legs. Do not settle for a basic "Dusty Ghost." You should save at least **1,000 to 1,500 mula** to purchase a Legendary or Mythical horse from the stables.

*   **Speed:** High-tier horses allow you to outrun Stand hunters.
*   **Durability:** Better horses can survive more hits before being despawned.
*   **Combat:** Use the horse's jump and kick (F) to keep enemies at bay while your nail shots recharge.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I evolve Tusk to Act 4 in Bridger: Western?**

A: As of the current 2026 update, Tusk Act 4 has not been fully implemented for all players or may require a specific world event. Most players currently focus on optimizing Act 2 for sniping or Act 3 for raw DPS.

**Q: Can I use a gun if I have a Stand?**

A: If you have Tusk, you cannot use any weapons. Your nails replace your firearms. However, other Stands obtained via the Arrow Shard may allow the use of traditional weapons alongside Stand abilities.

**Q: What happens if someone kills my horse while I'm playing Tusk?**

A: You will be extremely vulnerable. You will have to crawl or roll until your horse's cooldown (approximately 2 minutes) expires. It is highly recommended to travel with a teammate if you are using a crippled Stand build.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Corpse Parts?**

A: Corpse Parts spawn randomly, but they are always marked by a large golden beam. The best strategy is to stay in a server with roughly 10-15 players to balance the spawn rate (1/115) with a lower risk of being "jumped" by a massive group.
