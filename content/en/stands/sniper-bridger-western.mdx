## Understanding the Malcolm 70 Sniper Rifle

The Malcolm 70 is the definitive long-range tool in Bridger: WESTERN. Unlike many other shooters where snipers are hitscan or high-velocity, this rifle introduces a significant learning curve. The most striking feature of this weapon is its requirement for ADS (Aim Down Sights). You cannot fire the Malcolm 70 from the hip; the trigger simply will not engage unless you are looking through the long-range scope.

While the scope provides a significant zoom advantage, it also limits your peripheral vision, making you vulnerable to flankers. The trade-off for this vulnerability is high base damage that can effectively "laser" opponents who are caught out in the open. However, the weapon is often described as "broken" by the community—not because it is overpowered, but because its projectile speed is surprisingly low for a high-caliber rifle.

## Mastering Projectile Lead and Travel Time

The most common mistake new players make with the **sniper bridger western** kit is firing directly at an enemy's current position. Because the Malcolm 70 features the slowest projectile speed in the game, the bullet travels more like a heavy slug than a modern high-velocity round. If a target is running horizontally across your field of view, you may need to aim several character-widths ahead of them to score a hit.

This "slow-motion" bullet physics means that at extreme ranges, an attentive enemy can actually see the shot coming and attempt to dodge. To counter this, successful snipers often wait for "animation locks"—moments when an enemy is locked into a specific movement, such as fishing, reloading, or climbing.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to engage in close-quarters combat with the Malcolm 70. Since you cannot fire without zooming, a player with a revolver or shotgun will almost always win a point-blank duel.

## Horse-Mounted Sniping Tactics

One of the most effective ways to utilize the Malcolm 70 in 2026 is by combining it with the game's horse mechanics. By "stationing up" on your horse, you gain a mobile vantage point that allows for quick repositions if your location is compromised. Furthermore, certain character cards and rerolls in Bridger: WESTERN allow you to deal increased damage while mounted, or provide better stability while firing from horseback.

Using a horse also allows you to maintain a "hit and run" playstyle. You can fire a shot, immediately gallop to a new ridge, and reset your stealth. This is particularly effective in the desert biomes where cover is sparse, and height advantage is everything.

## Comparing the Sniper to Other Western Armaments

To truly appreciate the **sniper bridger western** playstyle, one must compare it to the more forgiving weapons in the game, such as the Repeaters or the various Revolvers. While the Repeater offers a faster fire rate and better mid-range utility, it lacks the raw intimidation factor of the Malcolm 70.

| Weapon Type | Range | Ease of Use | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Malcolm 70** | **Extreme** | Hard | Long-range harassment and assassinations. |
| **Repeater** | Mid-Long | Medium | General skirmishing and objective defense. |
| **Revolver** | Short-Mid | Easy | Self-defense and close-range duels. |
| **Shotgun** | Short | Easy | Clearing buildings and ambush play. |

The Malcolm 70 deals roughly 31 damage on a standard body shot, which is quite high compared to the rapid-fire but lower-damage pistols. If you can land a headshot, you are looking at a near-instant kill or, at the very least, leaving the enemy in a state where a single follow-up from a teammate will finish them.

## The Role of Cards and Rerolls

In Bridger: WESTERN, your build is often defined by the cards you draw or reroll for. For a sniper-centric build, you want to look for attributes that complement long-distance engagement. Cards that increase "Ocular Prowess" or visibility can help you spot enemies before they spot you. 

Some players prefer to reroll until they get cards that specifically buff damage while stationary or while mounted on a horse. This turns the Malcolm 70 from a difficult-to-use rifle into a terrifying "laser" that can deplete half an enemy's health bar in a single click.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself struggling with the projectile speed, look for cards that increase projectile velocity. Even a small percentage buff can make the Malcolm 70 feel significantly more responsive.

## Strategic Positioning in the Desert

The desert map is the primary playground for the **sniper bridger western** enthusiast. The wide-open vistas and lack of dense foliage mean that anyone moving without a horse is a sitting duck. However, staying in one spot for too long is a death sentence. 

1. **High Ground:** Always seek the ridges. Being above your target makes it easier to judge the distance for projectile drop.
2. **Sun Placement:** If possible, keep the sun at your back. While the game doesn't have a complex "glint" mechanic for all scopes, it makes you harder to spot against the bright sky.
3. **Third-Party Opportunities:** The best time to fire is when two other players are already fighting. They will be distracted and less likely to notice your bullet tracers.
4. **Basement/Indoor Safety:** If you are being hunted by a faster player, retreat to a basement or building where they have to funnel through a door, then swap to your secondary weapon.

## How to Counter a Malcolm 70 User

If you find yourself on the receiving end of a **sniper bridger western** assault, don't panic. Because the bullet travels so slowly, "serpentining" (moving in a zig-zag pattern) is highly effective. Most snipers will over-lead their shots, causing them to miss if you suddenly change direction.

Additionally, closing the distance is key. Once you get within the mid-range of a sniper, their scope becomes a liability. They cannot hip-fire, meaning they must spend time entering the zoom animation to shoot at you. Use this window to pepper them with repeater fire or close in for a shotgun blast.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I shoot my sniper rifle without zooming in?**

A: The Malcolm 70 in Bridger: WESTERN is designed specifically as a long-range tool. The game mechanics prevent hip-firing to ensure the weapon isn't used as a high-damage shotgun in close quarters. You must use the scope to engage the firing pin.

**Q: Is the Malcolm 70 the best weapon in the game?**

A: It is highly situational. In the hands of a master who can predict projectile travel, it is devastating. However, for most players, a Repeater is more consistent due to the faster bullet speed and better versatility at multiple ranges.

**Q: How much damage does the sniper do?**

A: Base damage typically ranges between 29 and 31 for body shots. Headshots deal significantly more, often enough to take down an opponent who isn't at full health or lacks defensive cards.

**Q: What is the best secondary weapon to pair with the sniper bridger western build?**

A: A fast-firing revolver or a sawed-off shotgun is recommended. Since the Malcolm 70 is useless at close range, you need a backup that can handle enemies who manage to rush your position.
