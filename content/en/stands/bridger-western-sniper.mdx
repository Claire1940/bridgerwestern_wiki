## The Malcolm 70: Weapon Profile and Stats

The Malcolm 70 is the definitive long-range option in the current meta. Unlike other firearms that allow for hip-fire or quick-draw snapshots, this rifle is strictly a precision instrument. One of its most defining—and often frustrating—characteristics is the requirement to use the scope. You cannot fire the weapon unless you are fully zoomed in, making it virtually useless in close-quarters "panic" situations.

| Attribute | Statistic | Performance Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon Type** | Sniper Rifle | Long-range specialist |
| **Optics** | Long Scope | Mandatory for firing |
| **Projectile Speed** | Slowest in Game | Requires significant leading |
| **Base Damage** | 29 - 31 | High single-shot impact |
| **Firing Mode** | Bolt-Action | Slow follow-up potential |
| **Special Mechanic** | Horseback Bonus | Increased lethality while mounted |

As seen in the table above, the trade-off for high damage is the projectile speed. In Bridger: WESTERN, bullets aren't instantaneous. The Malcolm 70 features the slowest projectile travel time of any primary weapon. This means that at extreme ranges, you must aim where your target *will be*, rather than where they are currently standing.

## Mechanics of the Bridger Western Sniper

To effectively use the **bridger western sniper**, you must adapt to a very specific rhythm of play. Because the gun cannot be fired without the scope, your field of vision is constantly being restricted. This makes you vulnerable to "third-party" attacks from players who might be flanking your position while you are focused on a distant target.

### The Zoom Requirement
The Malcolm 70 is hard-coded to prevent hip-firing. If you attempt to click the fire button without holding the aim button, the weapon will simply not respond. This design choice forces the player into a dedicated sniper role. It prevents the rifle from being used as a makeshift shotgun in close range, which balances its high damage potential.

### Projectile Velocity and Leading Shots
Because the bullet travels so slowly, moving targets are your greatest enemy. If a target is running perpendicular to your position, you may need to aim several "body widths" ahead of them. 

> **Warning:** Do not expect this weapon to behave like snipers in hitscan-based shooters. The travel time is significant enough that targets can sometimes move out of the way after the shot has been fired.

## Strategic Positioning and "Ocular Prow"

In high-level play, visibility is everything. Many players utilize the "Ocular Prow" ability to spot enemies from distances that would normally be impossible. As a **bridger western sniper** user, you can exploit this by staying just outside the render distance or detection range of your enemies. 

### Finding the High Ground
The desert maps in Bridger: WESTERN are filled with plateaus and rocky outcroppings. Taking the high ground is not just a tactical cliché; it is a mechanical necessity for the Malcolm 70. 
1. **Scout the area:** Use your scope to identify targets who are distracted, such as those fishing or engaged in close-range duels.
2. **Set your mount:** Position your horse so you can quickly retreat if spotted.
3. **Lock in:** Once you have a target, stay zoomed in and wait for them to stop moving or enter a predictable path.

> **💡 Tip:** Targeting players who are fishing is a common tactic for leveling up your sniper skills. These targets are stationary and often lack the "Ocular Prow" awareness to see you coming.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I shoot my sniper rifle without zooming in?**

A: The Malcolm 70 is designed as a dedicated precision weapon. The game mechanics require you to use the long scope to fire, preventing it from being used effectively in close-range hip-fire scenarios. This is a balance feature intended to keep the **bridger western sniper** in its specific niche.

**Q: How do I deal more damage with the Malcolm 70?**

A: One of the most effective ways to increase your damage is to fire while mounted on your horse. Additionally, certain character cards can be rerolled to provide damage buffs or utility improvements specifically for long-range combat.

**Q: Is the Malcolm 70 the best gun in Bridger: WESTERN?**

A: It is highly situational. While it dominates at extreme ranges and offers high single-shot damage, its slow projectile speed and slow fire rate make it vulnerable to faster weapons like revolvers in close-to-mid-range combat.

**Q: How do I hit moving targets with such slow bullets?**

A: You must "lead" your shots. This means aiming at the space where the enemy will be by the time the bullet arrives. Because the Malcolm 70 has the slowest projectile speed in the game, this requires significant practice to master.
