## Finding the Repair NPC

Once the Rusty Mare's Leg is in your inventory, you will notice it cannot be equipped or fired. To fix this, you must locate a specific NPC specializing in gun restoration. This character is not found in the main town hubs like City Colony but is instead situated in a more rural location.

To find him, head toward **Ridge County**. From there, you will need to locate the large bridge. Cross the bridge and look for a lone NPC standing nearby. This character is the only one capable of cleaning the rust off your find and making it combat-ready.

## Retrieving Your Repaired Weapon

A common point of confusion for players is that the weapon does not immediately appear in your hands after you pay the 1,500 moola. Instead, the NPC "moves the gun to storage" for safety. To actually equip your newly restored Mare's Leg, you must travel to the **Bank** located in the City Colony.

1.  Enter the Bank building in the main city.
2.  Speak to the Bank Clerk NPC behind the counter.
3.  Select the "Open Storage" option.
4.  Find the **Mare's Leg** in your storage slots and click "Equip."

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ensure you have an open slot in your inventory before attempting to equip the weapon from the bank, or it may remain in storage until space is cleared.

## Mare's Leg vs. Winchester: Is it Worth It?

The Mare's Leg is often compared to the Winchester rifle, which is a staple for many players in [Bridger Western](https://www.roblox.com/games/14546654944/Bridger-Western). However, the two guns serve very different purposes. The Mare's Leg is essentially a "sawn-off" version of a lever-action rifle, offering better handling at the cost of raw power.

| Feature | Mare's Leg | Winchester Rifle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fire Rate** | **Fast** | Moderate |
| **Damage** | Lower | **Higher** |
| **Ammo Capacity** | 6 Rounds | **10+ Rounds** |
| **Range** | Medium | **Long** |
| **Obtainment** | Fishing + 1,500 Moola | Shop Purchase |

While the Mare's Leg looks incredibly stylish and offers a faster firing animation, many veteran players argue that the Winchester is the superior choice for serious combat. The Winchester's higher damage per shot and larger magazine size make it more reliable in a prolonged shootout. However, if you prefer a "speed-over-power" build, or simply want to show off a rare item, the Mare's Leg is a fantastic addition to your collection.

## Additional Quests and Secrets

The NPC who repairs your gun also offers a series of challenges that can help you recoup the 1,500 moola you just spent. These are skill-based "Can Shooting" quests.

*   **Tier 1:** Hit a thrown can 3 times in the air.

*   **Tier 3:** Hit a thrown can 6 times in the air.

Completing these challenges not only provides a cash reward but eventually unlocks the path to the **Vampire Quest**. This high-level questline takes you to the Vampire Ruins, offering some of the most unique gameplay experiences in the game. ## FAQ

**Q: Can I find the Rusty Mare's Leg anywhere other than fishing?**

A: Currently, fishing is the only confirmed way to obtain the rusted version of the gun. It is not sold by any vendors in its broken state.

**Q: What happens if I lose the gun after repairing it?**

A: Once repaired, the gun functions like any other owned weapon. It should remain in your bank storage even if you die, though you should always check the latest server rules regarding item drops upon death.

**Q: Is there a specific level required to use the Mare's Leg?**

A: There is no specific level requirement, but the 1,500 moola cost acts as a natural barrier for very new players. Most players find they have enough money by the time they successfully fish up the item.

**Q: How do I start the Vampire Quest mentioned by the NPC?**

A: You must first complete the series of can-shooting challenges offered by the repair NPC. After you demonstrate your accuracy by hitting the can multiple times, he will provide the dialogue to start the quest leading to the Vampire Ruins.

**Q: Why is the Mare's Leg doing less damage than my starter rifle?**

A: The Mare's Leg is designed for speed and style rather than raw damage. Its primary advantage is the faster fire rate, allowing you to get more shots off in a shorter window, which can be useful in close-quarters skirmishes where the Winchester might be too slow. Knowing **how to get rusty mares leg bridger western** is more about the journey and the unique fire rate than maximizing single-shot damage.
