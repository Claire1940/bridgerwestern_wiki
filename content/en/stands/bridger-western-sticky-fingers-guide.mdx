## How to Obtain Sticky Fingers

There are two primary ways to acquire Sticky Fingers in 2026. Because it is a Common Stand, your chances of pulling it from a standard gacha-style item are significantly higher than pulling rare variants like Star Platinum or The World.

### 1. The Corpse Part Method
This is the most "Western" way to get a Stand. You must find a Saint’s Corpse Part, which spawns randomly across the map. These parts are highly contested and will often lead to intense PvP shootouts.

*   **Spawn Trigger:** Requires at least **3 players** in the server.
*   **Visual Cues:** Look for a **Yellow Beam** and a **Sphere of Light**.
*   **Absorption:** Once you pick it up, you must hold it to "absorb" it. You will pulse yellow through walls, making you a target for everyone in the vicinity.
*   **Success Rate:** The longer you hold the part, the higher your chance to get a Stand (capping at 20%).

### 2. The Arrow Shard Method (Fishing)
If you prefer to avoid the "death traps" of the Dig Site, you can head to the Fishing Village. This is a pacifist route, though it requires immense patience.

*   **Drop Rate:** Arrow Shards have a **0.05% drop chance** from fishing.
*   **Benefit:** Unlike Corpse Parts, Arrow Shards are a guaranteed Stand pull and do not require the "survival" phase to activate.
*   **Inventory Rules:** Arrow Shards cannot be banked or dropped, but they are not lost upon death.

## How to Reroll or Remove Your Stand

If you followed this **bridger western sticky fingers guide** but decided you want to try for a higher-tier Stand like King Crimson, you cannot simply "delete" your current ability. You must undergo a specific ritual to "wipe your Path."

1.  **Find a Dogbane Herb:** This is a rare 4% drop found in chests from PvE encounters.
2.  **Locate the Mud Witch:** She resides in the swampy regions of the map.
3.  **Wipe Your Path:** Give her the herb and select the option to remove your Stand. This clears your slot, allowing you to use a new Arrow Shard or Corpse Part.

## Combat Strategy with Sticky Fingers

While Sticky Fingers is a C-Tier Stand, it excels in mobility and limb disruption. In the 2026 meta, players often rely on long-range snipers. Sticky Fingers allows you to close the gap or create environmental hazards that force snipers out of their cover.

*   **Barrage (Standard E):** Use this when an enemy is reloading their revolver. It deals consistent chip damage and prevents them from firing back immediately.
*   **Limb Targeting:** Focus on breaking the legs of your opponents. In Bridger Western, mobility is life. A player who cannot run is an easy target for your Stand's finishing moves.
*   **The Gun Tradeoff:** Since your gun damage is lower, use your Stand to "soften" the target before finishing them with a high-caliber sidearm.

## Advanced Tips for Stand Hunters

*   **The Sound of Success:** Corpse Parts emit a very distinct loud sound when they spawn. Keep your game volume up even while fishing so you can sprint to the nearest spawn location.
*   **The Yellow Pulse:** When carrying a Corpse Part, you are visible through walls. Never hide in a corner; stay moving and use the verticality of areas like Taco Bay to lose pursuers.
*   **Trading Economy:** If you pull a Stand you don't want, check the community Discord. Even Common Stands can sometimes be traded to collectors or new players for valuable herbs and utility items.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the bridger western sticky fingers guide relevant for mobile players?**

A: Yes, the mechanics for spawning and obtaining Stands are identical across all platforms. However, mobile players may find the "Dig Site" more difficult due to the precise jumping required to exit the pit via the rope.

**Q: Can I get Sticky Fingers through fishing?**

A: Yes. Sticky Fingers is part of the general Stand pool accessible via Arrow Shards. Since Arrow Shards are a Legendary fishing pull (0.05% chance), it is a safe but very slow way to obtain the Stand.

**Q: Does Sticky Fingers buff my melee damage?**

A: Yes, while your gun damage decreases, your Stand's physical attacks (like the Barrage) provide a new source of melee DPS that does not rely on ammunition.

**Q: What happens if I die while holding a Corpse Part?**

A: If you are knocked down or killed, you will drop the Corpse Part. Other players can then pick it up and begin their own absorption process. Always ensure you have full health before attempting to claim a part.
