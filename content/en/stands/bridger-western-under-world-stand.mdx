## Method 1: The Patient Angler's Path – Fishing for Stands

Fishing is often considered the safest and most accessible method for solo players to acquire a Stand, though it demands considerable patience. While it might be the "worst" in terms of immediate gratification due to its extremely low success rate, it's a consistent activity that can also yield money and other useful items.

To begin fishing, head to the fishing village, typically found by following the train tracks to the fishing area. Here, you can purchase a fishing rod and bait from an NPC. Once equipped, find a suitable fishing spot and cast your line. While primarily used for earning currency and leveling up, fishing offers a rare chance to reel in a Stand Arrow Shard.

> **💡 Tip:** Combine fishing with other activities. While casting your line, keep an eye on the server for announcements or visual cues indicating the spawn of a Corpse Part, allowing you to multitask effectively.

## Method 2: The High-Stakes Hunt – Corpse Parts

The most common and often fastest way to get a Stand in Bridger Western involves hunting for Corpse Parts. These valuable items spawn periodically across the map, and collecting them can grant you a Stand. However, this method comes with a significant catch: high competition and the need for strategic play.

Corpse Parts spawn more frequently in servers with a higher player count, roughly every 30 seconds. This creates a dynamic where more players mean more chances for spawns, but also more rivals vying for the same prize.

### Corpse Part Acquisition Strategies

Successfully acquiring Corpse Parts often depends on your server environment and whether you're playing solo or with a group.

#### Solo Player Strategy

For solo players, navigating the Corpse Part hunt requires careful consideration of server population:

*   **Low-Player Servers (3+ players):** Find a server with a minimal player count (e.g., 2-3 players). While Corpse Parts might spawn less frequently in these servers, the competition is drastically reduced. This increases your individual chances of securing a part if one appears, as you'll likely only contend with one or two other players, if any. To find low-player servers, go to the game's page on Roblox, scroll down to "Servers," and sort by "Ascending" player count.
*   **Popular Servers:** If you're confident in your combat skills and ability to outmaneuver others, a popular server offers a higher chance of Corpse Parts spawning. However, be prepared for intense PvP and fierce competition.

#### Group Player Strategy

Playing with friends significantly improves your chances of securing Corpse Parts. Teamwork can turn the tide in your favor:

*   **Small Friend Group (3+ players):** Coordinate with your friends to join a random server. When a Corpse Part spawns, work together to eliminate any competition. The group can then decide on a rotation or a fair system for who claims the part, ensuring everyone eventually gets a chance. The key is to protect the player attempting to pick up the Corpse Part.
*   **Organized Server Flooding (Large Group):** This is arguably the most effective, albeit requiring coordination, method for guaranteed Corpse Parts. By organizing a large group (e.g., 20+ players through a Discord server) to flood a low-player server, you can effectively control the environment. With an agreed-upon order, players can take turns collecting Corpse Parts with minimal, if any, external interference. This method almost guarantees a Stand for everyone involved, making it a highly sought-after strategy for obtaining powerful Stands like Tusk or Star Platinum.
