## D4C Move Set and Ability Breakdown

Once you have successfully unlocked D4C, you gain access to a versatile kit that focuses on high-frequency attacks and misdirection. While the stand offers incredible utility, it comes with a passive debuff: users of D4C typically have lower maximum HP than the average player. This makes mastering the **bridger western love train** defensive style mandatory for survival.

### Core Skills

*   **E Skill (Barrage):** A high-speed flurry of punches. Each hit deals approximately 6 damage. While the individual damage seems low, the sheer volume of hits can shred an opponent's health bar in seconds.
*   **R Skill (Auto-Tracking Chop):** A precision strike that tracks the nearest target. This move deals roughly 17 damage and is excellent for catching players who attempt to dodge or flee on horseback.
*   **T Skill (Clone Summon):** D4C reaches into another dimension to pull out a clone. These clones can spawn with different weapons, such as knives or guns, providing extra pressure on the battlefield.
*   **G Skill (Clone Switch):** This is the core of D4C’s survivability. By pressing G, you can instantaneously swap places with your active clone. If you are low on health, switching to a fresh clone can save your life and confuse your attacker.

## Advanced Combat Strategies

To truly master the **bridger western love train** playstyle, you must combine your stand abilities with the game's standard weaponry and equestrian mechanics. The most effective D4C users don't just rely on their stand; they use it to supplement their gunplay.

### The Horse Combo
One of the most devastating sequences in [Bridger: Western](https://www.roblox.com/games/13217434190/Bridger-Western) involves using your mount as a battering ram.
1.  **Initiate:** Trample the opponent with your horse to cause a stagger.
2.  **Follow-up:** Immediately use the **R Skill (Chop)** for guaranteed tracking damage.
3.  **Execute:** Finish the combo with a Winchest Repeater or a similar high-fire-rate firearm while the enemy is recovering from the knockback.

### Dimension Baiting
If you find yourself surrounded in a "ghost town" scenario where multiple players are targeting you, use the Poncho to enter the dimension. While inside, your clones remain in the physical world. If an enemy kills your clone, they often lower their guard, thinking they have defeated the real player. This is the perfect moment to exit the dimension and strike from behind.

## FAQ

**Q: How many kills do I actually need for D4C?**

A: You need a total of 100 confirmed kills on your account, followed by a specific 10-kill streak performed within the Desert Area while meeting the "knocked" requirement.

**Q: Why does my health look lower when I use D4C?**

A: This is a built-in balance mechanic. D4C is incredibly powerful due to its clones and dimensional hopping, so the game compensates by giving the user less base HP than other players. This makes the **bridger western love train** defensive poncho move even more important.

**Q: Can my clones use guns?**

A: Yes, clones summoned with the T skill can randomly spawn with different equipment, including knives and firearms. They will automatically target the nearest threat.

**Q: What happens if my clone dies?**

A: If your clone is destroyed, you lose the ability to swap positions (G skill) until you summon a new one. In 2026, players have become very good at "one-shotting" clones, so be prepared to summon them frequently during intense firefights.
