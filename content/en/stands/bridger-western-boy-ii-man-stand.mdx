## Understanding Stands in Bridger: Western

Stands are the core of character progression and combat power in *Bridger: Western*. These spectral companions offer unique abilities, from enhanced combat prowess to utility skills that can turn the tide of any encounter. There are two primary methods for obtaining a Stand: finding Corpse Parts or using Stand Arrows. Each method has its own set of challenges and probabilities, often influenced by server dynamics.

### Corpse Parts: The Golden Beam of Opportunity

Corpse Parts are highly sought-after items that periodically spawn across the map. When one appears, a prominent golden beam of light signals its location to all players on the server. Approaching and collecting a Corpse Part initiates a pulsing effect around your character. With each pulse, there's a 15% to 20% chance that you will obtain a Stand. This process can last several minutes, and success is not guaranteed, making the hunt for Corpse Parts a tense and competitive affair.

However, be warned: picking up a Corpse Part instantly makes you a target. The entire server will likely converge on your location, attempting to defeat you and claim the part for themselves. If you already possess a Stand, you cannot pick up another Corpse Part unless you reset your current Stand. This can be done by wiping your character or by using a specific plant obtained through fishing, which can then be given to the Witch for a Stand reset.

The probability of a Corpse Part spawning is directly tied to the number of players on the server. More players mean a higher chance for a Corpse Part to appear, but also fiercer competition.

| Server Player Count | Corpse Part Spawn Chance (Every 30 seconds) |
| :------------------ | :------------------------------------------ |
| **3 Players**       | 1 out of 150                                |
| **10 Players**      | 1 out of 115                                |
| **25 Players (Max)**| 1 out of 40                                 |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Engaging with Corpse Parts is a high-risk, high-reward activity. Always be prepared for intense player-versus-player combat. Consider teaming up with allies to increase your chances of survival and successful Stand acquisition.

### Fishing for Stand Arrows

For a less confrontational, albeit more patient, approach to Stand acquisition, fishing offers a unique opportunity to find a Stand Arrow Shard. While not as direct as Corpse Parts, these shards can also lead you to a Stand. To begin, head to Red Corner and purchase a fishing rod and some bait from the dockside shop.

Fishing involves waiting for bubbles to appear near your rod, then clicking to reel in your catch. Most of the time, you'll catch fish that can be sold for money or other common items. However, there's a legendary, albeit rare, outcome: an Arrow Shard.

| Fishing Result     | Chance      | Notes                                    |
| :----------------- | :---------- | :--------------------------------------- |
| **Common Fish**    | High        | Sell for money, gain XP                  |
| **Chests**         | Moderate    | Contains money or other useful items     |
| **Stand Arrow Shard** | **0.5%**    | Legendary result, can grant a Stand      |

Once you obtain a Stand Arrow Shard, you can use it to attempt to gain a Stand. The specifics of using the shard are not as detailed as Corpse Parts, but it serves as an alternative path for those who prefer to avoid direct PvP encounters for their initial Stand. This method could potentially lead to a variety of Stands, including the coveted **Bridger Western Boy II Man Stand**, if it's part of the general Stand pool.

## Beyond Stands: Talents and Character Progression

While Stands are a major power component, *Bridger: Western* offers other avenues for character development. Talents, for instance, provide additional perks and abilities that can complement your chosen Stand or enhance your base character.

To acquire talents, you must visit the Witch's Hut, often found in swampy areas. Talking to the Witch and choosing "Read my fortune" will reveal a selection of talents. The first talent you choose is free, but subsequent choices or re-rolls will cost money. Your character's "Tier" (level equivalent) determines how many talent slots you unlock, with a maximum of three.

| Tier Progression | Talent Slots Unlocked | Cost for New Talent/Re-roll |
| :--------------- | :-------------------- | :-------------------------- |
| **Tier 1**       | 1                     | Free (first choice)         |
| **Tier 2**       | 2                     | 50 Mula                     |
| **Tier 3**       | 3                     | 50 Mula                     |

Talents are not permanent; you can always change them by revisiting the Witch and paying the fee. This allows for flexibility in your build as you experiment with different Stands, including the potential **Bridger Western Boy II Man Stand**, and playstyles.

Another important, albeit less direct, progression system is your character's age. Accessible via the 'M' menu, your age increases by 1-2 each time you die. Combat logging (disconnecting during combat) results in a much harsher penalty, increasing your age by 10. While growing old causes cosmetic changes like wrinkles and gray hair, there's a common misconception about dying of old age. The game's Trello suggests it's primarily cosmetic, so don't fear an automatic wipe from old age in 2026.
