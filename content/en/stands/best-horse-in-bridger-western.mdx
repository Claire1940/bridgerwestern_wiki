## Where to Find the Horse Seller

Before you can start hunting for a top-tier mount, you need to know where the market is. Most players begin their journey near the fishing area. Locate the tackle base store and the docks; from there, a well-traveled path leads directly to the Horse Seller NPC.

When you interact with the Horse Seller, you are presented with two primary options:
1.  **I'll take it**: Purchases the horse currently on display.
2.  **Show me another**: Rerolls the available horse for a small fee, allowing you to cycle through different stats, rarities, and prices.

> **💡 Tip:** Never buy the first horse you see. Always keep enough gold on hand to reroll at least 10–20 times to ensure you aren't settling for a low-tier pack mule.

## Understanding Horse Stats and the 1.25 Cap

In Bridger Western, four primary stats define a horse's performance. Understanding these is crucial for determining which animal fits your specific playstyle.

| Stat | Impact on Gameplay | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Speed** | Determines the maximum velocity of the horse. | **High** |
| **Stamina** | Dictates how long the horse can maintain a full gallop. | **High** |
| **Jump** | Affects the height and distance of leaps over obstacles. | **Low** |
| **Damage** | Increases the impact damage if you trample NPCs or players. | **Medium** |

For the average player, **Speed and Stamina** are the "holy grail" of stats. A horse with high jump might look impressive, but it won't help you cross the desert before your water runs out. 

The most important mechanic to remember in 2026 is the **1.25 Stat Cap**. For a standard horse, no individual stat can exceed a value of 1.25. This serves as the baseline for "perfect" normal horses. If you find a horse with 1.25 in both speed and stamina, you have found one of the best non-rarity mounts in the game.

Always look for the descriptors the NPC uses, such as "Strong legs" or "Real beauty," as these hint at high hidden values.

## Legendary vs. Mythical Horses

Rarity in Bridger Western is indicated by the horse's name formatting. This is where many players get confused. A rarity tag does not mean the horse is "good"; it means the horse has the **potential** to break the standard stat caps.

### Legendary Horses
*   **Visual Indicator**: The name is in ALL CAPS with standard brackets (e.g., `[OLD MOON]`).
*   **Stat Boost**: One random stat is allowed to exceed the 1.25 cap, often reaching up to **1.35**.
*   **The Risk**: If the boosted stat is "Jump," the horse might still be slower than a 1,200-gold normal horse.

### Mythical Horses
*   **Visual Indicator**: The name is in ALL CAPS with thick, double brackets (e.g., `[[SHADOW]]`).
*   **Stat Boost**: Two random stats can exceed the 1.25 cap.
*   **Unique Features**: Mythical horses often come with unique coat colors not found on standard mounts.

> **Warning:** A legendary horse can cost as little as 500 gold. This usually indicates that while it *can* break the cap, its other stats are likely abysmal. Don't be blinded by the brackets!

## How to Reroll for the Best Horse

To find the absolute **best horse in bridger western**, you should follow a specific rerolling strategy. Since you cannot see the exact numerical stats until after the purchase, you must rely on the price and the NPC's dialogue.

1.  **Save your Gold**: Do not start serious rerolling until you have at least 2,500 gold. This covers the cost of a high-end horse and the reroll fees.
2.  **Identify the Target**: Look for horses priced between **1,200 and 1,600 gold**. 
3.  **Check for Rarity**: If a horse in that price range also has an **ALL CAPS** name, you have hit the jackpot. This means you are getting a horse with a high base stat pool *plus* the ability to break the 1.25 cap.
4.  **The 15-Minute Wait**: After purchasing a horse, its full stats are hidden behind a timer. You must wait approximately **916 seconds (15 minutes)** before the true potential of the horse is revealed in your menu.

## FAQ

**Q: Does a Legendary horse always have better stats than a normal horse?**

A: No. A legendary horse simply has the *ability* to have one stat go above the 1.25 limit. If the rest of its stats are low, a high-priced normal horse (1,400+ gold) will often outperform it in daily use.

**Q: What is the maximum speed a horse can reach?**

A: For a normal horse, the cap is 1.25. For the **best horse in bridger western** (Legendary or Mythical), the speed can reach approximately 1.35, though some players have reported slight variations depending on the specific horse roll.

**Q: Can I change my horse's name or color?**

A: Colors are predetermined at the time of purchase and are often tied to rarity (especially for Mythicals). Names are also generated by the Horse Seller, and the formatting (brackets/caps) is a permanent indicator of the horse's rarity tier.

**Q: Why can't I see my horse's stats immediately after buying it?**

A: This is a built-in game mechanic designed to add a sense of "breaking in" the animal. You must wait about 15 minutes (916 seconds) before the detailed stat breakdown becomes visible in your UI.
