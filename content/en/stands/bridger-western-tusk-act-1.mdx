## How to Obtain Tusk Act 1

Acquiring Tusk is not as simple as finding a random item on the map. It requires a specific "Saint’s Corpse" part and a significant amount of patience due to the RNG involved. You cannot use a Right Arm, a Rib Cage, or a Leg to unlock this specific stand; it must be the **Left Arm**.

| Item Required | Drop Source | Success Chance | Stand Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Saint's Left Arm** | Corpse Part Spawns / Server Floods | **10%** | Tusk Act 1 |
| Saint's Right Arm | Corpse Part Spawns | 0% | Different Stand |
| Saint's Rib Cage | Corpse Part Spawns | 0% | Different Stand |

To maximize your chances, many players participate in "server floods." This involves a coordinated group of players entering a server to trigger corpse part spawns. Once a Left Arm is found, the player wishing to obtain Tusk must use it. Note that even with the correct part, there is only a 10% chance of the stand manifesting. If you fail, you will need to find another Left Arm and try again.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Obtaining Tusk is a permanent commitment to the "Crippled" status. Once you acquire the stand, your character will lose the ability to walk or run normally.

## The Passives: Understanding the "Crippled" Status

The moment you unlock Tusk, your character undergoes a fundamental transformation. This is a "passive-heavy" stand that significantly alters your interaction with the game world. 

### 1. Permanent Mobility Lock
You can no longer walk or run. Your default movement state is crawling, which is incredibly slow and leaves you vulnerable to almost every enemy in the game. To compensate, you gain a **Roll** mechanic, which provides a brief burst of speed and can be used to dodge incoming projectiles.

### 2. Weapon Restriction
Tusk users are physically unable to hold traditional firearms or melee weapons. If you have a high-tier Revolver or a Saber in your inventory, the game will automatically prevent you from equipping them. You must rely entirely on your Stand abilities and your horse for combat.

### 3. Horse Dependency
Because you cannot walk, your horse becomes your lifeline. In Bridger: Western, a Tusk user without a horse is essentially a sitting duck. It is highly recommended to invest in a **Legendary or Mythical Horse** (costing approximately $1,400) to ensure you have the speed and stamina necessary to maintain distance from opponents.

## Tusk Act 1 Move Set & Mechanics

Act 1 is the foundation of the Tusk evolution line. While it lacks the raw power of later acts, it provides unique utility that remains useful even as you progress. You can switch between acts by pressing the **V** key once you have unlocked them.

| Keybind | Move Name | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **E (Toggle)** | Gun Form | Enters a combat stance where you can fire nail bullets. |
| **Click (in E)** | Nail Bullets | Rapid-fire projectiles. Headshots deal approx. **19 damage**. |
| **R** | Nail Glide | Uses rotation to glide across the ground for a short duration. |
| **G** | Nail Jump | A small vertical hop. **Exclusive to Act 1**. |
| **T** | Melee Strike | A short-range physical hit used for self-defense. |

Act 1’s strength lies in its "mini-gun" capability. While individual bullets deal lower damage than Act 2 or 3, the fire rate is significantly higher and consumes less stamina. This makes it the ideal form for finishing off low-health targets or suppressing enemies while your higher-damage moves are on cooldown.

## Best Perks for Tusk Builds

Since you cannot run away from a fight, your build must focus on durability and resource management. The following perks are considered the "Meta" for Tusk users in 2026.

| Perk Name | Effect | Why It’s Essential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sturdy** | +15% Max Health | Allows you to survive an extra shot from high-caliber rifles. |
| **Time Heals All Wounds** | Passive HP Regen | Crucial since you cannot easily retreat to find cover or use items. |
| **Free Runner** | +20 Stamina | While you can't slide, the extra stamina is vital for **Rolling** and keeping your Stand active. |

> **💡 Tip:** The "Free Runner" perk is often misunderstood. Even though the "Slide" mechanic is disabled for crippled users, the raw stamina boost allows you to stay in Act 3 longer, which has a much higher drain rate than Act 1.

## Advanced Combat Strategies

To succeed with **bridger western tusk act 1**, you must master the art of "Horse-Kiting." This involves circling your opponent at high speeds while firing your nail bullets. Because Act 2 (the next evolution) features homing bullets, you can often hit targets without precise aiming, but Act 1 requires more discipline.

### The Stamina Reset Trick
A common strategy used by top-tier Tusk mains involves the **V** key. If your stamina bar is nearly empty, rapidly cycling through your Acts (pressing V repeatedly) can sometimes reset the drain rate or allow for a faster recovery period. This is essential during long-drawn-out skirmishes where you cannot afford to de-summon your stand.

### Positional Play
Always seek the high ground. Since your Nail Glide move allows you to move horizontally quickly, being on a roof or a cliffside gives you a massive advantage. Enemies will have to climb to reach you, during which time you can pelt them with Act 1’s rapid-fire nails.

## Evolution: Moving Beyond Act 1

While this guide focuses on Act 1, it is important to know what comes next. As you grow stronger, you will unlock Acts 2 and 3.

*   **Act 2:** Introduces the "Homing" mechanic. If you miss a shot but the bullet hits a surface near the enemy, the "hole" will travel toward them. It also features a high-damage **Sniper Shot (G)** that can deal upwards of 90 damage on a headshot.
*   **Act 3:** Focuses on spatial manipulation. Bullets hit twice, creating a "wormhole" effect that deals massive damage to stationary targets.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I ever walk again after getting Tusk Act 1?**

A: No. In the current 2026 version of Bridger: Western, the crippled status is a permanent trade-off for the stand's power. You must use a horse or the Nail Glide ability for all movement.

**Q: Is Tusk Act 1 viable for solo players?**

A: It is difficult. Tusk is designed as a "Glass Cannon" or support stand. You are at your best when a teammate can distract enemies while you provide long-range fire. If caught alone without a horse, most humanoid stands like *The World* or *Star Platinum* will easily overwhelm you.

**Q: What happens if my horse dies in combat?**

A: You become extremely vulnerable. You should always carry horse revivers or high-quality fodder to keep your mount healthy. Without a horse, your only defensive options are the Roll and the Melee Strike.

**Q: How do I increase the damage of my nail bullets?**

A: Damage scales with your Act level and certain character stats. While Act 1 is meant for suppressive fire, transitioning to Act 2 or 3 will significantly increase your burst damage potential.
