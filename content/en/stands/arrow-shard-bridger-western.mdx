## What is an Arrow Shard in Bridger Western?

The Stand Arrow Shard is a Legendary-tier consumable item. Unlike standard equipment, its sole purpose is to grant the player a random Stand from the available pool. In the lore of the game, these shards are fragments of a larger, mystical arrow that chooses those worthy of great power.

When you use an arrow shard from your inventory, your character will undergo a transformation, manifesting a Stand. It is important to note that Stands function alongside your traditional weaponry. While your revolvers handle long-range engagements, your Stand provides melee protection, specialized skills, and unique buffs that can turn the tide of a duel.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you already possess a Stand, using an **arrow shard bridger western** will result in nothing. The item will be consumed, but your Stand will not change. To reroll your Stand, you must seek out Corpse Parts or use specific in-game reset mechanics.

### Step-by-Step Fishing Method

To maximize your efficiency and reduce the downtime between casts, follow this optimized community-tested method:

1.  **Find a Quiet Spot:** Travel to a remote dock or shoreline where other players won't disturb you or initiate combat.
2.  **Organize Your Hotbar:** Place your Fishing Rod and your Bait in adjacent slots (e.g., slots 1 and 2).
3.  **The Bait Trick:** Click on your Bait first, then immediately click on your Fishing Rod. This ensures the bait is applied correctly to the rod, which significantly increases the frequency of fish biting.
4.  **Monitor the QTE:** When a legendary item like an arrow shard hooks, a Quick Time Event (QTE) will appear. For the Arrow Shard, this QTE is significantly longer and more difficult than the one for standard chests or the Rusty Mares Leg.
5.  **Reapply After Every Catch:** Every time you successfully pull an item out of the water, you must re-click the bait and then the rod to ensure the next cast is buffed.

## Arrow Shards vs. Corpse Parts

While the **arrow shard bridger western** is the go-to for beginners, veteran players often transition to hunting **Corpse Parts**. Understanding the difference between these two "Stand-givers" is vital for high-level play.

| Feature | Stand Arrow Shard | Saint's Corpse Part |
|---|---|---|
| **Obtainment** | Fishing (0.05% chance) | Map Spawn (Yellow Beacon) |
| **Spawn Rate** | Constant (based on fishing speed) | Every 30 seconds (RNG based) |
| **Difficulty** | Low (Patience/QTE) | High (Server-wide PvP) |
| **Rarity Tier** | Random (Includes Commons) | Guaranteed Rare/Default |
| **Player Requirement** | 1 Player | 3+ Players for spawns |

Corpse Parts are highly contested. When one spawns, a yellow beacon of light appears on the map. You must claim the part and hold it for 30 seconds without dying to absorb its power. The odds of a Corpse Part spawning increase with the number of players on the server. On a full server of 25 people, the odds are as high as 1 in 40 every 30 seconds.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling with the 0.05% fishing rate, try joining a high-population server and team up with friends to secure a Corpse Part instead.
