## How to Obtain the Tusk Stand

To begin your journey with Tusk, you must first secure a specific Saint's Corpse part. While other stands might accept various items, Tusk is strictly tied to the **Left Arm of the Saint**. These parts spawn randomly across the map, and competition for them is often fierce.

### The Left Arm Requirement
Once you possess the Left Arm, using it does not guarantee the stand immediately. There is a **5% to 10% chance** of obtaining Tusk upon using the item. If the attempt fails, the item is consumed, and you must find another. 

| Item Required | Spawn Type | Success Rate | Stand Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Arm of the Saint** | World Spawn | 5% - 10% | Tusk Act 1 |
| **Saint's Ribcage** | World Spawn | 0% | Unrelated Stands |
| **Saint's Right Arm** | World Spawn | 0% | Unrelated Stands |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Obtaining Tusk will immediately delete all weapons in your inventory, including guns and sabers. You will be unable to purchase or equip weapons as long as you hold this stand.

### The "Crippled" Passive
The most significant change when you acquire Tusk is your mobility. Your character will lose the ability to walk or run normally. Instead, you will crawl on the ground, making you extremely vulnerable to players with high-mobility stands like Star Platinum or The World. To mitigate this, you must rely entirely on a high-quality horse for travel.

## Evolution: Tusk Act 1 through Act 3

The **bridger western tusk act stand** is not a static power. By pressing the **V key**, players can cycle through the different Acts they have unlocked. Currently, Acts 1 through 3 are available in the game, with Act 4 anticipated in future 2026 updates.

### Tusk Act 1: The Suppression Specialist
Act 1 is characterized by high-speed, low-damage nail projectiles. It functions similarly to a mini-gun, allowing you to overwhelm opponents with a constant stream of fire.

*   **Nail Bullets (E + Click):** Rapid-fire nails that deal approximately 10 damage per body shot and 19 per headshot.
*   **Nail Glide:** A short-duration movement ability that allows you to slide across the ground.
*   **Jump:** A small vertical boost exclusive to Act 1, providing a minor reprieve from your crawling state.

### Tusk Act 2: The Sniper
Act 2 shifts the focus from quantity to quality. This form introduces homing mechanics and massive burst damage, making it the preferred choice for many competitive players.

*   **Sniper Shot (G):** A high-damage projectile that can deal 60+ damage and frequently removes limbs from the target.
*   **Homing Nails:** If a nail misses but lands near an opponent, it will "track" toward them, making it very difficult to dodge in open terrain.
*   **Nail Traps:** You can shoot the ground to set up traps that fire at enemies who wander too close.

### Tusk Act 3: The Wormhole
Act 3 is often considered the most niche of the current forms. It focuses on spatial manipulation through "wormhole" bullets.

*   **Spatial Nails:** When a nail hits a surface or an enemy, it creates a secondary hit effect, essentially hitting the target twice for approximately 35 total damage.
*   **Nail Glide:** Standard movement shared with the previous acts.

## Survival Strategies for Tusk Users

Playing as a Tusk user in Bridger Western requires a complete shift in strategy. Because you are vulnerable on the ground, your preparation determines your success.

### 1. Invest in a Legendary Horse
Since your base movement is a crawl, your horse is your lifeblood. Do not attempt to use Tusk with a standard horse. Aim for a **Legendary or Mythical horse** to ensure you can outrun aggressive players and maintain distance for your nail shots.

### 2. Manage Your Resources
Tusk utilizes two resource bars:
1.  **Stamina (Blue Bar):** Consumed by movement abilities like Nail Glide.
2.  **Nail Count (Pink Bar):** Consumed by firing projectiles. 
Running out of nails in the middle of a fight is a death sentence, as you cannot switch to a sidearm gun.

### 3. Use the Environment
Because Act 2 and Act 3 have homing and double-hit properties, you should fight in areas with cover. Shooting a wall near an enemy with Act 2 nails allows the homing property to take effect, hitting targets hiding behind corners.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a "Log Main Herb" in your bank. If you find the crippled lifestyle too difficult, this herb is the only way to remove the stand and regain your ability to walk.

## Advanced Farming: The Discord Method

Finding the Left Arm of the Saint solo can take dozens of hours. Professional players in 2026 utilize "Private Server Rotations." By joining active community Discord servers, you can participate in organized item hunts.

*   **Server Flooding:** Large groups join a server to force item spawns.
*   **Priority Lists:** Groups create a list of who gets the next Corpse Part. If you are hunting for Tusk, you negotiate for the Left Arm while others take Ribcages or Legs.
*   **Protection:** Since Tusk users are crippled, having a teammate with a defensive stand like White Snake or Star Platinum can protect you while you farm the XP needed for future evolutions.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use Tusk Act 4 in Bridger Western?**

A: Currently, Act 4 is not implemented in the game. Developers have hinted at its arrival later in 2026, where it is expected to be the strongest stand in the game, likely requiring a "Saint's Eye" or a specific quest involving the Spin.

**Q: Why did my guns disappear when I got the bridger western tusk act stand?**

A: This is a built-in mechanic to balance the stand. Tusk users are "Spin" users who rely entirely on their nails. You cannot hold any weapons, including sabers or pistols, while Tusk is equipped.

**Q: What is the best way to get XP for stand missions?**

A: The most efficient ways to earn XP are fishing, killing NPCs, and participating in the Red Corner boxing matches. Boxing matches provide roughly 50 XP per win, though fishing is often safer for crippled Tusk users.

**Q: Is Tusk better than Star Platinum The World?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. SPTW is better for close-quarters combat and time-stop utility. Tusk is superior for long-range harassment and horseback combat. If you prefer staying mobile on a horse, Tusk is the better choice.
