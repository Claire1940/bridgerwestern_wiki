## Move Set and Keybinds Breakdown

The **bridger western white snake** is not designed to be a "tank" or a high-DPS brawler. Instead, it relies on a series of tactical moves that disable the opponent. Its primary strength lies in its ability to remove an enemy's stand entirely, leaving them defenseless for a significant duration.

### Combat Abilities Table

| Keybind | Move Name | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **E** | Barrage | A standard rapid-punch attack. Low damage but useful for keeping enemies at bay. |
| **R** | Disc Steal (Grab) | Grabs the enemy and extracts their stand as a physical **Disc**. |
| **T** | Disc Shot | Fires a projectile that inverts the target's controls. Missed shots may explode. |
| **V** | Pilot Mode | Allows the user to control the stand remotely, similar to Hierophant Green. |
| **G** | Poison Spit | The ultimate move; creates a poison puddle that induces a 7-second sleep stun. |

### The Disc Mechanic
The most iconic feature of the **bridger western white snake** is the "R" move. When you successfully land this grab, the opponent's stand is physically removed from their body and dropped on the ground as a CD/Disc. 

*   **Stand Lockdown:** The victim cannot use any stand abilities while their disc is on the ground.
*   **Retrieval:** The victim must walk over to the disc and pick it up to regain their powers.
*   **Auto-Return:** If the disc is not picked up, it will automatically return to the owner after approximately 30 to 60 seconds.
*   **Safety:** Other players cannot steal the disc; only the original owner can interact with it to restore their stand.

## Advanced Tactics and Pilot Mechanics

The **bridger western white snake** is one of the few stands that features a full **Pilot Mode (V)**. This allows the user to hide behind cover while the stand traverses the battlefield. In Bridger Western, the pilot mode has significant range and stamina, making it ideal for scouting and initiating fights without putting the player character at risk.

### PvP Strategy: The "Hacker" Playstyle
In PvP, your goal is to be as annoying as possible. Use the **Disc Shot (T)** to invert an enemy's controls. While they are struggling to move correctly, close the distance and use the **Disc Steal (R)**. Once their stand is on the ground, follow up with the **Poison Spit (G)**.

The Poison Spit creates a purple puddle. Anyone who steps into this puddle is inflicted with a "Sleep" status that lasts for roughly 7 seconds. This is currently one of the longest stuns in the game. During this window, you can land a full barrage or reposition yourself for the next cycle of abilities.

### PvE Utility: NPC Alliance
A little-known feature of the White Snake's **T move** is its interaction with NPCs. If you hit a hostile NPC (such as a zombie or a guard) with the Disc Shot, they will stop attacking you. Instead, they will become allied to you for a short duration and begin attacking other players in the vicinity. This makes White Snake an excellent choice for navigating dangerous areas or distracting mobs while you focus on a specific objective.

| Status Effect | Duration | Impact on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Inverted Controls** | ~5-10 Seconds | Swaps WASD inputs, making movement difficult. |
| **Stand Loss** | 30-60 Seconds | Removes all stand-related skills until the disc is retrieved. |
| **Sleep/Poison** | ~7 Seconds | Complete immobilization; the longest stun currently in-game. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** While in Pilot Mode, your physical body is vulnerable. Always ensure you are hidden in a bush or behind a building before activating the V key.

## White Snake vs. GER: Which is Better?

Since many players received the **bridger western white snake** as a replacement for Gold Experience Requiem, the question of which stand is superior is frequently debated. While White Snake has incredible crowd control, GER is widely considered to have higher raw damage and better defensive "reversal" capabilities.

White Snake is a "technical" stand. It requires the player to land skill shots and manage the battlefield. If you enjoy a playstyle that involves disabling your opponent and making them play by your rules, White Snake is the superior choice. However, if you prefer a stand that can brute-force its way through a fight with high-damage barrages and healing, you may find White Snake's lower damage output frustrating.

## FAQ

**Q: Can other players steal the stand disc once it is on the ground?**

A: No. While the disc is a physical object on the floor, only the player who lost the stand can pick it up to reclaim it. Other players cannot interact with it or "steal" the stand for themselves.

**Q: How long does it take to get my stand back if I can't find the disc?**

A: If you are hit by a **bridger western white snake** and lose your stand, it will automatically return to you after 30 to 60 seconds if you do not manually pick up the disc.

**Q: Is White Snake good for farming NPCs?**

A: Yes, it is excellent for PvE. The ability to turn NPCs into allies using the T move allows you to clear waves of enemies without taking damage, and the poison puddle (G) provides great area-of-effect control.

**Q: Does White Snake have a pilot range limit?**

A: Like most pilot stands in the game, there is a maximum distance the stand can travel from the user. However, the range is quite generous, allowing you to engage in combat from a safe distance across most urban environments in the game.
