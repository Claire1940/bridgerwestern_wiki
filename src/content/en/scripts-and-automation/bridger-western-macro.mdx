## How to Obtain Stands: The Definitive Ranking

Stands are the pinnacle of power in Bridger: Western, but obtaining them is notoriously difficult. Currently, there are three primary ways to secure a Stand, each with its own risk-to-reward ratio. While some players prefer the high-octane combat of fighting over Corpse Parts, others opt for the "set it and forget it" approach by utilizing a **bridger western macro** for long fishing sessions.

### 1. Fishing (The Safe RNG Method)
Fishing is widely considered the most time-consuming method, but it is also the safest. You have a **0.5% chance** to pull an Arrow Shard from the water. Because this process is entirely luck-based and involves repetitive inputs, it is the primary activity where a **bridger western macro** provides the most utility.

| Method | Success Rate | Risk Level | Time Investment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fishing** | 0.5% (Arrow Shard) | Low | Very High |
| **Corpse Parts** | Variable (Server Based) | High | Medium |
| **Trading** | 100% (Guaranteed) | Low | Variable |

### 2. Corpse Parts (The Combat Method)
Corpse Parts spawn randomly across the map. The spawn rate is tied to the number of players in the server. If you are a solo player, your best bet is to join a "low-player" server (at least 3 players) to minimize competition. However, be aware that the spawn frequency decreases with fewer people.

### 3. Trading
Trading is a relatively new feature that allows players to swap Stands. To participate, you generally need to be part of the official community Discord. Trading windows often open at specific times of the day, so staying active in the community is essential for those looking for specific Stands like Star Platinum or Tusk.

## Optimizing Your Farming with a Bridger Western Macro

The grind for an Arrow Shard can take hours, if not days, of manual clicking. A **bridger western macro** is often used by veteran players to automate the fishing mini-game. By setting up a script that detects the "bite" notification and automatically reels in the line, you can farm for shards while away from your keyboard.

> **⚠️ Warning:** While macros are common in the community for fishing, always ensure you are following the latest game rules. Over-automating combat or movement can lead to account flags in some Roblox experiences.

To maximize your efficiency while using a **bridger western macro**, consider the following tips:
*   **Location Matters:** Find a secluded fishing spot away from high-traffic PvP zones to avoid being killed while AFK.
*   **Inventory Management:** Ensure you have enough space or a way to clear "trash" fish so your inventory doesn't clog up, preventing you from receiving the Arrow Shard.
*   **Server Choice:** Use the server browser to find a server with 3-5 players. This keeps the Stand spawn mechanics active without attracting large gangs of hunters.

## Cards and Talents: Building Your Character

Cards (or Talents) provide passive buffs that can drastically alter your PvP performance. Choosing the right cards is just as important as your weapon choice. If you are using a **bridger western macro** to farm silver, you will eventually have enough currency to roll for the best possible kit.

### Essential PvP Cards
1.  **High Noon (Ocular Vision):** This provides an auto-aim feature when pressing X. The trade-off is a permanent **-20% Max HP** penalty. If you miss your shots during the auto-aim window, you are left extremely vulnerable.
2.  **Veteran:** Perfect for players who struggle with ammo management. It helps you keep track of your rounds and improves reload efficiency.
3.  **Time Heals All Wounds:** Provides essential health regeneration, allowing you to stay in the fight longer during drawn-out skirmishes.
4.  **Show Me a Good Time:** Specifically for Saber users. This card increases Saber damage by 10% and allows you to **parry bullets**, making you a nightmare for gun-users.

## Advanced Survival Strategies

In the world of Bridger: Western, protection is just as important as firepower. Accessories like the **Cowboy Hat** are mandatory for any serious player, as they provide protection against headshots—a mechanic that can end a fight instantly.

*   **The Lasso:** Use this to tie up opponents, rendering them immobile while you line up a finishing shot with a Whitney Dragon.
*   **Silver Bullets:** Always keep a stack of silver bullets in your bank. With the 2026 updates introducing Vampires, silver is the only reliable way to deal damage to these supernatural threats besides using Hamon or specific Stands.
*   **Server Hopping:** If a server is dominated by a large gang, don't be afraid to hop. Use the "Ascending" sort method on the server list to find quiet environments where you can farm in peace.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are part of a friend group, "flood" a low-player server together. This ensures that when a Corpse Part spawns, your group has a 100% chance of securing it, allowing you to take turns getting Stands for everyone in the party.

## FAQ

**Q: Is using a bridger western macro allowed for fishing?**

A: Most players use basic macros for the fishing mini-game due to the low 0.5% drop rate of Arrow Shards. However, you should always check the official game Discord for the latest rules on automation to ensure your account remains in good standing.

**Q: Which gun is best for a new player?**

A: The Scullfield is the best all-around choice. It deals significantly more damage than the starter Taurus but has much more manageable recoil than the Whitney Dragon, making it ideal for learning the game's combat mechanics.

**Q: How do I survive against Vampire players?**

A: You must use Silver Bullets or Silver Stakes. Standard ammunition does very little damage to Vampires. Additionally, having a Stand or Hamon is highly recommended for dealing with supernatural threats in the 2026 update.

**Q: How do I find low-player servers for farming?**

A: Go to the Bridger: Western game page on Roblox, scroll down to "Servers," change the sort order to "Ascending," and look for servers with 3-5 players. This is the "sweet spot" for spawning Corpse Parts with minimal interference.
